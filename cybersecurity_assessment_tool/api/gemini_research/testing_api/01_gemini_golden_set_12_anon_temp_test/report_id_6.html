Analysis of Control Gaps

The questionnaire reveals critical weaknesses in access control and security governance:

  • MFA Gaps: The lack of MFA on email and sensitive data systems is a vulnerability. Email is a primary target for phishing attacks, and a compromised account can lead to widespread system access. Protecting sensitive data without MFA fails to meet industry best practices.

  • Policy Gaps: The absence of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) creates ambiguity for employees regarding the proper use of company assets and data, increasing the risk of insider threats and compliance violations.

  • Training Gaps: While training for new employees is a good first step, security is an ongoing effort. Without annual refresher training, employee knowledge of current threats (like new phishing techniques) degrades, making them more susceptible to social engineering attacks.

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are prioritized based on the risk assessment. Addressing these items will significantly improve the security posture of [Organization Name].

Critical Priority

Implement MFA on All Email and Sensitive Data Systems:

This is the single most effective control to prevent unauthorized access to critical systems and data. All user accounts, especially those with administrative privileges, must be protected.

  • Justification: Mitigates risks from stolen credentials, phishing, and brute-force attacks. It is a foundational element of modern cybersecurity defense.

  • Action: Procure and deploy an MFA solution compatible with your email provider (e.g., O365, Google Workspace) and other critical applications immediately.

High Priority

Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

Create a formal policy that clearly defines the rules for using company networks, devices, and data. This policy should be signed by all employees.

  • Justification: Establishes a baseline for secure employee behavior, reduces insider risk, and provides a legal framework for enforcing security rules.

  • Action: Draft an AUP covering topics such as data handling, internet usage, and personal device use. Integrate it into the new-hire onboarding process and distribute it to all current employees.

Establish a Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training Program:

Implement a recurring training program for all employees that covers current cyber threats, company policies, and best practices.

  • Justification: The threat landscape evolves continuously. Regular training ensures that the "human firewall" remains effective against phishing, social engineering, and other common attack vectors.

  • Action: Select a training platform or develop a curriculum. Schedule and track completion for all employees on an annual basis.

Informational

Update Internal Risk Register:

The risk "Unencrypted Web Server" has been validated as mitigated by the external network scan.

  • Justification: Maintaining an accurate risk register is crucial for focusing resources on active threats.

  • Action: Formally close the risk item related to port 80 in your internal documentation.
