Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity posture assessment conducted for [Organization Name]. The assessment combined a review of self-reported security controls with an analysis of technical scan data and pre-existing risk information.

The analysis revealed several critical and high-risk deficiencies in foundational security controls. The most pressing concerns stem from the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and endpoint access, the absence of a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and inadequate security training for new employees. These gaps significantly elevate the risk of unauthorized access, data breaches, and insider threats.

Furthermore, the provided technical network scan data and the list of current organizational risks were found to be corrupted and unusable. This represents a significant operational risk, as it creates a critical blind spot regarding the external attack surface and the organization’s known vulnerability landscape.

Immediate remediation is required to address the identified control gaps. Recommendations focus on the rapid implementation of MFA, development of core security policies, and establishment of a mandatory security training program. A full rescan of the external network is also a top priority to restore visibility.

Analysis of Control Gaps

The questionnaire reveals critical weaknesses in identity and access management and internal governance:

  • Lack of MFA: The absence of MFA for email and computer logins is a critical vulnerability. Compromised credentials could directly lead to a major breach.

  • Policy Gaps: Without an Acceptable Use Policy, there is no formal standard for employee behavior regarding IT assets, increasing the risk of misuse.

  • Onboarding Training: Failing to train new employees on security best practices leaves a crucial window of vulnerability when they are most susceptible to social engineering attacks.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks. They are prioritized based on severity.

  • (Critical) Implement Comprehensive MFA:

    • Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all user accounts for both email access (e.g., Office 365, Google Workspace) and endpoint logins (e.g., Windows Hello, Duo).

    • This is the single most effective control to mitigate the risk of account compromise.

  • (High) Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a formal AUP that clearly defines the rules for using company networks, devices, and data.

    • Require all current employees to read and acknowledge the policy. Integrate this step into the new-hire onboarding process.

  • (High) Establish a New Hire Security Training Program:

    • Create a mandatory security awareness training module for all new employees to be completed during their first week.

    • The training should cover phishing, password security, data handling, and the new AUP.

  • (Medium) Restore Security Data Integrity and Visibility:

    • Investigate the root cause of the corrupted data feeds for network scans and risk registers.

    • Execute a new, authenticated external network scan against target [Target IP] to identify and assess vulnerabilities on internet-facing systems.
