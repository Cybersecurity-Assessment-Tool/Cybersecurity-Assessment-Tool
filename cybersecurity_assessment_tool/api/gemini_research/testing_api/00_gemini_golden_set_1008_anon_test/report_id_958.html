Recommendations

To enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of , the following recommendations are provided. These are actionable steps designed to mitigate identified risks and establish a stronger security foundation.

High Priority Recommendations

  1. Implement Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Description: Develop and implement a mandatory, comprehensive security awareness training program for all employees. This training should be conducted at least annually and cover topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, data handling best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Rationale: Human error remains one of the leading causes of security breaches. Regular training ensures that employees are continuously updated on current threats and reinforced in secure behaviors, significantly reducing the organization’s attack surface.

    • Impact: Significantly reduces the risk of successful social engineering attacks, malware infections, and data loss due to employee error.

Medium Priority Recommendations (Foundational)

  1. Initiate Regular Network Vulnerability Scans:

    • Description: Establish a schedule for regular (e.g., quarterly or monthly) external and internal network vulnerability scans. These scans should identify open ports, running services, software versions, and potential misconfigurations.

    • Rationale: Proactive identification of technical vulnerabilities is crucial for maintaining a secure network perimeter and internal infrastructure. It allows for timely patching and configuration adjustments before they can be exploited by malicious actors.

    • Impact: Provides visibility into the technical security posture, enables proactive remediation of vulnerabilities, and helps prevent unauthorized access or data exfiltration.

  2. Implement a Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Description: Beyond just scanning, establish a formal vulnerability management program that includes processes for vulnerability identification, assessment, prioritization, remediation, and verification.

    • Rationale: A structured program ensures that identified vulnerabilities are tracked and addressed effectively, reducing the overall risk exposure over time.

    • Impact: Ensures that technical weaknesses are consistently identified and mitigated, leading to a continuously improving security posture.

Conclusion

While demonstrates good foundational security practices in its policy and initial employee training, the lack of recurring security awareness training represents a significant human-element risk. Furthermore, the absence of technical scan data indicates a potential blind spot regarding the organization’s external network exposure. Addressing these areas through the implementation of annual training and regular technical assessments will substantially strengthen the overall cybersecurity posture of .
