Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The self-assessment reveals significant deficiencies in foundational security practices:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not required for email access, computer logins, or sensitive data systems. This represents a critical vulnerability, as compromised credentials (e.g., through phishing) could lead to full system access with just a username and password.

  • Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of an AUP means there are no formal guidelines for employee behavior concerning company IT resources. This increases the risk of misuse, data leakage, and non-compliance.

  • Insufficient Ongoing Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the absence of annual or recurring training leaves the organization vulnerable to evolving threats and ensures employees do not retain critical security knowledge over time.

Recommendations

To significantly improve [Organization Name]’s cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided, prioritizing the identified critical gaps:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Systems:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all email accounts, computer logins (especially administrative accounts), and access to all sensitive data systems.

    • Rationale: MFA is a fundamental security control that drastically reduces the risk of account compromise even if passwords are stolen or guessed.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, disseminate, and ensure all employees read and acknowledge an AUP that clearly outlines expectations for IT resource usage, data handling, and online conduct.

    • Rationale: An AUP establishes clear boundaries, educates employees on responsible use, and provides a basis for disciplinary action in case of violations, reducing internal risks.

  3. Establish a Recurring Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Implement mandatory annual (or more frequent) security awareness training for all employees, covering topics like phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, and data protection.

    • Rationale: Ongoing training ensures employees remain vigilant against evolving threats and reinforces secure behaviors, reducing the likelihood of human-factor incidents.

  4. Conduct Comprehensive Network Scans (Internal and External):

    • Action: Perform regular, authenticated internal and external vulnerability scans and penetration tests against all relevant assets to identify active services, outdated software, misconfigurations, and other technical vulnerabilities.

    • Rationale: The current empty scan results leave a significant blind spot regarding the actual technical attack surface. Comprehensive scanning is essential for identifying and addressing technical weaknesses.

  5. Establish a Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Action: Implement a process for tracking, prioritizing, and remediating identified vulnerabilities. This includes regular patching of systems and applications.

    • Rationale: A structured program ensures that vulnerabilities, once discovered, are promptly addressed, minimizing the window of opportunity for attackers.
