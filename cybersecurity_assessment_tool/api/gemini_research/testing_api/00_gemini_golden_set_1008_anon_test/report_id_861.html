Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity posture assessment for [Organization Name], combining insights from a network scan, a security questionnaire, and a review of pre-existing risk data. The assessment reveals a mixed security posture with several critical vulnerabilities and significant policy gaps.

Most notably, a public-facing service identified on port 8080 exposes an interface titled "TOP SECRET DB". This finding directly contradicts a previous internal assessment which deemed port 8080 "secure and a false positive" with a CVSS score of 0.0. This discrepancy represents a critical and immediate threat, indicating direct exposure of potentially highly sensitive information and a severe misclassification within the organization’s risk management process.

Furthermore, the organization lacks a formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and does not conduct annual security awareness training for all employees. These policy and training deficiencies significantly elevate the human factor risk, leaving the organization vulnerable to social engineering attacks, insider threats, and general security hygiene failures.

Immediate action is recommended to secure the exposed database interface and rectify the identified policy and training gaps. A thorough review of the organization’s risk assessment and management processes is also highly advisable.

Recommendations

Based on the assessment findings, the following recommendations are provided to improve the organization’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions

  1. Secure Port 8080 Immediately:

    • Verify the nature and content of the "TOP SECRET DB" interface accessible on port 8080.

    • Immediately restrict public access to port 8080. Implement firewall rules to block unauthorized inbound connections.

    • If the service is legitimate and business-critical, implement strong authentication (preferably MFA), role-based access control, and ensure all communication is encrypted (e.g., using HTTPS).

    • Investigate the origin of this exposure and the data it potentially contains.

  2. Review and Correct Risk Assessment for Port 8080:

    • Conduct an urgent post-mortem to understand why port 8080 was previously deemed "secure and a false positive" despite the presence of a sensitive interface.

    • Update the internal risk register with the accurate, critical severity for the exposed database interface.

Mid-Term Actions

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT assets, networks, and data.

    • Ensure the AUP covers topics such as internet browsing, email usage, data handling, use of personal devices (BYOD), and incident reporting.

    • Communicate the AUP to all employees, requiring acknowledgement of understanding and compliance.

  2. Implement Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Establish a program for mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, not just new hires.

    • Training should cover current threats such as phishing, ransomware, social engineering, password best practices, and secure data handling.

    • Conduct regular phishing simulations to test employee awareness and reinforce training.

Long-Term Actions

  1. Enhance Risk Management Processes:

    • Implement a robust risk management framework that includes regular vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, and a formal process for validating and updating risk assessments.

    • Ensure that discrepancies between technical findings and existing risk registers are promptly investigated and rectified.

    • Conduct periodic internal audits of security policies and their enforcement.
