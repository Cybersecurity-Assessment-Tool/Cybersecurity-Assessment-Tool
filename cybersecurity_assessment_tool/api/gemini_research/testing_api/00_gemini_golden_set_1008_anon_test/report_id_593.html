  2. Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Correlation: Identified directly from the security questionnaire.

    • Impact: High risk of email account compromise through phishing or credential stuffing, leading to business email compromise (BEC), data leakage, and further internal network access.

  3. Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Correlation: Identified directly from the security questionnaire.

    • Impact: Critical risk of unauthorized access to sensitive data, potentially leading to severe data breaches, regulatory non-compliance, and significant reputational damage.

  4. Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Correlation: Identified directly from the security questionnaire. This is a foundational governance gap.

    • Impact: Increased risk of inappropriate system usage, data mishandling, and a general lack of clarity on employee responsibilities regarding cybersecurity, leading to higher human error risks.

  5. Lack of Security Awareness Training:

    • Correlation: Identified directly from the security questionnaire (both for new and existing employees).

    • Impact: Employees are more susceptible to social engineering, phishing, malware, and other attacks. This significantly increases the likelihood of a successful breach due to human error.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and strengthen the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  1. Secure RDP Exposure (Port 3389):

    • Recommendation (Easy Fix): Implement strict firewall rules to limit RDP access to trusted source IPs only. If remote access is required, place RDP behind a VPN (Virtual Private Network).

    • Recommendation (Long-term Fix): Implement a more secure remote access solution like a Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) solution or a VPN with strong MFA, ensuring RDP is never directly exposed to the internet.

    • Existing Recommendation: Close Port. VPN.

  2. Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Recommendation: Mandate and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts across the organization. This significantly reduces the risk of phishing and credential stuffing attacks.

  3. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Recommendation: Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all systems and applications that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data.

Policy and Training Enhancements

  1. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Recommendation: Create a clear and comprehensive AUP outlining employee responsibilities, acceptable use of IT resources, and consequences of non-compliance. Ensure all employees read and acknowledge the policy annually.

  2. Establish and Conduct Regular Security Awareness Training:

    • Recommendation: Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during onboarding.

    • Recommendation: Conduct annual, ongoing security awareness training for all employees to keep them informed about current threats, best practices, and organizational policies. Include phishing simulations.

General Security Posture Improvements

  1. Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Recommendation: Conduct regular external and internal vulnerability scans to identify new exposures. Periodically engage third-party penetration testers to simulate real-world attacks.

  2. Principle of Least Privilege:

    • Recommendation: Review and implement the principle of least privilege across all user accounts and systems, ensuring users and applications only have the minimum necessary access required to perform their functions.

Conclusion

The assessment reveals that while some security measures are in place, [Organization Name] faces several critical risks stemming from exposed services and significant gaps in foundational security controls and employee education. Addressing these areas, particularly the RDP exposure, MFA implementation, and policy/training initiatives, is paramount to enhancing the organization’s overall cybersecurity resilience. Proactive implementation of the recommended actions will substantially reduce the attack surface and strengthen defenses against a wide range of cyber threats.
