6. Recommendations

To significantly enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following actionable recommendations are provided, focusing on the identified gaps and limitations in data.

  1. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a detailed AUP that clearly outlines the proper and acceptable use of all company IT resources, including networks, systems, software, data, internet access, email, and mobile devices.

    • Ensure the AUP addresses expectations for data handling, password policies, incident reporting, and compliance with relevant regulations.

    • Mandate that all employees review, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP during onboarding and annually thereafter.

    • Integrate AUP principles directly into security awareness training programs.

  2. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement a recurring, mandatory security awareness training program for all employees, not limited to new hires, to be conducted at least once per year.

    • Training content should be current, engaging, and cover evolving threats such as advanced phishing techniques, ransomware, social engineering, secure remote work practices, data protection principles, and the process for reporting suspicious activities or incidents.

    • Consider conducting periodic simulated phishing campaigns to test employee vigilance and reinforce training effectiveness.

  3. Conduct a Comprehensive Technical Network Scan:

    • Initiate a new, thorough network port scan of all external-facing and critical internal systems to identify all open ports, active services, underlying products, and their precise versions.

    • Analyze scan results for outdated software, known vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and unnecessary service exposure.

    • Prioritize remediation of identified vulnerabilities through patching, configuration changes, or service removal.

    • Implement regular scanning as part of a continuous vulnerability management program.

  4. Provide and Maintain Complete Vulnerability Data:

    • Establish a systematic process for tracking, assessing, and remediating all identified vulnerabilities (from scans, penetration tests, audits, etc.).

    • Maintain an up-to-date vulnerability register detailing risk name, overview, severity, affected assets, and remediation status.

    • Integrate vulnerability management into the overall risk management framework to ensure continuous identification, evaluation, and mitigation of security risks.
