Critical Gaps Identified from Questionnaire

Based on the "No" responses, the following critical gaps in security controls have been identified:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Without MFA, email accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks. Compromised email can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data exfiltration, and further network penetration.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Failure to enforce MFA for sensitive data systems exposes critical business information to unauthorized access if primary credentials are stolen or guessed.

  • Absence of Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees are often a primary target for social engineering attacks due to unfamiliarity with organizational security policies and common threats. Lack of initial training leaves a significant human vulnerability.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name].

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all email accounts across the organization.

    • Priority: Critical

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if passwords are stolen, by requiring a second verification factor.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Extend MFA enforcement to all systems that store or process sensitive organizational data.

    • Priority: Critical

    • Benefit: Protects critical information assets from unauthorized access, thereby mitigating the risk of data breaches and maintaining data integrity and confidentiality.

  • Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires, to be completed as part of their onboarding process.

    • Priority: High

    • Benefit: Equips new employees with the knowledge to identify and report phishing attempts, social engineering tactics, and other common cyber threats, reducing the human element risk.

  • Regular Review of Security Controls:

    • Action: Conduct periodic reviews (at least annually) of all implemented security controls, including acceptable use policies and security awareness training content, to ensure they remain relevant and effective against evolving threats.

    • Priority: Medium

    • Benefit: Ensures the organization’s security posture adapts to new risks and maintains a strong defensive stance.
