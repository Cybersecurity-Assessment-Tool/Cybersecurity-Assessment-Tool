Identified Gaps from Questionnaire

The questionnaire results highlight several critical deficiencies in security controls:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not required for email access, computer logins, or sensitive data systems. This represents a severe vulnerability, making the organization highly susceptible to credential theft and unauthorized access if single-factor authentication is compromised.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of an AUP means employees may not have clear guidelines regarding the appropriate use of organizational IT resources, potentially leading to misuse, data leakage, or introduction of malware.

  • Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the absence of annual training for all employees leaves the organization vulnerable to evolving threat landscapes and makes it difficult to maintain a consistent security-aware culture.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name]:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Urgently deploy and enforce MFA for all email access, computer logins (both local and domain accounts), and access to all sensitive data systems. This is the single most impactful step to prevent unauthorized access from stolen credentials.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the proper and prohibited use of organizational IT assets, networks, and data. Ensure all employees review and acknowledge this policy.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover phishing, malware, data handling, social engineering, and the AUP. Reinforce security best practices regularly.

  4. Regular Security Audits and Assessments:

    • Conduct periodic internal and external vulnerability scans and penetration tests to identify and address emerging technical weaknesses.

    • Regularly review and update security policies and procedures to adapt to evolving threats and organizational changes.

  5. Maintain Risk Register and False Positive Validation:

    • Continue to maintain a detailed risk register. For findings like Port 8080 that are deemed false positives, ensure thorough documentation and validation processes are in place to prevent unnecessary re-investigation in future assessments.

These recommendations, when implemented, will address the most pressing security deficiencies and contribute to a more robust and resilient cybersecurity environment for [Organization Name].
