Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of the current cybersecurity posture for , derived from three distinct data sources: a recent network scan, responses to an organizational security questionnaire, and a list of pre-existing risks. While the network scan commendably identified no open ports, indicating a minimal direct network attack surface, significant security control gaps were uncovered in areas such as Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), employee training, and policy implementation.

A critical finding is the discrepancy between the network scan and a pre-existing risk entry concerning an "Unencrypted Web Server." The scan reported Port 80 as closed, directly contradicting the pre-existing risk which states Port 80 is open. This highlights the necessity for continuous validation of risk registers to ensure their accuracy and relevance.

Immediate recommendations prioritize addressing the identified MFA gaps, establishing a clear Acceptable Use Policy, and implementing annual security awareness training for all employees. These steps are crucial for enhancing the organization’s resilience against common cyber threats.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actionable recommendations are provided to improve ’s cybersecurity posture, prioritized by severity and potential impact.

High Priority Recommendations

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across Key Systems:

    • Email Access (R-001): Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all organizational email accounts. This is a foundational control against credential theft and account compromise.

    • Computer Login (R-002): Implement MFA for all user logins to workstations and critical internal systems to prevent unauthorized access, even if primary credentials are breached.

  2. Re-validate and Update Risk Register (R-005):

    • Conduct an immediate review and re-validation of the entire risk register. Prioritize investigating the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk (R-005) to confirm the current status of Port 80 and update the risk entry accordingly. This ensures the risk management process is based on accurate, up-to-date information.

Medium Priority Recommendations

  1. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (R-003):

    • Create a clear, comprehensive AUP outlining proper conduct for using company IT resources (e.g., internet, email, hardware, software).

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, with refresher training conducted periodically.

  2. Enhance Security Awareness Training Program (R-004):

    • Extend mandatory security awareness training to all employees (not just new hires) on an annual basis.

    • Training content should be engaging and cover current threats such as sophisticated phishing techniques, social engineering tactics, secure data handling, and incident reporting procedures.

General Recommendations

  • Implement Regular Vulnerability Scanning: Conduct routine external and internal vulnerability scans (e.g., quarterly) to proactively identify and address new exposures before they can be exploited.

  • Maintain a Dynamic Risk Register: Establish a process for continuous review and update of the organizational risk register, ensuring it accurately reflects the current threat landscape, asset inventory, and the effectiveness of implemented controls.
