Key Finding: Lack of Annual Security Awareness Training

The organization currently reports that it does not conduct annual security awareness training for all employees. This represents a significant human factor vulnerability, as employees are often the first line of defense against social engineering, phishing attacks, and malware. Without regular reinforcement, the effectiveness of initial training diminishes over time, increasing the risk of successful cyberattacks.

Technical Vulnerability: Exposed RDP (Port 3389)

The presence of an open Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) service (port 3389) directly exposed to the internet is a critical security risk. RDP is a frequent target for attackers attempting brute-force attacks, credential stuffing, and exploitation of known vulnerabilities to gain unauthorized access. Without stringent security measures like Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and strict access restrictions (e.g., VPN requirement, IP whitelisting), this exposure significantly increases the organization’s attack surface and correlates directly with the pre-existing risk of "RDP Exposure."

Recommendations

To address the identified vulnerabilities and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions

  • Restrict RDP Access (Port 3389):

    • Implement firewall rules to block incoming connections to port 3389 from the internet for all non-essential systems.

    • If remote access is required, restrict it to known, trusted IP addresses or enforce access exclusively through a Virtual Private Network (VPN) with strong authentication.

    • Ensure all RDP accounts utilize strong, unique passwords and are protected by Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

Long-Term Strategic Initiatives

  • Implement Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Develop and deploy a comprehensive annual security awareness training program for all employees.

    • Training should cover critical topics such as phishing, social engineering, ransomware, password hygiene, data handling best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Regularly update training content to reflect current threat landscapes and emerging attack vectors.

  • Secure Remote Access Solutions:

    • For any remote administrative access, prioritize VPN solutions that require strong authentication (e.g., MFA) before allowing connection to internal network resources, including RDP.

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning:

    • Conduct periodic internal and external vulnerability scans to proactively identify and remediate security weaknesses across the IT infrastructure.

  • Review and Update Security Policies:

    • Ensure that the Acceptable Use Policy and other relevant security policies are regularly reviewed and updated to reflect current best practices, regulatory requirements, and organizational operational needs.

Conclusion

The assessment reveals critical areas requiring immediate attention, specifically the direct internet exposure of RDP services and the absence of annual security awareness training for all employees. Addressing these items will significantly reduce the organization’s risk profile and strengthen its resilience against cyber threats. Continuous monitoring, regular security assessments, and ongoing employee education are vital for maintaining a robust cybersecurity posture in an evolving threat landscape.
