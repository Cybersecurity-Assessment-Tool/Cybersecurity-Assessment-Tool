Analysis of Security Control Gaps:

The questionnaire reveals critical gaps in security practices that could significantly increase the organization’s risk exposure:

  • MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Lack of MFA on systems handling sensitive data is a major vulnerability, increasing the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an AUP means employees may not be aware of their responsibilities regarding IT asset usage, data handling, and security expectations, leading to potential misuse or accidental breaches.

  • Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Failing to train new employees immediately upon hire leaves a critical window during which they are most susceptible to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing) and may inadvertently introduce risks.

Identified Critical Gaps and High Risks:

Based on the security control review, the following represent high-priority risks requiring immediate attention:

  1. Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: This exposes critical organizational data to compromise through credential theft. Severity: High.

  2. Absence of Acceptable Use Policy: Without clear guidelines, employees may engage in activities that inadvertently expose the organization to legal, reputational, or security risks. Severity: High.

  3. No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New hires are often targets for social engineering. Lack of initial training makes them vulnerable and a potential entry point for attackers. Severity: High.

Recommendations

To strengthen the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions (High Priority):

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if credentials are stolen.

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities, permitted and prohibited activities regarding company IT resources, data handling, and internet usage. Ensure all employees review and acknowledge the policy annually.

  • Establish Onboarding Security Awareness Training: Integrate mandatory security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new employees. This training should cover phishing, malware, password hygiene, data handling best practices, and the importance of reporting suspicious activities.

Verification and Continuous Improvement:

  • Verify Status of "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk: Confirm that Port 80 is indeed closed and remains closed, or if it has been replaced by a secure (HTTPS) service. Update the risk register accordingly.

  • Regular Security Awareness Training: Continue with annual security awareness training for all employees, ensuring content is updated to reflect current threat landscapes.

  • Periodic Policy Review: Regularly review and update all security policies (including the AUP) to ensure they remain relevant and effective against evolving threats.

  • Comprehensive Vulnerability Management: Implement a continuous vulnerability scanning and patch management program to proactively identify and remediate technical vulnerabilities across all systems.
