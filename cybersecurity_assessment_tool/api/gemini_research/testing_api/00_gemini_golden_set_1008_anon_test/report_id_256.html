Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

  • MFA for Email Access: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all employee email accounts. This should be a top priority due to the critical role email plays in business operations and its common use in phishing attacks. Consider integrating with existing Identity and Access Management (IAM) solutions.

  • MFA for Computer Login: Implement MFA for logging into all company-owned computers and workstations. This adds a crucial layer of defense against compromised credentials for internal network access.

  • MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Mandate MFA for accessing all systems containing or processing sensitive organizational data. This directly protects high-value assets from unauthorized access and data breaches.

Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

  • Create a comprehensive AUP outlining acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources, data, and internet access. The policy should clearly define employee responsibilities regarding data protection, software installation, and online behavior.

  • Distribute the AUP to all employees and obtain signed acknowledgments to ensure understanding and compliance.

  • Integrate AUP review and acknowledgment into the annual security awareness training program.

Enhance Employee Security Awareness Training

  • Onboarding Security Training: Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees as part of their onboarding process. This training should cover the AUP, phishing identification, password hygiene, data handling procedures, and common social engineering tactics.

  • Regular Training Reinforcement: While annual training is conducted, ensure the content is up-to-date, engaging, and covers relevant threat vectors. Consider more frequent, shorter training modules or simulated phishing campaigns to reinforce learning throughout the year.

Continuous Risk Management

  • Establish a formal process for regularly identifying, assessing, and documenting cybersecurity risks across the organization. This includes performing regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing.

  • Implement a vulnerability management program to periodically scan internal and external assets for misconfigurations, outdated software, and known vulnerabilities.
