3.2. Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The questionnaire responses clearly reveal several critical gaps in the organization’s foundational security posture, which introduce notable risks:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Login: The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems. If an attacker compromises a user’s password, the lack of a second authentication factor provides no additional barrier to entry.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Failure to enforce MFA on systems that house sensitive organizational or customer data represents a severe vulnerability. This oversight directly increases the likelihood of a data breach and potential non-compliance with data protection regulations.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may lack clear guidelines regarding the responsible use of company assets, data, and networks. This ambiguity can lead to unintentional misuse, security incidents, and difficulties in enforcing cybersecurity standards.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New hires are frequently targeted by social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing). The lack of initial, mandatory security awareness training leaves them highly susceptible, thereby introducing a significant human element risk to the entire organization from day one.

6. Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and significantly improve the overall cybersecurity posture of the organization. Prioritizing these steps will help to build a more resilient defense against current and future cyber threats.

6.1. Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  • Implement MFA for Computer Login (Addressing R-001): Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all user accounts accessing organizational computers and internal systems. This crucial step creates a strong barrier against credential theft and significantly reduces the impact of compromised passwords.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (Addressing R-002): Mandate MFA for all access to systems containing sensitive organizational or customer data. Prioritize the rollout based on data classification, system criticality, and regulatory requirements to protect the most valuable assets first.

6.2. Policy & Training Enhancements (Medium Priority)

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (Addressing R-003): Create a clear, comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that explicitly outlines expectations for appropriate use of company assets, networks, and data. Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge this policy annually, with disciplinary actions for non-compliance.

  • Implement Security Awareness Training for New Employees (Addressing R-004): Establish a mandatory security awareness training program as a critical component of the onboarding process for all new hires. This training should cover common cyber threats (e.g., phishing, malware, social engineering), company security policies, and best practices for protecting information.

6.3. Continuous Improvement Initiatives

  • Regular Vulnerability Assessments: While the current scan indicated no open ports, this is a point-in-time assessment. Implement a program of regular vulnerability scans and, periodically, penetration tests across the entire IT infrastructure (network, applications, cloud environments) to identify potential weaknesses proactively.

  • Review and Update Policies Regularly: Establish a schedule for periodically reviewing and updating all security policies and procedures (including the AUP) to ensure they remain relevant, effective, and aligned with evolving industry best practices, new technologies, and regulatory changes.

  • Ongoing Security Awareness Training: Continue with annual security awareness training for all employees and consider implementing more frequent, targeted training sessions on emerging threats or specific organizational risks identified throughout the year.
