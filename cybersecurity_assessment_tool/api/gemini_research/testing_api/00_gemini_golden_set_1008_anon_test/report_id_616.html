Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance and validate the cybersecurity posture of .

  1. Re-run and Validate Network Scan:

    • Conduct a targeted Nmap scan against a clearly defined and verifiable external IP address or domain relevant to ’s internet-facing assets.

    • Ensure the scan is performed with appropriate permissions and covers common port ranges and service detection.

    • Analyze the new scan results for open ports, identified services, product versions, and potential vulnerabilities.

  2. Maintain and Enhance Existing Controls:

    • Continue the excellent practice of requiring MFA for email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems. Regularly review MFA policies and technologies to ensure they remain effective against evolving threats.

    • Periodically review and update the employee acceptable use policy to reflect current organizational needs and threat landscapes.

    • Continue with regular (at least annual) security awareness training for all employees, and ensure new hires receive comprehensive training. Incorporate current threat intelligence and phishing examples.

  3. Conduct Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Implement regular (e.g., quarterly) authenticated vulnerability scans for both external and internal networks.

    • Consider engaging third-party security firms for periodic external and internal penetration tests to simulate real-world attacks and identify complex vulnerabilities.

  4. Implement Continuous Security Monitoring:

    • Deploy Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems to aggregate and analyze security logs from various sources (firewalls, servers, endpoints).

    • Establish a robust incident response plan and conduct regular drills to ensure preparedness for security incidents.

  5. Establish a Risk Register and Management Process:

    • Develop a formal process for identifying, assessing, and tracking risks. Maintain a comprehensive risk register that includes risk name, overview, severity, and mitigation strategies. This will ensure that all identified risks (including those from future scans or internal audits) are systematically managed.
