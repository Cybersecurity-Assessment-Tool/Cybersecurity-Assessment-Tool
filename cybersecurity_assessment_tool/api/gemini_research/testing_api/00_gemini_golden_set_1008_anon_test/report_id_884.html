Risk Assessment

This section synthesizes the findings from the questionnaire, technical scan, and pre-existing risk register into a comprehensive risk assessment.

Identified Gaps from Questionnaire

The following critical security gaps were identified based on the "No" responses in the security questionnaire:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Without Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), email accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks. This poses a significant risk of unauthorized access to sensitive communications and potential lateral movement within the network.

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Login: The absence of MFA for computer logins dramatically increases the risk of unauthorized access to workstations and servers, especially through compromised credentials.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Critical data systems not protected by MFA are a prime target for attackers, potentially leading to data breaches, intellectual property theft, or regulatory non-compliance.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees, without foundational security training, are more likely to fall victim to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing), introduce malware, or violate security policies inadvertently.

  • No Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Without regular, annual security awareness training, employees may forget best practices, become complacent, or be unaware of evolving threats, leaving the organization vulnerable.

Technical Findings

  • Open Port 80 with Undetermined Service: The presence of an open port 80 without detailed service information presents an unknown attack surface. While legitimate web services often run on this port, an unmanaged or unpatched service could be exploited. The lack of version information prevents an immediate assessment for known vulnerabilities.

Recommendations

Based on the assessment, the following recommendations are provided to improve [Organization Name]’s cybersecurity posture:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Email Access: Immediately implement MFA for all email accounts to prevent unauthorized access.

    • Computer Logins: Deploy MFA solutions for user authentication to all workstations and servers.

    • Sensitive Data Systems: Mandate MFA for accessing any systems storing or processing sensitive organizational data.

  2. Enhance Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • New Employee Training: Establish mandatory security awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new employees.

    • Annual Refresher Training: Conduct annual security awareness training for all employees, covering current threats, best practices, and organizational policies.

  3. Investigate and Secure Open Port 80:

    • Service Identification: Immediately investigate the service running on port 80 to identify its purpose, product, and version.

    • Vulnerability Assessment: Perform a detailed vulnerability scan and manual review of the identified service.

    • Hardening and Patching: Apply all necessary security patches and implement hardening best practices for the service and its underlying operating system.

    • Access Control: Restrict access to the service only to necessary sources, ideally behind a firewall. If the service is not essential, consider closing the port.

Conclusion

The cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name] shows critical deficiencies, particularly in the areas of Multi-Factor Authentication and security awareness training. The presence of an open web port without detailed service information also represents a potential unmanaged risk. Addressing the identified gaps and implementing the recommended actions will significantly reduce the organization’s attack surface and strengthen its overall resilience against cyber threats. Continuous monitoring, regular assessments, and ongoing employee education are crucial for maintaining a robust security posture in the long term.
