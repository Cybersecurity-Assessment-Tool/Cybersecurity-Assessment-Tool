Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive cybersecurity assessment based on an analysis of network scan data, organizational security questionnaire responses, and pre-existing risk information. The objective is to identify critical security gaps, technical vulnerabilities, and provide actionable recommendations to enhance the overall security posture.

Key findings indicate a proactive approach in some areas, such as requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins and sensitive data systems, and conducting annual security awareness training. However, critical gaps were identified, notably the lack of MFA for email access and the absence of security awareness training for new employees, significantly increasing exposure to phishing and social engineering attacks.

The conducted network scan revealed no open ports on the target [Target IP], suggesting a well-hardened external perimeter or that the scanned target does not host publicly accessible services on standard ports. A pre-existing risk concerning an "Unencrypted Web Server" (Port 80 open) was noted, but the Nmap scan results contradict this, showing Port 80 as closed. This suggests the risk may be mitigated or requires further investigation to verify its applicability to the scanned target.

Prioritizing the implementation of MFA for email and mandatory security training for new hires are immediate recommendations to address the most pressing human-centric vulnerabilities.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the organization’s overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided.

Immediate Actions

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts across the organization. This is a critical control against credential theft and unauthorized access.

  • Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Hires: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees during their onboarding process. This training should cover phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, and data handling best practices.

Short-Term Actions

  • Verify "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk Status: Investigate the status of the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk. If Port 80 is indeed closed on all external-facing assets as per the Nmap scan, this risk can be marked as mitigated or a false positive. If the service exists on internal networks or other external IPs, ensure it is either secured with HTTPS/TLS or decommissioned.

Long-Term Strategies

  • Regular Security Awareness Training Refreshers: Continue and enhance the annual security awareness training for all employees, ensuring content is updated to reflect current threat landscapes and organizational policies.

  • Periodic Vulnerability Assessments: Implement a schedule for regular vulnerability scanning and penetration testing of both external and internal network assets to proactively identify and address potential weaknesses.

  • Review and Update Security Policies: Periodically review and update existing security policies, including the acceptable use policy, to ensure they remain relevant and effective against evolving cyber threats.
