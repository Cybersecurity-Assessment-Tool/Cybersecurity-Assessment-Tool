Analysis of Questionnaire Responses

The questionnaire responses indicate significant gaps in critical security controls, leading to elevated risk:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not enforced for email access (SCQ-001) or for accessing sensitive data systems (SCQ-003). This is a critical vulnerability, as compromised credentials could lead to unauthorized access to pivotal organizational assets, including sensitive data and communication channels. While MFA is used for computer logins, its absence in other high-risk areas severely weakens the overall authentication posture.

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an employee acceptable use policy (SCQ-004) leaves the organization exposed to various risks, including misuse of IT resources, data leakage, and legal liabilities. An AUP sets clear expectations for employee behavior regarding organizational assets, defining acceptable and unacceptable practices.

  • Security Awareness Training for New Employees: While annual training for all employees is conducted (SCQ-006), new employees do not receive dedicated security awareness training (SCQ-005). This oversight can introduce immediate vulnerabilities, as new hires may not be aware of organizational security policies, best practices, and common threat vectors (e.g., phishing) from their first day.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and significantly improve the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email and Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Immediately enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all organizational email accounts.

    • Action: Extend MFA requirements to all systems storing or processing sensitive organizational data.

    • Benefit: This will drastically reduce the risk of account compromise and unauthorized data access, even if primary credentials are stolen.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that clearly outlines employee responsibilities and acceptable conduct regarding organizational IT resources, data, and internet usage.

    • Action: Ensure all employees review and formally acknowledge the AUP annually, and that new hires acknowledge it upon onboarding.

    • Benefit: Provides clear guidelines, reduces misuse of resources, and mitigates legal and compliance risks.

  3. Introduce Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Develop and implement mandatory security awareness training as an integral part of the onboarding process for all new employees.

    • Content: This training should cover essential security policies, phishing recognition, strong password hygiene, proper data handling, and reporting suspicious activities.

    • Benefit: Ensures all staff are equipped with foundational security knowledge from day one, reducing human-factor risks.

  4. Harden and Monitor Exposed SSH Services:

    • Action: Immediately review the configuration of the exposed SSH service on .

    • Action: Enforce strong, key-based authentication and disable password-based authentication.

    • Action: Limit SSH access to only necessary source IP addresses/networks using firewall rules or host-based access controls.

    • Action: Implement rate-limiting and intrusion detection/prevention systems to monitor and block brute-force attempts.

    • Action: Ensure the SSH daemon is running the latest stable version and is regularly patched. Conduct a more detailed scan or manual inspection to identify the exact service version for specific vulnerability assessment.

    • Benefit: Reduces the attack surface and mitigates risks associated with unauthorized access to critical systems.

  5. Conduct Regular and Detailed Network and Vulnerability Scans:

    • Action: Implement a schedule for regular, authenticated network and vulnerability scans across all external and internal assets.

    • Action: Utilize tools capable of deeper service and version detection to obtain comprehensive vulnerability intelligence.

    • Benefit: Provides continuous visibility into the security posture, helps identify new vulnerabilities promptly, and enables proactive risk management.

Conclusion

The assessment reveals a cybersecurity posture with significant vulnerabilities, primarily stemming from insufficient MFA coverage for critical systems, the absence of crucial organizational policies like an Acceptable Use Policy, and gaps in security awareness training for new hires.
