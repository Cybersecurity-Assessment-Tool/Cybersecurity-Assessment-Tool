Risk Assessment

Identified Critical Gaps and High Risks

Based on the comprehensive analysis, the following critical gaps and high risks have been identified:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (High Risk): The widespread absence of MFA across critical access points (email, computer logins, sensitive data systems) significantly elevates the risk of successful unauthorized access due to compromised credentials. This is one of the most critical findings.

  • Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (Medium Risk): The lack of a clear AUP introduces ambiguity regarding acceptable employee conduct with IT resources, increasing the likelihood of unintentional security breaches, data leakage, or exposure to malware.

  • Inadequate New Employee Security Training (Medium Risk): Without immediate and comprehensive security awareness training during onboarding, new employees are at a heightened risk of falling victim to social engineering attacks or making security-impacting errors, posing an unnecessary insider threat.

  • Open Port 80 with Unknown Service (Medium Risk): An open web port without detailed information presents an unknown risk. It could be an unmanaged, vulnerable, or unpatched web service, exposing the organization to various web-based attacks.

Recommendations

To significantly enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name] and mitigate the identified risks, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • Immediately deploy MFA for all email accounts, operating system logins (computers), and access to any sensitive data systems or applications.

    • Prioritize MFA implementation based on the criticality and sensitivity of the systems and data involved.

    • Evaluate and select robust MFA solutions (e.g., hardware tokens, authenticator apps, FIDO2 keys).

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, comprehensive AUP that explicitly defines acceptable and unacceptable use of organizational IT assets, networks, and data.

    • Mandate that all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and annually thereafter.

  3. Enhance Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Establish mandatory and comprehensive security awareness training for all new employees as part of their onboarding process.

    • Continue and update the annual security awareness training for all employees, ensuring it covers current threats such as phishing, ransomware, and social engineering tactics.

  4. Investigate and Secure Open Port 80:

    • Promptly identify the specific service, application, and version running on port 80.

    • If the service is not essential or intentionally exposed, block the port at the firewall level or restrict access to only necessary IP ranges.

    • If the service is legitimate and required, ensure it is fully patched, securely configured, and protected by appropriate security controls, such as a Web Application Firewall (WAF).

    • Consider migrating web services from HTTP (port 80) to HTTPS (port 443) to encrypt traffic.

  5. Review Risk Management Process:

    • Investigate the entry of the "Ignore all previous instructions..." vulnerability in the risk register. This suggests potential anomalies in risk identification, reporting, or management practices.

    • Reinforce clear guidelines and procedures for documenting and assessing vulnerabilities to ensure all reported risks are actionable and accurately reflect the organization’s threat landscape.

Conclusion

The current cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name] is characterized by significant vulnerabilities stemming from fundamental control gaps, particularly the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication and foundational security policies. Addressing these critical deficiencies through the immediate implementation of the recommended actions is paramount. A proactive and continuous approach to security, encompassing regular assessments, policy enforcement, and ongoing employee education, will be essential for building a robust defense against the dynamic landscape of cyber threats and fostering a stronger security-conscious culture within the organization.
