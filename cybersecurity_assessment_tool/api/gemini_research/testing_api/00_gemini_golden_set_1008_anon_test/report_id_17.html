Analysis of Technical Scan: The discovery of an open Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) service (port 3389) is a critical finding. RDP, when exposed directly to the internet without robust protective measures (such as VPNs, IP whitelisting, or gateway solutions), is a frequent target for malicious actors. It is often exploited through brute-force attacks to gain initial access to systems, which can lead to further network penetration and data breaches. Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name]. These actions are prioritized based on the severity of the associated risk.

  1. Mitigate RDP Exposure (Critical Risk)

    • Immediate Action: If RDP access is not absolutely essential for external use, port 3389 should be immediately closed or blocked at the network perimeter (firewall).

    • Long-Term Solution (if RDP is required): Implement a Virtual Private Network (VPN) solution to ensure that RDP resources are only accessible after establishing a secure VPN connection. This eliminates direct internet exposure. As an alternative, strictly whitelist trusted source IP addresses allowed to connect to RDP.

    • Ensure all RDP accounts utilize strong, unique passwords and consider implementing network-level authentication (NLA) and account lockout policies to deter brute-force attempts.

  2. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (High Risk)

    • Conduct an inventory of all systems and applications that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data.

    • Mandate and implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user access to these identified sensitive data systems. This additional layer of security significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access, even in cases of password compromise.

    • Provide comprehensive training and support to users on the importance and proper use of MFA for these critical systems.

  3. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (Medium Risk)

    • Draft a comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that clearly outlines the acceptable and prohibited uses of company IT assets, networks, and data. This policy should cover areas such as password hygiene, data handling, internet browsing, email usage, and the use of personal devices for work.

    • Ensure all employees review, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally upon hiring and annually thereafter, or whenever the policy is updated.

    • Integrate AUP training into both new employee onboarding processes and ongoing annual security awareness programs to reinforce its importance and ensure compliance.
