Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Email Access (Critical Priority):

    • Action: Enable and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all user accounts accessing organizational email services. This significantly reduces the risk of account compromise even if passwords are stolen.

    • Details: Evaluate available MFA solutions compatible with your email provider. Ensure a phased rollout with clear communication and support for users.

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins (Critical Priority):

    • Action: Implement MFA requirements for all organizational computer logins, particularly for endpoints accessing sensitive data.

    • Details: Explore options such as Windows Hello for Business, smart card logins, or third-party MFA solutions integrated with Active Directory or other identity providers.

  • Introduce Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Hires (High Priority):

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees as part of their onboarding process.

    • Details: This training should cover essential topics like phishing recognition, password best practices, social engineering tactics, and incident reporting procedures.

  • Regular Review of External Attack Surface (Medium Priority):

    • Action: Despite the current finding of no open ports, continue with regular vulnerability scanning and periodic penetration testing of all external-facing systems and IP ranges.

    • Details: This proactive approach ensures that any newly exposed services or misconfigurations are identified and remediated promptly, preventing new attack vectors from emerging.

Conclusion

While [Organization Name] demonstrates a good practice in maintaining a low external attack surface and some internal controls, the absence of MFA for critical systems like email and general computer logins, alongside a lack of initial security awareness training for new employees, represents significant internal vulnerabilities. Addressing these high-priority items is crucial for building a robust defense against prevalent cyber threats and improving the organization’s overall security posture.
