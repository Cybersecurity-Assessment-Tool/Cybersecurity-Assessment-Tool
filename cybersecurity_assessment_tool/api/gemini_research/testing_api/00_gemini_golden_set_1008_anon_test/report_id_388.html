Risk Assessment

This section compiles the identified security gaps from the questionnaire, potential technical vulnerabilities, and any pre-existing risks to present a comprehensive view of the organization’s current risk posture.

5.1 Identified Gaps from Questionnaire

Based on the security control review, the following critical gaps have been identified:

  1. Risk Name: Inadequate MFA for Sensitive Data Systems

    • Overview: Multi-Factor Authentication is not required for accessing systems containing sensitive data. This leaves these critical systems vulnerable to unauthorized access through compromised credentials (e.g., via phishing, brute-force attacks, or credential stuffing).

    • Severity: High

  2. Risk Name: Absence of New Employee Security Awareness Training

    • Overview: New employees are not provided with security awareness training. This introduces a significant human factor risk, as new hires may be unaware of company security policies, common social engineering tactics, and secure operational practices, making them susceptible to manipulation and potential vectors for internal breaches.

    • Severity: Medium

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

6.1 Addressing Questionnaire Gaps

  1. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Immediately implement and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data.

    • Benefit: This will significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access even if user credentials are stolen or compromised.

    • Priority: Critical

  2. Establish New Employee Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees as part of their onboarding process.

    • Benefit: Ensures that all personnel, from day one, are aware of security policies, common threats (e.g., phishing, malware), and their role in maintaining organizational security.

    • Priority: High

6.2 General Security Best Practices

  1. Conduct Regular Internal Network Scans and Penetration Tests:

    • Action: While the external scan showed no open ports, it is crucial to conduct regular internal network vulnerability scans and penetration tests to identify misconfigurations, outdated software, and other weaknesses within the internal network.

    • Benefit: Provides a comprehensive view of the internal attack surface, which is often more exposed to insider threats or compromises originating from internal network access.

    • Priority: Medium

  2. Maintain Up-to-Date Inventory of Assets:

    • Action: Implement and maintain a detailed inventory of all IT assets, including servers, workstations, network devices, and software, to ensure proper patching and configuration management.

    • Benefit: Essential for effective vulnerability management and incident response.

    • Priority: Medium

  3. Develop and Test an Incident Response Plan:

    • Action: Create or update a comprehensive incident response plan and conduct regular tabletop exercises to ensure the organization can effectively detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents.

    • Benefit: Minimizes the impact and recovery time of security breaches.

    • Priority: Medium
