Analysis of Controls

The questionnaire results highlight two critical gaps in the organization’s security posture:

  • Lack of MFA for Email: The absence of mandatory MFA for email access significantly increases the risk of account takeover via phishing, credential stuffing, or brute-force attacks. Email is a common entry point for attackers, and without MFA, a compromised password can grant full access to an attacker.

  • Inadequate Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Failing to provide security awareness training to new employees leaves the organization vulnerable to human error-related incidents from the moment an employee joins. New hires may not be familiar with the organization’s security policies, common threat vectors, or best practices, increasing the risk of social engineering attacks, malware infections, or policy violations.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and strengthen the organization’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions

  1. Investigate and Secure Port 8080: Immediately investigate the system exposed on port 8080 at [Target IP]. Determine the nature of the "TOP SECRET DB" and verify its security status. If sensitive data is indeed exposed, take immediate steps to restrict access, implement robust authentication, or remove it from internet-facing networks.

  2. Validate Risk Register for Port 8080: Urgently review and update the organization’s risk register regarding Port 8080 to reflect the actual findings. Determine why the previous assessment concluded it was secure and address any underlying issues in vulnerability assessment procedures.

Policy and Training

  1. Implement New Employee Security Training: Develop and enforce a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees. This training should be completed before new hires are granted access to critical systems and data.

  2. Review Existing Training Content: Ensure that annual security awareness training for all employees is comprehensive and covers current threat landscapes, including phishing, social engineering, and data handling best practices.

  3. Conduct Regular Risk Register Reviews: Establish a process for regular (e.g., quarterly or semi-annual) reviews and updates of the organization’s risk register, ensuring all entries accurately reflect the current threat landscape and internal posture.

Technical Controls

  1. Mandate MFA for Email Access: Implement and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise.

  2. Periodic External Vulnerability Scans: Conduct regular external vulnerability scans (at least quarterly) on all internet-facing assets to proactively identify and remediate exposed services or vulnerabilities.
