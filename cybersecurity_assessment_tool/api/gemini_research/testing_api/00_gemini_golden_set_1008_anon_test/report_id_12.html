Analysis of Controls

The questionnaire results highlight significant deficiencies in fundamental security controls:

  • MFA Gaps: Multi-Factor Authentication is critical for protecting access to accounts. The absence of MFA for email access and computer logins represents a severe vulnerability, making these systems highly susceptible to credential-based attacks. While MFA is used for sensitive data systems, the lack thereof in more common access points significantly increases overall risk.

  • Policy Deficiency: The lack of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a foundational gap. An AUP provides clear guidelines for employees regarding the proper use of organizational IT assets, reducing the risk of misuse, data leakage, and non-compliance.

  • Training Gaps: While annual security awareness training is conducted, the absence of specific training for new employees is a critical oversight. New hires, often unfamiliar with organizational security practices and common threats, present a significant social engineering and phishing risk until adequately trained.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts across the organization. This should be prioritized as a critical control to prevent email compromise.

  2. Implement MFA for Computer Login: Roll out MFA for all organizational computer logins, starting with privileged accounts and then extending to all user workstations.

  3. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a clear, comprehensive, and legally reviewed AUP. This policy must be communicated to all employees, signed upon onboarding, and reviewed annually with refreshed training.

  4. Mandate Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Establish a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires. This training should be completed before they gain access to organizational IT systems and should cover basic security hygiene, phishing, and organizational policies.

  5. Conduct Comprehensive Network Scan: Arrange for a full, unhindered network vulnerability scan of all organizational external and internal assets. Ensure that valid and complete scan data is collected for analysis to identify technical weaknesses, misconfigurations, and outdated software/services.

  6. Establish and Maintain a Risk Register: Implement a formal process for identifying, documenting, assessing, and tracking all organizational risks and vulnerabilities. This includes creating a centralized risk register and assigning ownership for risk mitigation.
