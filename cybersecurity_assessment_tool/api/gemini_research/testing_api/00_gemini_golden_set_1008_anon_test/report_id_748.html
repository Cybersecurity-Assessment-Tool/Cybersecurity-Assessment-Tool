Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The questionnaire highlights critical deficiencies in foundational security practices related to human factors and policy:

  • Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without an AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding company IT assets and data, leading to misuse, data breaches, or compliance violations.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees are particularly vulnerable to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing) and may inadvertently introduce risks if not immediately educated on security best practices.

  • No Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Regular training ensures that all employees stay updated on evolving threats, company policies, and best practices, reinforcing a strong security culture.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement a Comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Develop, approve, and disseminate a clear AUP that outlines employee responsibilities for using company IT resources, data handling, and internet usage.

    • Ensure all employees read and acknowledge the AUP annually.

  2. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Integrate comprehensive security awareness training as a mandatory component of the new employee onboarding process.

    • Cover topics such as phishing, malware, password hygiene, data handling, and incident reporting.

  3. Conduct Regular (Annual) Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement an annual or bi-annual security awareness training program for all staff.

    • Keep content updated with current threat landscapes and common attack vectors.

    • Utilize engaging formats (e.g., interactive modules, simulated phishing campaigns) to improve retention.

  4. Consider Deeper Technical Assessments:

    • While the external scan showed no open ports, consider authenticated vulnerability scans and penetration tests for internal networks to identify potential weaknesses behind the perimeter.

    • Regularly review firewall rules and network configurations to ensure ongoing protection.

  5. Maintain MFA Implementation:

    • Continue the excellent practice of requiring MFA for email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems. Explore expanding MFA to other critical applications where feasible.

Conclusion

The cybersecurity assessment for [Organization Name] reveals a critical need to bolster human-centric security controls. While the organization benefits from robust MFA implementation and a seemingly hardened external network perimeter (for the scanned target), the absence of fundamental policies like an AUP and comprehensive security awareness training introduces significant and easily exploitable risks. By prioritizing the development of these policies and educational programs, [Organization Name] can significantly improve its resilience against a wide range of cyber threats and foster a more secure operational environment.
