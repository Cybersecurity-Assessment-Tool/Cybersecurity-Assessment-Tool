Risk Assessment

This section synthesizes findings from the security questionnaire, technical scans, and existing risk data to provide a comprehensive risk assessment.

Identified Critical Gaps (from Questionnaire)

Based on the questionnaire responses, the following critical gaps in security controls were identified:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): The absence of MFA for email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems significantly increases the risk of account compromise through phishing, brute-force attacks, or credential stuffing. This is a fundamental security control that is critically missing.

  • Absence of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without an AUP, employees may not be aware of acceptable behavior regarding organizational assets, data, and network usage. This can lead to increased risks from insider threats, data leakage, and compliance violations.

    • Impact: High risk of data exfiltration, unauthorized access to sensitive data, potential system compromise, and severe reputational damage.

    • Severity: Critical

Recommendations

To address the identified critical gaps and vulnerabilities, the following recommendations are provided, prioritized by urgency and impact.

Immediate Actions

  • Secure or Close Port 8080/tcp Immediately: Investigate the service running on port 8080 with the title TOP SECRET DB. This port must be immediately secured, blocked from external access, or the service moved behind robust access controls and firewalls. The exposure of such a clearly labeled sensitive asset is an extreme risk.

  • Review and Correct Risk Register Accuracy: Conduct an urgent review of the organization’s risk assessment process and risk register. The critical discrepancy regarding port 8080 indicates a significant weakness in identifying, tracking, and mitigating actual threats.

Strategic Improvements

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Priority: High

    • Action: Implement MFA for all critical access points, including email systems, computer logins, and access to all sensitive data systems. This significantly reduces the risk of successful credential-based attacks.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Priority: High

    • Action: Create, disseminate, and enforce a comprehensive AUP for all employees. This policy should clearly define acceptable use of company IT assets, data handling, and network access to reduce internal risks.

    Enhance Security Awareness Training:

    • Priority: Medium

    • Action: While new and annual training is conducted, reinforce training on secure practices, phishing recognition, and the importance of MFA (once implemented). Consider incorporating specific scenarios related to data exposure and proper handling of sensitive information.

  • Establish Regular Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Priority: High

    • Action: Implement a continuous vulnerability scanning and management program. This includes regular internal and external scans, penetration testing, and a formal process for patching and mitigating identified vulnerabilities. Ensure findings are accurately recorded and tracked in a continually updated risk register.
