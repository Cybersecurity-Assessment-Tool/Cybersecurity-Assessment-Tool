Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The "No" responses highlight the following significant weaknesses:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Email is a primary vector for credential theft and phishing attacks. The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication for email accounts significantly increases the risk of account compromise, which can lead to broader organizational breaches.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A formal AUP is crucial for setting clear expectations for employee conduct regarding IT resources and data. Its absence can lead to inconsistent behavior, potential misuse of company assets, and increased insider threat risk.

  • Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the lack of annual refresher training for all employees means that security awareness may degrade over time, and employees may not be updated on evolving threats and best practices. Technical Analysis

The scan identified port 3389, associated with Microsoft Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP), as open. RDP is a widely used service for remote administration, but its exposure to the internet is a critical security concern. Without proper protections such as a VPN, network-level authentication (NLA), or strong Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on an RDP gateway, it is highly susceptible to brute-force attacks, credential stuffing, and potential exploitation of known vulnerabilities. The lack of specific product and version information in the scan results means its patch status cannot be definitively determined, but any internet-exposed RDP service warrants immediate attention.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name]. These are prioritized by severity and impact.

High-Priority Recommendations

  1. Mitigate RDP Exposure:

    • Immediate Action: Implement strict firewall rules to limit inbound access to port 3389 (RDP) to only trusted source IP addresses.

    • Long-Term Solution: Place RDP services behind a Virtual Private Network (VPN) requiring user authentication, or implement an RDP Gateway with Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to control and secure access. Ensure RDP is configured with Network Level Authentication (NLA) and all systems running RDP are fully patched.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Roll out and enforce mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication for all organizational email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of successful phishing and credential stuffing attacks.

Medium-Priority Recommendations

  1. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft, formally approve, and widely disseminate a comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy. Ensure all employees acknowledge and understand the policy, integrating it into both new hire onboarding and ongoing training programs.

  2. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threat landscapes, best practices for phishing detection, password hygiene, data handling, and compliance with organizational policies.

General Best Practices

  • Conduct regular vulnerability scanning and penetration tests to proactively identify and address security weaknesses.

  • Ensure a robust patch management program is in place for all operating systems, applications, and network devices, especially those exposed to the internet.

  • Implement a strong password policy, including complexity requirements and periodic changes.
