Recommendations

Based on the assessment, the following actionable recommendations are provided to improve [Organization Name]’s cybersecurity posture:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all email accounts across the organization. This is a critical control that significantly reduces the risk of account compromise through phishing, credential stuffing, or brute-force attacks, which are common initial access vectors for adversaries.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems: Mandate MFA for all access to systems containing sensitive organizational or client data. This crucial step helps prevent unauthorized data access, protects against insider threats, and minimizes the impact of potential data breaches, ensuring compliance with data protection regulations.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training: Develop and implement a mandatory, annual security awareness training program for all employees. Regular, engaging training ensures employees remain vigilant against evolving threats such as social engineering, phishing, and malware, effectively strengthening the human element of the organization’s defense.

  4. Consider Internal Vulnerability Scanning: While the external perimeter appears strong, it is highly recommended to conduct regular internal vulnerability scans. These scans help identify potential weaknesses within the internal network infrastructure and applications that an attacker could exploit once inside the network, complementing external defenses.

  5. Regular Policy and Control Review: Periodically review and update all security policies, procedures, and technical controls. This ensures they adapt to new and emerging threats, comply with evolving industry regulations, and remain aligned with the organization’s changing operational landscape and business objectives.
