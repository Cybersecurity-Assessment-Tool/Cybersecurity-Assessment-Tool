Risk Assessment

Based on the provided information, several significant risks have been identified, primarily stemming from gaps in organizational security controls. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided in the input data.

Identified Cybersecurity Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access significantly increases the risk of account compromise through phishing, credential stuffing, or brute-force attacks. Compromised email accounts can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data breaches, and further network infiltration. High
Lack of MFA for Computer Login Without MFA for computer logins, attacker success in gaining unauthorized access to internal systems is significantly higher. This bypasses a critical layer of defense against stolen or weak credentials, potentially leading to unauthorized data access, malware deployment, or lateral movement within the network. High
Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without an established and enforced AUP, employees may engage in activities that inadvertently or intentionally expose the organization to security risks, data loss, or legal liabilities. This includes improper use of company assets, unauthorized software installation, or risky internet browsing. Medium
Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training While new employees receive training, the lack of annual refresher training for all employees means that critical security best practices, emerging threats, and policy updates may not be consistently communicated or reinforced. This increases the human element risk in cybersecurity incidents. Medium

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access (Critical): Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all email accounts across the organization. This is a fundamental security control to prevent unauthorized access even if primary credentials are stolen.

  2. Implement MFA for Computer Logins (Critical): Require MFA for all user logins to company computers and endpoints. This will significantly bolster defenses against credential-based attacks targeting internal systems.

  3. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (High): Create a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that clearly defines employee responsibilities regarding the use of company IT assets, data handling, and internet conduct. Ensure all employees are trained on the AUP and acknowledge its terms.

  4. Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training (High): Establish a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires. This training should cover current threat landscapes, phishing recognition, data handling best practices, and policy updates to reinforce a strong security culture.

  5. Conduct Comprehensive Technical Assessments (Advisory): In future assessments, provide full and accurate organizational details, including network diagrams and target IP ranges, to enable comprehensive technical vulnerability scanning and penetration testing. This will provide a more complete picture of externally and internally facing technical risks.

  6. Establish a Vulnerability Management Program (Advisory): Implement a process for regularly identifying, assessing, and remediating technical vulnerabilities across all systems. This includes regular patching, configuration reviews, and internal/external vulnerability scans.

Conclusion

The assessment reveals that while [Organization Name] has some foundational security awareness training for new employees and MFA for sensitive data systems, there are significant and critical gaps in fundamental security controls, particularly concerning the widespread adoption of Multi-Factor Authentication and essential security policies. Addressing these issues with urgency will substantially reduce the organization’s attack surface and improve its resilience against common cyber threats. A more comprehensive assessment, including detailed technical scans and a review of existing vulnerabilities, would provide further insights into specific system-level risks.
