  • Risk Name: Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems

    • Overview: Without MFA, the compromise of a single set of credentials (username and password) is sufficient for an attacker to gain unauthorized access to critical and sensitive organizational data. This greatly increases the likelihood and impact of data breaches, intellectual property loss, regulatory non-compliance, and reputational damage.

    • Severity: High

  • Risk Name: Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training

    • Overview: Employees are often the primary target for initial compromise via social engineering, phishing, or malware. A lack of regular, comprehensive security awareness training means the workforce may not be equipped to recognize and defend against current cyber threats, making the organization highly susceptible to human-factor attacks that can lead to system compromise, data exfiltration, or financial loss.

    • Severity: Medium-High

  • Risk Name: Undetermined Technical Vulnerabilities

    • Overview: Due to the unavailability of network scan data, the external and potentially internal technical vulnerabilities of the organization remain unknown. This creates a significant blind spot, as critical vulnerabilities (e.g., exposed services, unpatched systems, weak configurations) could exist and be actively exploited without the organization’s knowledge.

Recommendations

Based on the assessment, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance [Organization Name]’s cybersecurity posture. These address the identified critical gaps and missing information necessary for a comprehensive security overview.

  1. Implement Mandatory MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Immediately mandate and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all access points to systems and applications containing sensitive data (e.g., customer databases, financial systems, intellectual property repositories, privileged access workstations).

    • Benefit: Drastically reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary user credentials are compromised, adding a crucial layer of defense against data breaches and account takeovers.

    • Priority: Critical

  2. Establish a Comprehensive Annual Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory, engaging, and up-to-date annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threat vectors (e.g., advanced phishing, ransomware, social engineering), secure password practices, data handling best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Benefit: Empowers employees to become an effective human firewall, significantly reducing the likelihood of successful human-factor cyberattacks and fostering a security-conscious culture.

    • Priority: High

  3. Conduct a Professional Network Scan and Vulnerability Assessment:

    • Action: Engage cybersecurity professionals to perform a thorough and well-documented network scan and vulnerability assessment against all internet-facing assets and critical internal infrastructure. Ensure the output is in a standard, parsable format.

    • Benefit: Identifies exploitable vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, outdated software, and potential entry points that could be leveraged by attackers, providing crucial data for technical risk mitigation.

    • Priority: High

  4. Develop a Formal Risk Management Framework:

    • Action: Establish a structured process for identifying, assessing, documenting, treating, and monitoring cybersecurity risks. This includes creating a risk register and defining clear roles and responsibilities for risk ownership and review.

    • Benefit: Provides a comprehensive and dynamic view of the organization’s risk landscape, enabling informed decision-making, effective resource allocation for risk mitigation, and compliance with industry standards.

    • Priority: Medium
