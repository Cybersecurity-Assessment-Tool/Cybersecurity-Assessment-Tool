Recommendations

To mitigate the identified critical gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following actionable recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  1. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear and comprehensive AUP that explicitly outlines the proper and prohibited use of all company IT assets, including computers, networks, internet access, email, and data handling procedures.

    • Ensure all employees, contractors, and relevant third parties formally review, understand, and acknowledge their compliance with the AUP.

    • Integrate AUP review and acknowledgment into the new hire onboarding process and mandatory annual security awareness training.

  2. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement a robust program for mandatory annual security awareness training that covers essential topics such as identifying phishing and social engineering attacks, strong password practices, data privacy, incident reporting procedures, and the organization’s AUP.

    • Utilize engaging and up-to-date content, and track completion rates to ensure full compliance.

    • Consider supplemental training or awareness campaigns throughout the year to address new, emerging threats or specific organizational needs.

Future Considerations

  1. Conduct Comprehensive Technical Assessments: Given the absence of technical scan data in this report, it is highly recommended to perform regular and thorough technical assessments, including:

    • External Vulnerability Scans: Regularly scan all internet-facing assets to identify known vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and outdated software.

    • Internal Vulnerability Scans: Conduct scans within the internal network to detect vulnerabilities in workstations, servers, and network devices.

    • Penetration Testing: Engage third-party experts to simulate real-world attacks to identify exploitable weaknesses in systems, applications, and human processes.

  2. Develop/Review Incident Response Plan: Ensure an up-to-date and tested incident response plan is in place to effectively detect, respond to, contain, and recover from security incidents. Regular drills and tabletop exercises are crucial.

  3. Regular Policy Reviews: Establish a schedule for reviewing and updating all security policies, including the AUP and training materials, to ensure they remain relevant to the evolving threat landscape and organizational changes.

Conclusion

[Organization Name] has a commendable foundation in its use of MFA and initial security awareness training. However, the absence of a formal Acceptable Use Policy and recurring annual security awareness training for all employees presents significant, addressable risks. By prioritizing and implementing the recommended policy and training enhancements, [Organization Name] can substantially strengthen its human firewall and reduce its overall attack surface. Furthermore, conducting comprehensive technical assessments will be crucial for a complete understanding of the organization’s security posture and for identifying and mitigating technical vulnerabilities.
