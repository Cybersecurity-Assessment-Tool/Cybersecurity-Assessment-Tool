Recommendations

To enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, concise, and comprehensive AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of all company IT resources, including networks, systems, software, data, email, and internet access.

    • Ensure the AUP addresses critical areas such as password management, data classification and handling, personal device usage (BYOD if applicable), social media guidelines, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Communicate the AUP to all employees, ensure they understand its contents, and obtain formal acknowledgment of their compliance.

    • Integrate the AUP into new employee onboarding and annual security awareness training.

  2. Conduct Regular Network Vulnerability Scans:

    • Establish a robust schedule for regular, authenticated network vulnerability scans of both internal and external infrastructure.

    • Ensure scans are configured to identify outdated software versions, missing patches, misconfigurations, default credentials, and known vulnerabilities in operating systems and applications.

    • Implement a formal process for analyzing scan results, prioritizing identified vulnerabilities based on severity and business impact, and coordinating timely remediation efforts.

    • Consider engaging third-party penetration testing services periodically to identify more complex vulnerabilities.

  3. Establish and Maintain a Centralized Risk Register:

    • Develop a centralized system or database to track, assess, and manage all identified cybersecurity risks, regardless of their source (e.g., policy gaps, technical vulnerabilities, third-party risks).

    • For each risk, document the risk name, description, potential impact, likelihood, current controls, severity, and proposed mitigation strategies.

    • Assign ownership for each risk and conduct regular reviews to monitor progress on mitigation actions and reassess residual risk.
