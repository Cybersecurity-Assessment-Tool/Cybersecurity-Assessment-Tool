Recommendations

Based on the available data and the identified critical gaps, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name].

Immediate High-Priority Actions

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all email accounts (e.g., Office 365, Google Workspace, on-premise Exchange).

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email compromise, a primary vector for phishing and BEC attacks.

  2. Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy MFA solutions for user logins to corporate workstations and servers, both on-premise and remote.

    • Benefit: Protects against credential theft and unauthorized access to internal network resources.

  3. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Ensure all systems housing sensitive or critical organizational data are protected with MFA. This includes databases, ERP systems, CRM, and cloud storage.

    • Benefit: Prevents unauthorized access to critical data, reducing the risk of data breaches and compliance violations.

Urgent Follow-Up Actions

  1. Conduct a Comprehensive Network Vulnerability Scan:

    • Action: Perform an external and internal vulnerability scan against all organizational IP ranges and critical systems.

    • Benefit: Identify and remediate unpatched systems, misconfigured services, and other technical vulnerabilities that were unable to be assessed in this report.

  2. Review and Establish a Formal Risk Register:

    • Action: Develop or update a comprehensive risk register that includes identified threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, impact, and current mitigation strategies.

    • Benefit: Provides a clear, ongoing view of the organization’s risk landscape, enabling informed decision-making and resource allocation for risk management.

General Security Best Practices (Already in Place - Maintain and Enhance)

  1. Maintain and Review Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Periodically review and update the AUP to ensure it remains relevant to current technology and organizational practices.

    • Benefit: Sets clear expectations for employee behavior and technology usage, reducing human-related risks.

  2. Continue and Enhance Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Regularly conduct security awareness training for all employees, including new hires, focusing on emerging threats like phishing, social engineering, and the importance of MFA.

    • Benefit: Empowers employees to be the first line of defense against cyber threats.
