Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and further enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins (High Priority)

    • Description: Mandate the use of Multi-Factor Authentication for all user logins to organizational computers, including workstations, servers, and remote access solutions.

    • Benefits: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials (username and password) are compromised through phishing, keylogging, or brute-force attacks. Adds a crucial layer of security.

    • Actionable Steps:

      • Research and select an MFA solution compatible with existing identity management systems (e.g., Active Directory, Azure AD, Okta).

      • Develop a phased rollout plan, starting with critical users or departments, then expanding enterprise-wide.

      • Provide comprehensive training and support to employees on how to use the new MFA system.

      • Establish clear policies for MFA enforcement and exception handling.

  2. Continue and Enhance Security Awareness Training

    • Description: Continue the existing regular security awareness training programs for all employees, and consider enhancing content to include emerging threats and the importance of MFA.

    • Benefits: Maintains and improves employee vigilance, making them a stronger first line of defense against social engineering and phishing attacks. Reinforces the importance of new security measures like MFA.

    • Actionable Steps:

      • Incorporate real-world examples of cyberattacks relevant to the organization.

      • Conduct periodic simulated phishing campaigns to test employee responsiveness and identify areas for further training.

      • Include modules specifically on the importance and proper use of MFA.

  3. Implement Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing

    • Description: Establish a schedule for regular external and internal vulnerability scans and periodic penetration tests of the network and critical systems.

    • Benefits: Proactively identifies new vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and outdated software that could be exploited by attackers. Provides a more comprehensive view of the security posture than point-in-time scans.

    • Actionable Steps:

      • Define a scope and frequency for vulnerability scanning (e.g., quarterly for external, monthly for internal).

      • Engage third-party experts for annual penetration testing to simulate real-world attacks.

      • Establish a robust remediation process for identified vulnerabilities, including tracking and verification.
