4. Risk Assessment

Based on the security control review and technical findings, the following risks have been identified. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided for this assessment.

Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access significantly increases the risk of account compromise. Phishing attacks, credential stuffing, and brute-force attacks can lead to unauthorized access to email accounts, potentially resulting in data breaches, business email compromise (BEC), and further internal network penetration. High
Infrequent Security Awareness Training Failing to conduct security awareness training for all employees at least once per year leaves the organization vulnerable to evolving social engineering tactics and other cyber threats. Employees may not be up-to-date on current phishing trends, malware prevention, and secure data handling practices, increasing the likelihood of human error leading to security incidents. Medium

5. Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Immediately enable and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing email services.

    • Communicate the importance and benefits of MFA to all employees and provide clear instructions for setup and usage.

  • Mandate Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Establish a mandatory annual (or more frequent) security awareness training program for all employees.

    • The training should cover current threats such as phishing, ransomware, social engineering, password best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Consider simulated phishing campaigns to reinforce training and assess employee vigilance.

  • Comprehensive Network Asset Inventory and Scanning:

    • Conduct a full discovery of all external-facing IP addresses and domain names belonging to [Organization Name] (if applicable).

    • Perform regular, authenticated and unauthenticated, vulnerability scans against all identified assets to detect potential weaknesses.

    • Implement regular penetration testing to simulate real-world attacks.

  • Review and Update Security Policies:

    • Periodically review and update all security policies, including the acceptable use policy, to ensure they remain relevant and address emerging threats.

    • Ensure employees acknowledge and understand these policies annually.

Conclusion

While the external network scan showed a minimal attack surface, the identified gaps in core security controls related to MFA for email and regular security awareness training pose significant risks. Prioritizing the implementation of these recommendations will substantially improve the organization’s defense against common cyber threats and strengthen its overall security posture.
