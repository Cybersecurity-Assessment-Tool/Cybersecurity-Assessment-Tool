Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The review highlights a critical gap: the absence of an employee acceptable use policy. An AUP is fundamental for establishing expectations for employees’ use of organizational assets, data, and systems. Without it, the organization faces increased risks related to:

  • Misuse of company resources.

  • Data leakage or unauthorized access.

  • Legal and compliance issues.

  • Inconsistent disciplinary actions for security violations.

The strong adoption of MFA and consistent security awareness training are commendable strengths that should be maintained.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture, addressing the identified gaps and risks.

High Priority Recommendations

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a comprehensive AUP outlining acceptable and unacceptable use of company information systems, networks, and data.

    • Obtain legal review and formal approval for the AUP.

    • Communicate the AUP to all employees, ensuring understanding and requiring acknowledgment of receipt.

    • Integrate AUP review into annual security awareness training.

Medium Priority Recommendations

  1. Verify Mitigation of "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk:

    • Confirm that Port 80 is indeed intentionally closed and not just filtered from the scan’s perspective.

    • Investigate if any other unencrypted web services are running on non-standard ports.

    • If web services are required, ensure they are served over HTTPS with valid SSL/TLS certificates.

  2. Expand Scope of Network Scans:

    • Conduct more comprehensive scans (e.g., full port range, different target IP ranges if applicable) to ensure no other unexpected services are exposed.

    • Implement regular vulnerability scanning for all internet-facing assets and internal networks.

General Best Practices

  1. Maintain Strong MFA Practices: Continue to enforce MFA for all critical access points (email, computers, sensitive systems). Regularly review MFA configurations and employee enrollment.

  2. Sustain Security Awareness Training: Continue with regular and engaging security awareness training for all employees, covering current threats and policy updates.

  3. Regular Policy Review: Establish a schedule for regular review and update of all security policies and procedures to ensure they remain relevant and effective against evolving threats.
