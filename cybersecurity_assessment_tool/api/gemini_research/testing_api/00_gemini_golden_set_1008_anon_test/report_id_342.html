Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The "No" responses highlight critical security gaps that could expose the organization to various risks:

  • Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of a formal AUP means employees may lack clear guidelines on the appropriate use of IT resources. This can lead to security vulnerabilities, non-compliance issues, and increased legal exposure due to employee misuse or negligence.

  • Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training: While new hires receive initial training, the lack of annual, organization-wide security awareness training leaves all employees susceptible to evolving cyber threats, such as sophisticated phishing campaigns, social engineering tactics, and emerging malware. Regular training is essential for maintaining a strong human firewall and adapting to the dynamic threat landscape.

Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive analysis, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name].

  1. Develop and Implement a Formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, concise, and comprehensive AUP document that defines the acceptable and unacceptable use of all organizational IT assets, including networks, systems, software, and data.

    • Ensure mandatory review and acknowledgement of the AUP by all employees upon hiring and annually thereafter.

    • Integrate AUP awareness into new hire and ongoing security training programs.

  2. Establish and Enforce Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement a mandatory and recurring security awareness training program for all employees, conducted at least once per year. This should extend beyond initial onboarding training for new hires.

    • The training content should be updated regularly to address current threats (e.g., phishing techniques, ransomware, business email compromise) and reinforce best practices for data handling, password hygiene, and incident reporting.

    • Consider utilizing engaging and interactive training modules, including simulated phishing exercises, to enhance employee retention and effectiveness.

  3. Verify and Reassess Previous Technical Findings:

    • Investigate the discrepancy between the current scan finding (port 80 closed) and the previously identified "Unencrypted Web Server" risk (port 80 open).

    • Confirm if the previous assessment referred to the same target IP address or a different system. If the current target was indeed the subject of the prior risk, document the successful remediation of port 80 being closed.

    • If the risk pertains to a different system or IP address, perform a targeted scan on that specific system to validate its current status and ensure that appropriate encryption (SSL/TLS) is enforced for any public-facing web services.
