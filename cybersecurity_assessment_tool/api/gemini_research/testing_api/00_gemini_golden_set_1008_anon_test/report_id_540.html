Analysis of Questionnaire Results

The questionnaire highlights the following critical gaps:

  • MFA for Email Access: The absence of mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access is a significant vulnerability. Email is a primary vector for phishing and Business Email Compromise (BEC) attacks, and without MFA, compromised credentials can grant attackers full access, leading to data breaches, financial fraud, and reputational damage.

  • Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the lack of annual security awareness training for all employees is a serious oversight. The threat landscape evolves rapidly, and employees are often the first line of defense. Without ongoing education, they are more susceptible to new social engineering techniques, malware, and other threats.

Positive findings include the use of MFA for computer logins and sensitive data systems, the presence of an acceptable use policy, and initial security awareness training for new hires. These are good foundational elements, but the identified gaps pose substantial risks.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of . Prioritization should be given to critical and high-severity risks.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access (Critical Priority):

    • Mandate MFA for all user accounts accessing email services, including administrative accounts.

    • Explore advanced MFA solutions that support adaptive authentication or conditional access based on location, device, or other risk factors.

    • Provide clear instructions and support for employees during the MFA rollout.

  2. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training (High Priority):

    • Develop and implement a comprehensive, mandatory annual (or more frequent) security awareness training program for all employees.

    • Training should cover current threats such as phishing, social engineering, ransomware, secure data handling, password hygiene, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Conduct regular simulated phishing exercises to test employee vigilance and reinforce training concepts.

  3. Ensure Functional Network Scan Capabilities (Medium Priority):

    • Investigate and rectify the issues preventing successful retrieval and processing of network scan data.

    • Establish a routine schedule for external and internal network vulnerability scanning.

    • Implement a process to regularly review scan results, identify vulnerabilities, and prioritize remediation efforts.

  4. Implement a Vulnerability Management Program (Medium Priority):

    • Establish a system or process to systematically identify, assess, track, and remediate vulnerabilities across all assets.

    • Maintain a central repository for all identified risks, including their overview, severity, and current mitigation status.

    • Assign clear ownership and deadlines for vulnerability remediation tasks.

  5. Data Integrity and Completeness:

    • Ensure that all data inputs required for security assessments are provided in a complete and correctly formatted manner to facilitate comprehensive analysis in future reviews.
