Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The questionnaire responses reveal two significant security control gaps that pose a notable risk to the organization:

  1. Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Login: Not requiring MFA for logging into computers significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials, potentially leading to breaches of internal systems and sensitive data.

  2. Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding the appropriate use of organizational IT assets. This lack of guidance can lead to potential misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance with regulatory requirements.

Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive assessment, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name] and mitigate identified risks.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all employee computer logins, particularly for endpoints accessing sensitive data or internal networks.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials (username and password) are stolen or compromised, thereby enhancing identity and access management.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft a comprehensive AUP outlining the proper use of organizational IT resources, data handling procedures, internet usage guidelines, and clear security responsibilities for all employees.

    • Action: Ensure all employees receive, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, with mandatory annual refreshers to reinforce its importance.

    • Benefit: Clarifies expectations, reduces insider threats, minimizes accidental data exposure, strengthens legal defensibility, and supports overall compliance efforts.

  3. Re-validate "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk Status:

    • Action: Investigate the discrepancy regarding the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk. Conduct further scans or manual checks to confirm if Port 80 is indeed closed on the intended asset. Identify the specific asset the original risk referred to.

    • Action: If Port 80 is confirmed closed on the relevant asset, update or archive the risk accordingly. If the risk pertains to a different asset, ensure the asset details are accurately recorded in the risk register.

    • Benefit: Maintains an accurate and current risk register, ensuring that security resources are focused on active, relevant threats and vulnerabilities.
