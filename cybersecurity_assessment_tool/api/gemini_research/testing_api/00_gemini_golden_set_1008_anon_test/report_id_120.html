Risk Assessment

Based on the available organizational data and the security control review, several high-priority risks have been identified. These risks primarily stem from gaps in foundational security practices.

Summary of Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access Email is a primary vector for phishing and compromise. Without MFA, an attacker only needs a password to gain access, leading to potential data breaches, business email compromise (BEC), and further internal lateral movement. Critical
Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems Access to systems containing sensitive data without MFA significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access. A compromised credential could lead to exfiltration or manipulation of critical information, resulting in severe data breach consequences. High
Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without a clear AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding company IT resources, leading to misuse, shadow IT, or behaviors that introduce security risks. This lack of guidance can also hinder disciplinary actions for security violations. High
Inadequate Onboarding Security Training New employees, particularly those unfamiliar with the organization’s specific security culture and threats, are highly susceptible to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing). Lack of initial training leaves them vulnerable and increases the overall organizational risk. High

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Immediately enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of account compromise due to phishing or stolen credentials.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Deploy MFA for all systems that handle or store sensitive organizational data. This adds a critical layer of security for high-value assets.

  • Conduct a Comprehensive External Network Scan: Initiate a new, complete external network scan from an unhindered source to accurately assess perimeter vulnerabilities and external attack surface.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Develop and Disseminate an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a clear, comprehensive AUP that outlines employee responsibilities and permissible use of organizational IT resources. Ensure all employees acknowledge and understand this policy.

  • Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Hires: Implement a mandatory security awareness training program as part of the onboarding process for all new employees. This ensures new staff are immediately aware of their security responsibilities and common threats.

  • Regular Security Audits: Implement a schedule for regular security audits and vulnerability assessments (both internal and external) to proactively identify and mitigate new risks.

  • Incident Response Plan Review: While not explicitly assessed, it is critical to have a well-defined and tested incident response plan to minimize the impact of security incidents.

Conclusion

The cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name] shows critical gaps in fundamental security controls, particularly concerning MFA implementation, policy documentation, and initial employee training.
