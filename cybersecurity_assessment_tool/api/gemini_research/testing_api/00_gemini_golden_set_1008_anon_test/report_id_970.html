Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all systems containing or accessing sensitive organizational data. This will provide a crucial layer of defense against credential theft and unauthorized access.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities, acceptable use of company assets (hardware, software, network), internet usage guidelines, and prohibited activities. Ensure all employees are required to read, understand, and acknowledge this policy annually.

  3. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Integrate mandatory security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new hires. This training should cover common threats, organizational security policies, reporting procedures, and best practices for secure computing.

  4. Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Implement a program for mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees. This training should be updated regularly to reflect current threat landscapes, new vulnerabilities, and evolving organizational policies.

  5. Regular Policy and Training Review: Establish a schedule for regular review and update of all security policies and training materials to ensure they remain relevant and effective against emerging threats.

  6. Consider Regular Vulnerability Assessments: While the initial scan was clear, it is recommended to conduct regular internal and external vulnerability assessments and penetration tests to proactively identify and remediate technical vulnerabilities across all IT assets.
