Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The responses indicate significant weaknesses in foundational security practices:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: This is a critical vulnerability. Email is a primary vector for phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and credential theft. Without MFA, a single compromised password can lead to unauthorized access to sensitive communications and systems.

  • Absence of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A formal AUP is crucial for setting clear expectations regarding employee conduct, data handling, and use of IT resources. Its absence can lead to inconsistent practices, increased risk of data leakage, and challenges in enforcing security standards.

  • Inadequate Security Awareness Training: The complete lack of security awareness training for new and existing employees leaves the organization highly susceptible to social engineering attacks, malware infections, and other human-factor vulnerabilities. Employees are often the first and last line of defense, and without proper training, this defense is severely weakened.

Technical Analysis

The discovery of an open Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) port (3389/tcp) directly accessible from the internet represents a critical security finding. RDP is a frequent target for cyber attackers, utilized for brute-force credential attacks, exploitation of software vulnerabilities, and unauthorized access to internal networks. Its exposure significantly increases the organization’s attack surface and risk of external compromise. This finding directly correlates with an existing risk, underscoring its severity.

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name].

  1. Mitigate RDP Exposure:

    • Immediate Action: Close port 3389 on all external-facing firewalls if direct RDP access from the internet is not absolutely essential.

    • Long-Term Solution: Implement a Virtual Private Network (VPN) solution for all remote access to internal resources, including RDP. Ensure VPN access is protected by strong authentication, preferably Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). If direct RDP exposure is unavoidable, implement strict IP address whitelisting, strong account lockout policies, and network-level rate limiting.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Enable and enforce mandatory MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This significantly reduces the risk of account compromise due to stolen or phished credentials.

  3. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines appropriate and inappropriate use of organizational IT assets, data, and internet access. The policy should cover topics such as password security, data handling, software installation, and communication etiquette.

    • Ensure all employees review, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally during onboarding and annually thereafter.

  4. Establish a Robust Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees as part of their onboarding process.

    • Conduct regular (at least annual) mandatory security awareness training for all existing employees.

    • Training content should cover critical topics such as identifying phishing attempts, safe browsing practices, password hygiene, social engineering tactics, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Consider implementing regular simulated phishing exercises to test and reinforce employee awareness and response.
