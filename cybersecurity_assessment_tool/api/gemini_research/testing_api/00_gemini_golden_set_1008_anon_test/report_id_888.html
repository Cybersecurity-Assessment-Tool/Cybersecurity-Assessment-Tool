5. Recommendations

Given the limitations of the input data, these recommendations focus on improving data collection for future assessments and reinforcing general cybersecurity best practices.

5.1 Data Collection & Assessment Improvement

  • Provide Complete and Valid Data: For future assessments, ensure all JSON inputs are correctly formatted and contain comprehensive data for organizational details, network scans (including target, scan date, open ports, services, versions), and existing vulnerability registers.

  • Regular Network Scans: Implement a schedule for regular, authenticated (if possible) network scans across all internal and external-facing assets. This provides a real-time view of the attack surface.

  • Maintain a Vulnerability Management Program: Establish and maintain a robust vulnerability management program that includes regular scanning, identification, prioritization, and remediation of security flaws. Document all identified vulnerabilities and their current status.

5.2 General Security Enhancements (Reinforcing Strengths)

While specific technical issues could not be identified, the organization’s strong questionnaire responses warrant further reinforcement:

  • Regular Review of MFA Implementation: Continuously review and update MFA policies and technologies to ensure they cover all critical systems and are resilient against evolving attack techniques (e.g., SIM swapping, MFA fatigue attacks).

  • Enhance Security Awareness Training: While current training is in place, consider advanced, scenario-based training that simulates real-world threats (e.g., phishing simulations, social engineering tests) to further educate employees.

  • Policy Review and Enforcement: Regularly review the Acceptable Use Policy and other security policies to ensure they remain current with technological changes and threat landscapes, and that they are consistently enforced.

  • Implement Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR): Deploy EDR solutions on all endpoints to provide advanced threat detection, investigation, and response capabilities beyond traditional antivirus.

  • Regular Patch Management: Ensure all operating systems, applications, and network devices are kept up-to-date with the latest security patches to mitigate known vulnerabilities.

Conclusion

This assessment highlights the critical importance of comprehensive and accurate data for conducting a thorough cybersecurity posture analysis. While the organizational self-assessment indicates strong foundational controls, the absence of detailed technical scan results and pre-existing risk information severely limits the scope and depth of this report. Future assessments, armed with complete data, will be able to provide a much more actionable and detailed risk profile.
