Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The security questionnaire responses reveal several critical weaknesses and significant gaps in the organization’s security posture:

  • Critical MFA Gaps: The most striking finding is the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication for both email access and computer login. While MFA is implemented for sensitive data systems, its absence for email and general computer access exposes the organization to severe risks. Compromised credentials for these entry points are a primary vector for data breaches, ransomware, and business email compromise (BEC).

  • Insufficient Security Awareness Training: The organization currently lacks security awareness training for both new employees and ongoing training for all employees. This is a major vulnerability, as human error and susceptibility to social engineering (e.g., phishing, vishing, smishing) are significant factors in successful cyberattacks. Without proper training, employees cannot effectively act as a strong line of defense.

  • Positive Control: The presence of an employee acceptable use policy is a positive step, indicating some foundational policy work. However, its effectiveness is diminished without accompanying security awareness training to ensure employees understand and adhere to its principles.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and significantly improve the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided. These actions prioritize mitigating the most critical identified gaps.

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all organizational email accounts. This is a foundational security control crucial for protecting against credential theft and Business Email Compromise (BEC).

  • Implement MFA for Computer Login: Implement and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all computer logins, especially for accounts with administrative or privileged access. This adds a critical layer of defense against unauthorized access to internal systems.

  • Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Develop and implement a mandatory cybersecurity awareness training program for all new hires. This training should be completed before new employees are granted access to any organizational systems or networks.

  • Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Establish a recurring, mandatory annual (or more frequent) security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats, best practices, and organizational security policies.

Mid-Term Actions (Moderate Priority)

  • Review and Update Security Policies: Conduct a comprehensive review of all existing security policies, including the acceptable use policy, to ensure they are current, cover identified risks, and align with industry best practices and regulatory requirements. Ensure policies are regularly communicated and acknowledged by employees.

  • Develop Incident Response Plan: If not already in place, develop a formal Incident Response Plan (IRP). This plan should outline procedures for detecting, analyzing, containing, eradicating, recovering from, and post-incident review of cybersecurity incidents. Regular drills and tabletop exercises should be conducted.
