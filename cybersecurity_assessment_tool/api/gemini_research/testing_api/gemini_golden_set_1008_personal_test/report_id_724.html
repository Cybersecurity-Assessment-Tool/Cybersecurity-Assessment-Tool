The objective is to identify critical vulnerabilities, assess the current security controls, and provide actionable recommendations to mitigate identified risks and strengthen the organization’s defense mechanisms.

Questionnaire Analysis

The questionnaire responses reveal significant gaps:

  • Lack of MFA for Email and Sensitive Data: The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access and sensitive data systems is a critical vulnerability. This significantly increases the risk of account compromise through phishing or brute-force attacks, potentially leading to data breaches or unauthorized access to critical information.

  • Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of a formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy is a major governance and compliance gap. An AUP sets clear expectations for employee behavior regarding company IT resources, reducing insider threats and ensuring adherence to security best practices. Without one, employees may unknowingly engage in risky behavior.

The finding of no open ports on the scanned target 192.168.1.100 is generally positive from a network perimeter perspective for this specific host, suggesting a well-hardened configuration for external network exposure. However, it is crucial to understand that this scan provides a snapshot of network-level services and does not assess:

  • Internal vulnerabilities (e.g., misconfigured software, missing patches).

Therefore, while the initial network scan is favorable for this specific host, it should not be taken as a comprehensive assurance of the system’s security posture.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance Bluebird Bio’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access (Critical):

    • Action: Immediately enable and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all user accounts accessing email systems.

    • Benefit: This will significantly reduce the risk of account takeover even if passwords are compromised, protecting against phishing, credential stuffing, and business email compromise (BEC) attacks.

  2. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (Critical):

    • Action: Require MFA for logging into all systems that contain or provide access to sensitive organizational data.

    • Benefit: This adds a crucial layer of security, protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access and potential data exfiltration.

  3. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (High):

    • Action: Draft, disseminate, and enforce a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy for all employees regarding the use of company IT assets, networks, and data.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces insider threats, minimizes accidental data exposure, and supports compliance with industry regulations. Ensure all employees acknowledge and understand the policy.

  4. Continue Robust Security Awareness Training (Good Practice):

    • Action: Maintain and enhance existing security awareness training programs for new hires and annually for all employees. Incorporate specific modules on identifying phishing attempts and the importance of MFA and AUP.

    • Benefit: A well-informed workforce is the first line of defense against social engineering attacks and helps foster a security-conscious culture.

  5. Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Internal Scans (Ongoing):

    • Action: While the recent scan showed no open ports on the target, conduct regular, authenticated vulnerability scans and penetration tests of internal networks, applications, and systems.

    • Benefit: Identifies misconfigurations, unpatched software, and other vulnerabilities that a non-credentialed network scan might miss, providing a more complete picture of the internal security posture.

Conclusion

Bluebird Bio demonstrates a foundational understanding of security with strong security awareness training programs and MFA for computer logins. However, critical vulnerabilities exist due to the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication for email and sensitive data systems, and the absence of a formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy. The technical scan of a specific internal host was positive with no open ports detected. By promptly addressing the identified critical and high-severity risks through the recommended actions, Bluebird Bio can significantly enhance its resilience against common cyber threats and strengthen its overall security posture.
