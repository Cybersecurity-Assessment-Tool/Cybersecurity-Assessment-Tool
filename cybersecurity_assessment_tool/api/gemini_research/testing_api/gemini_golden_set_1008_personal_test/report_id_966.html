3.1 Key Gaps Identified from Questionnaire

  • Lack of MFA for Email and Computer Login: This is a critical risk. Without Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), compromised credentials (e.g., via phishing attacks) can grant attackers full access to employee email accounts and internal systems, leading to data breaches and business email compromise.

  • Absence of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A foundational policy gap. An AUP sets clear expectations for employee conduct regarding IT assets, reducing the risk of insider threats, misuse of resources, and potential legal liabilities.

  • Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training: Employees are often the weakest link in an organization’s security posture. Regular, comprehensive training for all employees is vital to keep them informed about evolving cyber threats and best practices for identifying and avoiding social engineering attacks.

6. Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive analysis, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance True North Travel’s cybersecurity posture and mitigate identified risks.

6.1 Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  • Verify Port 80 Status and Risk Accuracy: Immediately investigate the discrepancy regarding Port 80. Confirm whether an unencrypted web server is indeed operational on any asset within the organization’s scope. If an unencrypted server is found, prioritize its closure or migration to HTTPS. Update the risk register accordingly.

  • Implement MFA for Email and Computer Login: Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all employee email accounts and computer logins. This is a critical and highly effective control against credential theft and unauthorized access.

6.2 Strategic Actions (Medium Priority)

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the proper and acceptable use of organizational IT resources, data, and communication systems. Ensure all employees acknowledge and adhere to the policy through regular training and sign-offs.

  • Mandate Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Establish and conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, not just new hires. This training should cover common threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), secure data handling practices, and company-specific security policies.

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning: Implement a schedule for regular internal and external vulnerability scanning of all IT assets to continuously identify and remediate technical weaknesses before they can be exploited.

6.3 Long-Term Enhancements

  • Risk Register Management: Establish a robust process for regularly reviewing, updating, and prioritizing the organization’s risk register to ensure accuracy, relevance, and effective risk mitigation.

  • Incident Response Plan: Develop and regularly test a comprehensive incident response plan to ensure the organization can effectively detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents with minimal impact.

  • Continuous Security Monitoring: Implement continuous security monitoring solutions (e.g., SIEM, EDR) to detect suspicious activities and potential threats in real-time across the network and endpoints.
