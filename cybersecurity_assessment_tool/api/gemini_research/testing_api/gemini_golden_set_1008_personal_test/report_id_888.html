5. Risk Assessment

Based on the available information, several high-priority risks and critical gaps have been identified. The absence of comprehensive technical scan data and pre-existing vulnerability information means this assessment is primarily based on self-reported security controls, and the actual technical risk landscape may be more severe.

5.1 Identified Gaps and Associated Risks

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access:

    • Risk: High. Compromised email accounts (e.g., via phishing) can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data exfiltration, account takeovers, and lateral movement within the network. Without MFA, a simple password compromise grants full access.

    • Severity: Critical

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Risk: High. Similar to email, compromised user credentials without MFA can grant attackers access to internal systems, potentially leading to ransomware, data breaches, and disruption of operations.

    • Severity: Critical

  • Absence of Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Risk: High. Employees are often the weakest link in the security chain. Without regular, up-to-date training, employees are more susceptible to social engineering attacks (phishing, vishing, smishing), inadvertently clicking malicious links, or falling for scams that bypass technical controls.

    • Severity: High

  • Unassessed External Attack Surface:

    • Risk: High. Due to the lack of network scan data, unknown open ports, outdated services, or misconfigurations on the perimeter (including 175.36.146.2) could expose critical vulnerabilities to external attackers.

    • Severity: Critical (based on potential unknown threats)

  • Unknown Internal Vulnerabilities and Current Risk Landscape:

    • Risk: High. Without an existing vulnerability register or internal scan data, the organization is unaware of its internal security weaknesses, making it difficult to prioritize remediation efforts and potentially leaving critical systems exposed.

    • Severity: High (based on potential unknown threats)

6. Recommendations

Based on the identified gaps and risks, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Solid State’s cybersecurity posture:

6.1 Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all email access across the organization.

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all computer logins for employees.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of credential compromise leading to unauthorized access.

  2. Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory, annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires.

    • Benefit: Improves employee vigilance against social engineering and common cyber threats, reinforcing a culture of security.

6.2 Urgent Data Acquisition (Critical for Future Assessments)

  1. Perform Comprehensive External Network Vulnerability Scan:

    • Action: Engage a cybersecurity firm to conduct an in-depth vulnerability scan of all external-facing assets, including the external IP address 175.36.146.2 and any public-facing domains.

    • Benefit: Identifies exploitable weaknesses on the organization’s perimeter, allowing for proactive remediation.

  2. Establish a Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Action: Implement processes for regular vulnerability scanning (internal and external), penetration testing, and maintenance of a central vulnerability register to track, prioritize, and remediate discovered issues.

    • Benefit: Provides continuous visibility into the organization’s evolving risk landscape and ensures timely patching and configuration management.

6.3 Ongoing Security Enhancements

  1. Review and Update Security Policies:

    • Action: Regularly review and update existing security policies, including the acceptable use policy, to reflect current threats and best practices.

    • Benefit: Ensures that organizational guidelines remain relevant and effective.

  2. Consider Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR):

    • Action: Research and consider implementing an EDR solution across all endpoints to provide advanced threat detection, investigation, and response capabilities.

    • Benefit: Enhances visibility into endpoint activity and provides rapid containment capabilities against sophisticated attacks.

7. Conclusion

Solid State currently faces significant cybersecurity risks due to critical gaps in security controls, particularly regarding Multi-Factor Authentication and comprehensive security awareness training. The inability to analyze technical scan results and existing vulnerabilities presents a substantial blind spot. Addressing the identified MFA and training deficiencies should be the immediate priority. Concurrently, a full technical assessment is urgently needed to establish a baseline understanding of the external attack surface and internal vulnerabilities. Proactive measures, combined with a robust vulnerability management program, are essential for improving Solid State’s overall security posture.
