Executive Summary

The cybersecurity assessment of Summit Peak Partners reveals a mixed security posture. The organization demonstrates good practices in several areas, such as employee security awareness training and MFA for sensitive data systems. However, two critical areas require immediate attention:

  1. Critical Data Exposure: A network scan identified an open port (8080) exposing an interface explicitly titled "TOP SECRET DB". This finding directly contradicts an existing risk register entry classifying this port as secure and a false positive, indicating a severe oversight and potential data breach risk.

  2. MFA for Email Deficiency: Multi-Factor Authentication is not required for email access, making email accounts highly susceptible to compromise, which can lead to further breaches.

These issues pose significant threats to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational data. Urgent implementation of the provided recommendations is paramount to strengthen the organization’s defenses.

Key Findings from Questionnaire

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following prioritized recommendations are provided to enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture:

  1. Immediate Action: Secure Exposed "TOP SECRET DB" (High Priority):

    • Instantly implement firewall rules to restrict access to port 8080 to only necessary, authorized internal IP addresses or systems. If external access is required, ensure it is via a VPN or other secure, authenticated tunnel.

    • Investigate the service running on port 8080. Determine its purpose, what data it contains, and why it is exposed.

    • Implement strong authentication and authorization mechanisms for this service.

    • Re-assess the necessity of this service being network accessible. Consider relocating it to a more secure, isolated segment or air-gapping it if possible.

  2. Implement MFA for Email Access (High Priority):

    • Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all email accounts across the organization. Prioritize strong MFA methods such as hardware tokens (e.g., FIDO2/U2F) or authenticator apps (e.g., Microsoft Authenticator, Google Authenticator) over SMS-based MFA.

    • Conduct an awareness campaign to educate employees on the importance of MFA and how to enroll/use it.

  3. Update and Validate Risk Register (Medium Priority):

    • Review and update the internal risk register. The entry for "Port 8080 Secured" must be re-evaluated and corrected to reflect the critical exposure identified.

    • Establish a continuous process for validating risk register entries against new scan data, penetration tests, and threat intelligence to ensure its accuracy and relevance.

  4. Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing (Medium Priority):

    • Implement a schedule for regular, authenticated vulnerability scanning of internal and external assets.

    • Conduct periodic penetration tests to simulate real-world attacks and identify weaknesses that automated scans might miss.

  5. Continue Security Awareness Training (Low Priority - Ongoing):

    • Maintain and enhance current security awareness training programs, especially focusing on phishing detection and the importance of MFA.
