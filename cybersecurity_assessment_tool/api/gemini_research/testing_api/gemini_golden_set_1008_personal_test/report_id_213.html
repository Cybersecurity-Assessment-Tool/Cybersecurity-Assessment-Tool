Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Clear Path based on an analysis of network scan data, organizational security control responses, and existing risk registers. While the organization demonstrates strengths in some areas, such as Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computers, and regular security awareness training, critical gaps have been identified. Notably, a service advertising "TOP SECRET DB" on port 8080 was discovered, directly contradicting the organization’s prior assessment of this port as secure. Additionally, the lack of MFA for sensitive data systems and the absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy present significant risks. Immediate attention to these findings, particularly the discrepancy regarding port 8080, is highly recommended to mitigate potential data exposure and enhance overall security.

This suggests potential exposure of highly sensitive information to the network. This finding directly contradicts the organization’s existing risk register (Input 3), which identifies Port 8080 as "Secured" and a "false positive" with a CVSS score of 0.0. This major discrepancy indicates either an outdated risk assessment, an unaddressed vulnerability, or a severe misconfiguration. Immediate and thorough investigation is warranted to ascertain the nature and actual sensitivity of the data, and to secure the service.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance Clear Path’s cybersecurity posture and address identified risks:

  1. Immediate Investigation and Remediation of Port 8080 ():

    • Conduct an urgent and thorough forensic investigation into the service running on port 8080 on the target system.

    • Determine the exact nature of the "TOP SECRET DB" advertised. Verify if sensitive data is indeed exposed or if this is a misleading banner.

    • If sensitive data is exposed, immediately isolate the system, remove the public exposure, and implement robust security controls.

    • Update the organizational risk register to accurately reflect the true status and severity of this finding.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Prioritize the design and implementation of MFA for all systems that contain, process, or provide access to sensitive organizational data.

    • This crucial control significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary user credentials are compromised.

  3. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities, acceptable use of company IT resources (hardware, software, network, data), and prohibited activities.

    • Ensure all employees undergo mandatory training on the AUP and provide documented acknowledgment of its contents.

    • Establish a regular review cycle for the AUP to ensure it remains current with technological changes and organizational policies.

  4. Review and Update Risk Register Management Processes:

    • Establish a robust process for regular review and validation of existing risk assessments against current technical scans, audit findings, and organizational changes.

    • Ensure that any discrepancies between new findings and recorded risks are promptly investigated, re-assessed, and resolved.

Conclusion

Clear Path demonstrates a foundational commitment to cybersecurity through existing security awareness programs and MFA implementation for core systems. However, the critical vulnerabilities identified, particularly the highly concerning "TOP SECRET DB" exposure on port 8080 and the lack of MFA for sensitive data, require immediate and decisive action. Addressing these issues systematically will significantly strengthen the organization’s overall defense against cyber threats and ensure the robust protection of its sensitive assets.
