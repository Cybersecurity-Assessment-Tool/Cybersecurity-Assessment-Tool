Analysis of Security Controls

The organization demonstrates good practice in implementing MFA for email and computer logins. However, significant gaps exist in critical areas:

  • MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: The absence of MFA for accessing sensitive data systems represents a high risk, as it leaves critical assets vulnerable to unauthorized access via compromised credentials.

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without an AUP, employees may not be aware of their responsibilities regarding IT resource usage, increasing the risk of misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance.

  • Security Awareness Training: The complete lack of security awareness training for both new and existing employees is a critical vulnerability. Human error remains a leading cause of security incidents, and a well-trained workforce is the first line of defense against phishing, social engineering, and other common attack vectors.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of , the following recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions (Critical Risks)

  • Investigate "" (on ):

    • Immediately investigate the service running on Port 22 of and determine if its exposure is intended and properly secured.

    • If unintended, disable or restrict access to the service. If necessary, apply appropriate firewall rules.

    • Ensure strong authentication mechanisms are in place if the service is required and intended to be accessible.

Strategic Improvements (High Risks)

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all access to systems containing sensitive organizational or client data. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access due to stolen or weak credentials.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines appropriate use of company IT resources, internet, email, and data.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and acknowledge the AUP, and integrate it into employee onboarding and ongoing training.

  • Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • For New Employees: Implement mandatory security awareness training as part of the onboarding process, covering fundamental cybersecurity principles, common threats (e.g., phishing), and organizational policies.

    • Annual Refresher Training: Conduct annual mandatory security awareness training for all employees to keep them informed about new threats, reinforce best practices, and update them on policy changes.
