Executive Summary

This report details the findings from a cybersecurity posture assessment conducted for Bluebird Bio. The assessment involved a review of organizational security controls through a questionnaire and an external network scan.

Overall, Bluebird Bio demonstrates a foundational understanding of critical security practices, particularly in requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and sensitive data systems. However, several key areas require immediate attention to strengthen the organization’s security posture. Critical gaps were identified concerning the absence of MFA for computer logins, a lack of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and insufficient annual security awareness training for all employees. The external network scan did not reveal any open ports, which is a positive indicator for external attack surface management.

Addressing the identified gaps, especially those related to MFA and clear employee policies, is paramount to mitigating significant risks such as unauthorized access, data misuse, and human-factor vulnerabilities. This report outlines specific recommendations to address these findings and enhance the overall security resilience of Bluebird Bio.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance Bluebird Bio’s overall cybersecurity posture. These are prioritized based on the severity of the associated risks.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins (High Priority):

    • Action: Evaluate and deploy an MFA solution for all computer login processes across the organization. This could involve hardware tokens, smartphone apps, or biometric authentication integrated with your identity provider.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access to workstations and servers even if primary credentials are stolen or compromised.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (Medium Priority):

    • Action: Draft a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities regarding the use of company assets, data, internet, email, and software.

    • Action: Distribute the AUP to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgements, and integrate it into new employee onboarding processes.

    • Benefit: Provides clear guidelines for employees, reduces accidental misuse of resources, and establishes a foundation for disciplinary actions in case of policy violations.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees (Medium Priority):

    • Action: Implement a recurring (at least annual) security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires.

    • Action: Training should cover current threat landscapes (e.g., phishing, ransomware), organizational policies, and best practices for data protection and incident reporting.

    • Benefit: Keeps employees informed about evolving threats, reinforces security best practices, and reduces the likelihood of human-factor security incidents.

Conclusion

Bluebird Bio has a solid foundation in some aspects of cybersecurity, such as protecting sensitive data systems and email with MFA, and maintaining a clean external attack surface. However, critical vulnerabilities exist in the areas of endpoint login security, formal employee policy, and ongoing security education. By diligently implementing the recommended actions, Bluebird Bio can significantly enhance its security posture, reduce its overall risk profile, and better protect its valuable assets from cyber threats. Continued vigilance and a proactive approach to cybersecurity are essential for maintaining a strong defense against an ever-evolving threat landscape.
