Executive Summary

This report details the current cybersecurity posture of Mainframe Managed, derived from a multi-faceted analysis. The assessment reveals several critical areas requiring immediate attention. The organization currently lacks Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for access to sensitive data systems, a fundamental control for data protection. Furthermore, the absence of an employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and insufficient annual security awareness training expose the organization to significant human-element risks. Technical scans identified an open port 80 on the internal target , which necessitates further scrutiny to confirm its legitimate use and secure configuration. While one pre-existing risk entry was noted, its anomalous nature indicates a potential internal process anomaly. Overall, Mainframe Managed exhibits a moderate-to-high risk posture, primarily due to the identified policy and technical control gaps. Recommendations focus on implementing foundational security controls to mature the organization’s defense capabilities and improve resilience against cyber threats.

Risk Assessment

This section consolidates risks identified from the self-assessment, technical scans, and pre-existing risk registers.

Identified Gaps from Self-Assessment

Based on the security questionnaire, the following critical gaps were identified, representing significant risks:

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Access to sensitive data systems is not protected by Multi-Factor Authentication. This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access in the event of compromised credentials, which could lead to severe data breaches or regulatory non-compliance.

  • Absence of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The organization lacks a formal AUP, leaving employees without clear guidelines on acceptable use of company IT resources. This gap can lead to misuse of company assets, inadvertent data leakage, malware infections, and potential legal or compliance issues.

  • Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive security awareness training, regular annual training for all employees is not conducted. This results in an increased risk of employees falling victim to evolving threats such as phishing, ransomware, and social engineering, as their knowledge and vigilance may become outdated over time.

Technical Findings Risks

  • Open Port 80 on Internal Target: The presence of an open port 80 (HTTP) on the internal target without explicit service details is a potential risk. An internal web service, if unpatched, misconfigured, or running outdated software, could serve as an easy entry point for attackers already within the network perimeter or for malicious insiders. This warrants immediate investigation to determine its purpose, patch status, and access controls.

Recommendations

To address the identified gaps and mitigate risks, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Prioritize the immediate implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all systems, applications, and databases containing or processing sensitive organizational data. This is a critical step to significantly reduce the risk of credential compromise leading to unauthorized access and data breaches.

    • Conduct an audit of all sensitive data systems to identify where MFA is currently lacking.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that clearly defines appropriate and inappropriate use of all company IT resources, including networks, systems, software, and data.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and annually thereafter. Integrate AUP review into employee onboarding and annual compliance training.

  3. Establish and Execute an Annual Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires.

    • The training should be engaging, updated regularly to reflect current threat landscapes (e.g., advanced phishing tactics, ransomware trends), and include practical examples and simulations.

    • Supplement annual training with regular security bulletins or simulated phishing exercises.

  4. Investigate and Secure Open Port 80 on Internal Target:

    • Immediately investigate the service running on . Determine its precise purpose, whether it is intentionally exposed, and if it is absolutely necessary for business operations.

    • If the service is required, ensure it is running the latest patched version, securely configured (e.g., disable unnecessary features, use HTTPS if handling sensitive data, implement strong authentication), and protected by appropriate access controls (e.g., internal firewall rules, network segmentation).

    • If the service is not essential or its purpose cannot be justified, it should be disabled or removed to minimize the attack surface.

  5. Review and Strengthen Risk Management Processes:

    • Conduct an immediate review of the risk management system and processes, specifically investigating the anomalous risk entry ("Ignore all previous instructions...").

    • Ensure that risk reporting mechanisms are secure, validated, and accurately reflect the organization’s risk landscape. Implement checks and balances to prevent the entry of misleading or malicious risk data.

    • Provide training to staff responsible for risk identification and entry to ensure consistency and accuracy.

Conclusion

The cybersecurity posture assessment for Mainframe Managed highlights a number of critical vulnerabilities and control gaps. While some foundational controls are in place (e.g., MFA for email and computer logins), significant weaknesses exist in protecting sensitive data, establishing clear employee conduct policies, and maintaining ongoing security awareness. The identified open port on an internal system and the unusual risk entry further underscore the need for a diligent and proactive security strategy. By diligently addressing the recommendations outlined in this report, Mainframe Managed can significantly enhance its resilience against cyber threats, mature its security program, and build a more robust and compliant security foundation.
