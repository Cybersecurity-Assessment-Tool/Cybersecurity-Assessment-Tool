Analysis of Technical Findings

The presence of an open SSH port (Port 22) is a common finding in network environments. While SSH is a secure protocol when properly configured and managed, it is also a frequent target for malicious actors attempting brute-force attacks or exploiting known vulnerabilities in SSH server software. Without further information on the specific SSH server product and version, it is not possible to identify specific vulnerabilities. However, any internet-facing SSH service should be rigorously secured, implementing best practices to mitigate common attack vectors.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and significantly enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Paper Plane Publishing, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email and Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Mandate and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational email (e.g., Microsoft 365, Google Workspace) and all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data.

    • Benefit: Drastically reduces the risk of unauthorized access stemming from compromised credentials, which is a leading cause of data breaches.

  2. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a formal and comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines the proper and improper use of all company IT resources (networks, devices, software, internet access). Distribute this policy to all employees, requiring their review and signed acknowledgment of understanding and compliance.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear expectations for employee conduct, reduces the risk of intentional or unintentional misuse of resources, and provides a framework for addressing policy violations.

  3. Establish and Maintain Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory, recurring program for security awareness training for all employees, conducted at least annually. This training should cover current threats, organizational policies, and best practices for identifying and reporting suspicious activities.

    • Benefit: Ensures that all employees are continuously informed about evolving cyber threats (e.g., phishing, social engineering, ransomware) and best practices, strengthening the organization’s human firewall and reducing human-factor risks.

  4. Harden and Monitor Exposed SSH Service (Port 22):

    • Action: For the identified open SSH service:

    • Benefit: Minimizes the attack surface for brute-force attacks, credential theft, and exploitation attempts against the SSH service, safeguarding remote administration capabilities.

Conclusion

Paper Plane Publishing demonstrates a foundational understanding of some security practices, such as requiring MFA for computer logins and initial security training for new employees. However, this assessment has identified several critical gaps in the comprehensive application of Multi-Factor Authentication, the formalization of employee conduct through an Acceptable Use Policy, and ongoing organization-wide security education. Furthermore, the presence of an exposed SSH service necessitates diligent hardening and monitoring. Proactive and thorough implementation of the provided recommendations will significantly elevate the organization’s cybersecurity posture, enhance its resilience against common cyber threats, and better protect its assets and data.
