Questionnaire Analysis

The questionnaire results indicate a strong commitment to Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), which significantly enhances protection against unauthorized access across email, computers, and sensitive data systems. The regular security awareness training for both new and existing employees is also a commendable practice that strengthens the human firewall.

However, a critical gap was identified: the absence of an employee acceptable use policy. This policy is fundamental for setting clear expectations regarding the appropriate use of company IT resources, data, and internet access, thereby mitigating various insider risks and enhancing compliance.

Recommendations

To enhance Iron Oak Furniture’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, formalize, and implement a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities regarding the use of company systems, internet, email, data, and other IT resources.

    • Details: The policy should cover topics such as prohibited activities, privacy expectations, intellectual property, data handling, software installation, and reporting security incidents.

    • Benefit: This will mitigate insider threats, reduce data leakage risks, ensure compliance with relevant regulations, and provide a basis for disciplinary action in case of misuse.

  2. Conduct Regular Policy Reviews and Training:

    • Action: Integrate the new AUP into new employee onboarding processes and regular security awareness training sessions for all employees.

    • Details: Ensure employees understand the policy and acknowledge their agreement to abide by it. Review the AUP annually or as business needs and regulations change.

    • Benefit: Ensures that the policy remains relevant and that all employees are continuously aware of their responsibilities.

  3. Expand Network Scanning Scope (Optional but Recommended):

    • Action: While the scan of yielded positive results, consider expanding future network scans to cover a broader range of internal and external IP addresses, critical servers, and network devices.

    • Details: This will provide a more comprehensive view of the organization’s entire attack surface.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies potential vulnerabilities across the whole infrastructure before they can be exploited.

Conclusion

Iron Oak Furniture demonstrates a commendable stance on crucial security controls like Multi-Factor Authentication and regular security awareness training. Addressing the absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy is the most immediate and impactful step to further solidify the organization’s cybersecurity defenses. By implementing the recommended policy, Iron Oak Furniture can significantly reduce operational and compliance risks, fostering a more secure and responsible work environment.
