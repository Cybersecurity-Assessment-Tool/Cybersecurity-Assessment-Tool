Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Sovereign Trust:

Immediate Actions (Critical Risks)

  • Secure Port (TOP SECRET DB):

    • Review and Remediate: Immediately investigate the service running on port . Determine its exact purpose, the data it contains, whether it should be publicly accessible, and the true nature of the "TOP SECRET DB" it claims to expose.

    • Access Control: If the service is deemed necessary, restrict its access strictly to authorized internal IP ranges and implement robust authentication (preferably MFA) and encryption protocols. Consider placing it behind a firewall or VPN to isolate it from direct external access.

    • Data Minimization: Ensure that only absolutely necessary data is accessible through this service, adhering to principles of least privilege.

    • Confirm Discrepancy: Reconcile the conflict between the current scan findings and the previous "Port Secured" assessment to ensure accurate risk reporting.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Mandate MFA: Configure all sensitive data systems to require Multi-Factor Authentication for all user and administrative access attempts.

    • Phased Rollout: Prioritize implementation for systems holding the most critical and high-impact data assets first.

High Priority Actions

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Mandate MFA for Email: Enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing email. This is a primary and highly effective defense against phishing, credential stuffing, and email account takeover.

    • User Training and Support: Provide clear instructions, documentation, and technical support for users during the MFA rollout process.

  • Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • New Employee Training: Develop and implement mandatory security awareness training for all new hires as part of their onboarding process.

    • Annual Refresher Training: Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, covering essential topics such as phishing, social engineering, strong password hygiene, data handling best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Phishing Simulations: Implement regular simulated phishing campaigns to test employee vigilance, reinforce training concepts, and identify areas for further education.

Continuous Improvement

  • Regular Scans and Assessments: Conduct periodic vulnerability scans and penetration tests to proactively identify and remediate new exposures and misconfigurations.

  • Policy Review: Regularly review and update existing security policies and procedures, including the acceptable use policy, to align with current threat landscapes and industry best practices.

  • Risk Register Maintenance: Ensure the organizational risk register is accurately maintained and consistently updated with current findings, remediation statuses, and any identified discrepancies.
