Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of organizational data, security questionnaire responses, and technical scan results, the following risks have been identified. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided for review.

Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication for Email Access Without MFA, compromised credentials can grant attackers full access to email, leading to phishing, business email compromise, and sensitive data breaches. Critical
Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication for Computer Login Weakens endpoint security. If a user’s password is stolen or guessed, an attacker can gain access to the system without an additional authentication factor, potentially leading to broader network compromise. Critical
Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without a clear AUP, employees may inadvertently or intentionally misuse company resources, leading to security incidents, data leakage, and compliance issues due to undefined boundaries. High
Inadequate Security Awareness Training for New Hires New employees are often prime targets for social engineering attacks. A lack of initial training leaves them vulnerable to phishing, malware, and other threats, increasing the overall risk of breaches. High

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance Nova Terra’s overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access: Deploy and rigorously enforce MFA for all employee email accounts. This critical measure significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised through phishing or other means.

  2. Implement Mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins: Deploy and enforce MFA for all employee computer logins, especially for devices accessing sensitive internal resources. This adds a crucial layer of security to endpoints and prevents unauthorized access via stolen or guessed passwords.

  3. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Draft a clear and comprehensive AUP outlining acceptable use of company assets, networks, and data. Ensure all employees formally review, understand, and acknowledge this policy as a condition of employment.

  4. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Integrate comprehensive security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new hires. This training should cover common threats like phishing, social engineering, password best practices, and secure data handling procedures.

  5. Conduct Regular Internal Network Scans and Penetration Testing: While the provided scan showed no open ports, this was a limited view of a single internal IP. Conduct regular internal network vulnerability scans and targeted penetration tests across the entire internal infrastructure to identify potential weaknesses in systems, applications, and configurations that may not be externally exposed.

Conclusion

Nova Terra demonstrates some positive security practices, such as annual security awareness training and the use of MFA for sensitive data systems. However, the identified critical gaps in MFA implementation for email and general computer access, coupled with the absence of fundamental policies (AUP) and new hire security training, expose the organization to significant and largely preventable risks. Proactively addressing these weaknesses is paramount to strengthening Nova Terra’s defense against the evolving landscape of cyber threats and fostering a more robust security culture.
