Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The review of security controls indicates a generally positive stance towards security awareness and policy implementation. However, one significant vulnerability stands out: the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access (SC1). This is a critical gap, as email accounts are primary targets for adversaries due to their access to sensitive communications, password resets, and internal systems. A compromised email account can quickly lead to broader network intrusion.

Detailed Findings

The scan identified one open port with a notable service:

  • Port: 8080/tcp

  • State: Open

  • Service Script Output (http-title): TOP SECRET DB

Technical Analysis

The discovery of port 8080 being open on an internal IP address (10.5.5.5) and displaying the title "TOP SECRET DB" is highly concerning. While 10.5.5.5 is a private IP, meaning it’s likely not directly exposed to the internet, its accessibility within the internal network poses a significant risk. The descriptive title implies that this service hosts sensitive information, potentially a database containing critical organizational data. The use of a non-standard port for HTTP (8080 instead of 80 or 443) can sometimes be an attempt at obscurity, but it does not diminish the inherent risk of an exposed data source.

Correlation and Discrepancies

The most significant discrepancy found is regarding port 8080. Input 3, "Current Risks," dismisses "Port 8080 Secured" as a false positive with a CVSS score of 0.0. However, the Nmap scan (Input 1) explicitly found port 8080 open on with the title "TOP SECRET DB." This directly contradicts the existing risk assessment and elevates the potential severity of this finding from negligible to critical. The Nmap finding suggests that the "Port 8080 Secured" entry might either refer to a different instance or is based on outdated/incorrect information. Immediate re-evaluation of this service is required.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to mitigate the identified risks and improve Atlas Mapping’s cybersecurity posture.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access (R1):

    • Immediate Fix: Deploy and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing email. Consider strong MFA methods like FIDO2 security keys or authenticator apps.

    • Long-Term Fix: Integrate MFA across all critical applications and services where possible. Implement conditional access policies to enhance protection for email and other cloud services.

  2. Investigate and Secure "TOP SECRET DB" on Port 8080 (R2):

    • Immediate Fix: Urgently investigate the service running on . Determine the nature of the "TOP SECRET DB," what data it holds, and who has access. If it’s not absolutely essential to be network-accessible in this manner, disable or firewall the port immediately.

    • Long-Term Fix: Implement robust access controls, network segmentation, and encryption for any sensitive databases. If the service is legitimate and required, ensure it is properly authenticated, authorized, and monitored. Update the existing risk register with accurate information regarding this service. Review all existing "false positive" or "low severity" risks for similar discrepancies.

  3. Review and Update Risk Register:

    • Establish a process to regularly review and validate entries in the organization’s risk register, especially those marked as false positives or low severity, against current scan data and operational context. This helps ensure accuracy and prevents critical vulnerabilities from being overlooked.

Conclusion

Atlas Mapping demonstrates a good foundation in several cybersecurity practices, particularly regarding security awareness training and acceptable use policies. However, the identified lack of MFA for email and the exposed "TOP SECRET DB" service on port 8080 represent significant critical risks that warrant immediate attention. Addressing these vulnerabilities by implementing robust MFA for email and thoroughly investigating and securing the exposed database service will substantially improve Atlas Mapping’s security posture and protect against potential data breaches and unauthorized access.
