Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Open Door based on a review of organizational data, a network scan, and existing risk information. The assessment reveals a mixed security landscape. While the network scan of 192.168.1.100 indicated a relatively secure perimeter with no open ports, significant policy-level gaps were identified through the security questionnaire. Critical findings include the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for sensitive data systems and the lack of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP). These internal control deficiencies represent high-severity risks that warrant immediate attention. The organization does, however, maintain good practices in security awareness training. Recommendations focus on addressing these foundational policy gaps to significantly enhance the overall security posture.

Recommendations

Based on the assessment, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of Open Door:

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all systems and applications that store, process, or provide access to sensitive organizational data.

    • Benefit: This critical control significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access, even if user credentials are stolen or compromised.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the proper use of all organizational IT resources (e.g., computers, networks, internet, email, software), data handling procedures, and security responsibilities.

    • Action: Ensure all employees review, understand, and formally acknowledge their adherence to the AUP. Integrate AUP training into new employee onboarding and annual security awareness programs.

    • Benefit: A formal AUP sets clear expectations for employee behavior, reduces insider threats, minimizes accidental data exposures, and provides a legal basis for enforcing security policies.

Strategic Enhancements (Medium Priority)

  1. Expand Network Scan Scope:

    • Action: While the current scan of yielded positive results, consider expanding future network vulnerability assessments to cover a wider range of internal network assets (servers, workstations, network devices), other external-facing IP addresses, and critical infrastructure.

    • Benefit: A broader scanning strategy provides a more complete and accurate view of the organization’s entire attack surface, helping to identify hidden vulnerabilities or misconfigurations.

  2. Regular Security Posture Review:

    • Action: Establish a schedule for periodic reviews and updates of all security policies, controls, and technical configurations. This should include an annual review of the overall cybersecurity posture.

    • Benefit: Ensures that security measures remain effective and adapt to evolving threat landscapes, technological changes, and organizational growth.
