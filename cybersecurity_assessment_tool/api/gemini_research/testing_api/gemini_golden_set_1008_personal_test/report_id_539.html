Executive Summary

This report provides a cybersecurity posture assessment for Vertex Solutions, based on a combination of organizational data, a limited network scan, and pre-existing risk information. The assessment reveals a generally strong stance on Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across critical systems and good initial security awareness training for new employees. However, significant gaps exist in foundational security policies and ongoing employee education. Specifically, the absence of an acceptable use policy and regular annual security awareness training for all employees presents considerable human-factor risks. Furthermore, a critical vulnerability, "Localhost Exposed" (CVSS 10.0), affecting the internal loopback address 127.0.0.1 with an open SSH port, was identified and correlated with existing documentation. Addressing these policy, training, and critical technical vulnerabilities is paramount to enhancing the overall security posture and reducing the attack surface.

Risk Assessment

This section synthesizes risks identified from the security questionnaire, technical scan, and existing risk documentation.

Questionnaire-Identified Gaps

Based on the security control review, the following critical gaps were identified:

  • Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without an AUP, employees may not be aware of their responsibilities regarding company IT assets and data, increasing the risk of misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance.

  • Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the lack of regular annual training leaves the workforce susceptible to evolving threats such as phishing, social engineering, and malware, potentially leading to security incidents.

Technical Scan Findings

  • Open SSH Port on Localhost (127.0.0.1): The discovery of an open SSH service on the internal loopback interface, without further detail on its configuration or purpose, represents a potential exposure. While ‘127.0.0.1‘ is typically restricted to local machine access, its presence correlates with a documented critical vulnerability.

Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Vertex Solutions’ cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions (Critical & High Severity)

  • Address "Localhost Exposed" Vulnerability:

    • Urgently investigate the open SSH port on 127.0.0.1. Determine if this service is necessary, its purpose, and if it’s securely configured.

    • If not required, disable the SSH service. If required, ensure it is configured with strong authentication, patched to the latest version, and access is strictly limited to authorized users/processes.

    • Review the system where ‘127.0.0.1‘ is running to understand why it’s documented as "exposed" with a CVSS 10.0. This might indicate an underlying misconfiguration or a broader system vulnerability.

  • Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, concise AUP outlining employee responsibilities, prohibited activities, and consequences for non-compliance.

    • Ensure all employees review and acknowledge the AUP annually.

  • Implement Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Establish a mandatory, comprehensive annual security awareness training program for all employees.

    • Training should cover topics such as phishing, social engineering, password best practices, data handling, and incident reporting.

    • Track completion rates and provide refresher courses as needed.

Further Recommendations

  • Conduct Comprehensive Vulnerability Assessments: Perform regular, in-depth vulnerability scans and penetration tests for both internal and external networks to identify other potential exposures, including those on external IP 99.246.119.204.

  • Enhance Network Scan Details: If possible, configure future network scans to obtain more detailed service and version information (e.g., Nmap’s ‘-sV‘ option) to facilitate more thorough vulnerability analysis.

  • Regular Policy Review: Periodically review and update all security policies, including the AUP, to reflect changes in technology, threats, and regulatory requirements.
