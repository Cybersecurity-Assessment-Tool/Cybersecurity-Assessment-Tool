Executive Summary

This report presents a cybersecurity posture assessment for Cinder & Ash, based on a self-assessment questionnaire, and an attempted technical network scan. Due to issues with the provided technical scan data (Input_1_Network_Scan_JSON) and existing risks data (Input_3_Current_Risks_JSON), the technical analysis and correlation with pre-existing risks are significantly limited.

The primary findings indicate critical gaps in foundational security controls identified through the organizational self-assessment. Specifically, the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computer logins, the absence of an employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and the complete lack of regular security awareness training for employees present significant human-factor and credential-based risks. While MFA is used for sensitive data systems, this inconsistency creates critical points of failure elsewhere.

Key Findings:

  • Critical Authentication Gaps: Absence of MFA for email and general computer logins.

  • Policy Deficiencies: No organizational Acceptable Use Policy.

  • Human Factor Vulnerability: No security awareness training for new or existing employees.

  • Undetermined Technical Risks: Inability to assess technical vulnerabilities due to broken scan data.

Immediate action is recommended to implement MFA, establish a comprehensive AUP, and institute mandatory security awareness training to significantly enhance the organization’s defense against common cyber threats.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Cinder & Ash, the following recommendations are provided. These should be prioritized for immediate implementation.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • Email Access: Mandate MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This is a critical defense against phishing and credential stuffing attacks.

    • Computer Logins: Implement MFA for logging into all company computers and systems, particularly for administrative accounts.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, concise, and comprehensive AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources, data, and internet access.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and periodically thereafter.

  3. Establish a Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • Conduct annual (or more frequent, e.g., biannual) refresher training for all existing employees to keep them informed about current threats and best practices.

    • Topics should include phishing recognition, password hygiene, safe internet browsing, and incident reporting procedures.

  4. Conduct a Comprehensive Technical Vulnerability Scan:

    • A full and valid network vulnerability scan should be performed to accurately identify technical weaknesses, outdated software versions, and misconfigurations within the IT infrastructure.

    • Develop a remediation plan for all identified vulnerabilities based on their severity.

  5. Regular Security Reviews and Updates:

    • Establish a schedule for periodic reviews of security policies, configurations, and overall posture to adapt to evolving threat landscapes.

    • Ensure all software and systems are regularly patched and updated to protect against known vulnerabilities.

Addressing these fundamental control gaps will significantly improve Cinder & Ash’s resilience against common cyberattacks.
