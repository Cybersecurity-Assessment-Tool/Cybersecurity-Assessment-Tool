Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Vertex Solutions, derived from the analysis and correlation of a recent network scan, a detailed organizational security questionnaire, and the organization’s existing risk register.

The assessment identifies several critical vulnerabilities and significant control gaps. Notably, the organization lacks Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access, posing a severe risk of account compromise. Furthermore, there is an absence of consistent security awareness training for both new and existing employees, significantly increasing the human element risk from social engineering and phishing attacks.

Technically, the network scan on revealed no open ports, which is a positive indicator for the perimeter’s immediate security. However, this finding contradicts an existing risk register entry for an "Unencrypted Web Server" on Port 80, highlighting a need for risk register reconciliation. Addressing the identified control deficiencies and ensuring the accuracy of risk documentation are paramount for elevating Vertex Solutions’ overall security resilience.

Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive analysis, the following actionable recommendations are provided to address identified risks and enhance Vertex Solutions’ overall cybersecurity posture:

  1. Mandate and Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all organizational email accounts. This is the single most impactful step to mitigate the risk of email account compromise.

    • Guidance: Provide clear instructions, training, and support to all employees during the rollout phase to ensure smooth adoption.

  2. Establish and Maintain a Robust Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Develop and integrate mandatory security awareness training for all new employees as part of their onboarding process.

    • Action: Implement annual mandatory refresher training for all employees, covering current threat landscapes, phishing recognition, secure browsing, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Consideration: Implement regular phishing simulation exercises to test and reinforce employee vigilance in a controlled environment.

  3. Reconcile and Validate Risk Register Entries:

    • Action: Conduct an immediate review of the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk in the risk register.

    • Investigation: Verify if Port 80 was indeed remediated and closed on the target , or if the risk pertains to another system not covered by this scan.

    • Update: Update the risk register to accurately reflect the current state of assets and associated risks, marking remediated risks as closed or providing clarification for discrepancies.

  4. Implement Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Action: Establish a recurring schedule for internal and external network vulnerability scans to proactively identify and address security weaknesses before they can be exploited.

    • Action: Consider periodic penetration tests to simulate real-world attacks and assess the effectiveness of existing security controls and incident response capabilities.

  5. Review and Update Security Policies Periodically:

    • Action: Conduct annual reviews of all security policies, including the Acceptable Use Policy, to ensure they remain current, relevant, and enforceable given changes in technology, threats, and organizational structure.
