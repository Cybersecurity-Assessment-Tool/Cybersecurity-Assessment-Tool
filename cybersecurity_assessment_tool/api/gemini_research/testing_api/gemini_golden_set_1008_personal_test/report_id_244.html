5. Risk Assessment

Based on the security control review and technical scan, the following risks have been identified:

Identified Cybersecurity Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access Without Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), email accounts are highly vulnerable to compromise through phishing, credential stuffing, or stolen passwords, leading to potential data breaches or business email compromise (BEC) attacks. High
Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems Access to systems containing sensitive organizational data is not protected by MFA, significantly increasing the risk of unauthorized access, data exfiltration, or tampering if primary credentials are compromised. High
Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy Without a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), employees may be unaware of proper conduct regarding IT resource usage, increasing the likelihood of misuse, accidental data exposure, or policy violations that could lead to security incidents. Medium

6. Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Arcane Security, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Mandate MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This significantly strengthens protection against credential theft and phishing attacks.

    • Consider various MFA methods (e.g., authenticator apps, hardware tokens) based on usability and security requirements.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Extend MFA requirements to all systems, applications, and databases that store or process sensitive organizational data.

    • Prioritize systems based on data classification and potential impact of compromise.

  3. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines acceptable and unacceptable use of all organizational IT resources, including networks, hardware, software, and data.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and periodically thereafter.

    • Integrate AUP review into annual security awareness training.
