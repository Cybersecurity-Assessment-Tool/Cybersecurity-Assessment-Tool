Identified Risks
Risk Name Severity Category Overview
Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Login High Authentication Employees can log into computers using only a single factor (e.g., password). This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems and data if credentials are compromised through phishing, keylogging, or brute-force attacks.
Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Medium-High Policy & Governance Without an AUP, employees lack clear, formalized guidelines on the acceptable use of organizational IT resources, data, and communication channels. Lack of Security Awareness Training for New Employees High Human Factor New employees are onboarded without mandatory security awareness training, making them particularly vulnerable to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing, pretexting) and increasing the likelihood of unintentional security lapses or the introduction of vulnerabilities through unaware actions.

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to mitigate the identified risks and significantly enhance Blackwood Industries’ overall cybersecurity posture:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for All Computer Logins: Mandate and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all user authentications to corporate endpoints, servers, and internal applications. This critical control adds a substantial layer of security against credential compromise, making it significantly harder for unauthorized individuals to gain access even if they obtain a user’s password.

  2. Develop and Implement a Formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive and clearly articulated Employee Acceptable Use Policy. This policy should define acceptable use of company IT assets (hardware, software, networks), proper data handling procedures, guidelines for internet and email usage, and prohibited activities. The AUP must be distributed to all employees, with mandatory acknowledgment of understanding and compliance, to establish clear expectations and reduce organizational risk.

  3. Mandate Security Awareness Training for New Hires: Integrate a mandatory, comprehensive security awareness training program as a critical component of the onboarding process for all new employees. This training should cover common cybersecurity threats such as phishing, social engineering tactics, secure password practices, data protection protocols, and reporting suspicious activities. Early and consistent training will equip new hires with the knowledge to identify and avoid common threats, reducing the human factor risk.
