Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of Blue Marble.

Immediate Recommendations

  • Implement Mandatory New Employee Security Awareness Training: Develop and enforce a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees during their initial onboarding. This training should cover essential topics such as:

    • Phishing and social engineering identification.

    • Password management and multi-factor authentication best practices.

    • Incident reporting procedures.

    • Data classification and handling policies.

    • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and other relevant security policies.

    This training should be completed before new employees are granted access to critical organizational systems and data.

Long-Term / General Recommendations

  • Conduct Comprehensive Vulnerability Assessments: Initiate regular external and internal vulnerability scans and penetration tests. The provided scan was inconclusive; therefore, a more thorough and recurrent assessment is crucial to identify and remediate actual technical weaknesses across the IT infrastructure.

  • Periodic Security Policy Review and Updates: Establish a schedule for periodically reviewing and updating all security policies, including the acceptable use policy, to ensure they remain current with evolving threat landscapes, regulatory requirements, and organizational changes.

  • Develop/Refine Incident Response Plan (IRP): Ensure a well-documented, actionable, and regularly tested Incident Response Plan is in place. This plan should clearly define roles, responsibilities, and procedures for detecting, containing, eradicating, recovering from, and post-incident analysis of security incidents.

  • Reinforce Security Awareness Continuously: While annual training is performed, consider supplementing it with more frequent, bite-sized security awareness modules, simulated phishing campaigns, and regular security communications throughout the year. This helps reinforce learning and keeps security principles top-of-mind for all employees.

Conclusion

Blue Marble has established a strong foundation with the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication and an annual security awareness program. However, addressing the critical gap in new employee security training is paramount to mitigate immediate human-factor risks and prevent potential breaches stemming from lack of initial awareness. A proactive and continuous approach to cybersecurity, encompassing regular technical assessments, ongoing policy refinement, and consistent employee education, will significantly bolster the organization’s defense against the constantly evolving landscape of cyber threats.
