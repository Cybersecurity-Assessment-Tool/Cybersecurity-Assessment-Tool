Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture assessment for Vanguard Heritage, conducted on 2026-01-27. The assessment involved a comprehensive review of organizational security controls through a structured questionnaire, an internal network scan, and an analysis of existing reported risks.

Key findings indicate several critical vulnerabilities and significant security gaps. From a technical standpoint, an exposed FTP service on target 10.0.0.15 running an outdated vsftpd 2.3.4 with anonymous access enabled presents an immediate and severe risk of data breach, unauthorized access, and system compromise. Furthermore, the organization lacks Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access, does not have a formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and fails to provide essential security awareness training for new employees. These policy and procedural shortcomings significantly increase the organization’s overall attack surface through social engineering and internal policy violations. Additionally, existing risks include the use of outdated operating systems (Windows 7), which are beyond their end-of-life and susceptible to unpatched vulnerabilities. Immediate action is strongly recommended to address these critical findings and proactively improve the organization’s cybersecurity posture.

Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The security control review reveals several significant policy and procedural deficiencies:

  • No MFA for Email Access: Email remains a primary vector for phishing and credential theft attacks. The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication for email accounts leaves them highly vulnerable to compromise, potentially leading to unauthorized access to sensitive communications and systems.

  • Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding the proper use of company IT assets, data handling procedures, and security protocols. This increases the risk of misuse, inadvertent data leakage, and security incidents stemming from employee actions.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees are typically more susceptible to social engineering and phishing attacks due to a lack of familiarization with organizational security practices. The absence of initial security awareness training makes them potential weak links in the security chain, increasing the risk of external compromise.

Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive assessment, the following recommendations are provided to mitigate identified risks and substantially improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Vanguard Heritage. These recommendations are prioritized by severity.

Immediate Actions (Critical & High Severity)

  • Disable Anonymous FTP & Secure/Remove FTP Service:

    • Immediate Action: Disable anonymous FTP login on without delay.

    • Evaluation: Determine if the FTP service is truly essential for business operations. If not, the service should be completely removed to eliminate the attack surface.

    • Upgrade/Replace: If FTP functionality is required, upgrade vsftpd to the latest stable and supported version. Alternatively, migrate to a more secure file transfer protocol such as SFTP (SSH File Transfer Protocol) or SCP (Secure Copy Protocol), which leverage encryption and stronger authentication mechanisms.

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Implement and strictly enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all organizational email accounts. This critical security control significantly reduces the risk of credential-based attacks, even if passwords are compromised.

  • Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft, formally implement, and communicate a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines the proper use of company IT resources, data handling best practices, and security expectations for all employees.

    • Ensure all employees read and acknowledge their understanding and agreement to abide by the AUP.

  • Mandate Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Integrate mandatory, role-specific security awareness training as a fundamental part of the onboarding process for all new employees.

    • The training should cover essential topics such as identifying phishing attempts, social engineering tactics, strong password hygiene, data classification, and incident reporting procedures.

Mid-Term Actions (Medium Severity)

  • Upgrade End-of-Life Operating Systems:

    • Develop and execute a detailed project plan to upgrade all workstations currently running Windows 7 (and any other End-of-Life operating systems) to a currently supported and patched operating system (e.g., Windows 10/11).

    • Prioritize the upgrade of machines that handle sensitive data, are exposed to external networks, or are used by high-privilege users.

General Best Practices

  • Regular Vulnerability Management Program: Establish and maintain a continuous vulnerability management program that includes regular internal and external vulnerability scans, penetration testing, and timely remediation of identified weaknesses.

  • Robust Patch Management Program: Implement an automated and systematic patch management program to ensure all operating systems, applications, and network devices are consistently kept up-to-date with the latest security patches and firmware updates.

  • Principle of Least Privilege: Enforce the principle of least privilege for all user accounts, service accounts, and system processes, ensuring they are granted only the minimum necessary permissions to perform their required functions.

  • Network Segmentation: Consider implementing network segmentation to logically divide the network into isolated zones. This limits lateral movement for attackers and contains the impact of potential breaches to specific segments.
