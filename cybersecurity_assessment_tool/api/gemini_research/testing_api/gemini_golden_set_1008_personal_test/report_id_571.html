Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Willow Creek Health based on a network scan, organizational security questionnaire responses, and a review of existing risks. The assessment identified several strengths, including the implementation of an acceptable use policy and regular security awareness training. However, critical vulnerabilities were noted, primarily the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access and computer logins. A single open port (SSH) was detected on the external target, requiring further investigation due to the absence of specific service and version details. Immediate recommendations focus on implementing MFA across critical access points and conducting a deeper technical analysis of the open port.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Willow Creek Health.

High Priority Recommendations

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of successful phishing and credential stuffing attacks, protecting sensitive communications and reducing the likelihood of business email compromise.

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins: Require Multi-Factor Authentication for logging into all internal computers and workstations. This adds a crucial layer of security, especially for endpoints that may have access to sensitive internal resources, preventing unauthorized access even if primary credentials are stolen.

Medium Priority Recommendations

  • Detailed Assessment of Open SSH Port: Conduct a more in-depth scan or manual investigation of the SSH service running on on port 22. Identify the exact SSH daemon, its version, and configuration (e.g., permitted authentication methods, disabled root login, allowed users). Ensure it is patched to the latest stable version and configured securely (e.g., strong ciphers, key-based authentication only, rate limiting). Consider restricting access to trusted IP ranges if feasible and applicable.

General Security Enhancements

  • Regular Vulnerability Scans: Implement a schedule for regular internal and external vulnerability scanning to proactively identify and address potential weaknesses before they can be exploited.

  • Incident Response Plan Review: Ensure the organization has a well-documented and tested incident response plan to effectively handle security incidents, minimizing their impact and recovery time.

  • Principle of Least Privilege: Review and enforce the principle of least privilege for all users and systems, ensuring access is granted only to what is absolutely necessary for job functions, thereby limiting the scope of potential breaches.

Conclusion

Willow Creek Health demonstrates a commendable commitment to certain security practices, particularly in security awareness training and acceptable use policies. However, the identified gaps in Multi-Factor Authentication for email and computer access represent significant risks that must be addressed urgently. Proactive mitigation of these issues, alongside a thorough review of externally exposed services like SSH, will substantially enhance the organization’s resilience against cyber threats and bolster its overall security posture.
