Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity posture assessment for Wildfire Communications. The assessment combines a review of organizational security controls, an internal network scan, and an analysis of pre-existing risk data.

Overall, Wildfire Communications demonstrates strong adherence to Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across key access points. However, critical gaps were identified in foundational security policies and employee training programs. Furthermore, the presence of exposed Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) services, both as a new finding and a pre-existing risk, highlights a significant technical vulnerability.

Key findings include:

  • Policy & Training Deficiencies: Absence of an employee acceptable use policy and a lack of regular security awareness training.

  • RDP Exposure: An internal network scan identified an open RDP port on 10.10.10.51, correlating with a pre-existing high-severity risk concerning RDP exposure on 10.10.10.50. This indicates a broader issue with RDP management and hardening.

  • MFA Strengths: Strong implementation of MFA for email, computer login, and sensitive data access.

Recommendations focus on establishing essential security policies, implementing comprehensive security awareness training, and immediately addressing RDP exposure through hardening and access control measures. Proactive steps are crucial to mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall security posture.

Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The organization has commendably implemented Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across critical access vectors, which significantly enhances protection against unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

However, significant deficiencies exist in foundational security practices:

  • Lack of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an AUP means employees lack clear guidelines on the acceptable use of organizational IT resources, increasing the risk of misuse, data leakage, and compliance issues.

  • Insufficient Security Awareness Training: The lack of both new hire and annual security awareness training leaves employees vulnerable to phishing, social engineering, and other cyber threats. This is a primary attack vector for many organizations.

These gaps represent high-priority areas for immediate improvement, as human factors are often the weakest link in cybersecurity defenses.

Analysis of Technical Findings

The scan identified an open Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) service (port 3389) on the host 10.10.10.51. RDP, while a legitimate tool for remote administration, is a frequent target for attackers due to historical vulnerabilities, brute-force attacks, and credential stuffing. Without robust security controls, such as being behind a VPN, strong and unique passwords, account lockout policies, and Multi-Factor Authentication, an exposed RDP service represents a significant entry point for malicious actors.

The Nmap scan did not provide product or version information for the RDP service, which can sometimes indicate a lack of advanced fingerprinting or a hardened configuration that prevents version disclosure. Regardless, the mere presence of the open port warrants immediate attention.

Correlation and Impact

The most prominent and critical risk identified is the widespread RDP exposure. The new finding on 10.10.10.51 directly correlates with the pre-existing risk on 10.10.10.50, suggesting a systemic issue in how RDP is deployed and secured within the network. This consistent exposure presents a significant attack surface that could lead to unauthorized access, internal network compromise, and data breaches.

The lack of security awareness training further exacerbates the RDP exposure risk, as employees might fall victim to credential theft attempts that could then be used to access RDP services. The absence of an AUP contributes to an unmanaged environment, potentially hindering incident response and accountability. While the organization exhibits strength in MFA implementation, these foundational weaknesses undermine overall security.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided. These are prioritized based on their potential impact and urgency.

Immediate Technical Remediation

  • Secure RDP Services:

    • Isolate/Restrict Access: Implement network segmentation, firewalls, and Access Control Lists (ACLs) to restrict RDP access only to trusted administrative hosts or specific IP ranges.

    • VPN Requirement: For any external RDP access, mandate connectivity exclusively through a Virtual Private Network (VPN).

    • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Implement MFA for all RDP logins.

    • Strong Passwords & Account Lockout: Enforce strong, unique passwords and configure account lockout policies to thwart brute-force attacks.

    • Patching & Updates: Ensure all systems running RDP are regularly patched and updated to protect against known vulnerabilities.

    • Review Existing RDP Exposure: Address the pre-existing RDP exposure on 10.10.10.50 by applying the same hardening principles.

Policy and Training Enhancements

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a clear and comprehensive AUP outlining the appropriate use of company IT resources, data, and internet access.

    • Disseminate the AUP to all employees and require acknowledgment of understanding and compliance.

    • Integrate AUP review into employee onboarding and annual training.

  • Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • New Employee Onboarding: Mandate security awareness training for all new hires, covering common threats (phishing, social engineering), password best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Annual Refresher Training: Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, refreshing knowledge and addressing emerging threats.

    • Phishing Simulations: Regularly conduct simulated phishing campaigns to test employee vigilance and reinforce training.

General Security Best Practices

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning: Implement a schedule for regular internal and external vulnerability scans to proactively identify and remediate security weaknesses.

  • Incident Response Plan: Ensure a well-documented and tested incident response plan is in place to effectively handle security breaches.
