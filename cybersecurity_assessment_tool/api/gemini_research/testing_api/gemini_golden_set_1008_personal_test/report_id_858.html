Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Hidden Gem, derived from an analysis of organizational data, a recent network scan, and existing risk information. The assessment reveals a nuanced security landscape characterized by foundational policy strengths but also significant operational and technical vulnerabilities. Key findings include a concerning lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across crucial access vectors and a gap in security awareness training for new employees. While a limited network scan of a specified internal target indicated no open ports, a conflict was identified with an existing risk stating an "Unencrypted Web Server" with an open Port 80. Strategic recommendations are provided to mitigate these risks, prioritizing the implementation of MFA and enhanced training programs, to significantly bolster the organization’s defense capabilities.

Analysis of Security Control Review

The questionnaire responses indicate a critical deficiency in the deployment of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). The absence of MFA for email access, computer logins, and sensitive data systems represents a severe vulnerability, making the organization highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and other forms of unauthorized access. Another significant gap is the lack of dedicated security awareness training for new employees, which leaves new hires ill-equipped to recognize and respond to cyber threats during their initial employment period. Positively, Hidden Gem has an acceptable use policy in place and conducts annual security awareness training for all employees, demonstrating a commitment to policy and ongoing education for existing staff.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance Hidden Gem’s overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Systems (High Priority):

    • Deploy MFA for all user accounts accessing email, irrespective of location, to significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

    • Enforce MFA for all user logins to company computers, network resources, and VPNs to secure workstation and network access.

    • Implement MFA for all sensitive data systems and applications to protect critical information assets from unauthorized access.

  2. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees (High Priority):

    • Develop and integrate a comprehensive cybersecurity awareness training module into the onboarding process for all new hires. This training should cover topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, safe browsing, and data handling policies.

  3. Review and Validate Existing Risk Register (Medium Priority):

    • Conduct an immediate review of the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk (R5) to determine its current applicability. Verify if Port 80 is indeed closed on all relevant production systems. Update or retire the risk if it is no longer valid or if the service has been moved to HTTPS. Extend this review to all entries in the existing risk register to ensure accuracy and relevance.

  4. Conduct Comprehensive Network Scans (Medium Priority):

    • Expand network scanning efforts beyond single targets and specific ports to gain a more complete and accurate inventory of all network-facing assets. This includes regular scans of external perimeter, internal network segments, and cloud environments for open ports, vulnerable services, and misconfigurations.

  5. Regularly Review and Update Policies and Training (Low Priority):

    • While an acceptable use policy and annual training are in place, ensure these documents and materials are reviewed at least annually and updated to reflect the latest threat landscape, technological changes, and organizational best practices.
