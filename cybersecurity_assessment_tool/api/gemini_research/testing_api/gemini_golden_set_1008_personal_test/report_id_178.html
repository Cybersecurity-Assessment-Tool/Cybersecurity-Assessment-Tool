Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Hearth & Home. Implementing these will significantly strengthen the organization’s defenses against prevalent cyber threats.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access: Mandate MFA for all email accounts across the organization. This critical control adds an essential layer of security, making it significantly harder for unauthorized individuals to gain access even if credentials are stolen, thereby mitigating risks from phishing and credential stuffing attacks.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities and acceptable conduct when utilizing company IT resources, accessing company data, and browsing the internet. Ensure all employees are thoroughly educated on the policy, and that they read, understand, and formally acknowledge it upon hiring and periodically thereafter.

  3. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Implement a robust program for mandatory, comprehensive security awareness training for all employees at least once per year. The training should cover current threat landscapes, effective identification of phishing attempts, awareness of social engineering tactics, best practices for data handling, and the importance of adhering to company security policies.

Conclusion

The current cybersecurity posture of Hearth & Home, as assessed through this report, indicates several areas requiring significant improvement. While no immediate technical vulnerabilities were identified from the provided scan data, the foundational security gaps highlighted by the questionnaire responses pose substantial risks. Prioritizing the implementation of MFA for email accounts, developing a comprehensive acceptable use policy, and establishing regular, mandatory security awareness training for all employees are critical steps. Addressing these fundamental security controls will dramatically strengthen the organization’s defense mechanisms and foster a more resilient cybersecurity environment.
