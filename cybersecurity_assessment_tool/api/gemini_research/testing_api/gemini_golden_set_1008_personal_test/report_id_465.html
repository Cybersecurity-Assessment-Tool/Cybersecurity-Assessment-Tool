Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive assessment of Apex Legends Group’s current cybersecurity posture, synthesizing findings from network scans, organizational security questionnaire responses, and pre-existing risk data. The analysis highlights areas of strength, notably the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins and sensitive data systems. However, several critical vulnerabilities and significant control gaps were identified, including the absence of MFA for email, a formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and robust security awareness training programs. A concerning "TOP SECRET DB" service was also discovered on port 8080, which requires immediate investigation despite a prior organizational assessment marking it as a false positive. Addressing these identified risks is crucial for enhancing the overall security resilience of Apex Legends Group.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Apex Legends Group, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all email accounts across the organization.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized email access even if user credentials are compromised.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines permissible and impermissible uses of company IT resources. Ensure all employees review and sign the AUP.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces misuse of resources, and provides a basis for disciplinary action if policies are violated.

  3. Establish a Robust Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Develop and implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process. Conduct annual refresher training for all employees, including regular simulated phishing campaigns.

    • Benefit: Educates employees on current threats, best practices, and organizational policies, transforming them into a strong line of defense against social engineering and other attacks.

  4. Investigate and Secure Port 8080 and "TOP SECRET DB" Service:

    • Action:

      1. Immediately investigate the service running on identifying as "TOP SECRET DB".

      2. Reconcile this finding with the prior assessment of "Port 8080 Secured" and "false positive." Determine if the prior assessment is still valid given the new information, or if it was specific to external exposure while this finding is internal.

      3. Assess the data stored/processed by this "TOP SECRET DB" service and its criticality.

      4. If the service is required, implement robust access controls, authentication, and encryption. If it is not required, ensure it is properly decommissioned and the port is closed.

      5. Ensure that no sensitive information is inadvertently exposed through service banners or metadata.

    • Benefit: Prevents unauthorized access to potentially critical data, eliminates a clear information disclosure risk, and ensures accurate asset inventory and risk management.

Conclusion

Apex Legends Group demonstrates commendable practices in securing sensitive data systems and computer logins with MFA. However, critical vulnerabilities exist in fundamental areas such as email security, employee policy, and human-element training. The discovery of an openly identified "TOP SECRET DB" service further underscores the need for a thorough re-evaluation of current security controls and asset management. Proactive implementation of the recommended actions will significantly strengthen the organization’s defenses against prevailing cyber threats and foster a more secure operating environment.
