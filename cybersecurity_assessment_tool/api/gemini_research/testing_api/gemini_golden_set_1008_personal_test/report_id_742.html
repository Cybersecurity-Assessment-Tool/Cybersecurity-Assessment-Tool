Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance Grizzly Peak’s cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for All Computer Logins:

    • Action: Enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational computers, including workstations and servers.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of credential compromise and unauthorized system access, even if passwords are stolen or guessed.

    • Priority: Immediate.

  2. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Integrate comprehensive security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new employees. This training should cover topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, data handling policies, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Benefit: Ensures that all employees, from day one, understand their role in maintaining organizational security and are equipped to identify and report potential threats.

    • Priority: Immediate.

  3. Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Network Scans:

    • Action: Schedule and execute regular, authenticated network vulnerability scans of both internal and external assets. Ensure that the scan results are fully captured and analyzed.

    • Benefit: Provides continuous visibility into the organization’s attack surface, identifies newly exposed services, outdated software, and misconfigurations that could be exploited by attackers.

    • Priority: High (Foundational).

Conclusion

Grizzly Peak’s cybersecurity posture, while demonstrating strengths in some areas like MFA for sensitive data and annual security training, has critical gaps that require immediate attention. The absence of MFA for computer logins and the lack of security awareness training for new employees pose significant risks that could be exploited. Addressing these areas, alongside implementing a robust network scanning regime, will substantially strengthen the organization’s defenses against evolving cyber threats. Continuous monitoring, regular assessments, and a proactive approach to security are vital for maintaining a resilient cybersecurity posture.
