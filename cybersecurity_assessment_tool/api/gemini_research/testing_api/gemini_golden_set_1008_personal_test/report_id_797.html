Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Modern Myth, based on a combination of organizational self-assessment, network scan data, and existing risk registers. Our analysis reveals several critical vulnerabilities, primarily concerning Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) exposure on multiple hosts, significant gaps in Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation for email and sensitive data systems, and the absence of a crucial Acceptable Use Policy (AUP). While the organization does perform security awareness training, the identified technical and policy gaps present a high level of risk to the organization’s assets and data. Immediate attention is required to remediate these findings and strengthen the overall security framework.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and strengthen Modern Myth’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Secure Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) Access:

    • Immediately review and restrict access to RDP on both 10.10.10.50 and 10.10.10.51.

    • Implement a Virtual Private Network (VPN) for all remote access to RDP services, ensuring RDP is not directly exposed to internal or external networks without proper authentication and encryption layers.

    • Enforce strong, complex passwords and account lockout policies for all accounts with RDP access.

    • Consider moving to a more secure remote access solution or implementing a jump server.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Deploy MFA for all email accounts to prevent unauthorized access even if passwords are compromised, significantly reducing the impact of phishing attacks.

    • Implement MFA for all sensitive data systems. This is a critical control to protect against data breaches and unauthorized access to valuable organizational data.

    • Educate employees on the importance and proper usage of MFA.

  3. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities and acceptable behaviors when using company IT resources.

    • Ensure all employees read and acknowledge the AUP, preferably annually, and integrate it into onboarding processes.

    • Enforce the AUP consistently across the organization.

  4. Regular Security Audits and Vulnerability Assessments:

    • Conduct regular internal and external vulnerability scans and penetration tests to identify and remediate emerging threats and misconfigurations.

    • Periodically review and update all security policies and procedures to adapt to the evolving threat landscape.

Conclusion

The current cybersecurity posture of Modern Myth presents several critical areas for immediate improvement. Addressing the RDP exposures on both identified hosts, implementing robust MFA across critical systems like email and sensitive data, and establishing foundational policies like an Acceptable Use Policy will significantly reduce the organization’s attack surface and enhance its resilience against cyber threats. Prompt action on these recommendations is strongly advised to protect organizational assets and maintain data integrity.
