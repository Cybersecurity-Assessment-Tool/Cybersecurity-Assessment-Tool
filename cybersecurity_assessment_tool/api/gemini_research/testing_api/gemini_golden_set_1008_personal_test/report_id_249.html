Executive Summary

This report details a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Aeon Pharmaceuticals, synthesizing findings from an external network scan, a security control questionnaire, and a review of existing risk data. The analysis reveals several critical vulnerabilities and significant gaps in security controls, primarily concerning multi-factor authentication (MFA) implementation, security awareness training, and policy enforcement.

A particularly alarming finding is the discovery of an open port 8080 on the target system 10.5.5.5, explicitly identified via its HTTP title as "TOP SECRET DB." This direct exposure of what appears to be a sensitive data system critically contradicts the organization’s existing risk assessment, which incorrectly lists Port 8080 as "confirmed secure and false positive." This exposure represents an immediate and severe risk of unauthorized data access or system compromise.

Combined with identified deficiencies in MFA for email and sensitive data systems, absence of an Acceptable Use Policy, and lack of annual security awareness training, Aeon Pharmaceuticals’ security posture is significantly weakened. Immediate action is imperative to address these critical findings and bolster the organization’s defenses against current cyber threats.

2.1 Key Gaps Identified from Questionnaire:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication for email access dramatically increases the risk of account compromise through phishing, credential stuffing, or brute-force attacks. Such compromises can lead to Business Email Compromise (BEC), data exfiltration, or broader network intrusion.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Failure to implement MFA for sensitive data systems represents a direct and critical risk of unauthorized access to confidential information. This significantly elevates the likelihood and impact of a data breach.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of proper conduct regarding organizational IT resources, data, and communication systems. This lack of clear guidelines can lead to data leakage, misuse of company assets, and inadvertent introduction of malware.

  • No Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive initial training, the lack of annual refreshers means that employee awareness of evolving cyber threats, best practices, and organizational policies may become outdated. This increases susceptibility to social engineering attacks, phishing, and other human-factor vulnerabilities.

5. Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive assessment, the following actionable recommendations are provided to mitigate identified risks and substantially enhance Aeon Pharmaceuticals’ cybersecurity posture:

5.1 Immediate Critical Actions

  • Investigate and Secure Exposed System on Port 8080:

    • Immediately investigate the system exposed on . Determine its exact purpose, the nature of data it stores, and current access controls.

    • If it is indeed a sensitive database or application, immediately restrict all external access. Implement stringent firewall rules to permit access only from authorized internal networks or via a secure, authenticated gateway (e.g., VPN).

    • Remove the highly sensitive "TOP SECRET DB" title from public visibility if it’s not intended to be exposed.

    • For any legitimate web interface, implement strong authentication mechanisms, including Multi-Factor Authentication, and ensure all communication is encrypted (HTTPS).

    • Formally re-evaluate and update the prior risk assessment that deemed this port "secure and false positive," clearly documenting the new critical finding.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Prioritize the deployment and enforcement of Multi-Factor Authentication for all systems that process, store, or transmit sensitive organizational data. This is a critical control to protect against unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised.

5.2 High Priority Actions

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all employee and administrative email accounts within the organization. This will significantly reduce the risk of email account compromise and subsequent attacks like BEC.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy that clearly outlines proper and improper use of all organizational IT resources, data, internet access, and communication systems.

    • Distribute the AUP to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgments, and integrate AUP review into new hire onboarding and annual security awareness training.

5.3 Medium Priority Actions

  • Establish Annual Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Develop and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should be updated regularly to cover current cyber threats (e.g., advanced phishing techniques, ransomware, social engineering) and reinforce organizational policies and best practices.

    • Conduct regular simulated phishing exercises to test and reinforce employee awareness and provide targeted education.

Conclusion

Aeon Pharmaceuticals currently faces significant cybersecurity challenges, highlighted by critical exposures such as the "TOP SECRET DB" on port 8080 and widespread gaps in Multi-Factor Authentication implementation. These issues, combined with deficiencies in security policies and training, create a substantial attack surface. Addressing these findings with utmost urgency, beginning with the immediate critical recommendations, will be essential to substantially enhance the organization’s defense against modern cyber threats and protect its valuable assets. A proactive, adaptive, and continuous approach to security is paramount for maintaining a resilient cybersecurity posture.
