Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Infinity Loop. These are prioritized based on their severity and potential impact.

Critical Priority Recommendations

  • Address Localhost Exposure (CVSS 10.0):

    • Immediate Action: Investigate the necessity of the open SSH port (22) on 127.0.0.1. If not required, disable or remove the service immediately.

    • If Required: If the service is intentionally running, ensure it is configured with the highest security standards:

      • Restrict access to only necessary users/processes.

      • Enforce strong, complex passwords and multi-factor authentication (MFA).

      • Implement key-based authentication where possible and disable password authentication.

      • Regularly patch and update the SSH daemon to the latest secure version.

      • Monitor logs for suspicious activity.

    • Broader Review: Conduct a comprehensive review of all instances of "Localhost Exposed" as per the existing risk register to ensure no other critical internal exposures exist.

High Priority Recommendations

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Strategy: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all organizational email accounts without delay.

    • Implementation: Choose a robust MFA solution (e.g., authenticator apps, hardware tokens).

    • Awareness: Educate all employees on the importance of MFA and how to use it effectively.

  • Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Hires:

    • Strategy: Integrate mandatory security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new employees.

    • Content: Training should cover common threats (phishing, malware), strong password practices, data handling, and organizational security policies.

    • Regular Refresher: Ensure this initial training is reinforced by the annual security awareness training for all employees.

Medium Priority Recommendations

  • Develop and Implement Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Development: Create a clear, comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy outlining guidelines for the use of company IT assets, networks, data, and communication systems.

    • Dissemination: Distribute the AUP to all employees, requiring acknowledgement of receipt and understanding.

    • Enforcement: Establish clear consequences for violations and ensure consistent enforcement.

    • Review: Periodically review and update the AUP to reflect changes in technology, threats, and organizational policies.

Conclusion

While Infinity Loop demonstrates some awareness of cybersecurity with existing MFA for computers and sensitive data, and annual security training, significant critical gaps remain. The presence of an open SSH port on localhost, coupled with the lack of MFA for email and inadequate security policies and training for new employees, presents considerable risk. Addressing these vulnerabilities, particularly the critical "Localhost Exposed" and "Missing MFA for Email Access," should be the immediate focus to significantly enhance the organization’s defense against cyber threats. Continuous monitoring, policy enforcement, and employee education will be key to maintaining a strong security posture.
