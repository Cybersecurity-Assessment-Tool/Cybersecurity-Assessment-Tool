Executive Summary

This report provides a cybersecurity posture assessment for Crestview Analytics, derived from a recent network scan, responses to an organizational security questionnaire, and pre-existing risk data. The analysis highlights areas of strength, particularly in the implementation of multi-factor authentication (MFA) for email and computer logins. However, significant security gaps were identified, including the lack of MFA for sensitive data systems, the absence of a formal acceptable use policy (AUP), and a complete deficiency in employee security awareness training. A limited network scan detected an open Port 80 on the internal network, though further details about the service running were unavailable. The report also addresses an anomalous, informational risk entry found in the provided data. Actionable recommendations are provided to address identified vulnerabilities and enhance the overall security posture.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Crestview Analytics, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Mandate and implement MFA for all access to systems, applications, and databases that store or process sensitive organizational or client data.

    • Priority: High

  2. Develop and Enforce a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a clear, concise, and enforceable AUP. Distribute it to all employees, ensure acknowledgment of understanding, and integrate it into onboarding and annual reviews.

    • Priority: High

  3. Establish and Maintain a Robust Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new hires during their onboarding process. Conduct annual refresher training for all employees, covering current threats like phishing, social engineering, and data handling best practices.

    • Priority: High

  4. Investigate and Secure Open Port 80:

    • Action: Determine the exact purpose and necessity of the service running on Port 80. If it is required, conduct a detailed investigation into the running service (product, version, configuration) and ensure it is hardened, patched, and securely configured. If not required, close the port to minimize the attack surface.

    • Priority: Medium

  5. Conduct More In-Depth Technical Vulnerability Assessments:

    • Action: Schedule and execute regular, comprehensive network and vulnerability scans that include detailed service version enumeration, operating system detection, and checks against known vulnerabilities (CVEs) to gain a clearer and more actionable understanding of the technical attack surface.

    • Priority: Medium

  6. Review and Clarify Anomalous Risk Entries:

    • Action: Investigate the source and intent of unusual or contradictory risk entries (such as the one reported from the provided data) to ensure data integrity, prevent potential manipulation of security reporting, and maintain an accurate risk register.

    • Priority: Informational (for internal process improvement)
