Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture assessment for Blackwood Industries, conducted through a review of organizational security controls and an external network scan. The assessment identified several commendable security practices, particularly the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and sensitive data systems, and the presence of an acceptable use policy. However, critical gaps were identified regarding the absence of MFA for computer logins and a lack of consistent security awareness training for both new and existing employees. The external network scan did not identify any open ports or services exposed for the target scanned, suggesting a relatively locked-down external perimeter or that the scan did not yield active responses. Addressing the identified high-severity risks related to authentication and human-element training is paramount to enhancing the overall security resilience of the organization.

Risk Assessment

Based on the security control review, several high-severity risks were identified. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided, and the technical scan yielded no findings. The primary risks, therefore, stem from gaps in fundamental organizational security practices.

Identified Cybersecurity Risks
Risk ID Risk Name Overview Severity
R-01 Lack of MFA for Computer Logins The organization does not require Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for employees to log into their computers. This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems if credentials are compromised through phishing, malware, or brute-force attacks. High
R-02 Absence of New Employee Security Awareness Training New employees are not receiving security awareness training. This could lead to early missteps, increased susceptibility to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing), and policy violations due to a lack of understanding of organizational security policies and best practices from day one. High
R-03 Infrequent Security Awareness Training for All Employees The organization does not conduct annual security awareness training for all employees. This allows security knowledge to degrade over time, making employees more vulnerable to evolving threats, and potentially leading to a decrease in overall security posture. High

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance Blackwood Industries’ cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins (Addressing R-01):

    • Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all employee computer logins, especially for devices accessing sensitive data or network resources. This adds a crucial layer of security beyond just passwords.

    • Evaluate suitable MFA solutions compatible with the organization’s existing IT infrastructure.

  • Establish Mandatory New Employee Security Awareness Training (Addressing R-02):

    • Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires as part of their onboarding process.

    • The training should cover essential topics such as phishing, password best practices, incident reporting procedures, and the acceptable use policy.

  • Conduct Regular All-Employee Security Awareness Training (Addressing R-03):

    • Implement a recurring (at least annual) security awareness training program for all employees.

    • Training should be engaging, updated with current threat landscapes, and include simulated phishing exercises to test employee vigilance.

    • Track completion rates and provide refresher training as needed.

  • Continuous Monitoring (General Recommendation):

    • Implement continuous monitoring of external-facing assets to detect any unauthorized services or changes.

    • Regularly review and update security policies and controls to adapt to evolving threats.

Conclusion

Blackwood Industries demonstrates a foundational understanding of cybersecurity by implementing MFA for critical systems and maintaining an acceptable use policy. However, the lack of MFA for computer access and the absence of comprehensive and recurring security awareness training represent significant vulnerabilities that could be exploited. By promptly addressing these high-priority recommendations, Blackwood Industries can significantly bolster its defenses against common cyber threats and foster a stronger security-aware culture.
