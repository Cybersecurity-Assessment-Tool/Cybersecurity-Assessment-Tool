1. Executive Summary

Skyward Bound demonstrates a commitment to foundational security through the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computer logins, alongside annual security awareness training for all employees. However, the assessment reveals critical vulnerabilities in key areas. Specifically, there is a lack of MFA for sensitive data systems, an absence of a formal employee acceptable use policy, and a gap in providing initial security awareness training for new employees. The network scan did not identify any publicly exposed services, though this finding is limited by the unspecified target of the scan. Addressing the identified policy and control gaps is paramount to fortifying the organization’s security posture and reducing its overall attack surface.

5. Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of the security questionnaire, the following risks have been identified:

Risk Name Overview Severity
R-01: Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems Sensitive data systems are not protected by Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to critical information through compromised credentials (e.g., phishing, brute-force, or stolen passwords). High
R-02: Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without a formal Acceptable Use Policy, employees may lack clear guidelines on the appropriate use of company IT resources, data handling, and security expectations. This can lead to increased instances of misuse, data exfiltration, and compliance violations. High
R-03: No Security Awareness Training for New Employees New employees are not receiving initial security awareness training. This leaves them immediately susceptible to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing, pretexting) and unaware of crucial security protocols and reporting mechanisms from their first day of employment. Medium

6. Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance Skyward Bound’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

6.1. Technical Control Enhancements

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (Addressing R-01):

    • Action: Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across all systems handling sensitive data. This should include data repositories, critical applications, and administrative interfaces.

    • Priority: Critical

  • Conduct Targeted Network Scans:

    • Action: Provide specific target IP addresses or domains for future network scans. This will enable a comprehensive assessment of the external attack surface and identification of any exposed services or vulnerabilities.

    • Priority: Medium

6.2. Policy and Governance Improvements

  • Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (Addressing R-02):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy that clearly outlines acceptable use of company IT resources, data handling procedures, and security expectations. This policy should be formally communicated to all employees, and their acknowledgment of understanding and adherence should be obtained.

    • Priority: High

6.3. Security Awareness and Training

  • Establish Onboarding Security Awareness Training (Addressing R-03):

    • Action: Integrate mandatory security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new employees. This training should cover essential topics such as phishing identification, malware prevention, password hygiene, social engineering tactics, and the correct procedure for reporting suspicious activities.

    • Priority: High
