Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The review of security controls reveals a mixed posture. While the organization demonstrates good practices in employee training and acceptable use policies, critical gaps exist regarding Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA). Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Maple Leaf Logistics, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing organizational email.

    • Benefit: This will significantly reduce the risk of email account compromise through stolen credentials, phishing, or brute-force attacks, protecting sensitive communications and preventing impersonation.

  2. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all access to systems containing sensitive organizational data.

    • Benefit: This critical control adds an essential layer of security, safeguarding valuable data even if primary user credentials are compromised.

  3. Regular Security Assessments:

    • Action: Conduct periodic vulnerability assessments and penetration tests, including internal network scans, to identify and address potential weaknesses before they can be exploited.

    • Benefit: Proactive identification of vulnerabilities reduces the risk of successful cyberattacks.

  4. Continuous Security Awareness Training (Reinforcement):

    • Action: While current training is good, continuously reinforce the importance of strong passwords, identifying phishing attempts, and the benefits of MFA through ongoing campaigns and simulated attacks.

    • Benefit: A well-informed workforce is the first line of defense against many cyber threats.

Conclusion

Maple Leaf Logistics exhibits a commendable stance on employee security awareness and maintains a tight external network perimeter for the scanned host. However, the critical absence of Multi-Factor Authentication for email and sensitive data systems poses substantial risks that could lead to significant security incidents. Addressing these MFA gaps should be the highest priority to fortify the organization’s defenses and protect its critical assets.
