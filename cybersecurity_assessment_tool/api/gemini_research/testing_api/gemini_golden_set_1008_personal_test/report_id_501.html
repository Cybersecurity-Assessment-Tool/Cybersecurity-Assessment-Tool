Analysis of Security Controls

The review highlights several critical gaps that could significantly impact the organization’s security posture:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: Not requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access via compromised credentials, potentially leading to broader system compromise.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a clear AUP, employees may lack explicit guidelines on the appropriate use of organizational assets, data handling, and acceptable internet usage. This increases the risk of misuse, data leakage, and non-compliance with security protocols.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New hires are often highly susceptible to social engineering, phishing, and other cyberattacks due to unfamiliarity with organizational security practices. The absence of initial, mandatory security awareness training leaves the organization exposed to this elevated human-factor risk.

Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive assessment, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Catalyst Consulting’s cybersecurity posture and mitigate identified risks:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all user authentications to company workstations, servers, and VPN access.

    • Benefit: This significantly reduces the risk of successful attacks from compromised credentials, enhancing overall access control security.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft and implement a clear AUP outlining appropriate use of company IT resources, data handling, internet usage policies, and device usage.

    • Benefit: Provides clear guidelines for employees, reduces misuse of company resources, and strengthens data protection practices. All employees should review and acknowledge the AUP annually.

  3. Implement Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Integrate comprehensive security awareness training as a mandatory component of the onboarding process for all new hires.

    • Benefit: Equips new employees with essential knowledge to identify and resist social engineering attempts, phishing, and other common cyber threats from day one, reducing the human attack surface.

  4. Immediately Investigate and Secure Port 8080 Exposure:

    • Action: Urgently investigate the service running on Port 8080 identified with the "TOP SECRET DB" title. Determine the exact nature of the data/system exposed and its criticality.

    • Action: Implement immediate security controls: restrict network access to only authorized personnel and systems (e.g., via firewall rules), remove public exposure, and ensure robust authentication, authorization, and encryption are in place for any legitimate access.

    • Action: Update the internal risk register to accurately reflect the true, critical severity of this exposure, overriding the previous "secured" assessment.

    • Benefit: Prevents unauthorized access to highly sensitive information or critical systems, mitigating a severe data breach or operational disruption risk.

Conclusion

Catalyst Consulting exhibits a mixed cybersecurity posture, characterized by some foundational security controls existing alongside significant vulnerabilities. The critical discovery of an exposed "TOP SECRET DB" on port 8080, in direct conflict with existing risk assessments, highlights the necessity for continuous vigilance, proactive threat hunting, and rigorous validation of security controls. Addressing the identified gaps and vulnerabilities, particularly the immediate securing of Port 8080, will substantially improve the organization’s resilience against cyber threats and bolster its overall security posture.
