Executive Summary

This report details a cybersecurity posture assessment for New Era, based on self-attested organizational data. Due to significant data unavailability regarding network scan results and existing risk registers, this assessment primarily focuses on identified gaps in security controls as reported by the organization.

Key findings include critical gaps in Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation for computer logins and sensitive data systems, alongside a lack of mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees. These areas represent significant vulnerabilities that could be exploited by adversaries, increasing the risk of unauthorized access and data breaches.

A comprehensive technical vulnerability assessment could not be performed due to the lack of network scan data. This limitation means potential technical vulnerabilities, such as outdated software, exposed services, or misconfigurations, remain unassessed. Similarly, pre-existing organizational risks could not be incorporated.

Recommendations prioritize the immediate implementation of MFA across critical access points, establishing regular security awareness training, and conducting thorough technical vulnerability scans to gain a complete understanding of the organization’s technical risk landscape.

Recommendations

To address the identified gaps and strengthen the overall cybersecurity posture of New Era, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Evaluate and deploy robust MFA solutions (e.g., FIDO2, smart cards, authenticator apps) for all user log-ins to organizational computers and workstations.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems, even if passwords are stolen through phishing or other credential compromise techniques.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all access to systems containing sensitive organizational or client data, including databases, file shares, and critical business applications.

    • Benefit: Adds a critical layer of security to protect high-value assets from unauthorized breaches, enhancing data confidentiality and integrity.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory, comprehensive annual security awareness training program for all employees, covering topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, data handling, and organizational policies.

    • Benefit: Educates employees on evolving cyber threats, best practices, and organizational policies, turning them into a stronger line of defense against human-factor attacks.

  4. Conduct a Comprehensive Network Vulnerability Scan:

    • Action: Immediately perform regular, authenticated and unauthenticated vulnerability scans of all internal and external network assets (including 170.55.244.71 and any other relevant infrastructure).

    • Benefit: Identifies open ports, vulnerable services, outdated software, misconfigurations, and other technical weaknesses that could be exploited by attackers, providing crucial data for proactive remediation.

  5. Develop and Maintain an Organizational Risk Register:

    • Action: Establish and regularly review a formal risk register to document, assess, and track all identified cybersecurity risks, including those from technical scans, policy gaps, and compliance requirements.

    • Benefit: Provides a structured approach to risk management, ensuring that risks are understood, prioritized, and mitigated effectively, and offers a clear view of the organization’s risk landscape.

  6. Regular Policy Review and Enforcement:

    • Action: Periodically review and update all security policies, including the acceptable use policy, to ensure they remain relevant, reflect current threats, and are effectively communicated and enforced across the organization.

    • Benefit: Maintains a clear framework for secure behavior and operational security, minimizing policy-related vulnerabilities.
