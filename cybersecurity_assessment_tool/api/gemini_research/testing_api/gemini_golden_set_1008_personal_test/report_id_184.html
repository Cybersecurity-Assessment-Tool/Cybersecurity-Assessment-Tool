Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Echo Chamber Arts, incorporating findings from a security questionnaire and a targeted network scan.

The assessment identifies a critical need for strengthening fundamental security controls, particularly regarding Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and organizational policies. While the targeted network scan revealed no open ports on the host , suggesting a limited direct network attack surface on common ports for that specific device, significant internal control weaknesses pose substantial risks. Critical vulnerabilities include the complete absence of MFA for email access, computer logins, and sensitive data systems. Furthermore, the organization currently lacks an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), which is a crucial governance document for promoting secure practices.

On a positive note, Echo Chamber Arts demonstrates a commendable commitment to security education by consistently conducting security awareness training for both new hires and all employees annually. To mitigate identified risks, immediate actions are recommended, including the widespread implementation of MFA and the formal establishment of an AUP.

Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The questionnaire results highlight significant deficiencies in fundamental security controls, which are categorized as follows:

  • Critical Gap: Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not mandated for email access, computer logins, or access to sensitive data systems. This represents a critical vulnerability. In the event of compromised credentials (e.g., through phishing or credential stuffing), an attacker could gain unauthorized access to email, internal systems, and highly sensitive organizational data without an additional layer of authentication.

  • High Gap: Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The organization currently lacks a formal Acceptable Use Policy. Without clear guidelines, employees may inadvertently engage in risky behaviors related to IT asset usage, data handling, internet browsing, and software installations, increasing the likelihood of security incidents or data breaches.

These identified gaps are significant and require immediate attention to reduce the organization’s overall risk exposure.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and significantly enhance the cybersecurity posture of Echo Chamber Arts, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Access Points:

    • Email Systems: Immediately deploy MFA for all organizational email accounts. This is a primary defense against phishing and credential stuffing attacks.

    • Computer Logins: Implement MFA for all computer logins throughout the organization, extending this protection to workstations and servers.

    • Sensitive Data Systems: Mandate MFA for access to all systems and applications that store, process, or transmit sensitive data.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Policy Creation: Create a clear, concise, and comprehensive AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT assets, data handling procedures, internet browsing rules, and overall security responsibilities for all employees.

    • Training and Acknowledgment: Ensure all employees are thoroughly trained on the AUP, understand its implications, and formally acknowledge their compliance. Conduct regular refresher training sessions.

  3. Conduct Further Network Scans and Vulnerability Assessments:

    • Expanded Scope: Extend network scans to cover a broader range of internal and external IP addresses and port ranges relevant to the entire organizational infrastructure, not just a single host.

    • Authenticated Scans: Perform authenticated vulnerability scans. These scans, conducted with credentials, can identify weaknesses within operating systems, installed applications, and misconfigurations that are not visible through unauthenticated port scans.

    • Web Application Testing: If applicable, conduct web application penetration testing for any publicly accessible web services.

  4. Implement Regular Security Audits and Penetration Testing:

    • Periodic Audits: Establish a schedule for regular internal and external security audits to continuously monitor for new vulnerabilities, assess the effectiveness of implemented controls, and ensure compliance with best practices.

    • Penetration Testing: Consider engaging third-party experts to conduct periodic penetration tests. These simulated attacks can uncover weaknesses that automated tools might miss and provide a realistic view of the organization’s defensive capabilities.
