1. Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Midnight Oil Studios, based on a review of organizational security controls, a targeted network scan, and an analysis of reported risks.

The organization demonstrates good practice in implementing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems, as well as providing initial security awareness training for new employees. These foundational controls are critical for protecting access and increasing initial awareness.

However, two significant organizational gaps were identified: the absence of a formal employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and the lack of mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees. These gaps represent high risks that could lead to misuse of company assets, data breaches, and a decline in overall security posture over time.

A targeted network scan on the internal host revealed no open ports, suggesting a relatively well-secured perimeter for the scanned device at the time of assessment. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were reported in the provided data.

The recommendations section outlines actionable steps to mitigate the identified organizational risks and further strengthen the organization’s cybersecurity defenses.

6. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  • Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a detailed AUP that clearly defines employee responsibilities regarding the use of company IT resources, data handling, internet usage, and communication.

    • Ensure all employees, including new hires, review and formally acknowledge their understanding and acceptance of the AUP. This should be a mandatory annual process.

    • Integrate the AUP into HR policies and disciplinary frameworks.

  • Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement a recurring, mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not limited to just new hires.

    • The training content should be updated regularly to cover the latest threats (e.g., sophisticated phishing techniques, ransomware, supply chain attacks) and reinforce best practices for data protection, password hygiene, and suspicious activity reporting.

    • Consider conducting simulated phishing exercises periodically to assess and improve employee vigilance and identify areas for further training.

  • Maintain and Verify Strong Technical Controls:

    • Continue the robust practice of limiting exposed services and ensuring strong firewall configurations, as indicated by the current network scan results for the target host.

    • Implement an asset inventory system to track all network-connected devices.

    • Regularly review network configurations, firewall rules, and conduct periodic vulnerability scans across the entire internal network to ensure no unintended services become exposed or vulnerabilities arise.

  • Implement a Regular Security Posture Review Process:

    • Establish a schedule for regular internal reviews of all security policies, procedures, and technical controls.

    • Consider engaging independent third-party experts for annual penetration testing and advanced security assessments to identify blind spots and continuously improve defenses.

7. Conclusion

Midnight Oil Studios has a commendable foundation in cybersecurity, notably with strong MFA implementation and initial employee training. However, the identified critical gaps concerning a formal Acceptable Use Policy and recurring annual security awareness training present significant organizational risks that, if left unaddressed, could substantially compromise data integrity, operational continuity, and compliance. By proactively implementing the recommended actions, Midnight Oil Studios can significantly mature its overall cybersecurity posture, reduce its attack surface, and foster a more secure operational environment.
