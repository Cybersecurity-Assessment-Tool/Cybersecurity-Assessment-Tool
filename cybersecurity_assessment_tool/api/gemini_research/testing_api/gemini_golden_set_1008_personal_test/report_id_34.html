1. Executive Summary

This report outlines the cybersecurity posture of Neon Pulse Entertainment based on a review of organizational security controls and an analysis of provided network scan data. While no external technical vulnerabilities were identified due to the absence of specific scan results, the organizational control review revealed significant gaps. Specifically, the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computer logins, the absence of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and the complete lack of security awareness training for employees represent critical risks to the organization’s information security. Addressing these fundamental deficiencies is crucial for enhancing the overall security posture and reducing the likelihood of successful cyberattacks.

5. Risk Assessment

Based on the security control review, several critical risks have been identified due to the absence or insufficient implementation of fundamental security practices. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided in the input data, and no technical vulnerabilities were found from the provided scan data.

Identified Risks
Risk Name Severity Overview
Lack of MFA on Email High Without Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), email accounts are highly vulnerable to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks. A compromised email account can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data exfiltration, and unauthorized access to other linked services.
Lack of MFA on Computers High The absence of MFA for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems. If credentials are stolen or guessed, attackers can gain a foothold within the network, potentially leading to data breaches, ransomware, or other malicious activities.
No Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Medium Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding the secure and appropriate use of IT resources. This lack of clear guidelines increases the risk of misuse, data leakage, legal non-compliance, and operational inefficiencies.
No Security Awareness Training for New Employees High New employees often represent a higher risk as they may not be immediately familiar with the organization’s specific security protocols, policies, and common threat vectors. This makes them particularly susceptible to social engineering attacks such as phishing, leading to potential compromises.
No Annual Security Awareness Training High Regular, recurring security awareness training is crucial for keeping all employees updated on evolving threat landscapes, new attack methods, and best practices. Without annual training, employee vigilance may decline, and their knowledge of current cyber threats can become outdated, increasing the overall human attack surface.

6. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Neon Pulse Entertainment, the following recommendations are provided:

6.1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

  • Recommendation: Mandate and implement MFA for all employee email accounts. This should be a top priority.

  • Recommendation: Mandate and implement MFA for all computer logins, especially for devices accessing sensitive organizational data or network resources.

  • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are stolen, making it much harder for attackers to compromise accounts and systems.

6.2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

  • Recommendation: Develop a clear, comprehensive, and legally reviewed Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that outlines employee responsibilities for using organizational IT resources.

  • Recommendation: Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and periodically thereafter.

  • Benefit: Establishes clear expectations for secure behavior, reduces misuse of IT assets, and helps mitigate risks associated with insider threats and compliance violations.

6.3. Establish a Robust Security Awareness Training Program

  • Recommendation: Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process. This training should cover common threats (e.g., phishing, social engineering), password best practices, data handling, and organizational security policies.

  • Recommendation: Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all existing employees. This training should be updated regularly to reflect current threat trends and reinforce secure behaviors.

  • Benefit: Empowers employees to recognize and report suspicious activities, transforms them into a stronger line of defense, and significantly reduces the organization’s susceptibility to human-factor cyberattacks.

6.4. Conduct Comprehensive Technical Scans

  • Recommendation: Perform regular, authenticated internal and external network vulnerability scans.

  • Recommendation: Conduct periodic penetration tests against critical systems and applications.

  • Benefit: Identifies technical vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and outdated software that could be exploited by attackers, providing actionable insights for remediation.
