Executive Summary

This cybersecurity posture assessment for Tidal Wave Sports reveals a foundational commitment to security, particularly in implementing MFA for sensitive systems and computers, and establishing an acceptable use policy. Initial security awareness training for new employees is a positive step. However, two significant vulnerabilities were identified through the organizational questionnaire: the absence of MFA for email access and the lack of annual, recurring security awareness training for all staff. Both areas represent high-priority risks that could lead to unauthorized access, phishing attacks, and a general weakening of the human firewall. The absence of network scan data means no external attack surface analysis could be performed, and no pre-existing vulnerabilities were reported. Recommendations focus on addressing the identified control gaps to bolster the organization’s defenses against common cyber threats.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Tidal Wave Sports, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Action: Immediately enable and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all organizational email accounts. This should include all users, including administrative and privileged accounts.

    • Rationale: Email is a primary vector for cyberattacks (e.g., phishing, BEC). MFA significantly reduces the risk of account compromise even if passwords are stolen or weak.

    • Priority: Critical

  2. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory annual (or more frequent) security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires. This training should cover current threats such as phishing, social engineering, ransomware, and proper data handling.

    • Rationale: The threat landscape is constantly evolving. Regular training ensures that all employees are up-to-date on best practices and emerging threats, strengthening the human firewall.

    • Priority: High

  3. Conduct Regular External Network Scans:

    • Action: Engage in regular external vulnerability scanning and penetration testing of your public-facing IP addresses and infrastructure.

    • Rationale: Without scan data, the external attack surface remains unknown. Regular scans can identify open ports, vulnerable services, and misconfigurations that attackers could exploit.

    • Priority: Medium (for future planning)

  4. Maintain a Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Action: Implement a formal vulnerability management program to identify, assess, prioritize, and remediate security vulnerabilities across all systems and applications. This includes keeping track of discovered vulnerabilities, even if initially none were provided.

    • Rationale: Proactive vulnerability management is crucial for maintaining a strong security posture and reducing the likelihood of successful attacks.

    • Priority: Medium (for future planning)
