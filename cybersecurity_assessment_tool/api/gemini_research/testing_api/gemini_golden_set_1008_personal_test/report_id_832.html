Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Astraeus Aerospace, based on a review of organizational data, a security questionnaire, and technical network scan results. The assessment reveals a generally positive technical footprint with no open ports identified on the scanned internal target, but highlights significant gaps in foundational security controls, particularly concerning multi-factor authentication (MFA) implementation for critical systems and the frequency of security awareness training. These deficiencies introduce considerable risk to the organization’s data and operations. Immediate action is recommended to implement comprehensive MFA and enhance security training programs.

Analysis of Questionnaire Responses

The questionnaire highlights several critical areas requiring attention:

  • MFA for Email Access: The lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access is a significant vulnerability. Phishing and credential stuffing attacks could easily compromise email accounts, leading to unauthorized access, data breaches, or further network penetration.

  • MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Failure to enforce MFA for systems containing sensitive data exposes critical assets to unauthorized access, increasing the risk of data loss, compliance violations, and severe reputational damage.

  • Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive security training, the absence of annual refresher training for all employees means that critical security knowledge may not be reinforced or updated regularly. This leaves the organization susceptible to evolving threats and social engineering tactics.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Astraeus Aerospace, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Mandate the use of MFA for all email accounts across the organization. Implement a robust MFA solution that supports various authentication methods (e.g., authenticator apps, hardware tokens).

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if passwords are stolen, by adding an essential layer of security.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Extend MFA requirements to all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data (e.g., CRM, ERP, HR systems, critical databases).

    • Benefit: Provides an additional, strong layer of security, protecting critical data from unauthorized access and helping meet compliance requirements.

  3. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), best practices for data handling, and organizational security policies.

    • Benefit: Enhances the human firewall, empowering employees to recognize, avoid, and report potential cyber threats, thereby reducing the risk of successful social engineering attacks and human error-induced breaches.

  4. Maintain Strong Network Perimeter and Conduct Regular Scans:

    • Action: Continue to regularly review and update firewall rules and network configurations to ensure that only necessary ports are open and legitimate services are exposed. Conduct periodic internal and external vulnerability scans and penetration tests to proactively identify and address potential weaknesses.

    • Benefit: Keeps the external and internal attack surface minimal, limiting entry points for potential attackers and providing ongoing assurance of network security.

Conclusion

While the technical scan revealed a clean external footprint for the specified target, the organizational security questionnaire exposed critical gaps in Astraeus Aerospace’s foundational security controls. The absence of comprehensive Multi-Factor Authentication for email and sensitive data systems, coupled with inadequate annual security awareness training, represents significant vulnerabilities that could be exploited by adversaries. Addressing these core issues through the recommended actions will substantially strengthen the organization’s defense against cyber threats and improve its overall security posture.
