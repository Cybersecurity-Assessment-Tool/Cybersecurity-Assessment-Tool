While a recent internal network scan found no open ports on the targeted host 192.168.1.100, this positive technical finding is critically overshadowed by extensive gaps in organizational security practices. Immediate attention is required to implement robust MFA across critical systems, establish essential security policies, and initiate comprehensive security awareness programs to mitigate substantial and identified risks.

6. Recommendations

Based on the identified risks, the following actionable recommendations are provided to significantly improve Fable & Lore’s cybersecurity posture:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • Priority: Immediately deploy and enforce mandatory MFA for all email accounts, given the high risk of compromise.

    • Expansion: Extend MFA requirements to all computer and workstation logins, especially for administrative accounts and systems processing sensitive data. This includes domain logins and access to critical internal resources.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities for using company IT resources, internet, email, and mobile devices.

    • Ensure all employees formally review and acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and annually thereafter.

    • Integrate the AUP into new employee onboarding processes and ongoing training.

  3. Establish and Maintain a Robust Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Onboarding: Implement mandatory, thorough security awareness training for all new hires during their onboarding process to instill best practices from day one.

    • Annual Training: Conduct mandatory, organization-wide security awareness training for all employees at least once per year. This training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware, social engineering) and reinforce company policies.

    • Testing: Include regular simulated phishing exercises and other social engineering tests to evaluate employee vigilance and reinforce training concepts.

  4. Conduct a Comprehensive Internal Network Assessment:

    • While the scan on 192.168.1.100 showed no open ports, a broader and more in-depth internal network scan across all critical assets and subnets is recommended. This will ensure full visibility of all open ports, services, and potential vulnerabilities within the entire infrastructure.

    • Verify that the specific host 192.168.1.100 aligns with its intended function and security baseline, confirming that the absence of open ports is intentional and secure.
