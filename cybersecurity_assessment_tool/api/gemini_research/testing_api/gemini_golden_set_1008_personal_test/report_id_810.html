Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Paper Plane Publishing based on a review of organizational data, a targeted network scan, and existing risk information. The network scan of the target 192.168.0.5 revealed no open ports, which is a positive indicator for the perimeter security of the scanned host. This finding suggests a previously identified risk of an "Unencrypted Web Server" (port 80 open) may be mitigated or outdated. However, a review of internal security controls via questionnaire highlighted several critical gaps: the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins, a lack of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and insufficient annual security awareness training for all employees. These internal control deficiencies represent significant risks that could be exploited through social engineering or credential compromise. Recommendations focus on addressing these identified control gaps to enhance overall security.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Paper Plane Publishing, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Mandate MFA for all user logins to company computers, laptops, and critical internal systems to significantly reduce the risk of credential theft and unauthorized access.

    • Evaluate suitable MFA solutions that integrate with existing IT infrastructure.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, comprehensive AUP that outlines acceptable use of company assets (hardware, software, networks), data handling procedures, and internet/email usage guidelines.

    • Ensure all employees review, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and annually thereafter.

  3. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Implement a recurring, mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees.

    • Training content should be updated regularly to cover current threats, phishing detection, ransomware, social engineering tactics, and company-specific policies and procedures.

  4. Review and Update Risk Register:

    • Verify the current status of the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk and other existing risks against the latest scan findings and any operational changes.

    • Update the organizational risk register to accurately reflect the current risk landscape.

  5. Expand Security Scans:

    • Consider expanding network scanning efforts to cover a broader range of internal and external assets, including other publicly exposed services, to identify any additional potential vulnerabilities.

    • Implement regular, automated vulnerability scanning as part of a continuous security monitoring program.
