Executive Summary

This report details the findings from a cybersecurity posture assessment for Prism Logic, encompassing a review of organizational security controls, an external network scan, and an evaluation of existing risk data. The overall security posture reveals significant foundational gaps primarily related to multi-factor authentication (MFA) implementation. While the external network scan did not identify any immediate open ports or services, and no pre-existing vulnerabilities were documented in the provided data, the lack of MFA across critical access points (email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems) presents a critical risk. Addressing these control deficiencies is paramount to enhancing the organization’s resilience against common cyber threats, particularly unauthorized access attempts. Immediate recommendations focus on the strategic implementation of MFA and continuous security best practices.

Recommendations

Based on the assessment findings, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of Prism Logic.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Action: Mandate and configure MFA for all email accounts. Prioritize administrative accounts and those with access to sensitive information.

    • Benefit: Drastically reduces the risk of email account compromise, a primary vector for phishing and business email compromise (BEC) attacks.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy MFA solutions for all employee computer logins, particularly for endpoints accessing the corporate network or sensitive systems.

    • Benefit: Adds a critical layer of defense against unauthorized access to workstations and internal network resources, even if primary credentials are stolen.

  3. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Ensure all systems containing or processing sensitive data (e.g., HR, finance, customer data platforms) require MFA for access.

    • Benefit: Protects the organization’s most valuable assets from unauthorized access, reducing the likelihood and impact of data breaches.

  4. Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Technical Assessments:

    • Action: Perform regular internal and external vulnerability scans, including authenticated scans, to identify potential weaknesses in network devices, servers, and applications.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies technical vulnerabilities before they can be exploited by malicious actors.

  5. Maintain Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Continue with and regularly update security awareness training for all employees, emphasizing new threats and the importance of reporting suspicious activities.

    • Benefit: Empowers employees to act as a strong line of defense against social engineering and other human-centric attacks.
