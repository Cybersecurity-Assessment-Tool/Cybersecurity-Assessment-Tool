Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive assessment of the cybersecurity posture for Deep Root Ecology, synthesized from recent network scan data, an organizational security questionnaire, and existing risk intelligence. Our analysis identifies several critical vulnerabilities and control gaps. Key findings include the exposure of Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) on an internal host, a notable lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins, the absence of a formal employee acceptable use policy (AUP), and insufficient regular security awareness training. These issues collectively represent a significant attack surface and internal control weaknesses that demand immediate attention. Proactive remediation of these identified risks is paramount to bolster Deep Root Ecology’s defense mechanisms against potential cyber threats and improve overall security resilience.

Recommendations

To effectively mitigate the identified risks and significantly enhance the cybersecurity posture of Deep Root Ecology, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Mandate and enforce MFA for all user logins to company computers, especially for privileged accounts and systems within the internal network.

    • Benefit: This is a fundamental control that drastically reduces the risk of unauthorized access, even if primary credentials are compromised.

  2. Secure and Isolate Exposed RDP Services:

    • Action: Immediately review all instances of RDP exposure (10.10.10.50, 10.10.10.51, and any other internal or external RDP endpoints). Implement the following hardening measures:

      • Restrict RDP access to a specific whitelist of trusted IP addresses.

      • Enable Network Level Authentication (NLA) to prevent unauthenticated access.

      • Enforce strong, complex password policies for all RDP users.

      • Integrate MFA for RDP access.

      • Prefer a Virtual Private Network (VPN) for remote access to the internal network, rather than direct RDP exposure.

      • Where direct RDP is unavoidable, consider implementing an RDP gateway with appropriate security controls.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the attack surface and mitigates a high-risk vector commonly exploited by cyber adversaries.

  3. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Formally develop and roll out a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of all company IT resources, data, and internet access.

    • Action: Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally during onboarding and annually thereafter.

    • Benefit: Provides clear guidelines for employee behavior, reduces the risk of accidental or malicious misuse of company assets, and supports compliance efforts.

  4. Establish and Maintain Regular Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • Action: Institute a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should be interactive and cover current threats such as phishing, ransomware, social engineering, password best practices, and secure data handling.

    • Action: Implement specific security awareness training for new hires during their onboarding process.

    • Benefit: Transforms employees into an active defense layer, enabling them to identify and report threats, thereby significantly reducing the success rate of human-centric attacks.

  5. Implement Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Patch Management:

    • Action: Establish a routine schedule for both internal and external vulnerability scanning to continuously identify new weaknesses in the infrastructure.

    • Action: Develop and enforce a robust patch management process to ensure all operating systems, applications, and network devices are kept up-to-date with the latest security patches.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies and remediates vulnerabilities before they can be exploited, reducing the organization’s overall attack surface.
