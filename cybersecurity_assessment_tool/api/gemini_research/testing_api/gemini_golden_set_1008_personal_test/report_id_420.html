Recommendations

Based on the identified risks and control gaps, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance Borealis Tech’s cybersecurity posture. Immediate action on the critical items is strongly advised.

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Systems (Critical Priority):

    • Immediate Priority: Deploy MFA for all email accounts (e.g., via Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, or other email providers).

    • Immediate Priority: Implement MFA for all computer logins, particularly for domain-joined machines and those with access to sensitive data. Consider solutions like Windows Hello for Business, third-party MFA providers, or conditional access policies.

    • Immediate Priority: Extend MFA to all sensitive data systems and applications, including cloud services, databases, and critical business applications.

    • Rationale: MFA is a cornerstone of modern identity and access management, significantly mitigating the risk of credential theft and unauthorized access.

  • Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (High Priority):

    • Create a clear and comprehensive AUP that outlines employee responsibilities, permitted uses of company IT assets (hardware, software, network, data), and prohibited activities.

    • Ensure all employees formally review and acknowledge the AUP, preferably as part of onboarding and annually thereafter, with signed confirmations.

    • Rationale: An AUP establishes clear expectations for employee conduct, helps manage legal and compliance risks, and fosters a more secure operational environment.

  • Enhance and Regularize Security Awareness Training for All Employees (High Priority):

    • While new employee training is commendable, implement mandatory annual (or more frequent) security awareness training for all employees, not just new hires.

    • Training content should be engaging and cover current threats such as sophisticated phishing, ransomware, social engineering tactics, strong password practices (supplemented by MFA), data handling best practices, and clear incident reporting procedures.

    • Conduct regular simulated phishing exercises to test employee vigilance and reinforce training concepts.

    • Rationale: Continuous training is essential to maintain a strong "human firewall" against evolving cyber threats, reducing the risk of human error-induced security incidents.

  • Address Data Inconsistencies for Future Assessments:

    • Ensure all data inputs for future security assessments are complete, accurate, and in the expected format to allow for a comprehensive and accurate analysis. This includes providing complete network scan data and any existing vulnerability registers.

    • Rationale: Complete and reliable data is fundamental for conducting a holistic, effective, and actionable cybersecurity posture assessment.
