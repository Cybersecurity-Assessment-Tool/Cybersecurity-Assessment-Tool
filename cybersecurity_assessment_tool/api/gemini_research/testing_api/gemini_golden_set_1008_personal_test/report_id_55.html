Introduction

This document presents the findings of a cybersecurity posture assessment for Fable & Lore. The assessment integrates data from a targeted network scan, an organizational security questionnaire, and a review of reported vulnerabilities to provide a holistic view of the current security landscape. The objective is to identify existing risks, highlight critical security gaps, and offer actionable recommendations for improvement.

Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The review highlights significant deficiencies in critical security practices:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not uniformly applied across key access points, specifically for email and sensitive data systems. This significantly increases the risk of account compromise through phishing or credential stuffing attacks.

  • Security Policies & Training: The absence of an acceptable use policy and security awareness training (for both new and existing employees) indicates a substantial gap in establishing a security-conscious culture and ensuring employees understand their roles in protecting organizational assets.

Analysis of Technical Findings

  • Open Port 22 (SSH): The presence of an open SSH port suggests potential administrative access or a service running on this port. While SSH is a secure protocol, its exposure to the internet carries inherent risks if not properly configured and monitored. Key concerns include:

    • Lack of specific service, product, and version information from the scan prevents an assessment of known vulnerabilities against the running software.

    • Brute-force attacks or credential stuffing against SSH can lead to unauthorized access if strong authentication mechanisms (e.g., key-based authentication, MFA) are not enforced.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance Fable & Lore’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • High Priority: Immediately implement and enforce MFA for all email accounts.

    • High Priority: Implement and enforce MFA for all access to systems containing sensitive data.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • High Priority: Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines permissible and prohibited use of organizational IT resources, and ensure all employees review and acknowledge it.

  3. Establish Robust Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • High Priority: Develop and conduct mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • High Priority: Implement annual mandatory security awareness training for all existing employees, covering current threats like phishing, social engineering, and safe internet practices.

  4. Secure Exposed SSH Service (Port 22 on ):

    • Medium Priority: Conduct a detailed review of the SSH service on to determine its necessity and confirm it’s not exposing sensitive information or access points.

    • Medium Priority: If necessary, ensure it’s configured with strong, key-based authentication (disabling password authentication), uses non-standard ports, and is restricted by IP address whitelisting wherever possible.

    • Medium Priority: Ensure the SSH server software is patched and up-to-date to protect against known vulnerabilities.

    • Medium Priority: Consider placing the service behind a firewall or VPN, limiting direct internet exposure.

  5. Conduct Comprehensive Vulnerability Scanning:

    • Low Priority (Ongoing): Implement regular, authenticated vulnerability scans to identify outdated software, misconfigurations, and known vulnerabilities on all internet-facing and internal assets.

Conclusion

Fable & Lore faces significant cybersecurity risks primarily due to critical gaps in foundational security controls such as Multi-Factor Authentication, employee training, and policy enforcement. The presence of an open SSH port warrants further investigation. Addressing these identified areas through the recommended actions will substantially enhance the organization’s defense against common cyber threats and foster a more secure operating environment.
