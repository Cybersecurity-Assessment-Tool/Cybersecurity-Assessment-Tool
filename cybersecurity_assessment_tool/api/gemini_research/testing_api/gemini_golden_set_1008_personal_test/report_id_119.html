Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of the cybersecurity posture for Cloud Nine Software, based on a network scan, organizational security questionnaire, and pre-existing risk documentation. The assessment reveals several critical areas requiring immediate attention, particularly concerning sensitive data access controls and foundational security policies. While some controls are well-implemented, such as MFA for email and computer login, significant gaps exist in MFA for sensitive data systems and the lack of an acceptable use policy. A critical vulnerability identified is the exposure of 127.0.0.1 (localhost), with an open port 22, indicating a severe internal system misconfiguration or unintended access, corroborated by both scan data and pre-existing risk registers. This report outlines detailed findings and actionable recommendations to mitigate identified risks and enhance the overall security posture.

Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Cloud Nine Softwareâ€™s cybersecurity posture:

Policy and Procedure Enhancements

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication for Sensitive Data Systems: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all systems that process, store, or transmit sensitive organizational or customer data. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a clear and concise AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources, data, and networks. Ensure all employees review, understand, and acknowledge this policy upon hiring and periodically thereafter.

  • Regular Policy Review and Updates: Establish a schedule for reviewing and updating all security policies, including the AUP and MFA requirements, to ensure they remain relevant and effective against evolving threats.

Technical Control Enhancements

  • Address Localhost Exposure (127.0.0.1): Investigate the nature of the "Localhost Exposed" vulnerability (Severity 10.0). Determine why 127.0.0.1 is accessible and ensure it is properly secured, restricted, or isolated according to its intended function. This may involve network segmentation, firewall rules, or application-level access controls.

  • Harden SSH Configuration on 127.0.0.1: For the open port 22 (SSH), if its exposure is legitimate and necessary, immediately implement SSH hardening best practices:

    • Disable root login.

    • Use key-based authentication exclusively; disable password authentication.

    • Configure strong ciphers and MACs.

    • Limit access to specific trusted IP addresses or networks.

    • Implement a robust logging and monitoring solution for SSH access attempts.

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing: Implement a routine schedule for internal and external vulnerability scans, complemented by periodic penetration tests, to proactively identify and address weaknesses.

Security Awareness and Training

  • Integrate Policy Education into Training: Ensure that the new AUP and updated MFA policies are thoroughly covered in both new hire and annual security awareness training programs.

  • Targeted Training on Data Handling: Provide specialized training for employees who regularly handle sensitive data, emphasizing the importance of MFA and secure data handling practices.
