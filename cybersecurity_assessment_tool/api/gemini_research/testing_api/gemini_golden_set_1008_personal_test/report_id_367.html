Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Cinder & Ash, synthesizing findings from organizational data, a targeted network scan, and an evaluation of current risks. The assessment reveals several critical gaps in security controls, particularly concerning multi-factor authentication for email access, the absence of an acceptable use policy, and insufficient annual security awareness training. While the network scan identified an open SSH port, further configuration details are needed to assess its security posture. The organization’s overall risk posture is assessed as Moderate, with several high-impact recommendations provided to enhance resilience against cyber threats.

Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of security controls, technical scan results, and organizational data, the following risks have been identified:

ID Risk Name Overview Severity
RA01 Lack of MFA for Email Access The organization does not require Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access, significantly increasing the risk of account compromise through phishing or credential stuffing attacks. High
RA02 Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding secure IT usage, potentially leading to misuse of company resources or data exposure. High
RA03 Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training While new employees receive training, the lack of annual refreshers means that existing employees may not be up-to-date on the latest threats and best practices, reducing overall security posture. Medium
RA04 Open SSH Service (Port 22) An SSH service is exposed on the target host. Without details on its configuration (e.g., strong passwords, key-based authentication, rate limiting, updated software version), it represents a potential entry point for attackers. Medium

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Cinder & Ash, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access (Addressing RA01):

    • Action: Mandate and configure Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing email services.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (Addressing RA02):

    • Action: Create, disseminate, and ensure all employees sign an AUP that clearly outlines expectations for IT resource usage, data handling, and security responsibilities.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces insider risks, and improves overall employee security awareness and accountability.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training (SAT) (Addressing RA03):

    • Action: Implement a mandatory, annual security awareness training program for all employees. This should include topics such as phishing, social engineering, data protection, and password hygiene.

    • Benefit: Keeps employees informed about evolving threats, reinforces security best practices, and strengthens the human element of security.

  4. Secure SSH Service Configuration (Addressing RA04):

    • Action: Review and harden the configuration of the SSH service on . This includes:

      • Disabling password-based authentication in favor of strong key-based authentication.

      • Ensuring all SSH software is up-to-date with the latest security patches.

      • Implementing rate limiting and intrusion detection/prevention systems to detect and block brute-force attempts.

      • Changing the default SSH port (22) to a non-standard port, if practical and compatible with operational needs (obscurity, not security).

    • Benefit: Reduces the attack surface and significantly lowers the risk of unauthorized remote access to the host.

Conclusion

Cinder & Ash has a foundation for some security controls (MFA for computers/sensitive data, new employee SAT), but critical gaps remain, particularly in comprehensive MFA deployment, policy development, and ongoing security education. Addressing the identified risks and implementing the provided recommendations will significantly enhance the organization’s defense against common cyber threats and improve its overall security posture.
