Executive Summary

This report provides an assessment of the current cybersecurity posture based on organizational data, a targeted network scan, and existing risk information. The analysis reveals several critical gaps in fundamental security controls, primarily identified through the organizational questionnaire. While the conducted network scan for 192.168.0.5 yielded no immediately exploitable open ports, a pre-existing risk concerning an "Unencrypted Web Server" requires further investigation to reconcile with the scan findings. The most pressing issues revolve around the absence of multi-factor authentication (MFA) across critical access points, a lack of an employee acceptable use policy, and insufficient annual security awareness training. Addressing these foundational weaknesses is paramount to enhancing the overall security posture and reducing the attack surface.

Risk Assessment

This section correlates findings from the questionnaire, technical scan, and pre-existing risk data to provide a comprehensive view of the organization’s risk landscape.

Summary of Identified Risks:

  1. High Risk: Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

    • Overview: Without MFA on email, computers, and sensitive data systems, the organization is highly vulnerable to phishing, credential stuffing, and brute-force attacks. A single compromised password could grant an attacker significant access.

    • Severity: Critical

  2. High Risk: Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

    • Overview: Lacking an AUP can lead to inconsistent or insecure usage of company IT resources, increasing risks of data breaches, malware infections, and compliance violations due to undefined employee responsibilities.

    • Severity: High

  3. High Risk: Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training

    • Overview: While new employees receive training, the absence of annual refreshers means employees may forget best practices, become complacent, or fail to keep up with evolving threat landscapes, making them susceptible to social engineering and other attacks.

    • Severity: High

  4. Moderate Risk: Unencrypted Web Server (Pre-existing)

    • Risk Name: Unencrypted Web Server

    • Overview: The organization has an existing record of an "Unencrypted Web Server" where "Port 80 is open." The CVSS score assigned is 5.0.

    • Severity: Moderate (CVSS: 5.0)

    • Note on Correlation: It is important to highlight the discrepancy between this pre-existing risk, which states "Port 80 is open," and the recent technical scan results (Input 1), which show port 80 as closed on target 192.168.0.5. This could indicate:

      • The pre-existing risk refers to a different server not covered by the recent scan.

      • The pre-existing risk has been remediated, and the scan reflects the current state.

      • The scope of the pre-existing risk or the recent scan needs clarification.

      Further investigation is required to reconcile this information.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided, categorized by priority.

High Priority Recommendations

These recommendations address critical gaps that pose immediate and significant risks to the organization.

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Immediately enable and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing email, logging into computers, and accessing sensitive data systems. This is the single most effective control against credential compromise.

    • Consider phishing-resistant MFA solutions where possible.

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear and comprehensive AUP outlining employee responsibilities, permitted and prohibited activities regarding IT assets, and consequences of non-compliance.

    • Ensure all employees review and acknowledge the AUP annually.

  • Establish Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires.

    • Training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), data handling best practices, and policy reminders.

Moderate Priority Recommendations

These recommendations address the pre-existing risk and suggest further investigative steps.

  • Investigate and Reconcile "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk:

    • Conduct an investigation to clarify the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk (CVSS 5.0).

    • Determine if this risk pertains to the scanned target (192.168.0.5), another internal asset, or an external web service.

    • If the risk is still valid, ensure that all public-facing and internal web servers use HTTPS/TLS encryption for all traffic (Port 443) and redirect or close Port 80 to prevent unencrypted communication.

    • If it pertains to 192.168.0.5 and port 80 is indeed closed, update the risk register to reflect remediation.

  • Expand Network Scanning Scope:

    • Conduct more comprehensive network scans, including a wider range of ports, deeper service enumeration, and potentially authenticated scans for internal assets.

    • Regular scanning helps identify newly exposed services or misconfigurations.
