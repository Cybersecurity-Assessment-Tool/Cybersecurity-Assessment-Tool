Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of , the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Action: Mandate the use of MFA for all organizational email accounts.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if passwords are stolen, by requiring a second verification factor (e.g., a code from a mobile app, a hardware token).

    • Priority: High

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the proper and improper use of organizational IT resources, data, and communication systems. Ensure all employees read, understand, and acknowledge the policy upon hire and annually thereafter.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines for employee behavior, minimizes misuse of resources, and improves overall data protection and compliance.

    • Priority: Medium

  3. Introduce Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Implement mandatory security awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new hires. This training should cover topics such as phishing, password hygiene, data handling, and reporting suspicious activities.

    • Benefit: Equips new employees with essential cybersecurity knowledge from day one, reducing their susceptibility to common threats and fostering a security-conscious culture from the outset.

    • Priority: Medium

  4. Conduct Regular External Vulnerability Scans:

    • Action: Regularly perform authenticated and unauthenticated vulnerability scans on all public-facing assets (e.g., and ) to identify technical weaknesses.

    • Benefit: Proactively discovers outdated software, misconfigurations, and known vulnerabilities before they can be exploited by attackers.

    • Priority: Medium (Ongoing)

  5. Consider Advanced Email Security Solutions:

    • Action: Explore and implement advanced email security solutions that offer robust phishing protection, malicious attachment scanning, and DMARC/SPF/DKIM enforcement to prevent email spoofing and improve deliverability.

    • Benefit: Provides an additional layer of defense against sophisticated email-based threats, complementing MFA implementation.

    • Priority: Low (Consider after MFA implementation)

Conclusion

has taken positive steps in cybersecurity by implementing MFA for computer logins and sensitive data systems, as well as conducting annual security awareness training. However, critical gaps related to email MFA, an acceptable use policy, and initial security training for new employees expose the organization to preventable risks. Addressing these policy and human-factor vulnerabilities should be an immediate priority. Furthermore, ongoing technical assessments will be crucial for maintaining a robust security posture in an evolving threat landscape.
