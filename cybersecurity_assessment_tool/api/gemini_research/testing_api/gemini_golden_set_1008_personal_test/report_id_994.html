2.1. Analysis of Questionnaire Responses

The review indicates a strong adoption of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across critical access points, which is a significant security strength. However, two critical gaps were identified:

  • Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an AUP leaves the organization vulnerable to misuse of IT resources, potential data breaches due to unclear guidelines, and compliance risks.

  • Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive initial training, the lack of annual refresher training for all employees means the workforce may not be consistently updated on evolving cyber threats, making them susceptible to social engineering, phishing, and other human-factor exploits.

5. Recommendations

To address the identified gaps and enhance Nova Terra’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, comprehensive AUP that outlines proper usage of company IT resources, data handling, and internet/email policies.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, preferably annually.

    • Integrate AUP review into employee onboarding and annual compliance processes.

  • Establish a Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Implement a mandatory, ongoing security awareness training program for all employees, conducted at least once per year.

    • Training should cover current threat landscapes, best practices for identifying phishing and social engineering attempts, strong password hygiene, secure data handling, and clear incident reporting procedures.

    • Consider simulated phishing campaigns to test employee vigilance and reinforce training concepts.

  • Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Vulnerability Assessments:

    • Although the provided scan data was empty, it is critical to perform regular, authenticated internal and external vulnerability scans.

    • These scans should identify potential security weaknesses across the entire IT infrastructure, including network devices, servers, workstations, and web applications.

    • Prioritize and remediate identified vulnerabilities based on severity and potential impact.

  • Implement a Policy Review and Update Cycle:

    • Establish a regular (e.g., annual) review cycle for all security policies and procedures, including those related to MFA, AUP, and training.

    • Ensure policies remain current and effective against emerging threats and comply with relevant regulations.

6. Conclusion

Nova Terra demonstrates a commendable commitment to cybersecurity by implementing Multi-Factor Authentication for critical access points. However, the absence of an organization-wide Acceptable Use Policy and annual security awareness training for all employees presents significant human-factor risks that could be exploited. Addressing these gaps through the implementation of robust policies and ongoing training will substantially strengthen the organization’s defense against a wide range of cyber threats. While no external vulnerabilities were found in the provided scan data, it is prudent to establish a continuous vulnerability management program to ensure ongoing security. Proactive measures in these areas will significantly enhance Nova Terra’s overall security posture and resilience.
