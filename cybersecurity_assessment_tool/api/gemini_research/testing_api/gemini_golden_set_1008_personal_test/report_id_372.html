Critical Gaps Identified:

Based on the questionnaire responses, the following critical gaps were identified, indicating areas where current security practices deviate from best practices and introduce significant risk:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Not requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access leaves accounts highly vulnerable to phishing and credential stuffing attacks, potentially leading to business email compromise (BEC).

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Failure to enforce MFA for sensitive data systems significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to critical information, even if primary credentials areiled.

  • Infrequent Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the absence of annual security awareness training for all employees means that evolving threats and best practices may not be consistently communicated or reinforced, leading to a decay in security posture over time.

Recommendations

Based on the current assessment, the following recommendations are provided to improve Hearth & Home’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  1. Implement MFA for All Email Accounts: Mandate and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all organizational email accounts. This should be a top priority to mitigate the risk of Business Email Compromise (BEC) and unauthorized access.

  2. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Extend MFA requirements to all systems storing or processing sensitive organizational data. Identify all such systems and ensure robust MFA mechanisms are in place.

  3. Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training: Establish and enforce a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), best practices for data handling, and organizational policies.

Further Assessment and Ongoing Security

  1. Perform a Comprehensive Network Vulnerability Scan: Immediately schedule and conduct a professional, authenticated vulnerability scan of both external-facing assets (e.g., 6.62.34.27) and internal network infrastructure. This will identify technical weaknesses, outdated software, and misconfigurations not covered by the current assessment.

  2. Develop and Maintain a Risk Register: Establish a formal process for identifying, assessing, and documenting all organizational risks. This includes technical vulnerabilities, operational risks, and compliance risks. Regularly review and update this register.

  3. Review and Update Security Policies: Periodically review the Acceptable Use Policy and other security policies to ensure they remain relevant, comprehensive, and aligned with current threats and organizational practices.

  4. Incident Response Planning: Ensure an incident response plan is in place, regularly tested, and understood by key personnel.

Conclusion

While this report identifies critical gaps in Hearth & Home’s security controls, particularly concerning MFA implementation and security awareness training, the assessment is limited by the absence of parseable technical scan data and existing risk information. Addressing the identified questionnaire gaps is paramount for immediate risk reduction. Subsequent comprehensive technical assessments and risk register development are essential for building a robust and resilient cybersecurity posture.
