Lack of MFA for Email Access Without Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access, accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks. Compromised email can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data breaches, and further network intrusion. Critical

6. Recommendations

To significantly enhance the cybersecurity posture of Structure & Form, the following recommendations are strongly advised:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Systems:

    • Prioritize implementing MFA for all email access.

    • Deploy MFA for all computer logins, especially for administrative accounts and user workstations.

    • Extend MFA requirements to all systems storing or processing sensitive data.

    • Rationale: MFA is a foundational security control that dramatically reduces the risk of account compromise due to stolen or weak passwords.

  2. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Develop and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires.

    • Include current threat landscapes, phishing simulations, social engineering tactics, and data handling best practices.

    • Rationale: Regular training ensures that all employees remain vigilant and informed about the latest cyber threats, acting as a crucial line of defense.

  3. Ensure Data Completeness for Future Assessments:

    • For future assessments, ensure that all requested data, including network scan results and existing vulnerability lists, are provided in a complete and valid format.

    • Rationale: Complete data is essential for a thorough and accurate cybersecurity posture assessment, enabling the identification of a broader range of risks.

  4. Regularly Review and Update Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • While an AUP is in place, review it annually to ensure it remains relevant to current technologies and threats.

    • Rationale: An up-to-date AUP provides clear guidelines for employee behavior, minimizing security risks stemming from misuse.
