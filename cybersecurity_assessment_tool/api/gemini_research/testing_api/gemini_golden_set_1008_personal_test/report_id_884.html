Executive Summary

This report details the current cybersecurity posture of Open Door, based on a combination of organizational self-assessment, an external network scan, and a review of existing vulnerabilities. The assessment reveals significant gaps in fundamental security controls, particularly concerning multi-factor authentication (MFA), security awareness training, and policy enforcement. While the network scan identified minimal external exposure with one open port (80), the absence of crucial internal safeguards presents a high overall risk. Addressing the identified policy and training deficiencies is paramount to improving the organization’s security resilience.

Recommendations

Based on the assessment, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Open Door’s cybersecurity posture:

Critical Policy and Training Recommendations

  • Implement MFA for Email and Sensitive Data Systems: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts and access to systems containing sensitive data. This is a fundamental control against credential theft.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities and acceptable use of company IT resources. Ensure all employees read and acknowledge the policy.

  • Implement Comprehensive Security Awareness Training:

    • New Employee Training: Establish mandatory security awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new employees.

    • Annual Training: Conduct mandatory security awareness training for all employees at least once per year, covering current threats, phishing recognition, and company policies.

Technical Recommendations

  • Investigate and Secure Open Port 80: Conduct a thorough investigation of the service running on port 80 (172.16.0.1). Identify the specific application, product, and version. Ensure it is patched, securely configured, and necessary for external access. If not, consider blocking or restricting access.

  • Conduct Deeper Technical Scans: Perform authenticated vulnerability scans and deeper penetration tests to uncover potential misconfigurations or vulnerabilities within identified services, especially for services where version information was not obtained.

General Recommendations

  • Regular Risk Reviews: Establish a process for regular cybersecurity risk assessments and reviews of security controls to proactively identify and mitigate emerging threats.

  • Address Informational Risks: While the "Informational Risk Advisory" presents a peculiar entry, it should be logged and reviewed as part of a comprehensive risk management process to ensure no underlying issues are overlooked.
