A pre-existing high-severity risk, "Localhost Exposed" (CVSS 10.0), further highlights a potential attack surface that needs urgent investigation and remediation, even if the provided network scan data was minimal. Risk Assessment

This section synthesizes the findings from the security control review, technical scan, and pre-existing vulnerabilities into a comprehensive risk assessment.

Identified Gaps from Questionnaire

Based on the security control questionnaire, the following high-priority gaps were identified:

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Access: Without MFA, sensitive data systems are vulnerable to credential stuffing, phishing, and other password-based attacks, significantly increasing the risk of unauthorized access and data breaches.

  • Absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A missing AUP means employees may not have clear guidelines regarding the acceptable use of company assets and data. This can lead to increased risks from insider threats, accidental data exposure, and non-compliance with security best practices.

  • No Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: While new employees receive training, the lack of recurring annual training leaves the workforce susceptible to evolving cyber threats, social engineering attacks, and outdated security practices, diminishing the overall human firewall.

Recommendations

To enhance the cybersecurity posture of Wildfire Communications, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all systems that process or store sensitive organizational data. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the acceptable use of company IT resources, data, and internet access. Ensure all employees are trained on its contents and acknowledge their understanding and compliance.

  3. Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training: Establish and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threat landscapes, social engineering techniques, secure data handling practices, and phishing recognition.

  4. Investigate and Remediate "Localhost Exposed" Vulnerability: Urgently investigate the "Localhost Exposed" vulnerability. This includes determining the exact nature of the exposure, the service running on port 22 of 127.0.0.1, its necessity, and any associated vulnerabilities. Implement appropriate remediation, which may include disabling unnecessary services, restricting access, or applying security patches.

  5. Conduct Comprehensive Vulnerability Scans: Perform detailed vulnerability scans on all external and internal-facing assets (including the external IP 205.215.139.65) to identify all open ports, running services, and associated vulnerabilities, including version information. This will provide a more complete picture of the attack surface.
