Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Catalyst Consulting based on an organizational questionnaire, a network scan of target 192.168.0.5, and a review of pre-existing risk data.

Key findings indicate significant gaps in Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation and security awareness training, which are critical vulnerabilities. While the network scan did not identify any open ports on the scanned target, suggesting a reduced external attack surface for that specific host, the identified internal control weaknesses pose a substantial risk to the organization’s overall security.

A discrepancy was noted regarding a pre-existing risk: "Unencrypted Web Server" on Port 80, which stated the port was open. However, the current network scan indicates Port 80 is presently closed on the targeted host. This highlights a need for the organization to review and update its risk register based on current assessment data. Addressing the identified control gaps and maintaining an accurate risk posture are paramount for enhancing Catalyst Consulting’s cybersecurity resilience.

Analysis of Security Controls

The questionnaire results reveal significant vulnerabilities in Catalyst Consulting’s security posture, primarily centered around a lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and inadequate security awareness training.

  • MFA Gaps: The absence of MFA for email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems represents a critical vulnerability. This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access through compromised credentials, which remains one of the most common attack vectors.

  • Security Awareness Training Deficiencies: The lack of both new employee and annual security awareness training exposes the organization to social engineering attacks, phishing, and human error. Employees are often the first line of defense, and without proper training, they can inadvertently become an organization’s weakest link.

  • Acceptable Use Policy: The presence of an acceptable use policy is positive, but its effectiveness is diminished without accompanying training and regular reinforcement.

Correlation and Synthesis

The most significant risks stem from internal control weaknesses identified through the questionnaire. The complete absence of MFA across crucial access points (email, computer login, sensitive data systems) creates an enormous vulnerability to credential-based attacks. Similarly, the lack of consistent security awareness training leaves the organization susceptible to human-factor exploitation. These internal control deficiencies represent a higher immediate risk than any findings from the external scan.

The technical scan, while positive in its finding of no open ports on the scanned target, should not overshadow these critical internal control gaps. The finding that Port 80 is closed on 192.168.0.5 directly contradicts the pre-existing risk "Unencrypted Web Server," which states "Port 80 is open." This highlights a potential inaccuracy in the organization’s current risk register or that the pre-existing risk refers to a different asset. It is crucial for Catalyst Consulting to verify and update its risk register based on current assessments to ensure an accurate understanding of its risk posture.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to mitigate the identified risks and improve Catalyst Consulting’s overall cybersecurity posture:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Immediate Priority: Implement MFA for all email accounts, especially for administrators and executives, to prevent unauthorized access via compromised credentials.

    • Critical Systems: Roll out MFA for all computer logins (local and network) and sensitive data systems access. Prioritize systems handling Personally Identifiable Information (PII), financial data, or intellectual property.

  2. Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • New Hires: Mandate security awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new employees, covering foundational cybersecurity principles.

    • Annual Training: Implement mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, encompassing topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, and data handling best practices.

    • Continuous Reinforcement: Conduct regular simulated phishing exercises to test employee vigilance and reinforce training concepts throughout the year.

  3. Review and Update Risk Register:

    • Verification of Discrepancies: Thoroughly investigate the discrepancy between the pre-existing "Unencrypted Web Server" risk and the current scan findings. Determine if the risk refers to a different asset, if it has been remediated, or if the current scan did not cover the asset in question.

    • Process Establishment: Establish a formal process for regularly reviewing and updating the organization’s risk register to ensure it accurately reflects the current threat landscape, control effectiveness, and asset status.

  4. Enhance Internal Network Visibility (Future Consideration):

    • Broader Scanning Scope: Consider extending network scans to cover a wider range of internal assets and network segments to identify any unmanaged or vulnerable systems that may not be visible from a limited external scan.

    • Vulnerability Management Program: Implement a continuous vulnerability management program to regularly identify, assess, and remediate vulnerabilities across the entire IT infrastructure.
