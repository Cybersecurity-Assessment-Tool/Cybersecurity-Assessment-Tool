Executive Summary

This report details a cybersecurity posture assessment for Summit Peak Partners, integrating organizational data, security control reviews, and technical scan results. The analysis highlights several critical security gaps, predominantly in foundational areas such as Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access, the absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and a lack of dedicated security awareness training for new employees. While a limited internal network scan revealed no open ports on the target host (192.168.1.100), these policy and procedural deficiencies represent significant exploitable risks. Key recommendations include immediate implementation of MFA for email, development and enforcement of an AUP, and establishing mandatory security training for all new hires to significantly bolster the organization’s defense against common cyber threats.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Summit Peak Partners, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access: Immediately mandate and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all organizational email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if credentials are compromised.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that clearly outlines employee responsibilities and acceptable use of all company-provided IT assets, systems, and data. Ensure all employees acknowledge, understand, and adhere to this policy.

  3. Mandate Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Establish a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires, to be completed as part of their onboarding process. This ensures foundational security knowledge is imparted from day one.

  4. Enhance Annual Security Awareness Training: While annual training is conducted, consider expanding its scope to regularly cover emerging threats, recent phishing examples, and specific organizational policies. Regular reinforcement helps keep security top-of-mind.

  5. Conduct Comprehensive Internal Vulnerability Scans: Given that 192.168.1.100 is an internal IP, it is strongly recommended to perform regular, authenticated internal vulnerability scans across all network assets. This will identify application-layer weaknesses, misconfigurations, and other potential vulnerabilities beyond basic network port scanning.

  6. Regular Policy Review and Update: Establish a schedule for regular review and updates of all security policies, including the AUP and training materials, at least annually or whenever there are significant changes in the threat landscape, technology, or organizational structure.
