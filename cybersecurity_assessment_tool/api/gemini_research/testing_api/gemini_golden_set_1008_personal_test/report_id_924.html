Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve Moxie Marketing’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email (High Priority)

  • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing email services.

  • Rationale: MFA is one of the most effective controls against credential compromise, significantly reducing the success rate of phishing and password-based attacks.

  • Details: Choose a robust MFA solution (e.g., authenticator apps, FIDO2 security keys) over less secure methods like SMS-based MFA where possible. Ensure all employees are trained on its use.

Establish Regular, Mandatory Security Awareness Training (Medium Priority)

  • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees.

  • Rationale: Continuous training ensures employees are up-to-date with current threat landscapes, common attack vectors (e.g., phishing, ransomware), and best practices for data protection and incident reporting.

  • Details: Training should be engaging, cover relevant topics, and include simulated phishing exercises to test and reinforce learning. Document employee participation.

Conduct a Comprehensive Technical Vulnerability Assessment (High Priority)

  • Action: Provide complete and valid network scan data for a target system to enable a proper technical vulnerability assessment.

  • Rationale: Without a technical scan, unknown vulnerabilities on external-facing assets cannot be identified and remediated, leaving the organization exposed to potential exploits.

  • Details: Ensure the provided data includes details on open ports, services, product versions, and any detected vulnerabilities. This will allow for a detailed analysis of the attack surface.

Conclusion

This cybersecurity posture assessment has highlighted critical organizational gaps related to MFA for email and annual security awareness training. While the presence of MFA for computers and sensitive data systems, along with an acceptable use policy and new employee training, are positive aspects, the identified weaknesses pose significant risks to Moxie Marketing. Furthermore, the inability to conduct a technical vulnerability assessment due to incomplete data leaves a major area of risk unaddressed. Addressing these identified organizational risks and facilitating a comprehensive technical assessment are crucial next steps for strengthening Moxie Marketing’s overall cybersecurity resilience.
