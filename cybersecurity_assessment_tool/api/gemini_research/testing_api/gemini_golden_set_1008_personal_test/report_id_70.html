Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Digital Drift, based on an organizational questionnaire, a network scan, and a review of existing vulnerabilities. The assessment reveals a mixed security landscape. While Digital Drift demonstrates good practices in some areas, such as implementing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins and sensitive data systems, and conducting regular security awareness training, critical gaps were identified. Specifically, the absence of MFA for email access and the lack of an employee acceptable use policy pose significant risks. The network scan did not identify any immediate external vulnerabilities, and no pre-existing risks were provided for review. Recommendations are provided to address the identified weaknesses and further enhance the organization’s security posture.

5. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and further strengthen Digital Drift’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Enable and enforce MFA for all employee email accounts. This is a critical control against credential theft and unauthorized access to sensitive communications and data.

    • Educate employees on the importance of MFA and how to use it effectively.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the proper use of company IT resources, data handling procedures, internet usage, and security expectations.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally during onboarding and annually thereafter.

    • Integrate AUP principles into ongoing security awareness training.

  3. Conduct Regular External and Internal Network Scans:

    • While no open ports were identified in this assessment, regular vulnerability scanning of external-facing assets is crucial to detect new exposures as the infrastructure evolves.

    • Implement internal network vulnerability scanning to identify weaknesses within the internal network.

  4. Maintain and Enhance Existing Security Awareness Training (SAT):

    • Continue the current practice of security awareness training for new and all employees annually.

    • Consider incorporating phishing simulations and more advanced modules based on current threat landscapes.

  5. Continue Strong MFA Practices for Other Systems:

    • Maintain the requirement for MFA to log into computers and access sensitive data systems. These are excellent practices that should be continuously reinforced and potentially expanded to other critical systems.

Conclusion

Digital Drift has established a foundational security posture with positive controls such as MFA for critical systems and consistent security awareness training. However, the lack of MFA for email and the absence of an Acceptable Use Policy represent significant vulnerabilities that should be addressed promptly. By implementing the recommended actions, Digital Drift can significantly reduce its risk exposure and strengthen its overall cybersecurity resilience.
