6. Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Computer Login:

    • Action: Implement mandatory multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all computer logins across the organization. This applies to both local logins and remote access (e.g., VPN, RDP).

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access due to stolen, weak, or guessed passwords, especially for internal systems.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that clearly defines appropriate and inappropriate use of organizational IT resources, data, and internet access.

    • Action: Ensure all employees review, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally on an annual basis or upon significant policy updates.

    • Benefit: Provides clear guidelines, reduces risky employee behavior, aids in legal compliance, and establishes a foundation for disciplinary action if policies are violated.

  3. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), secure browsing habits, password best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Benefit: Reinforces security best practices, keeps employees informed about evolving threats, and cultivates a security-conscious culture, making them a stronger line of defense.

  4. Conduct Regular Network Scans:

    • Action: Implement a schedule for regular, authenticated, and unauthenticated network vulnerability scans of all internet-facing and internal assets.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies open ports, outdated software, misconfigurations, and other technical vulnerabilities that could be exploited by attackers.

7. Conclusion

North Star Education has taken initial steps towards securing its environment, particularly with MFA for email and sensitive data access, and security awareness training for new employees. However, critical vulnerabilities remain due to the lack of MFA for computer logins, a formal AUP, and ongoing annual security awareness training.
