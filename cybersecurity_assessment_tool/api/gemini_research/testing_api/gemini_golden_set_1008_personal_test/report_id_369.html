Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Hidden Gem based on an organizational questionnaire, a network scan, and an analysis of existing risk documentation. The assessment reveals several critical security gaps and a significant discrepancy in the current risk register regarding a potentially exposed sensitive service.

While some basic security controls are in place, a notable lack of multi-factor authentication (MFA) for computer logins, absence of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and deficiencies in security awareness training present considerable risks. Most critically, the network scan identified an open port (8080) hosting a service titled “TOP SECRET DB,” directly contradicting an existing risk entry that classified this port as “secured” with a severity of 0.0. This finding indicates a severe oversight and immediate action is required to investigate and secure this exposed asset.

The current security posture is assessed as Weak, primarily due to the combination of critical control gaps and the confirmed exposure of sensitive information that was previously misclassified. Immediate and comprehensive remediation efforts are highly recommended to mitigate the identified threats.

Technical Scan Results

A network scan was conducted on the client’s internal IP address, 10.5.5.5. The scan identified an open port with a critical service running.

Scan Metadata

  • Target IP Address: 10.5.5.5

  • Scanner Used: Nmap

Key Findings

  • Open Port: 8080/tcp

  • Service/Product: HTTP

  • Service Information: The HTTP service on port 8080 revealed a title of “TOP SECRET DB” via the http-title script. This indicates that a potentially highly sensitive database or interface is directly accessible over the network.

Analysis: The presence of an open port 8080, identified with the title “TOP SECRET DB,” is an extremely critical finding. This suggests that a database containing highly sensitive information might be exposed to the network, potentially without adequate authentication or encryption. This directly contradicts the existing risk register entry for Port 8080, which claims it is "secured" with a CVSS score of 0.0.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Hidden Gem.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Immediately implement MFA for all user logins to internal computers and systems.

    • Benefit: Drastically reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a clear and comprehensive AUP detailing acceptable use of company IT resources, data handling, and security expectations. Disseminate it to all employees and ensure acknowledgment.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces misuse of resources, and improves overall security awareness.

  3. Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • Action: Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • Action: Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, covering current threats like phishing, malware, and social engineering.

    • Benefit: Empowers employees to recognize and report threats, fostering a stronger human firewall.

  4. Immediate Investigation and Remediation of Exposed “TOP SECRET DB” on Port 8080:

    • Action: Immediately isolate or shut down the service on port 8080 on 10.5.5.5.

    • Action: Conduct an urgent investigation to determine the nature, contents, and criticality of the “TOP SECRET DB” service. Assess what data is exposed, who has access, and whether any compromise has occurred.

    • Action: Implement robust authentication, authorization, and encryption for any sensitive services. Ensure such services are not directly exposed to the network without adequate layers of security.

    • Action: Critically review and update the organization’s risk register, specifically re-evaluating the entry concerning “Port 8080 Secured” and implementing a process to prevent such severe misclassifications in the future.

    • Benefit: Prevents potential data breaches, unauthorized access to critical data, and brings the risk register into alignment with actual findings.
