5. Risk Assessment

Based on the provided data, several high-priority risks and critical gaps have been identified.

5.1 Identified Gaps and Risks from Questionnaire

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): The organization does not require MFA for email access, computer logins, or sensitive data systems.

    • Overview: This is a critical security vulnerability. Without MFA, a compromised username and password (e.g., via phishing, brute-force, or data breach) grants an attacker full access to the associated system or data. This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access, data breaches, and ransomware attacks.

    • Severity: Critical

  • Inadequate Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, existing employees are not trained at least once per year.

    • Overview: Regular, recurring security awareness training is crucial for keeping employees informed about evolving threats (e.g., new phishing techniques, social engineering tactics) and best practices. A lack of annual training can lead to a degradation of security posture as employees may forget past training or be unaware of new risks.

    • Severity: High

6. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Blue Horizon Initiative, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally (Critical Priority):

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all critical access points, including:

      • Email accounts (e.g., Microsoft 365, Google Workspace).

      • User logins to company computers and network resources.

      • Access to sensitive data systems, cloud applications, and internal tools.

    • Benefit: Drastically reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if passwords are stolen or compromised.

  2. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training (High Priority):

    • Action: Develop and implement a program for mandatory, comprehensive security awareness training for all employees at least once per year. This training should cover topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, safe browsing, and reporting suspicious activities.

    • Benefit: Keeps employees updated on the latest threats, reinforces secure behaviors, and strengthens the human firewall against cyberattacks.

  3. Conduct Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing (Advisory):

    • Action: While no scan data was available for this report, it is strongly recommended that Blue Horizon Initiative regularly performs authenticated and unauthenticated vulnerability scans of internal and external networks, as well as web applications. Consider engaging third-party penetration testers annually.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies technical vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and weaknesses before attackers can exploit them.

  4. Develop an Incident Response Plan (Advisory):

    • Action: Create and regularly test a formal incident response plan that outlines procedures for detecting, containing, eradicating, recovering from, and post-incident analysis of cybersecurity incidents.

    • Benefit: Minimizes the impact and recovery time from security breaches, ensuring business continuity.
