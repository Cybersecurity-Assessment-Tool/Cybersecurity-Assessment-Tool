3.2. Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The questionnaire responses reveal significant security control gaps that pose a high risk to the organization:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: Without MFA for computer logins, accounts are highly susceptible to credential theft and brute-force attacks, potentially leading to unauthorized system access.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Similar to computer logins, the absence of MFA for sensitive data systems leaves critical information vulnerable to compromise should credentials be stolen.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): An AUP is fundamental for establishing clear expectations regarding appropriate use of organizational assets, data, and networks. Its absence increases the risk of misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance.

6. Recommendations

Based on the analysis of the organizational data, security controls, and technical scan results, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of Symmetry Architecture:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy MFA solutions across all organizational computers, requiring it for all user logins.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access due to stolen or weak credentials.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Ensure all systems housing sensitive organizational data are protected by MFA.

    • Benefit: Provides an additional layer of security for critical assets, protecting against data breaches.

  3. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft a comprehensive AUP outlining the proper use of company devices, networks, and data. Distribute it to all employees and require acknowledgment of understanding.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces misuse, and helps foster a culture of security responsibility.

  4. Investigate and Secure Open Port 80 on 172.16.0.1:

    • Action: Determine the specific service running on port 80 of 172.16.0.1. Verify its necessity, ensure it is properly configured, patched, and secured. If not essential, disable it.

    • Benefit: Mitigates the risk of exploitation through unpatched vulnerabilities or misconfigurations on a public-facing service.

  5. Conduct In-depth Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing:

    • Action: Perform more comprehensive vulnerability scans and penetration tests on all internet-facing assets and internal critical systems.

    • Benefit: Identifies hidden vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and potential attack paths that a basic scan might miss.

  6. Review and Update Existing Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: While training is conducted, ensure content covers topics related to strong authentication practices (MFA importance) and acceptable use guidelines once the AUP is in place.

    • Benefit: Reinforces secure behaviors and educates employees on new policies and controls.
