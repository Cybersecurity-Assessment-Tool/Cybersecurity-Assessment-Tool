Executive Summary

This report details the findings from a cybersecurity posture assessment for Sterling Silver. The assessment involved a review of organizational security controls via a questionnaire and a network scan targeting .

The primary findings indicate significant security gaps related to the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for critical systems (email and computer login) and the lack of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP). While the network scan did not identify any open ports on the target, the foundational control weaknesses identified through the questionnaire present considerable risk. The organization currently has no formally documented pre-existing vulnerabilities.

Addressing the identified deficiencies, particularly the implementation of MFA and a comprehensive AUP, is crucial for improving Sterling Silver’s overall security posture and reducing the likelihood of successful cyberattacks.

Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of the security questionnaire and technical scan results, several risks have been identified. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided in the input data.

Identified Risks

Identified Cybersecurity Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access The organization does not require MFA for email access, making accounts highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks, potentially leading to unauthorized access to sensitive communications and data.
Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logon The organization does not require MFA for logging into computers, increasing the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems if user credentials are compromised. This can lead to lateral movement within the network and data exfiltration.
Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) The organization lacks an employee acceptable use policy, leading to unclear expectations for employee behavior regarding IT resource usage. This can increase the likelihood of security incidents, legal risks, and data breaches due to misuse or accidental disclosure.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Sterling Silver, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational email.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of account compromise from credential theft (phishing, brute-force) by requiring a second verification factor.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logon:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all user logins to company computers and endpoints, especially those connected to the internal network.

    • Benefit: Provides an additional layer of security, preventing unauthorized access even if primary login credentials are stolen.

  3. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, formalize, and disseminate a comprehensive AUP to all employees. Ensure all new hires receive and acknowledge it during onboarding.

    • Benefit: Clearly defines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources, data, and networks, reducing accidental and intentional misuse, improving compliance, and establishing a basis for disciplinary action.

  4. Regularly Conduct Internal Network Scans and Vulnerability Assessments:

    • Action: While the current scan found no open ports, this is a single snapshot. Implement a regular schedule for internal network scanning and authenticated vulnerability assessments for all internal systems, including workstations and servers.

    • Benefit: Identifies misconfigurations, outdated software, and potential vulnerabilities that might not be visible from external, unauthenticated scans.
