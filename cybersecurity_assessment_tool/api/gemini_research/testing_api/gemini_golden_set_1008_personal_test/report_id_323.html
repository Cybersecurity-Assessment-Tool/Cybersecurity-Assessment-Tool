Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Skyward Bound based on an analysis of provided organizational data, network scan results, and pre-existing risk information. Our assessment identified several critical areas requiring immediate attention, particularly concerning Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation for computer access and the consistent application of security awareness training across the organization. An internal network scan targeting 127.0.0.1 revealed an open port 22 (commonly SSH), and a pre-existing high-severity vulnerability related to localhost exposure was noted. Addressing these findings comprehensively will significantly enhance Skyward Bound’s defensive capabilities against common cyber threats and strengthen its overall security posture.

Analysis of Security Controls

The questionnaire results indicate strong adherence to MFA for email and sensitive data systems, alongside the presence of an acceptable use policy. However, critical gaps were identified in:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: This represents a significant vulnerability. If user credentials are compromised, attackers could gain direct access to internal systems without an additional layer of authentication, increasing the risk of lateral movement and data breaches.

  • Insufficient Security Awareness Training: The absence of security awareness training for new employees and annual refresher training for all employees leaves the organization susceptible to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing), malware infections, and other human-factor vulnerabilities. A well-informed workforce is a critical defense mechanism.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Mandate and enforce MFA for all user authentications to organizational computers, servers, and endpoints.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access due to stolen, weak, or brute-forced passwords, adding a crucial layer of security.

  2. Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Develop and implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • Action: Conduct annual, mandatory refresher security awareness training for all existing employees to keep them informed about evolving threats and best practices.

    • Benefit: Enhances employee vigilance against social engineering, phishing, and other cyberattacks, creating a more resilient human firewall.

  3. Address Pre-existing "Localhost Exposed" Vulnerability:

    • Action: Immediately investigate the root cause, scope, and impact of the "Localhost Exposed" vulnerability. Given its critical severity (CVSS 10.0), this should be the highest priority.

    • Action: Implement necessary controls to restrict access to localhost services to only authorized processes and users. This may involve network segmentation, firewall rules, or service configuration changes.

    • Benefit: Prevents exploitation of a critical internal exposure that could lead to full system compromise or data exfiltration.

  4. Secure Open Port 22 on Internal Interface:

    • Action: Review the configuration of the SSH service running on port 22 on 127.0.0.1.

    • Action: Ensure strong authentication methods (e.g., key-based authentication with strong passphrases) are enforced, and disable password authentication. Implement principles of least privilege by restricting access to only necessary users and source IP addresses.

    • Action: If SSH access is not required on this local interface, disable the service to remove the potential attack vector.

    • Benefit: Reduces the internal attack surface and prevents unauthorized internal access to the system.

  5. Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Vulnerability Scans:

    • Action: Implement a schedule for regular, authenticated vulnerability scans across all internal and external assets, ensuring comprehensive coverage and version detection.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies outdated software, misconfigurations, and other vulnerabilities before they can be exploited by malicious actors.

  6. Maintain an Up-to-date Inventory of Assets and Services:

    • Action: Establish and continuously maintain a complete and current inventory of all network assets, open ports, and running services within the organization.

    • Benefit: Provides essential visibility and context crucial for effective risk management, vulnerability prioritization, and incident response.

Conclusion

Skyward Bound has demonstrated a foundational understanding of cybersecurity controls in some areas. However, the identified deficiencies in Multi-Factor Authentication for computer logins and comprehensive security awareness training, coupled with a critical pre-existing vulnerability ("Localhost Exposed") and an open internal port 22, indicate significant areas for immediate improvement. By prioritizing and diligently implementing the recommended actions, Skyward Bound can substantially elevate its security posture, mitigate identified risks, and better protect its valuable assets from the evolving threat landscape.
