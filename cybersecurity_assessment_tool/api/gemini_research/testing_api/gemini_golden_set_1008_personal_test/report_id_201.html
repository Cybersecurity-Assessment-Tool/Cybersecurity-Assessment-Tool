Executive Summary

This report provides a cybersecurity posture assessment for Orchid Isle, based on a security questionnaire, a network scan, and a review of existing risk data. The organization demonstrates good practices in some areas, such as requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins and sensitive data access, and providing initial security awareness training.

However, critical gaps have been identified:

  • Lack of MFA for email access, leaving a primary communication vector vulnerable to compromise.

  • Absence of a formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), which is foundational for defining expected employee conduct regarding IT resources.

  • Insufficient annual security awareness training for all employees, diminishing the effectiveness of the "human firewall" against evolving threats.

Most critically, the network scan revealed an open port 8080 on target 10.5.5.5 exposing a service with the alarming title "TOP SECRET DB". This finding directly contradicts an existing internal risk record which incorrectly deems this port "secured" with a CVSS score of 0.0. This severe discrepancy highlights a critical failure in risk assessment processes, asset inventory, or vulnerability management, leading to a false sense of security and posing an urgent, high-priority risk of potential data breach or unauthorized access. Immediate investigation and remediation are paramount.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve Orchid Isle’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions (Critical Risks)

  1. Urgent Investigation of Port 8080 "TOP SECRET DB":

    • Immediately identify the system hosting the "TOP SECRET DB" service on .

    • Determine the purpose, contents, and criticality of the data exposed.

    • Assess whether this exposure is intentional, authorized, and adequately secured. If not, immediately restrict external and internal unauthorized access to the port/service and take steps to properly secure the service.

    • If it is a test or legacy system, ensure it is properly decommissioned, isolated, or secured.

  2. Review and Rectify Risk Assessment Processes:

    • Conduct an urgent audit of all existing risk records, particularly those relating to network services and open ports, to identify other potential discrepancies.

    • Investigate why the internal record for Port 8080 was marked as "Secured" with a CVSS of 0.0 despite the active "TOP SECRET DB" service. This indicates a severe flaw in current validation procedures.

    • Implement robust procedures for validating risk assessments against real-world network data and regularly update asset inventories and vulnerability statuses.

    • Establish a clear escalation path for discrepancies found between internal risk records and active scan results.

Strategic Actions (High Risks)

  1. Implement MFA for Email: Immediately enable and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all email accounts across the organization. Prioritize user education and support during the rollout.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities and acceptable conduct when using company resources. Distribute it to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgements, and integrate it into onboarding processes and periodic reviews.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training Program: Implement a mandatory, recurring (at least annual) security awareness training program for all employees. The training should cover current threats, phishing recognition, secure browsing, data handling, and incident reporting procedures. Track completion rates and evaluate effectiveness.

By addressing these critical and high-priority recommendations, Orchid Isle can significantly enhance its overall cybersecurity posture and reduce its attack surface.
