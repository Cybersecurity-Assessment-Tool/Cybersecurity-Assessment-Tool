Recommendations

Based on the identified risks, the following prioritized recommendations are provided to enhance Top Tier’s cybersecurity posture:

  1. Immediate Action: Secure Exposed ’TOP SECRET DB’ (Critical)

    • Immediately investigate the service running on port 8080 on . Determine its legitimate purpose, the sensitivity of data it handles, and its authorized access requirements.

    • Restrict access to this port from external networks and, if possible, from all unauthorized internal networks immediately. Implement strict firewall rules and IP whitelisting for any absolutely necessary access.

    • If the database is required, implement robust authentication mechanisms, including mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), for all legitimate users.

    • If the database is not intended to be exposed, shut down the service, decommission the system, or move it to a secured, isolated segment of the network.

    • Update the internal risk assessment for Port 8080 and similar services to accurately reflect their current state and associated risks.

  2. Implement MFA for Email Access (High)

    • Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all organizational email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise through phishing, brute-force, or credential stuffing attacks.

    • Provide comprehensive user training on MFA enrollment, usage, and the importance of secure account practices.

  3. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (High)

    • Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all access attempts to systems, applications, or databases that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data.

    • Conduct an inventory of all sensitive data systems to ensure MFA is consistently applied as a mandatory control across the board.

  4. Review and Update Risk Management Processes

    • Establish a regular and frequent schedule for reviewing and updating existing risk assessments, particularly for external-facing services and critical internal assets.

    • Implement a formal process to correlate technical scan findings with documented risks to identify discrepancies and ensure the accuracy and currency of the risk register.

  5. Conduct Regular Security Audits

    • Implement a recurring schedule for both internal and external penetration testing and vulnerability scanning to proactively identify new or evolving security weaknesses.

    • Conduct periodic procedural audits to ensure that security policies (e.g., Acceptable Use Policy, Security Awareness Training) are effectively implemented, understood, and followed by all employees.

Conclusion

The assessment reveals several significant security weaknesses, with the exposed ’TOP SECRET DB’ on port 8080 and the widespread lack of MFA for critical systems being the most pressing concerns. The contradiction in the existing risk assessment regarding Port 8080 underscores a potential weakness in the organization’s risk validation processes. Addressing these issues with the provided recommendations will substantially improve Top Tier’s defensive posture against common cyber threats and reduce its overall attack surface. Continued vigilance, regular security reviews, and a proactive approach to security are crucial for maintaining a strong and resilient cybersecurity environment.
