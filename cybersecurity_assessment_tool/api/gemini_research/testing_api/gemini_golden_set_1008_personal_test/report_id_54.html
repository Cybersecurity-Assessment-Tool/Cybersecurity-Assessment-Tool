Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of , the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user logins to company computers, workstations, and servers, especially for devices accessing sensitive internal networks and data.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access resulting from compromised credentials, even if passwords are stolen.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities, defines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources (hardware, software, networks, internet), and details the consequences of policy violations. Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge this policy.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear behavioral guidelines, reduces the likelihood of misuse of company resources, and provides a framework for addressing security incidents and non-compliance.

  3. Establish a Robust Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory and ongoing security awareness training program. This should include initial training for all new employees during onboarding and annual refresher training for all staff. Key topics should cover phishing and social engineering, strong password practices, safe data handling, incident reporting procedures, and the importance of adhering to company security policies.

    • Benefit: Educates employees on prevalent cyber threats, cultivates a strong security-conscious culture, and significantly reduces the organization’s susceptibility to human-error-related security incidents.

  4. Verify and Address Unencrypted Web Services:

    • Action: Investigate the discrepancy between the pre-existing risk (unencrypted web server) and the current network scan findings (port 80 closed on ). Determine if this risk applies to other systems within the organization’s infrastructure.

    • If relevant to other systems: Identify any other internal or external web servers operating without encryption (e.g., HTTP on port 80 or 8080 without a forced redirect to HTTPS). For any such identified servers, implement HTTPS by acquiring and installing valid SSL/TLS certificates.

    • Benefit: Protects data in transit from eavesdropping, tampering, and man-in-the-middle attacks, ensuring confidentiality and integrity for web-based communications.
