Key Questionnaire Gaps

Based on the questionnaire, two significant gaps were identified:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Email accounts are frequently targeted and often serve as an initial access vector for attackers. The absence of MFA significantly increases the risk of account compromise.

  • Absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without an AUP, employees may lack clear guidelines on the appropriate use of organizational IT resources, potentially leading to misuse, data breaches, or compliance violations.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Immediately enable and enforce MFA for all organizational email accounts.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if credentials are stolen through phishing or other means.

    • Priority: High

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive AUP outlining acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources, data, and internet access. Distribute it to all employees and require acknowledgment. Integrate it into new employee onboarding.

    • Benefit: Provides clear expectations for employee behavior, reduces insider risks, and establishes a foundation for disciplinary action if policies are violated.

    • Priority: Medium

  3. Secure Exposed SSH Service:

    • Action:

      1. Review the necessity of exposing SSH externally. If remote access is required, consider implementing a VPN for access or restricting source IPs.

      2. Ensure strong authentication methods (e.g., key-based authentication, strong passwords if passwords are used) are enforced.

      3. Disable root login directly via SSH.

      4. Implement rate limiting and intrusion detection/prevention systems to defend against brute-force attacks.

      5. Keep the SSH server software and underlying operating system fully patched and up-to-date.

      6. Consider changing the default SSH port (though this is a minor security enhancement).

    • Benefit: Reduces the attack surface and mitigates risks associated with an exposed remote administration service.

    • Priority: Medium

  4. Conduct Regular Security Audits and Penetration Testing:

    • Action: Schedule periodic external and internal security audits, including penetration testing, to proactively identify vulnerabilities and assess the effectiveness of security controls.

    • Benefit: Provides continuous validation of the security posture and identifies new risks as the environment evolves.

    • Priority: Ongoing

Conclusion

This assessment highlights several areas where Phoenix Rising can significantly enhance its cybersecurity posture. Addressing the identified gaps, particularly the implementation of MFA for email and the establishment of an Acceptable Use Policy, along with securing exposed services like SSH, will substantially reduce the organization’s overall risk profile. A proactive approach to cybersecurity, combining technical controls with policy enforcement and regular training, is crucial for maintaining a strong defense against evolving threats.
