Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The questionnaire analysis identifies two critical areas requiring immediate attention:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: Multi-factor authentication is a fundamental security control to prevent unauthorized access, even if credentials are stolen. Its absence for computer logins significantly increases the risk of successful phishing, brute-force, or credential stuffing attacks leading to internal network compromise.

  • Absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): An AUP is crucial for setting clear expectations for employees regarding the use of organizational IT resources. Without it, there is a higher risk of misuse of company assets, data breaches, and non-compliance with regulatory requirements. It also creates ambiguity regarding acceptable online behavior and data handling.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and strengthen the overall cybersecurity posture of Maple Leaf Logistics, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing company computers and network resources.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of credential theft leading to unauthorized access, even if passwords are compromised.

    • Priority: Critical

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, approve, and implement a clear Acceptable Use Policy for all employees. This policy should define appropriate use of company IT assets, internet, email, and data handling practices.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces risks associated with employee misuse of resources, enhances data protection, and supports regulatory compliance.

    • Priority: High

  3. Address Technical Scan Data (Future Action):

    • Action: Ensure future network scan data is provided in a valid, parsable format. This will enable a comprehensive technical analysis of open ports, services, and potential vulnerabilities.

    • Benefit: Provides a complete picture of the external attack surface and helps identify patch management deficiencies or misconfigurations.

    • Priority: Medium (for future assessments)

  4. Provide Valid Existing Risk Data (Future Action):

    • Action: Provide accurate and complete data regarding pre-existing vulnerabilities or risk registers.

    • Benefit: Allows for a holistic view of the risk landscape and helps prioritize remediation efforts by correlating new findings with existing known issues.

    • Priority: Medium (for future assessments)

While the absence of usable network scan data and pre-existing risk information limits the scope of this assessment, the review of organizational security controls for Maple Leaf Logistics has identified two critical areas of concern: the lack of MFA for computer logins and the absence of an Acceptable Use Policy.
