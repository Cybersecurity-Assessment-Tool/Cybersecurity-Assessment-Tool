Executive Summary

This report details the findings from a cybersecurity posture assessment for Borealis Tech, incorporating organizational self-reported data, external network scan results, and a review of existing risk documentation. The assessment highlights critical security gaps, particularly concerning Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation and the exposure of potentially sensitive information on an internal network port. A significant discrepancy was identified where an internal scan revealed an open port 8080 hosting a service titled "TOP SECRET DB," directly contradicting a previous internal assessment that marked this port as "Secured" and a false positive. Immediate action is required to address these vulnerabilities and fortify the organization’s security controls.

Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The questionnaire responses reveal critical gaps in Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation:

  • MFA for Email (Q1): The absence of MFA for email access is a severe vulnerability. Email accounts are frequent targets for phishing and account compromise, and without MFA, a single compromised password can grant attackers access to sensitive communications and pivot points within the organization.

  • MFA for Computer Logins (Q2): Similarly, not requiring MFA for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to workstations and internal network resources. If an attacker gains valid credentials, they can potentially move laterally within the network without further authentication challenges.

While MFA is used for sensitive data systems (Q3), the lack of it for email and computer logins creates significant entry points for attackers. Positive responses for AUP and security awareness training (Q4, Q5, Q6) are commendable, but their effectiveness is reduced if fundamental authentication controls are missing.

Detailed Findings

The Nmap scan revealed the following critical information:

  • Open Port: 8080

  • Service Details: A service running on port 8080 was identified, which, through an ‘http-title‘ script, exposed the title "TOP SECRET DB".

This finding is highly concerning. The presence of an open port displaying a title explicitly stating "TOP SECRET DB" strongly indicates exposure of sensitive data or a critical internal system. While the scan did not identify specific product versions, the title itself is sufficient to warrant immediate investigation and remediation.

Risk Correlation and Discrepancy Analysis

The most alarming finding is the direct contradiction between the recent network scan and the organization’s existing risk documentation. Input_3_Current_Risks_JSON indicated that "Port 8080 is confirmed secure and false positive" with a severity of 0.0. However, the Nmap scan (Input_1_Network_Scan_JSON) clearly shows port 8080 open on 10.5.5.5 with the HTTP title "TOP SECRET DB".

This discrepancy suggests:

  • The prior assessment was erroneous or based on outdated information.

  • The system configuration changed, exposing sensitive data, without a corresponding update to the risk register.

  • The "TOP SECRET DB" title itself indicates critical information is being exposed, regardless of previous assessments.

This contradiction elevates the risk associated with port 8080 to Critical and also raises concerns about the reliability of the existing risk management processes.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and strengthen Borealis Tech’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions (Critical Risks)

  • Investigate and Secure Port 8080 (10.5.5.5):

    • Immediately investigate the service running on port 8080 on host 10.5.5.5.

    • Determine the nature of the "TOP SECRET DB" and the type of data it contains.

    • If the service is not intended to be publicly accessible, apply immediate firewall rules to block access.

    • If the service is internal, ensure it is properly authenticated and authorized, and that sensitive information is not exposed in service banners or titles.

    • Change the service title to remove any indication of sensitive content.

  • Re-evaluate Risk Management Process:

    • Conduct an urgent review of the process by which risks, especially those marked as "false positives" or "secured," are validated and updated.

    • Ensure future risk assessments are correlated with up-to-date technical scans and vulnerability reports.

High Priority Recommendations (High Risks)

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all employee email accounts. This should be a top priority to protect against phishing and credential stuffing attacks.

    • Provide clear instructions and support for employees during the rollout.

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all computer logins, especially for devices accessing sensitive internal networks or data.

    • Explore solutions like smart cards, FIDO2 keys, or biometric authentication integrated with directory services.

General Security Enhancements

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning: Implement a regular schedule for internal and external vulnerability scanning to proactively identify and address security weaknesses.

  • Security Awareness Training Reinforcement: While security awareness training is conducted, reinforce the importance of identifying and reporting suspicious activity, especially in light of potential open ports or exposed sensitive titles.

  • Incident Response Plan: Ensure a well-defined and tested incident response plan is in place to handle potential security breaches, especially those involving sensitive data exposure.

  • Asset Inventory and Management: Maintain an accurate and up-to-date inventory of all network assets, including their purpose, location, and owners. This will help in understanding the context of services like the one on port 8080.
