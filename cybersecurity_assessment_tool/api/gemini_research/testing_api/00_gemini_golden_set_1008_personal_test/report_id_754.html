Executive Summary

Overall, Hearth & Home demonstrates some foundational security practices, such as an acceptable use policy and annual security awareness training. However, significant gaps were identified, primarily concerning the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins and sensitive data systems, and a lack of dedicated security awareness training for new employees. This finding must be interpreted with the understanding that the scan was limited to the provided target IP and could not provide an exhaustive view of all external assets. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were reported in the provided data.

These identified gaps represent critical risks that could be exploited, leading to unauthorized access, data breaches, or other adverse security incidents. Immediate attention and remediation efforts are strongly recommended to enhance the organization’s overall security posture.

Key Findings from Questionnaire

The following critical gaps and high risks were identified from the questionnaire responses:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: Not requiring Multi-Factor Authentication for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access if credentials are stolen or compromised.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Similar to computer logins, the absence of MFA for sensitive data systems poses a critical risk to data confidentiality and integrity.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: While annual training is conducted for all employees, the lack of initial security awareness training for new employees means they may not be adequately prepared to identify and mitigate common cyber threats from day one.

Recommendations

To address the identified gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Hearth & Home, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all user logins to company computers and internal systems.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are stolen, forming a stronger defense against credential-based attacks.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Enforce MFA for accessing all systems containing or processing sensitive organizational or customer data.

    • Benefit: Provides a critical layer of defense against data breaches and unauthorized data access, safeguarding confidential information.

  • Establish Onboarding Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Develop and implement mandatory cybersecurity awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new employees. This training should cover common threats like phishing, malware, social engineering, and proper data handling.

    • Benefit: Equips new hires with essential security knowledge from day one, reducing their susceptibility to cyberattacks and improving overall human firewall effectiveness.

  • Regularly Review and Update Security Policies:

    • Action: Conduct an annual review of the Acceptable Use Policy and other security policies to ensure they remain current with evolving threats and organizational practices.

    • Benefit: Maintains policy relevance and effectiveness in guiding employee behavior and organizational security, adapting to new challenges.

  • Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Technical Assessments:

    • Action: Perform regular external and internal vulnerability scans, penetration tests, and configuration audits across the entire IT infrastructure.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies technical vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and potential attack vectors before they can be exploited. This is especially important given the lack of findings in the initial external scan, which may have been limited in scope.

Conclusion

Hearth & Home has a foundation of cybersecurity practices, but critical areas require immediate attention. By implementing the recommended Multi-Factor Authentication controls and enhancing security awareness training, particularly for new employees, the organization can significantly mature its security posture and reduce its overall risk profile. Ongoing vigilance and continuous security improvements are essential in today’s dynamic threat landscape.
