Analysis of Controls

The questionnaire results reveal critical vulnerabilities in Oasis Wellness’s security posture:

  • Lack of MFA for Email and Computer Access: The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication for both email and computer logins represents a severe risk. Phishing attacks are a primary vector for credential compromise, and without MFA, a stolen password is often sufficient for an attacker to gain access, leading to data breaches, ransomware, or business email compromise. While MFA is used for sensitive data systems, the wider scope of email and general computer access remains exposed.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A formal AUP is crucial for defining acceptable conduct regarding the use of organizational IT resources. Its absence can lead to inconsistent employee behavior, increased risk of data leakage, malware infections, and legal liabilities. Without clear guidelines, employees may unknowingly engage in risky activities.

  • Positive Security Awareness Training: It is commendable that Oasis Wellness conducts security awareness training for both new and existing employees annually. This is a critical component of a strong security culture and helps mitigate human-factor risks.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Oasis Wellness, the following recommendations are provided, prioritized by severity and impact.

Immediate Priority Recommendations (Critical Risks)

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all email accounts.

    • Action: Implement MFA for all computer logins (endpoints, servers).

    • Benefit: This is the single most effective control against credential theft and unauthorized access.

  • Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a clear, comprehensive AUP outlining appropriate use of all organizational IT assets, internet, email, and data handling.

    • Action: Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP. Integrate it into new hire onboarding and annual reviews.

    • Benefit: Reduces insider risk, improves compliance, and fosters responsible employee behavior.

High Priority Recommendations (Visibility & Proactive Security)

  • Conduct Comprehensive Network and Vulnerability Scanning:

    • Action: Perform a professional external penetration test and vulnerability assessment against the organization’s public IP space () and web assets.

    • Action: Initiate internal vulnerability scans to identify weaknesses within the corporate network.

    • Benefit: Gain crucial visibility into the actual technical attack surface, identify outdated software, misconfigurations, and exploitable vulnerabilities that are currently unknown.

  • Establish a Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Action: Once vulnerabilities are identified, establish a process for tracking, prioritizing, and remediating them. This includes regular patching and configuration reviews.

    • Benefit: Ensures a continuous cycle of security improvement, reducing the window of opportunity for attackers.

General Security Enhancements

  • Regular Security Awareness Training Reinforcement: Continue and expand existing security awareness training, incorporating findings from current threats and internal incidents (if any).

  • Develop an Incident Response Plan: Create a formal plan detailing steps to take before, during, and after a cybersecurity incident. This includes roles, responsibilities, communication strategies, and technical procedures.
