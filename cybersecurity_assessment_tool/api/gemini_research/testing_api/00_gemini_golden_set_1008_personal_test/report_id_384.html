Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance Crestview Analytics’ cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Enable and strictly enforce MFA for all organizational email accounts immediately. This critical control dramatically reduces the risk of email account compromise, even in cases where credentials are stolen through phishing or other means.

  2. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear and comprehensive AUP that outlines the acceptable use of all company IT resources, data, and internet access.

    • Ensure all employees review, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally on an annual basis or upon policy updates.

  3. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, encompassing both new hires and existing staff.

    • The training should be dynamic, covering current threat landscapes (e.g., advanced phishing techniques, ransomware, social engineering), secure browsing habits, data handling best practices, and clear incident reporting procedures.

  4. Conduct a Comprehensive External Network Scan and Vulnerability Assessment:

    • Immediately perform a thorough network and vulnerability scan of all externally facing IP addresses, domains, and web applications. This is crucial to identify open ports, running services, outdated software versions, and potential vulnerabilities.

    • Prioritize the remediation of any identified critical or high-severity vulnerabilities.

    • Implement a regular schedule for such scans (e.g., quarterly) to maintain an up-to-date understanding of the organization’s evolving attack surface.

  5. Obtain and Review Existing Vulnerability Data:

    • Provide accurate and parseable data regarding any known pre-existing vulnerabilities or findings from previous assessments. This information is vital for a holistic risk assessment and to track the organization’s historical security posture and remediation efforts.
