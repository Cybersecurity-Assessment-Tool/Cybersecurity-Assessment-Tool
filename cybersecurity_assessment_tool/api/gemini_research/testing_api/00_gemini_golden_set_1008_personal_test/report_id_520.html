Recommendations

To enhance Maple Leaf Logistics’ cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a clear, comprehensive AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources, data, and communication systems.

    • Ensure the policy covers areas such as password hygiene, data handling, internet usage, software installation, and reporting security incidents.

    • Distribute the AUP to all employees and require acknowledgment of understanding and compliance.

    • Review and update the AUP annually or as organizational policies and threats evolve.

  2. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Implement a mandatory security awareness training program as part of the onboarding process for all new hires.

    • The training should cover fundamental cybersecurity concepts, common threats (e.g., phishing, malware), company policies (including the AUP), and incident reporting procedures.

    • Utilize engaging and interactive training materials to ensure comprehension and retention.

  3. Implement Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, regardless of their tenure.

    • Refresh key security concepts, update employees on emerging threats and attack techniques, and reinforce the importance of vigilance.

    • Consider phishing simulation exercises to test and improve employee awareness and response.

    • Tailor training content to be relevant to different roles and responsibilities within the organization.

  4. Maintain Strong Network Perimeter Security:

    • While the scan of showed no open ports, continuous vigilance is key. Regularly review firewall rules and network configurations to ensure only necessary ports are open and services exposed.

    • Implement Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems (IDPS) and regularly review logs for suspicious activity.

  5. Continue Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Best Practices:

    • Commendable use of MFA. Continue to enforce and expand MFA where appropriate, especially for remote access, cloud services, and privileged accounts.

Conclusion

Maple Leaf Logistics has a solid foundation with MFA implementation, but there are critical, easily remediable gaps in policy and training that expose the organization to significant human-element risks. Addressing the lack of an Acceptable Use Policy and comprehensive Security Awareness Training for both new and existing employees will significantly improve the organization’s overall security posture and resilience against common cyber threats. The positive technical scan results for the specified target are an encouraging sign of effective network segmentation or perimeter control for that particular asset.
