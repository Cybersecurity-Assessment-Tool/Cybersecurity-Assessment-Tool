Executive Summary

The organization demonstrates robust implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and initial security awareness training for new employees. However, a significant vulnerability lies in the absence of mandatory annual security awareness training for all personnel, which substantially elevates human-factor risks. Technically, an open HTTP port was identified on an internal device (172.16.0.1), requiring further investigation to ascertain its purpose and security state. A unique risk entry concerning reporting instructions was also noted. This report outlines specific recommendations to mitigate these findings and bolster the overall security framework.

Cinder & Ash exhibits a strong foundation in implementing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across critical access points, which is a commendable security practice. Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Cinder & Ash.

Critical Recommendations

  • Implement Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Develop and deploy a comprehensive security awareness training program for all employees, conducted at least once per year.

    • The curriculum should cover a range of critical topics including, but not limited to, phishing recognition, social engineering tactics, strong password hygiene, secure data handling procedures, and incident reporting protocols.

    • Establish a system to track employee completion rates and conduct periodic simulated phishing campaigns to assess and improve employee vigilance.

Important Recommendations

  • Investigate and Secure Open Internal HTTP Service:

    • Immediately identify the specific application, device, or service operating on port 80 of 172.16.0.1.

    • Assess the business necessity for this service to be active and exposed on the internal network.

    • Implement appropriate security measures, which may include strong authentication, network-level access controls (e.g., firewall rules to restrict source IPs), ensuring the software is fully patched and up-to-date, and enforcing secure configuration standards.

    • If the service is not essential or poses undue risk, consider disabling it or moving it to a more isolated and secure network segment.

    • If it is a management interface, ensure it is protected with strong, unique credentials, Multi-Factor Authentication (if supported), and only accessible from authorized administrative workstations.

Conclusion

Cinder & Ash has laid a solid foundation for its cybersecurity posture, particularly through its commitment to MFA and initial employee training. By diligently addressing the identified critical gap in annual security awareness training and proactively investigating the internal open HTTP service, the organization can significantly enhance its resilience against prevalent cyber threats. Continuous monitoring, regular security assessments, and a proactive approach to risk management will be paramount to maintaining and improving a robust and adaptive cybersecurity posture.
