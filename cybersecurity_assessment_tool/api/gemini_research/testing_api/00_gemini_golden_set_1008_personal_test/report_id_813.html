Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Symmetry Architecture based on a review of organizational data, existing risk assessments, and a recent network scan. While strong controls are noted for Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), critical gaps were identified in foundational security awareness training and policy enforcement, specifically regarding an Employee Acceptable Use Policy. A particularly concerning finding from the technical scan is an open Port 8080 on 10.5.5.5 explicitly identifying its service as a "TOP SECRET DB," which directly contradicts an internal assessment (Input 3) categorizing this port as "secure" and a "false positive" with zero severity. This discrepancy highlights a significant vulnerability management and risk assessment gap. Immediate attention is required to address these policy deficiencies and thoroughly investigate the exposed port to prevent potential data breaches and re-evaluate risk assessment processes.

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to mitigate the identified risks and significantly improve Symmetry Architecture’s overall cybersecurity posture.

Policy and Training Enhancements

  • Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, concise, and comprehensive AUP that explicitly outlines acceptable use of all company IT assets, networks, and data.

    • Ensure the AUP covers internet usage, email, software installation, data handling, password policies, and procedures for reporting suspicious activities.

    • Mandate signed acknowledgment of the AUP from all employees, ensuring they understand their responsibilities.

  • Establish Mandatory New Employee Security Awareness Training:

    • Implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires as part of their onboarding process.

    • Training should cover foundational security topics such as phishing identification, malware prevention, strong password practices, data classification, physical security, and incident reporting.

  • Implement Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Conduct mandatory, recurrent security awareness training for all employees at least once per year.

    • Update training content regularly to reflect the latest threat landscapes, evolving attack techniques, and organizational policy changes.

    • Utilize engaging and interactive formats (e.g., workshops, simulated phishing campaigns) to reinforce learning and measure effectiveness.

Technical and Process Improvements

  • Immediate Investigation and Remediation of Port 8080 on 10.5.5.5:

    • Urgent Action: Immediately isolate and investigate the service running on port 8080 on 10.5.5.5.

    • Determine Purpose and Sensitivity: Confirm the exact nature of the "TOP SECRET DB," the type of data it holds, and its intended function.

    • Access Control Review: If this service is intended to be exposed, ensure robust authentication, authorization, and encryption are in place. Strictly limit network access to only necessary internal hosts. If it’s not intended to be exposed, block access immediately.

    • Network Segmentation: Ensure this system is properly segmented and isolated from general user networks and external access points.

    • Vulnerability Scan & Penetration Test: Conduct a focused vulnerability scan and penetration test specifically targeting this service to identify potential exploit paths.

  • Review and Update Risk Assessment and Vulnerability Management Processes:

    • Re-evaluate Port 8080 Risk: Conduct an urgent and thorough re-assessment of the "Port 8080 Secured" finding from the existing risk register. Investigate why it was previously considered secure and a false positive despite the current scan revealing a service identifying as "TOP SECRET DB".

    • Process Improvement: Review and update the organization’s procedures for identifying, assessing, and documenting risks and vulnerabilities. Ensure that external scan results are regularly correlated with internal assessments and that discrepancies trigger immediate investigations.

    • Asset Inventory Accuracy: Ensure that the asset inventory is regularly updated and accurately reflects network services, their configurations, and their exposure levels.
