Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The organization demonstrates a strong commitment to multi-factor authentication (MFA) across critical access points and has established an acceptable use policy and initial security awareness training for new employees. However, a critical gap was identified:

  • Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: The organization does not conduct annual security awareness training for all employees. This is a significant risk, as cybersecurity threats evolve rapidly, and without regular updates, employees may become susceptible to new phishing techniques, social engineering attacks, and other emerging threats.

Recommendations

Based on the available information and analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Common Ground’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions

  • Implement Annual Security Awareness Training: Develop and deploy a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats such as phishing, ransomware, social engineering, password hygiene, and data handling best practices. Regular refreshers or micro-trainings throughout the year are also beneficial.

High-Priority Actions (Post Data Availability)

  • Conduct a Comprehensive Network Scan and Analysis: Obtain valid network scan data for the external IP (or other relevant assets) and conduct a thorough analysis. Identify open ports, services, versions, and configurations. Prioritize patching or securing any identified vulnerabilities, especially those exposing critical services to the internet.

  • Establish a Robust Vulnerability Management Program: Once scan data is available, integrate it into a continuous vulnerability management process. Regularly scan, identify, assess, report, and remediate vulnerabilities across all systems.

  • Review and Maintain a Current Risk Register: Implement or enhance a process for tracking, assessing, and mitigating organizational risks. Regularly review and update the risk register to ensure all known vulnerabilities and threats are documented and addressed.

General Best Practices

  • Regularly Update and Patch Systems: Ensure all operating systems, applications, and network devices are kept up-to-date with the latest security patches to protect against known vulnerabilities.

  • Implement Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR): Deploy EDR solutions on all endpoints to provide advanced threat detection, prevention, and response capabilities.

  • Maintain Strong Backup and Recovery Procedures: Regularly back up critical data and test recovery procedures to ensure business continuity in case of a cyber incident.

  • Develop an Incident Response Plan: Create and regularly test a comprehensive incident response plan to ensure the organization can effectively detect, contain, eradicate, recover from, and learn from security incidents.

Conclusion

While Common Ground has implemented several foundational security controls, particularly around MFA, the current assessment highlights a critical need for regular security awareness training for all employees. Furthermore, the inability to perform a full technical and existing risk assessment due to data issues means that potential technical vulnerabilities and a comprehensive view of the risk landscape remain unquantified. Addressing the identified training gap and obtaining complete data for future assessments are paramount next steps for strengthening the organization’s cybersecurity posture.
