Executive Summary

This report outlines the findings from a cybersecurity posture assessment for Astraeus Aerospace. The assessment combined an analysis of organizational security controls through a questionnaire, a review of internal network scan results, and an evaluation of existing risk documentation.

The findings reveal several critical deficiencies in foundational security controls, notably concerning the adoption of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and the implementation of a robust security awareness training program. A significant and critical discrepancy was identified pertaining to an internal service on Port 8080. This port, previously deemed secure, was found advertising a "TOP SECRET DB," presenting a major potential risk. Immediate investigation and remediation are strongly recommended to mitigate these identified vulnerabilities and enhance Astraeus Aerospace’s overall security posture.

Recommendations

To effectively address the identified critical gaps and risks, the following actionable recommendations are provided, categorized by priority and timeline.

Immediate Actions (Within 1 Week)

  • Urgent Investigation of Port 8080: Immediately investigate the service running on :8080. Determine its true nature, the data it handles, its accessibility, and the underlying reason for the "TOP SECRET DB" title.

    • If it is a sensitive system, isolate it from the network and implement robust authentication, authorization, and encryption controls.

    • If it is a non-sensitive system with a misleading title, reconfigure the service to remove any deceptive or sensitive-sounding information.

    • Disregard Previous Assessment: The previous assessment deeming Port 8080 as "secure" or a "false positive" must be considered invalid and updated based on this critical finding.

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Begin the immediate deployment and mandatory enforcement of Multi-Factor Authentication for all organizational email accounts.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Prioritize and deploy Multi-Factor Authentication for all systems that handle, store, or process sensitive organizational data.

Short-Term Actions (Within 1-3 Months)

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines acceptable employee behavior regarding company IT assets, data handling, internet usage, and email communication. Distribute this policy to all employees and obtain signed acknowledgments.

  • Establish Annual Security Awareness Training Program: Develop and roll out a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats, organizational policies, and best practices to maintain a high level of security awareness.

Long-Term Actions (Ongoing)

  • Regular Security Audits and Vulnerability Assessments: Establish a schedule for regular internal and external security audits, vulnerability assessments, and penetration testing to proactively identify and remediate security weaknesses.

  • Continuous Policy Review and Updates: Periodically review and update all cybersecurity policies, including the AUP and MFA enforcement guidelines, to ensure they remain relevant, effective, and align with the evolving threat landscape and regulatory requirements.

  • Security Culture Reinforcement: Foster a strong security culture through ongoing communication, reminders, and leadership support to ensure cybersecurity remains a top priority for all employees.
