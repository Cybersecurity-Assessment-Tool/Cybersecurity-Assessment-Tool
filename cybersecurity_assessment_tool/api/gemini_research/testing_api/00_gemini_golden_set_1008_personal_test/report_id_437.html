Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The questionnaire highlights significant deficiencies in foundational security practices, presenting potential high-risk vulnerabilities:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): The absence of MFA for email and sensitive data systems is a critical vulnerability. Compromised credentials could lead to unauthorized access, data breaches, and significant business disruption.

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of appropriate usage guidelines for IT resources, increasing risks of misuse, compliance violations, and data leakage.

  • Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the lack of annual refresher training means the workforce may not be kept current with evolving threats and best practices, making them more susceptible to social engineering attacks.

This finding is particularly concerning when correlated with pre-existing risk data, suggesting a systemic exposure.

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve Vanguard Heritage’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions (High/Critical Severity Risks)

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Immediately enable and enforce MFA for all email access across the organization.

    • Implement MFA for all sensitive data systems, prioritizing deployment based on data classification and potential impact of compromise.

  • Secure or Disable Exposed RDP Services:

    • For 10.10.10.51 and 10.10.10.50, conduct an immediate assessment to determine if RDP access is absolutely necessary.

    • If RDP is required, restrict access to trusted source IPs, enforce strong, complex passwords, and implement robust account lockout policies.

    • Implement a secure gateway or VPN requirement for all RDP connections, ensuring authentication and encryption occur before reaching the RDP server.

    • Consider disabling RDP entirely on hosts where remote access is not critical to operations.

    • Investigate the discrepancy in RDP host IPs (10.10.10.50 vs. 10.10.10.51) to confirm the full scope of RDP exposure and ensure all instances are secured.

Strategic Improvements (Medium Severity Risks)

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines the proper use of company IT resources, data handling procedures, and security expectations for all employees.

    • Ensure all employees review, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally as part of their onboarding and annual review process.

  • Establish a Robust Annual Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, supplementing the new-hire training.

    • Training should be engaging and cover current threats such as phishing, social engineering, password best practices, incident reporting, and data protection principles.

    • Consider incorporating phishing simulations and other interactive training methods to reinforce learning and evaluate effectiveness.

Conclusion

Vanguard Heritage faces significant cybersecurity challenges, particularly related to exposed services and fundamental control gaps. Addressing the immediate risks of exposed RDP services and implementing Multi-Factor Authentication are paramount to reducing the organization’s attack surface. Concurrently, strategic improvements in security policies and ongoing employee training will build a more robust and resilient security posture. Continuous monitoring and regular security assessments are strongly recommended to maintain an effective defense against evolving cyber threats and ensure long-term security.
