Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of organizational controls and technical scan results, the following risks have been identified:

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Without MFA, email accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks. Compromised email can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data exfiltration, and further network intrusion. Critical
Lack of MFA for Computer Login Without MFA for computer logins, unauthorized access to internal systems is significantly easier, increasing the risk of lateral movement and privilege escalation by attackers. Critical
Outdated Nginx Server The Nginx 1.18.0 server is End-of-Life and no longer receives security updates. This exposes the service to numerous known vulnerabilities that can be exploited for unauthorized access, denial of service, or data compromise. Critical
Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Lack of an AUP means employees may not be aware of appropriate conduct regarding company assets and data, leading to misuse, unintentional data exposure, or compliance violations. High
Inadequate Security Awareness Training The absence of security awareness training for new and existing employees leaves the organization vulnerable to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing, pretexting) and general human error, which are leading causes of security breaches. High

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Iron Bridge Legal. These should be prioritized based on their severity and potential impact.

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all email accounts and computer logins across the organization.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if credentials are stolen.

  • Upgrade and Patch Nginx Server:

    • Action: Immediately plan and execute an upgrade of the Nginx server on to the latest stable version (e.g., 1.26.x or newer). Ensure a proper testing and rollback plan is in place.

    • Benefit: Addresses known vulnerabilities, improves performance, and ensures ongoing security support. Consider migrating to a supported web server if Nginx is not critical.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, implement, and communicate a clear AUP to all employees. Obtain signed acknowledgments.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines for using company resources, reducing insider threats and accidental policy violations.

  • Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Develop and implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during onboarding.

    • Action: Conduct annual refresher security awareness training for all employees to keep them informed about current threats and best practices. Include simulated phishing exercises.

    • Benefit: Educates employees on how to identify and respond to threats, making them a stronger line of defense against social engineering and other attacks.
