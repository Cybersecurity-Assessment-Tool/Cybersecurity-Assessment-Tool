Analysis of Security Controls

The organization demonstrates good practice in establishing an Acceptable Use Policy and conducting security awareness training for both new and existing employees. However, a significant vulnerability exists due to the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access and computer login. These are critical entry points, and their protection solely by single-factor authentication (passwords) dramatically increases the risk of unauthorized access due to credential theft or brute-force attacks. While MFA is implemented for sensitive data systems, the absence of it at the perimeter and workstation level undermines this control.

Recommendations

Based on the assessment, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions (Critical)

  • Urgent Investigation of Port 8080:

    • Immediately verify the nature of the service running on Port 8080 on host .

    • Determine if the "TOP SECRET DB" title accurately reflects the content or function of the service.

    • If sensitive data or systems are indeed exposed, immediately restrict external access to this port and apply appropriate security controls (e.g., strong authentication, encryption, access control lists).

    • Revise the internal risk assessment for Port 8080 to accurately reflect its current exposure and potential impact.

High Priority Actions

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts, especially for administrative and executive personnel.

  • Implement MFA for Computer Login:

    • Implement MFA for logging into all corporate workstations, laptops, and servers. This should apply to both local and remote access.

General Security Enhancements

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning: Conduct regular external and internal vulnerability scans to proactively identify and address exposures.

  • Comprehensive Risk Assessment Review: Review and update the organization’s overall risk assessment methodology and inventory to ensure all identified assets and potential threats are accurately categorized and mitigated.

  • Security Policy Enforcement: Continuously monitor and enforce security policies to ensure compliance and effective risk reduction.
