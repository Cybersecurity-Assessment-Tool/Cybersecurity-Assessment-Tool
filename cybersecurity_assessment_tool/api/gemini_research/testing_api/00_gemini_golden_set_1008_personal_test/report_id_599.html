Executive Overview

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Nexus Dynamics, based on a review of organizational data, existing risk assessments, and a technical network scan. The assessment reveals several critical areas requiring immediate attention, primarily concerning multi-factor authentication (MFA) adoption, employee security awareness training, and a critical internal exposure.

The technical scan identified an open SSH port on the localhost, which, when correlated with existing risk data, is classified as a critical exposure (CVSS 10.0). Furthermore, significant gaps in MFA implementation across email and computer logins, alongside a complete absence of regular security awareness training, present high-priority risks that could lead to unauthorized access, data breaches, and compromise of organizational systems. Addressing these foundational security controls is paramount to improving the overall security posture.

Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The review of security control questions highlights several significant weaknesses:

  • Lack of MFA for Email and Computer Logins: Critical services like email and general computer access are not protected by MFA. This significantly increases the risk of account compromise through phishing, brute-force attacks, or stolen credentials. While MFA is used for sensitive data systems, its absence in more common access points creates a substantial attack surface.

  • Insufficient Security Awareness Training: The organization does not conduct security awareness training for new employees or annually for all employees. This lack of continuous education leaves employees vulnerable to social engineering attacks, phishing, and other human-centric threats, making them potential weak links in the security chain.

Correlation and Impact

The critical risks identified from the questionnaire (lack of MFA and security awareness training) create significant attack vectors for external adversaries. Compromised credentials due to these gaps could lead to unauthorized access to internal systems and sensitive data.

The technical finding of an open SSH port on directly correlates with the pre-existing risk "Localhost Exposed" with a CVSS score of 10.0. This indicates a severe internal exposure that could be exploited if an attacker gains initial access (potentially through one of the aforementioned MFA/training gaps) or if the localhost is unexpectedly accessible from other network segments or even externally. The critical severity underscores the potential for system-level compromise or privilege escalation.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of Nexus Dynamics.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Systems:

    • Email Access: Immediately implement mandatory MFA for all employee email accounts.

    • Computer Logins: Roll out MFA for all corporate computer logins to protect against stolen credentials.

    • Scope Expansion: Review all other critical internal and external systems to ensure MFA is enabled wherever possible.

  2. Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • New Hires: Implement mandatory security awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new employees.

    • Annual Training: Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, covering topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, and data handling best practices.

    • Phishing Simulations: Consider regular simulated phishing campaigns to test employee vigilance and reinforce training.

  3. Investigate and Remediate Localhost Exposure ( Port 22):

    • Assess External Exposure: Verify that the interface and its services (specifically SSH on port 22) are not exposed externally or to unauthorized internal network segments.

    • Review Justification: Determine the necessity of SSH running on localhost. If it is required, ensure it is configured with strong authentication (e.g., key-based authentication, disabled root login, restricted access), and logging is enabled.

    • Patching and Updates: Ensure the SSH service and underlying operating system are fully patched and up-to-date to mitigate known vulnerabilities.

    • Segmentation: If the localhost refers to an internal server, ensure proper network segmentation is in place to limit lateral movement if it were to be compromised.
