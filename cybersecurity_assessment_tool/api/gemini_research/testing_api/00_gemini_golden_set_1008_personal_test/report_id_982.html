Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The review of security controls highlights several critical areas requiring immediate attention:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: Not requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

  • Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of a formal AUP leaves employees without clear guidelines on acceptable use of company IT assets, potentially leading to misuse, data breaches, or compliance violations.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees often pose a higher risk without initial security training, making them susceptible to phishing, social engineering, and other common attacks.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for All Computer Logins:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all user accounts accessing company computers and systems.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access, even if primary credentials are stolen.

  2. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a clear, concise AUP outlining the proper use of company IT assets, data, and network resources. Ensure all employees read and sign the policy annually.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear expectations for employee behavior, reduces misuse of resources, and provides a basis for disciplinary action if policies are violated.

  3. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Integrate a dedicated security awareness training module into the onboarding process for all new hires before they gain access to company systems.

    • Benefit: Equips new employees with essential cybersecurity knowledge from day one, reducing their susceptibility to common attack vectors like phishing and social engineering.

  4. Conduct Regular Security Assessments:

    • Action: Perform periodic external and internal network vulnerability scans and penetration tests to identify and remediate potential technical weaknesses.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies vulnerabilities before they can be exploited by malicious actors.
