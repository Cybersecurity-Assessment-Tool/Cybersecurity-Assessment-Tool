Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Urban Jungle Planning based on an analysis of provided organizational data, a self-assessment questionnaire, and network scan results. The overall posture reveals several strengths, particularly in security awareness training and MFA for sensitive systems, but also highlights critical areas requiring immediate attention. Key findings include the lack of multi-factor authentication for computer logins and the absence of an employee acceptable use policy. While no external vulnerabilities were identified via the provided scan data, these internal control gaps present significant risks. Recommendations are provided to address these identified weaknesses and enhance the organization’s security stance.

Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of the organizational data, security control review, and technical scan results, the following risks have been identified.

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Computer Login Without Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins, unauthorized individuals could gain access to internal systems more easily if they compromise user credentials through phishing or other attacks. This significantly increases the risk of lateral movement and data breaches. High
Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) An organization lacking an AUP exposes itself to increased legal, regulatory, and security risks. Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and strengthen the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all Computer Logins:

    • Action: Evaluate and deploy an MFA solution (e.g., hardware tokens, authenticator apps, biometrics) for all user logins to company computers and endpoints.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if passwords are compromised.

    • Priority: Critical

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines permissible and prohibited uses of company IT resources, data, and networks. Ensure it covers data handling, internet usage, software installation, and device usage.

    • Action: Distribute the AUP to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgments, and integrate it into new employee onboarding and annual security awareness training.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear expectations for employee behavior, reduces the likelihood of misuse, and provides a basis for disciplinary action if policies are violated.

    • Priority: High

  3. Conduct a Comprehensive External Vulnerability Scan and Penetration Test:

    • Action: While the current scan data provided no findings, it is highly recommended to perform a thorough external vulnerability scan and, subsequently, a penetration test by an independent third party. This ensures a complete understanding of the external attack surface.

    • Benefit: Identifies potential entry points for attackers, uncovers misconfigurations, and validates the effectiveness of perimeter defenses.

    • Priority: Medium

Conclusion

Urban Jungle Planning has established a foundation of security awareness and MFA for sensitive data, which are commendable efforts. However, the identified gaps in MFA for computer access and the absence of an Acceptable Use Policy represent significant internal risks that could be exploited. Addressing these critical areas through the recommended actions will substantially enhance the organization’s defense against common cyber threats and foster a more secure operational environment. Continuous monitoring, regular security assessments, and ongoing employee training are essential for maintaining a robust cybersecurity posture in the evolving threat landscape.
