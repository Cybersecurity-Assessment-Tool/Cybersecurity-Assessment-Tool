Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Evergreen Alliance based on provided organizational data, security questionnaire responses, and a limited network scan. The assessment highlights several critical gaps in security controls, including the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins, a lack of an employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and insufficient security awareness training for new employees. A network scan on the target identified an open port 22, but crucial service and version details were missing, preventing a comprehensive vulnerability analysis for this service. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were reported by the organization. Comprehensive recommendations are provided to address these findings and enhance the overall security posture.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the cybersecurity posture of Evergreen Alliance, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all computer logins across the organization, including workstations and servers.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if credentials are stolen or compromised, adding an essential layer of security.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft and enforce a comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that clearly outlines expectations for appropriate use of company resources, data, and IT systems.

    • Benefit: Sets clear guidelines for employee conduct, minimizes insider threats, reduces legal and compliance risks, and ensures responsible use of company assets.

  3. Initiate Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Establish a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires, ideally as part of their onboarding process, covering topics such as phishing, password hygiene, data handling, and company policies.

    • Benefit: Equips new employees with essential security knowledge from day one, reducing their susceptibility to social engineering attacks and preventing accidental security incidents.

  4. Investigate and Secure Open Port 22:

    • Action: Conduct a more detailed investigation or manual inspection to identify the exact service, product, and version running on port 22 on the target .

    • Action: If the service is not essential for business operations, it should be immediately closed or blocked at the firewall.

    • Action: If the service is essential, ensure it is properly configured, regularly patched, and adheres to the principle of least privilege. Implement strong authentication mechanisms (e.g., SSH keys instead of passwords), consider IP-based access restrictions, or use a jump host for access control.

    • Benefit: Eliminates or minimizes the attack surface associated with this open port, prevents exploitation of unknown vulnerabilities, and ensures only necessary and secure services are exposed.
