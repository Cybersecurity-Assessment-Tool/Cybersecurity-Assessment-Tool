Executive Summary

This assessment outlines the current cybersecurity posture of Blackwood Industries. While the organization demonstrates commendable strengths in requiring Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for logging into computers and accessing sensitive data systems, as well as maintaining regular security awareness training, critical vulnerabilities remain. Specifically, the absence of MFA for email access and the lack of a formal employee acceptable use policy (AUP) present significant attack vectors and operational risks. The inability to analyze network scan data and pre-existing vulnerability information means this report’s scope is limited to self-reported controls. Consequently, a comprehensive technical vulnerability assessment is strongly recommended to identify and mitigate potential external and internal threats.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Blackwood Industries, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access (Critical Priority):

    • Action: Immediately enable and enforce MFA for all employee and administrative email accounts across the organization. This should be a top priority.

    • Benefit: Dramatically reduces the risk of email account compromise, protecting against phishing, credential stuffing, and other identity-based attacks.

    • Consideration: Evaluate various MFA methods, prioritizing phishing-resistant options like FIDO2 security keys where possible.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (High Priority):

    • Action: Draft, finalize, and formally implement a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines expectations for employee behavior regarding company IT resources, data, and networks.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces misuse of company assets, improves data handling practices, and provides a foundation for disciplinary actions when policies are violated.

    • Consideration: Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, integrating it into new employee onboarding and annual compliance training.

  3. Conduct a Comprehensive Technical Vulnerability Assessment (High Priority):

    • Action: Given the lack of network scan data, commission an immediate external and internal vulnerability scan and, ideally, a penetration test.

    • Benefit: Identifies exploitable vulnerabilities such as open ports, outdated software versions, misconfigured services, and weak network controls that are currently unknown.

    • Consideration: Prioritize remediation efforts based on the severity, exploitability, and potential business impact of identified vulnerabilities.

  4. Review and Enhance Existing Security Controls (Medium Priority):

    • Action: Periodically review the implementation of MFA for computer logins and sensitive data systems to ensure broad coverage, robust configuration, and adherence to best practices (e.g., using strong MFA factors).

    • Benefit: Ensures that existing controls remain effective against evolving threats and covers new systems or users.

    • Consideration: Continue and enhance security awareness training programs, incorporating specific modules on the importance of MFA, phishing prevention, and adherence to the new AUP.
