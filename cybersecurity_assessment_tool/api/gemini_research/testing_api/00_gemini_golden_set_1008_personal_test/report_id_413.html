Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive assessment of Aventine Research’s current cybersecurity posture, synthesizing data from recent network scans, organizational security questionnaire responses, and a review of pre-existing risk information. While the organization demonstrates positive steps in areas like acceptable use policies and initial employee security training, significant critical gaps have been identified. Key findings include a lack of multi-factor authentication (MFA) for email and sensitive data systems, a deficiency in regular security awareness training, and critical exposure of Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) on multiple internal assets. These vulnerabilities present a high risk for unauthorized access and potential data breaches. Urgent recommendations focus on implementing robust MFA, enhancing security training, and immediately securing RDP access.

Risk Details

  • RA-01: Lack of MFA for Email Access

    • Overview: Email accounts are a primary target for phishing and credential stuffing attacks. Without MFA, compromised passwords can lead directly to unauthorized access to an employee’s inbox, potentially giving attackers access to sensitive communications, internal systems through password resets, and lateral movement capabilities.

    • Impact: High risk of account takeover, data exfiltration, business email compromise (BEC), and phishing campaigns originating from compromised accounts.

    • Correlation: Directly from Security Control Review (SC-01).

  • RA-02: Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems

    • Overview: Access to systems containing sensitive data (e.g., customer information, financial records, intellectual property) without MFA is a critical vulnerability. If an attacker gains valid credentials, they can access and exfiltrate highly confidential information undetected.

    • Impact: High risk of data breach, regulatory non-compliance, reputational damage, and significant financial loss.

    • Correlation: Directly from Security Control Review (SC-03).

  • RA-03: Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training

    • Overview: While new employees receive training, a lack of annual, recurring security awareness training leaves the entire workforce susceptible to evolving threats. Employees may forget best practices, and new attack vectors (e.g., advanced phishing, social engineering) are not regularly communicated.

    • Impact: Increased susceptibility to social engineering, phishing, malware infections, and internal policy violations, leading to potential data breaches and system compromises.

    • Correlation: Directly from Security Control Review (SC-06).

  • RA-04: Exposed Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) on 10.10.10.51

    • Overview: RDP is a common target for brute-force attacks and exploits due to its widespread use for remote administration. Exposing RDP, especially without strong authentication (like MFA) or network restrictions, provides a direct entry point for adversaries into internal systems.

    • Impact: Critical risk of unauthorized access, ransomware deployment, privilege escalation, and lateral movement within the network.

    • Correlation: Identified via Nmap Technical Scan Results.

  • RA-05: Pre-existing RDP Exposure on 10.10.10.50

    • Overview: A pre-identified critical vulnerability indicates RDP exposure on another internal host (10.10.10.50) with a CVSS score of 9.0. This underscores a systemic issue with RDP management and security within the organization.

    • Impact: Same as RA-04, contributing to a broader attack surface and increased risk of compromise across multiple assets.

Recommendations

Addressing the identified risks requires a multi-faceted approach focusing on foundational security controls and immediate remediation of critical exposures.

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across the Board:

    • Priority: Critical

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all email accounts (RA-01) and all systems housing sensitive data (RA-02). Evaluate MFA for other critical internal applications.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of credential-based attacks, even if passwords are compromised.

  • Enhance Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Priority: High

    • Action: Establish and enforce a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees (RA-03), covering topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, and data handling best practices. Conduct regular simulated phishing exercises.

    • Benefit: Strengthens the "human firewall," making employees more resilient to social engineering and phishing attacks.

  • Secure Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) Access:

    • Priority: Critical (Immediate Action Required)

    • Action: For both (RA-04) and (RA-05), immediately implement strict access controls for RDP. This should include:

      • Restricting RDP access to only necessary administrators and source IP addresses via firewall rules.

      • Implementing a Virtual Private Network (VPN) solution as the sole method for external RDP access.

      • Deploying Network Level Authentication (NLA) and strong, complex passwords.

      • Considering alternative secure remote access solutions where possible.

    • Benefit: Eliminates direct exposure to the internet/internal network, drastically reducing the attack surface for RDP exploits and brute-force attacks.

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Patch Management:

    • Priority: Medium

    • Action: Establish a regular schedule for internal and external vulnerability scans. Implement a robust patch management process to ensure all operating systems and applications are kept up-to-date, especially on internet-facing systems and those running critical services.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies new vulnerabilities and reduces the window of opportunity for attackers to exploit known weaknesses.

Conclusion

Aventine Research has a foundation of some security controls, but critical vulnerabilities related to Multi-Factor Authentication, ongoing security awareness, and RDP exposure pose immediate and significant threats. Prioritizing the implementation of the outlined recommendations, especially concerning MFA and RDP security, will substantially enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture and mitigate the most pressing risks. A proactive and continuous approach to cybersecurity will be essential for long-term resilience against evolving threats.
