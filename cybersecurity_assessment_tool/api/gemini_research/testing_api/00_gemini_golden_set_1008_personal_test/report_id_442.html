Executive Summary

This report details the findings from a cybersecurity assessment conducted for Vertex Solutions. The assessment involved a review of the organization’s self-reported security controls via a questionnaire and an external network scan of [Target IP].

Overall, Vertex Solutions demonstrates a strong commitment to foundational security practices, particularly in its extensive use of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across critical access points and the implementation of an Acceptable Use Policy and initial security awareness training for new employees.

However, a critical gap was identified: the absence of mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees. This oversight significantly increases the organization’s susceptibility to social engineering attacks and other human-factor vulnerabilities. The network scan, though limited in scope (no open ports or services were identified on the provided target), did not reveal immediate external technical vulnerabilities.

Recommendations focus on addressing the identified training gap as a high priority to bolster the organization’s overall security posture.

Recommendations

To enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Vertex Solutions, the following recommendations are provided:

High Priority Recommendations

  1. Implement Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Establish and enforce a policy requiring all employees to undergo security awareness training at least once per year.

    • Justification: While new employee training is important, annual refreshers are critical for keeping employees updated on the latest threats (e.g., phishing techniques, ransomware, social engineering) and reinforcing security best practices. This strengthens the human firewall, which is often the weakest link in an organization’s security chain.

    • Content: Training should cover topics such as identifying phishing attempts, safe browsing habits, password hygiene, data handling policies, incident reporting procedures, and the importance of adhering to the acceptable use policy.

General Recommendations

  1. Conduct Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Action: Implement a schedule for regular external and internal vulnerability scans, and consider periodic penetration testing.

    • Justification: Although the current scan did not reveal open ports, continuous scanning helps identify newly introduced vulnerabilities or misconfigurations. Penetration testing provides a more in-depth assessment by simulating real-world attacks.

  2. Ensure Comprehensive Scan Coverage:

    • Action: Verify that all external-facing assets are included in future network scans.

    • Justification: An incomplete scan scope may leave critical vulnerabilities undiscovered.
