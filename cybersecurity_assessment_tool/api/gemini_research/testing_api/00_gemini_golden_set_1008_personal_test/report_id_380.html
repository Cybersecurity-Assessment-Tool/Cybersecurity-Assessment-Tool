Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Common Ground based on an analysis of network scan data, organizational security questionnaire responses, and a review of pre-existing risk findings. Our assessment identifies several critical gaps in security controls, including a lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for sensitive data systems, the absence of an employee acceptable use policy, and insufficient annual security awareness training. A basic network scan revealed an open port 80 on an internal target, which warrants further investigation. Additionally, one unusual pre-existing risk related to system override was noted with an informational severity. Comprehensive recommendations are provided to enhance the organization’s overall security posture.

Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The "No" responses indicate significant weaknesses in the organization’s security posture:

  • MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Lack of MFA for sensitive data access dramatically increases the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

  • Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an AUP leaves employees without clear guidelines on acceptable use of organizational IT resources, leading to potential misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance issues.

  • Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the lack of annual refreshers means employees may forget crucial security practices or not be aware of new threats, increasing human-factor risk.

Analysis of Technical Findings

  • An internal web server (e.g., for applications, monitoring, or management interfaces).

  • A misconfigured device inadvertently exposing HTTP services.

  • A device running an outdated or vulnerable web service.

Without specific service details, it is difficult to assess the exact risk. However, any open port represents a potential attack surface. Further investigation is required to identify the service running on port 80, its purpose, and ensure it is securely configured and patched.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to mitigate identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Common Ground.

Policy and Procedure Recommendations

  • Implement a Comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Develop and enforce a clear AUP that outlines proper usage of organizational IT assets, data handling, internet usage, and security responsibilities. Ensure all employees acknowledge and understand the policy.

Technical Control Recommendations

  • Mandate MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Implement and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all systems storing or processing sensitive organizational data. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised.

  • Investigate Open Port 80 on 172.16.0.1:

    • Identify the exact service, product, and version running on port 80 of 172.16.0.1.

    • Determine if the service is necessary and intended to be accessible from the internal network.

    • If necessary, ensure the service is patched to the latest version, securely configured, and protected by appropriate access controls (e.g., authentication, firewall rules).

    • If unnecessary, disable or remove the service.

Security Awareness and Training Recommendations

  • Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Implement a program that provides mandatory security awareness training to all employees at least once per year. Training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), organizational policies (e.g., AUP), and best practices for data protection. Regular refresher training is crucial for maintaining a strong security culture.

Conclusion

Common Ground exhibits a foundational understanding of some security practices, as evidenced by MFA for email and computer logins, and initial security training for new hires. However, critical vulnerabilities exist in the areas of sensitive data access controls, formal employee policies, and ongoing security education. The presence of an unidentified open service on an internal host also warrants attention. By addressing the recommendations outlined in this report, Common Ground can significantly enhance its defensive capabilities, reduce its attack surface, and foster a more robust security posture against evolving cyber threats.
