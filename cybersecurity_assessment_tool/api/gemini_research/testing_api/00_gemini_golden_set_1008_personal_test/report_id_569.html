Executive Summary

This report provides an assessment of Orchid Isle’s current cybersecurity posture, based on a network scan, organizational security questionnaire responses, and pre-existing risk data. The assessment identified several critical vulnerabilities and significant control gaps that require immediate attention. Key findings include multiple instances of Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) exposure on internal hosts, a critical lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access, and deficiencies in security awareness training and policy enforcement. Addressing these issues is crucial for enhancing the organization’s resilience against common cyber threats and mitigating potential risks from sophisticated attacks.

Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive assessment, the following recommendations are provided to improve Orchid Isle’s cybersecurity posture. These are prioritized to address the most critical and impactful risks first.

High-Priority Recommendations:

  • Secure Exposed RDP Instances:

    • For 10.10.10.50 and 10.10.10.51, immediately restrict inbound RDP access to only authorized internal networks or specific trusted IP addresses.

    • Implement and enforce strong, unique passwords for all RDP accounts.

    • Enable Network Level Authentication (NLA) for RDP connections to add an authentication layer before a full RDP session is established.

    • Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all RDP access. If direct external RDP access is necessary, ensure it is only available via a VPN connection with strong authentication.

    • Review the business necessity of RDP on these hosts; if not essential, disable the service to remove the attack surface.

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Immediately enable and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This is a foundational security control against phishing and credential stuffing attacks, significantly reducing the risk of email account compromise.

  • Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Develop and implement a comprehensive, mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats such as phishing, social engineering, ransomware, password hygiene, and secure data handling practices. Regular refreshers throughout the year are also highly recommended.

Medium-Priority Recommendations:

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear and comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that defines appropriate and prohibited use of all company-owned or managed IT systems, networks, software, and data.

    • Ensure all employees are required to read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally during onboarding and annually thereafter. The AUP should be easily accessible to all staff.

Conclusion

Addressing the identified critical vulnerabilities and significant control gaps will substantially enhance Orchid Isle’s overall security posture, reduce its attack surface, and improve its resilience against evolving cyber threats. It is imperative that these recommendations are acted upon promptly. Continuous monitoring, regular security assessments, and proactive risk management are vital to maintain a strong and adaptive defense in the dynamic cybersecurity landscape.
