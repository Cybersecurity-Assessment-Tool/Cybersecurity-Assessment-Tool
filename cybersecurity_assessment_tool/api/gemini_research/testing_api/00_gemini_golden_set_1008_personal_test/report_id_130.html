Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Neon Pulse Entertainment based on a review of organizational security controls and an external network scan. The assessment identified several critical gaps in security controls, particularly concerning Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation for email and sensitive data systems, the absence of an employee acceptable use policy, and a lack of annual security awareness training for all employees. No external network vulnerabilities were identified, primarily due to the absence of active scan data. Urgent recommendations are provided to address the identified weaknesses and enhance the organization’s overall security resilience.

Risk Assessment

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and strengthen Neon Pulse Entertainment’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

  • Implement MFA for Email Access (RISK-001): Immediately implement MFA for all employee email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of account compromise even if passwords are stolen or guessed. Consider solutions like Microsoft Authenticator, Google Authenticator, or hardware tokens.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (RISK-002): Enforce MFA for access to all systems, applications, and databases that store or process sensitive organizational or customer data. This adds a crucial layer of security to prevent unauthorized access to critical assets.

Policy and Governance

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (RISK-003): Draft, approve, and disseminate a comprehensive AUP to all employees. The AUP should clearly define acceptable and unacceptable uses of company IT resources, data handling guidelines, and consequences for non-compliance. Require all employees to review and acknowledge the policy annually.

Security Awareness Training

  • Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees (RISK-004): Beyond onboarding, implement a mandatory, annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats, phishing recognition, safe browsing, password hygiene, and the importance of reporting suspicious activities. Regular refreshers are crucial to maintain a strong security culture.

Conclusion

While the external network scan did not yield findings, the internal security control review highlights critical areas of concern for Neon Pulse Entertainment. Addressing the identified gaps in MFA implementation, policy documentation, and continuous security awareness training is paramount to building a robust defense against modern cyber threats. Prioritizing these recommendations will significantly enhance the organization’s resilience and reduce its overall cyber risk exposure.
