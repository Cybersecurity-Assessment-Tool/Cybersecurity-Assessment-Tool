Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Arcane Security, based on a comprehensive analysis of organizational questionnaire responses, a recent network vulnerability scan, and a review of existing risk data. The organization demonstrates strengths in enforcing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins and sensitive data access, maintaining an acceptable use policy, and conducting annual security awareness training.

However, critical gaps were identified concerning the lack of MFA for email access and the absence of security awareness training for new employees, which present significant risks. A recent network scan revealed no open ports on the internal target, specifically confirming that Port 80 is closed. This finding directly mitigates a previously identified risk regarding an unencrypted web server, suggesting the risk register may require an update. Actionable recommendations are provided to address all identified vulnerabilities and enhance the organization’s overall security posture.

Risk Assessment

This section correlates findings from the security control review, technical scans, and pre-existing risk data to provide a comprehensive view of the organization’s current risk landscape.

Identified Critical Gaps and High Risks (from Questionnaire)

Based on the security control review, the following critical gaps and high risks were identified:

  1. Lack of MFA for Email Access (High Risk)

    • Overview: The organization currently does not require Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access. Email accounts are primary targets for phishing and business email compromise (BEC) attacks. Without MFA, a single compromised password can lead to unauthorized access to sensitive communications, data, and potentially enable an attacker to impersonate employees for fraudulent activities.

    • Severity: High

  2. Absence of Security Awareness Training for New Employees (High Risk)

    • Overview: New employees are not receiving specific security awareness training upon joining the organization. This leaves them particularly vulnerable to social engineering tactics (e.g., phishing, vishing) and increases the likelihood of security policy violations due to a lack of understanding of organizational security best practices, threats, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Severity: High

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified critical gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Arcane Security.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Enable and mandate MFA for all employee email accounts. This should be a top priority.

    • Rationale: MFA significantly elevates the security of email accounts by requiring a second verification factor in addition to the password, making it substantially harder for unauthorized users to gain access even if the password is stolen.

    • Priority: Critical

  2. Establish Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Develop and integrate a comprehensive security awareness training program into the onboarding process for all new hires.

    • Rationale: Early and consistent training ensures that new employees are equipped with the knowledge to identify and resist social engineering attacks and understand their role in maintaining organizational security from their first day.

    • Priority: High

  3. Verify and Update the Risk Register:

    • Action: Conduct a thorough review of the organization’s existing risk register to ensure all identified risks are current, accurate, and reflect the actual technical state of systems. Specifically, formalize the status update for the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk based on the recent scan findings.

    • Rationale: An accurate and up-to-date risk register is fundamental for effective risk management, appropriate resource allocation, and maintaining a clear understanding of the organization’s threat landscape.

    • Priority: Medium
