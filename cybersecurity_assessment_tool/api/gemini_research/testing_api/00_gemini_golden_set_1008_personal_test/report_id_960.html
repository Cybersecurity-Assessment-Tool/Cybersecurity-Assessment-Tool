Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Zenith Point based on an organizational questionnaire, an attempted network scan, and an attempt to review existing vulnerability data. The assessment highlights critical gaps in foundational security controls, particularly concerning Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access and the absence of an employee acceptable use policy. Furthermore, the inability to parse network scan data and pre-existing vulnerability data indicates a significant lack of visibility into the external attack surface and internal risk management practices. While some positive security practices are in place (e.g., MFA for computer logins and regular security awareness training), the identified critical deficiencies and data visibility issues pose substantial risks that require immediate attention.

Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps:

  • MFA for Email Access: The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access is a critical vulnerability. Email is a primary vector for phishing attacks and account compromise. Without MFA, compromised credentials can grant attackers full access to an employee’s inbox, leading to business email compromise (BEC), data exfiltration, or further internal network penetration.

  • Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of an AUP means employees may not be fully aware of their responsibilities regarding the use of company IT resources. This can lead to inappropriate behavior, increased risk of malware infections, data leakage, and potential legal or compliance issues. An AUP sets clear boundaries and expectations for employee conduct.

Recommendations

To address the identified gaps and strengthen the cybersecurity posture of Zenith Point, the following recommendations are provided:

Immediate Critical Actions

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all email accounts across the organization. This is the single most effective control against credential theft and phishing attacks.

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a clear, concise, and comprehensive AUP that outlines employee responsibilities and permissible use of company IT resources. Ensure all employees are trained on the AUP and acknowledge their understanding and compliance.

Data Visibility and Assessment Enhancements

  • Conduct a Comprehensive External Network Scan: Perform a professional and thorough external network scan (e.g., using Nmap and vulnerability scanners) of all public-facing IP addresses and domains. This will identify open ports, running services, software versions, and potential vulnerabilities. Ensure the results are properly recorded and accessible for analysis.

  • Establish a Vulnerability Management Program: Implement a structured program for identifying, assessing, tracking, and remediating vulnerabilities. This should include regular internal and external scanning, penetration testing, and a defined process for addressing discovered risks.

General Security Best Practices

  • Regular Review of Security Controls: Periodically review all security controls and policies, especially those identified as "No" in the questionnaire, to ensure they meet current best practices and organizational needs.

  • Continuous Security Awareness: Maintain and expand security awareness training programs to include common attack vectors, policy updates, and the importance of reporting suspicious activities.

  • Incident Response Planning: Ensure an up-to-date incident response plan is in place and regularly tested, addressing potential scenarios like email account compromise or data breaches.
