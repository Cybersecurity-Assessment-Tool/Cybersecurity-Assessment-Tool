Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Willow Creek Health based on an analysis of network scan data, organizational security questionnaire responses, and pre-existing risk information. The assessment identifies several critical gaps and high-severity risks, primarily related to Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) exposure, insufficient Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA coverage, and deficiencies in foundational security policies and training. Immediate action is recommended to mitigate these vulnerabilities and enhance the overall security posture.

Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The "No" responses indicate critical vulnerabilities in the organization’s security posture:

  • MFA for Email Access (Critical Gap): Lack of MFA on email access significantly increases the risk of account compromise via phishing or brute-force attacks, potentially leading to business email compromise (BEC) or broader network intrusion.

  • MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (Critical Gap): Without MFA, access to sensitive data systems is vulnerable to compromised credentials, posing a high risk of data breach and regulatory non-compliance.

  • Employee Acceptable Use Policy (High Risk): The absence of an AUP leaves the organization exposed to risks from employee misuse of company assets, intellectual property theft, and legal liabilities.

  • Security Awareness Training for New Employees (High Risk): New employees are often prime targets for social engineering attacks due to lack of familiarity with organizational security practices. Skipping initial training leaves a significant entry point for attackers.

Technical Analysis

The presence of an open RDP port (3389) on represents a significant security concern. RDP is a frequent target for attackers attempting to gain initial access to networks through brute-force attacks, credential stuffing, or exploiting vulnerabilities in the RDP service itself. While version information was not available from this scan, any publicly or internally accessible RDP service should be secured with strong, complex passwords, network-level authentication (NLA), and ideally, be placed behind a VPN or bastion host to limit exposure. The discovery of an open RDP port on complements the pre-existing risk of RDP exposure on (detailed in the Risk Assessment), indicating a systemic issue within the network.

Severity Definitions

  • Critical (CVSS 9.0-10.0): Requires immediate attention. Exploitation likely to result in severe business impact (e.g., full system compromise, major data breach).

  • High (CVSS 7.0-8.9): Requires urgent attention. Exploitation could lead to significant impact (e.g., unauthorized access, data loss, service disruption).

  • Medium (CVSS 4.0-6.9): Requires attention. Exploitation could lead to moderate impact (e.g., information disclosure, minor disruption).

  • Low (CVSS 0.1-3.9): Minor impact or requires specific conditions for exploitation.

Recommendations

To significantly improve its cybersecurity posture, Willow Creek Health should prioritize the implementation of the following recommendations:

Immediate Actions

  • Mitigate RDP Exposure:

    • For and , restrict RDP access to only trusted, internal IP addresses or specific management hosts.

    • Implement a VPN requirement for all remote RDP connections.

    • Enable Network Level Authentication (NLA) for RDP connections.

    • Ensure all RDP accounts use strong, unique passwords and are not default administrator accounts.

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This is a critical defense against phishing and credential stuffing attacks.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Mandate MFA for all access to systems containing sensitive organizational or patient data to prevent unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised.

Mid-Term Actions

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a clear and comprehensive AUP outlining the proper use of company assets, data, and networks. Ensure all employees acknowledge and understand this policy.

  • Introduce Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Establish mandatory security awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new hires to instill fundamental security best practices from day one.

  • Review and Enhance Existing Security Awareness Training: While annual training is performed, consider enhancing content to cover current threat landscapes, including social engineering, phishing, and safe browsing habits.

Long-Term Strategy

  • Conduct regular vulnerability assessments and penetration tests to proactively identify and address security weaknesses.

  • Implement a robust patch management program for all operating systems and applications, especially those publicly exposed.

  • Develop and regularly test an Incident Response Plan (IRP) to ensure the organization can effectively respond to and recover from cybersecurity incidents.

  • Consider adopting a ’Zero Trust’ architecture to minimize implicit trust and enforce strict access controls.

Conclusion

Willow Creek Health faces several significant cybersecurity challenges, particularly concerning RDP exposure, MFA adoption, and foundational security policies and training. Addressing these issues with the recommended actions will substantially strengthen the organization’s defense against common attack vectors and improve its overall security posture. Proactive and continuous security management is essential in today’s evolving threat landscape.
