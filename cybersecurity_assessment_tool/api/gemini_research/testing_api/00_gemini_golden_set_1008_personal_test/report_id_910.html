Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Common Ground based on an organizational questionnaire, a network scan, and a review of existing risks. The organization demonstrates good practices in security awareness training for employees. However, critical gaps were identified, notably the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access and computer logins, and the lack of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy. The limited external network scan did not identify any open ports. Addressing these gaps is crucial for enhancing the overall security posture and reducing the risk of unauthorized access and data misuse.

Risk Assessment

Based on the provided data, several key risks have been identified. These are primarily derived from critical gaps in security controls as indicated by the organizational questionnaire.

Identified Cybersecurity Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access Email accounts are vulnerable to compromise via stolen or guessed credentials without multi-factor authentication, increasing the risk of phishing success and business email compromise. High
Lack of MFA for Computer Login User accounts for computer logins are susceptible to compromise without multi-factor authentication, increasing the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems and lateral movement within the network. High
Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without an AUP, employees may be unaware of expected conduct regarding company resources, data handling, and internet usage, increasing the risk of misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance. Medium

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance Common Groundâ€™s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access: Deploy and enforce MFA for all employee email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of account compromise even if passwords are stolen or weak.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins: Implement MFA for logging into all company computers and workstations. This provides an additional layer of security against unauthorized access to internal systems.

  3. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a clear and comprehensive AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources, data, and internet access. Ensure all employees are trained on and acknowledge the policy. Regularly review and update the AUP.
