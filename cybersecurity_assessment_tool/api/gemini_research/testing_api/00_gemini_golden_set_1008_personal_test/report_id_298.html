Recommendations

To address the identified risks and further enhance the cybersecurity posture of Sterling Silver, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Action: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing organizational email services. This should be a top priority.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if passwords are stolen, by requiring a second verification factor. This is crucial given email’s role as a primary communication and recovery channel.

  2. Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all user logins to company computers, whether they are domain-joined workstations or laptops.

    • Benefit: Enhances endpoint security by adding an additional layer of protection, making it much harder for attackers to gain access to internal systems via compromised credentials.

  3. Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Action: Although no open ports were identified in this assessment, conducting regular external and internal vulnerability scans and periodic penetration tests is recommended. This continuous monitoring helps to identify new exposures as the infrastructure evolves and provides deeper insight into potential attack paths.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies potential security weaknesses and exploitable vulnerabilities before they can be leveraged by malicious actors.

  4. Maintain and Evolve Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • Action: Continue with the current practice of regular security awareness training for all employees. Furthermore, ensure the training content is updated regularly to reflect current threat landscapes, covering topics such as advanced phishing techniques, social engineering, and secure browsing habits.

    • Benefit: A well-informed and vigilant workforce is the first line of defense against many cyber threats and helps to reinforce a strong security culture.

Conclusion

Sterling Silver demonstrates good foundational security practices in areas such as security awareness training and acceptable use policies, which are commendable. However, the critical absence of Multi-Factor Authentication for both email access and computer logins presents significant and immediate risks that require urgent attention. Addressing these MFA gaps will substantially strengthen the organization’s defense against common attack vectors and significantly improve its overall cybersecurity resilience. Continued vigilance, proactive security measures, and regular technical assessments are essential for maintaining a strong security posture in the evolving threat landscape.
