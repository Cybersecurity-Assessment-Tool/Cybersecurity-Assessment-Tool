Executive Summary

The cybersecurity assessment of Great Lakes reveals a mixed security posture. While the organization demonstrates proactive measures such as Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access and initial security awareness training for new employees, significant vulnerabilities exist in crucial areas. Critical gaps include the absence of MFA for computer logins and sensitive data systems, a lack of an organizational acceptable use policy (AUP), and insufficient annual security awareness training for all employees.

A network scan of 192.168.0.5 identified no open ports, with Port 80 explicitly reported as closed. This contradicts a previously recorded risk concerning an "Unencrypted Web Server" on an open Port 80, highlighting a potential discrepancy in risk registers or changes in infrastructure that need clarification.

This report details these findings, provides a consolidated view of identified risks, and offers actionable recommendations to bolster Great Lakes’s defenses against cyber threats.

Recommendations

Prioritized Action Items

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Great Lakes’s cybersecurity posture. They are prioritized based on their potential impact and the severity of the identified risks.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all employee logins to corporate computers and systems.

    • Rationale: This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if passwords are compromised.

    • Priority: High

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Require MFA for access to all systems containing or processing sensitive organizational data.

    • Rationale: Protects critical information from unauthorized access, aligning with data protection best practices.

    • Priority: High

  3. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create, disseminate, and enforce a clear AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources.

    • Rationale: Establishes clear expectations for employee conduct, reducing insider risks and improving compliance.

    • Priority: High

  4. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, covering current threats and best practices.

    • Rationale: Ensures continuous education on cybersecurity best practices, reducing human-factor vulnerabilities.

    • Priority: High

  5. Investigate Port 80 Discrepancy:

    • Action: Investigate why the existing risk register states "Port 80 is open" while the recent scan shows it as "closed."

    • Rationale: Reconcile conflicting information to ensure the risk register accurately reflects the current environment. If the port was indeed open previously, confirm proper remediation. If the port is currently closed, update the risk register accordingly.

    • Priority: Medium

  6. Conduct Regular Network Scans:

    • Action: Implement a schedule for periodic network vulnerability scanning to proactively identify open ports, services, and potential misconfigurations.

    • Rationale: Continuous monitoring helps detect new exposures and validates the effectiveness of security controls over time.

    • Priority: Medium
