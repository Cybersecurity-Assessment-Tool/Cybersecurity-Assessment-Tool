Executive Summary

This report provides an assessment of Verve & Vigor’s cybersecurity posture, based on a review of organizational data, security questionnaire responses, network scan results, and existing risk documentation.

The organization demonstrates a strong commitment to multi-factor authentication (MFA) across critical access points, which is a commendable security practice. However, foundational gaps were identified in critical areas such as the absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and a lack of security awareness training for new employees. These gaps present significant human-factor risks that could be exploited.

Technical analysis of the provided network scan data revealed an open port 80 on an internal host (172.16.0.1), though detailed service and version information was not available, limiting a comprehensive vulnerability assessment for this specific finding.

An unusual risk entry was identified in the provided risk register, which appears to be a meta-instruction rather than a traditional technical vulnerability. This unique entry requires internal clarification regarding risk categorization and management protocols.

Recommendations focus on establishing critical policies, enhancing employee training, and further investigating network services to strengthen the overall security posture.

Analysis of Gaps

The questionnaire results indicate two critical areas of concern:

  • Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an AUP leaves the organization vulnerable to misuse of IT resources, potential legal liabilities, and unclear expectations for employee conduct regarding company assets and data.

  • New Employee Security Awareness Training: Failing to provide security awareness training to new employees from the outset means they may not be equipped with the basic knowledge to identify and report security threats, significantly increasing the risk of social engineering attacks, phishing, and accidental data breaches.

The strong implementation of MFA is positive, but the identified policy and training gaps represent fundamental weaknesses that can undermine technical controls.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified gaps and risks, thereby enhancing Verve & Vigor’s cybersecurity posture.

Policy and Training Enhancements

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines the acceptable use of company IT resources, data, and communication systems.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP.

    • Integrate AUP review into annual employee training and new hire onboarding.

  2. Implement Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Establish mandatory security awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new hires.

    • Cover topics such as phishing, social engineering, password best practices, data handling, and reporting security incidents.

Technical Control Improvements

  1. Investigate Service on Port 80 on 172.16.0.1:

    • Conduct an internal audit to identify the specific application or service running on port 80 of 172.16.0.1.

    • Verify if the service is necessary and properly configured for internal-only access.

    • Assess for known vulnerabilities in the identified service, ensuring it is patched and up-to-date.

    • Consider implementing HTTPS (encrypted traffic) if sensitive information is exchanged, even internally.

    • If the service is not critical, consider disabling or restricting access to it.

Risk Management Process Review

  1. Review and Address Anomalous Risk Entries:

    • Investigate the origin and purpose of the risk entry titled "".

    • Clarify internal risk management processes to distinguish between genuine cybersecurity risks, meta-instructions, or data anomalies.

    • Ensure that risk registers contain actionable vulnerabilities and clear recommendations, maintaining the integrity and usefulness of risk assessments.

By addressing these recommendations, Verve & Vigor can significantly enhance its overall cybersecurity posture, mitigate identified risks, and foster a more secure operational environment.
