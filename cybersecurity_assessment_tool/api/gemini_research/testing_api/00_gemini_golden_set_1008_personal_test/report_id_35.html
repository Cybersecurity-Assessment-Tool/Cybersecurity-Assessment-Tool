Executive Summary

This report summarizes the cybersecurity posture of Pacific Rim Exports based on a review of organizational data, a security questionnaire, and limited network scan results targeting 127.0.0.1. Key findings indicate a generally positive approach to some security controls, particularly regarding Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and initial security awareness training. However, a critical policy gap was identified concerning the absence of an employee acceptable use policy. Furthermore, a pre-existing critical risk, "Localhost Exposed," was corroborated by the network scan, highlighting internal exposure that requires immediate attention. Overall, the organization demonstrates a foundational commitment to security, but requires focused effort to mature its policy framework and mitigate identified technical risks.

  • Overview: The organization lacks a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) for employees. An AUP defines the proper use of organizational assets, networks, and data, clarifying employee responsibilities and expected behavior. Without an AUP, the organization is exposed to increased risks from misuse of IT resources, potential legal liabilities, and a lack of clear guidance for employees regarding cybersecurity best practices.

  • Severity: High - Policy Gap

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of Pacific Rim Exports.

Immediate Actions

  1. Secure Localhost Exposure (Port 22 on 127.0.0.1):

    • Review Necessity: Determine if SSH access on 127.0.0.1 is strictly necessary. If not, disable the service.

    • Restrict Access: If necessary, configure SSH to only listen on specific, authorized internal interfaces or restrict access via firewall rules to only trusted IP addresses.

    • Strong Authentication: Ensure SSH is configured to use strong authentication methods (e.g., key-based authentication only, disable password authentication).

    • Principle of Least Privilege: Ensure users with SSH access have only the minimum necessary privileges.

    • Logging and Monitoring: Implement robust logging for SSH access attempts and monitor these logs for suspicious activity.

    • Regular Patching: Ensure the SSH server and underlying operating system are kept up-to-date with the latest security patches.

Strategic Recommendations

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft AUP: Create a comprehensive AUP outlining acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT resources (networks, devices, software, data, internet, email).

    • Legal Review: Have the AUP reviewed by legal counsel to ensure compliance with relevant laws and regulations.

    • Employee Communication: Clearly communicate the AUP to all employees, ensuring they understand its contents and implications.

    • Mandatory Acknowledgment: Require all employees to formally acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to abide by the AUP, typically upon hiring and periodically thereafter.

    • Integration with Training: Incorporate the AUP into new employee onboarding and annual security awareness training.

    • Enforcement: Establish clear procedures for enforcing the AUP, including consequences for violations.

Conclusion

Pacific Rim Exports has demonstrated a foundational understanding of cybersecurity best practices by implementing MFA and initial security awareness training. However, the absence of an employee acceptable use policy and the confirmed "Localhost Exposed" risk present significant areas for improvement. Addressing these issues through policy development, technical controls, and ongoing vigilance will significantly enhance the organization’s overall security posture against evolving threats.
