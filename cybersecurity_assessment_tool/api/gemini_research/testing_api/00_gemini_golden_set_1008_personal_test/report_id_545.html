Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive assessment of Firebrand Media’s cybersecurity posture, synthesized from an internal network scan, a review of organizational security controls, and analysis of existing risk data. Key findings reveal critical vulnerabilities, particularly the direct exposure of Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) services on internal network assets and significant gaps in fundamental security practices. These gaps include the absence of multi-factor authentication (MFA) for email and sensitive data systems, and a complete lack of consistent security awareness training for employees. The current security landscape indicates a high overall risk profile, necessitating immediate and focused remediation efforts to fortify the organization’s defenses against prevalent cyber threats.

Security Control Gaps (High Severity)

The following deficiencies identified during the security control review are categorized as high severity due to their significant potential impact on the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Firebrand Media’s assets and data:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access (SC-01): Without Multi-Factor Authentication, employee email accounts are highly vulnerable to various attacks, including credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attempts. A compromised email account can serve as a gateway for Business Email Compromise (BEC) fraud, data exfiltration, and further sophisticated network infiltration.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (SC-03): The absence of MFA for systems containing sensitive data drastically increases the risk of unauthorized access and data breaches. If user credentials are stolen or successfully guessed, attackers can gain direct access to critical organizational data without an additional layer of verification.

  • Absence of Comprehensive Security Awareness Training (SC-05, SC-06): Employees frequently represent the human element in cybersecurity defenses. Without regular and mandatory security awareness training for both new and existing personnel, employees are more susceptible to falling victim to phishing, social engineering attacks, and failing to adhere to essential security best practices. This significantly increases the likelihood of an initial compromise and subsequent security incidents.

Recommendations

To proactively enhance Firebrand Media’s cybersecurity posture and mitigate the identified risks, the following actionable recommendations are provided, with a strong emphasis on addressing critical and high-severity findings:

  1. Implement and Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across Critical Systems:

    • Email Systems: Immediately enable and mandate MFA for all employee email accounts to significantly reduce the risk of credential compromise.

    • Sensitive Data Systems: Implement robust MFA requirements for all access points to systems and applications that store or process sensitive organizational data.

  2. Secure Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) Access on Internal Assets:

    • For and (and similar hosts):

      • Restrict Access: Implement stringent firewall rules to limit RDP access to only specific, authorized internal IP addresses and users.

      • VPN Requirement: For any external or remote RDP access, mandate the use of a Virtual Private Network (VPN) connection. Ensure direct internet exposure of RDP is disabled.

      • Strong Authentication: Enforce strong, complex, and unique passwords for all accounts with RDP access. Consider RDP Gateway or similar solutions for centralized management and MFA enforcement.

      • Patch Management: Ensure all systems running RDP services are regularly updated with the latest security patches to protect against known vulnerabilities.

      • Disable or Replace: If RDP is not strictly necessary, disable the service. Alternatively, explore more secure remote administration alternatives.

  3. Establish a Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Onboarding Training: Develop and implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees as a critical component of their onboarding process.

    • Annual Training: Conduct mandatory, organization-wide security awareness training for all employees at least once per year, covering contemporary threats such as phishing, social engineering tactics, password hygiene, and proper data handling procedures.

    • Continuous Reinforcement: Implement regular simulated phishing campaigns and security bulletins to continuously test employee vigilance and reinforce training concepts.

  4. Review and Update Security Policies and Procedures:

    • Ensure the existing employee acceptable use policy (AUP) is up-to-date, clearly communicated, and actively enforced across the organization.

    • Develop and disseminate clear, comprehensive policies regarding remote access, data classification, password management, and incident response procedures.

Conclusion

Firebrand Media currently operates within a significant cybersecurity risk landscape, primarily characterized by exposed RDP services on internal hosts and critical deficiencies in fundamental security controls such as Multi-Factor Authentication and regular security awareness training. Addressing these identified risks and diligently implementing the provided remediation steps is paramount for substantially bolstering the organization’s defensive capabilities against prevalent cyber threats and safeguarding its valuable assets and data. A proactive, layered security approach, commencing with the immediate implementation of these foundational controls, will be indispensable for achieving a more resilient and secure operational environment.
