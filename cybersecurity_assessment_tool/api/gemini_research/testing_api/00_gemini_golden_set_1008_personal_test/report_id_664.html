Critical areas requiring immediate attention include the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and sensitive data systems, as well as a complete lack of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and regular security awareness training for employees. These deficiencies expose the organization to a high risk of phishing, account compromise, and insider threats.

While this might suggest a secure external posture or effective firewalling, it also implies a potentially limited scope of the scan or a need for deeper internal network visibility. No existing vulnerabilities were reported by the organization.

Immediate recommendations focus on implementing robust MFA, establishing comprehensive security policies, and initiating regular security awareness training to fortify the organization’s defenses against prevalent cyber threats.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actionable recommendations are provided to improve Phoenix Rising’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Remediation (Critical Risks)

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Enable and enforce MFA for all employee email accounts (e.g., via Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, or other email providers).

    • Prioritize strong MFA methods (e.g., authenticator apps, hardware tokens) over weaker ones (e.g., SMS-based OTP).

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Identify all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational or customer data.

    • Implement and enforce MFA for access to these systems, applications, and databases.

Strategic Improvements (High Risks)

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a clear and comprehensive AUP outlining acceptable use of organizational IT resources, data handling, and internet usage.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and sign the AUP upon hiring and periodically thereafter.

  • Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • Onboarding Training: Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • Annual Training: Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, covering current threats such as phishing, social engineering, malware, and data handling best practices.

    • Ongoing Education: Consider periodic short training modules or simulated phishing campaigns to reinforce learning and maintain vigilance throughout the year.

Further Technical Investigation

  • Expanded Network Scanning:

    • Conduct internal network scans from within the organization’s network to identify potential vulnerabilities on devices not exposed externally.

    • Perform a more comprehensive scan of if it is an externally facing asset, potentially with broader port ranges or different scanning techniques.

  • Asset Inventory and Hardening:

    • Maintain an up-to-date inventory of all network-connected assets.

    • Implement a regular patching and vulnerability management program for all operating systems and applications.

By addressing these recommendations, Phoenix Rising can significantly enhance its overall cybersecurity posture, reduce its attack surface, and better protect its valuable assets and data.
