Executive Summary

This report details the findings from a cybersecurity posture assessment conducted for Calyx Botany, incorporating network scan data, organizational security questionnaire responses, and a review of existing risk documentation. The assessment reveals a moderately concerning security posture with several critical vulnerabilities and high-risk areas requiring immediate attention.

Key findings include:

  • Critical Technical Exposure: A web service identified as "TOP SECRET DB" is openly accessible on port 8080, exposing potentially highly sensitive data.

  • Critical Discrepancy in Risk Management: The organization’s existing risk register incorrectly marks Port 8080 as "Secured" with a severity of 0.0, directly contradicting the active scan results. This indicates a severe flaw in risk identification, assessment, or inventory processes.

  • High Risk: Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems: The absence of MFA for critical systems significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access.

  • High Risk: Inadequate Annual Security Awareness Training: Infrequent security awareness training leaves employees vulnerable to evolving threats like phishing and social engineering.

Immediate remediation efforts are crucial to mitigate these risks and improve Calyx Botany’s overall security posture.

Identified Risks & Vulnerabilities

Based on the comprehensive analysis, the following risks and vulnerabilities have been identified:

Critical Risks

  • RV-C1: Exposed "TOP SECRET DB" on Port 8080

    • Description: A web service identified as "TOP SECRET DB" is openly accessible on port . This represents an extreme exposure of potentially highly sensitive data, making it vulnerable to unauthorized access, data theft, or manipulation.

    • Severity: Critical (CVSS equivalent: 9.8)

  • RV-C2: Inaccurate Risk Register for Port 8080

    • Description: The internal risk register incorrectly documents port 8080 as secure and a false positive, directly contradicting active scan results that show a critical data exposure. This indicates a severe systemic flaw in risk identification, assessment, asset inventory, or vulnerability management processes, which undermines the entire risk management framework.

    • Severity: Critical (CVSS equivalent: 9.0)

High Risks

  • RV-H1: Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems

    • Description: The organization does not enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for access to systems containing sensitive data. This leaves these critical systems vulnerable to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks if user credentials are compromised.

    • Severity: High (CVSS equivalent: 8.3)

  • RV-H2: Inadequate Annual Security Awareness Training

    • Description: Employees do not receive mandatory security awareness training at least once per year. This leads to a degradation of security hygiene over time, increasing susceptibility to evolving threats such as sophisticated phishing campaigns, social engineering tactics, and malware distribution.

    • Severity: High (CVSS equivalent: 7.0)

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve Calyx Botany’s cybersecurity posture. Recommendations are prioritized by severity.

Immediate Action (Critical Risks)

  • R-C1: Investigate and Secure Exposed "TOP SECRET DB" (RV-C1)

    • Action: Immediately investigate the service running on identified as "TOP SECRET DB."

    • Details: Determine the nature of the data being exposed, the purpose of this service, and who has access. If this exposure is unintentional or unauthorized, shut down or restrict access to the service immediately. If legitimate access is required, implement robust authentication (with MFA), authorization, and encryption protocols. Consider moving this service behind a firewall or VPN.

    • Owner: IT/Security Team

    • Target Date: Within 24-48 hours

  • R-C2: Review and Remediate Risk Register Inaccuracies (RV-C2)

    • Action: Conduct an immediate, comprehensive review of the organization’s entire risk register, asset inventory, and vulnerability assessment processes.

    • Details: Establish a robust, continuous process for validating existing risks, incorporating new findings from vulnerability scans, penetration tests, and security assessments. Ensure clear ownership, tracking, and regular review cycles for all identified risks and their remediation efforts. Prioritize ensuring asset inventory is accurate and kept up-to-date with active services.

    • Owner: Security Management / Risk Management

    • Target Date: Within 1 week (initial review), ongoing for process improvement

High Priority Actions

  • R-H1: Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (RV-H1)

    • Action: Mandate and implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all sensitive data systems and applications across the organization.

    • Details: Conduct an audit to identify all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive data. Prioritize MFA deployment based on criticality. Provide training and support to users for MFA enrollment and usage.

    • Owner: IT/Security Team

    • Target Date: Within 1-3 months

  • R-H2: Establish Annual Security Awareness Training (RV-H2)

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory, comprehensive annual security awareness training program for all employees.

    • Details: The training should cover current threats such as phishing, social engineering, ransomware, password hygiene best practices, data handling, and incident reporting procedures. Consider incorporating simulated phishing exercises.

    • Owner: HR / Security Team

    • Target Date: Within 2 months (program development), ongoing annually
