Executive Summary

This report summarizes the cybersecurity posture of Hidden Gem based on an organizational security questionnaire. It aims to identify critical security gaps and provide actionable recommendations. Due to issues with the provided data, comprehensive technical network scan results and pre-existing vulnerability information could not be included in this assessment.

The analysis of the organizational data reveals several critical areas for improvement:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email and Sensitive Data Systems: A significant vulnerability exists due to the absence of MFA for accessing organizational email and sensitive data systems, increasing the risk of unauthorized access via compromised credentials.

  • Inadequate Security Awareness Training for New Employees: The lack of mandatory security awareness training for new hires introduces a human element risk, making new employees potentially susceptible to social engineering attacks.

These findings highlight crucial areas requiring immediate attention to bolster the organization’s defensive capabilities against common cyber threats.

Recommendations

Based on the identified risks and gaps, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Hidden Gem’s cybersecurity posture:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Immediately implement and mandate MFA for all organizational email accounts.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if credentials are stolen.

  2. Extend Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all systems that store or process sensitive organizational data.

    • Benefit: Provides an additional layer of security for critical assets, protecting against unauthorized access and data breaches.

  3. Establish Mandatory Onboarding Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Develop and implement a comprehensive, mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees as part of their onboarding process.

    • Benefit: Equips new hires with the knowledge to identify and avoid common cyber threats, reducing the human risk factor from day one.

  4. Conduct Regular Technical Assessments:

    • Action: Implement a schedule for regular (e.g., quarterly) external and internal network vulnerability scans and annual penetration tests.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies technical vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and outdated software that could be exploited by attackers.

  5. Maintain a Centralized Risk Register and Management Process:

    • Action: Establish a formal process to identify, assess, track, and mitigate cybersecurity risks, maintaining a comprehensive risk register.

    • Benefit: Ensures that all known risks are documented, prioritized, and addressed systematically, improving overall risk management maturity.
