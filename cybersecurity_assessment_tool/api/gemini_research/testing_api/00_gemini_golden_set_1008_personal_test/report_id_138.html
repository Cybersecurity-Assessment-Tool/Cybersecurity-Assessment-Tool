Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The questionnaire responses indicate several critical gaps in the organization’s security posture:

  • Insufficient Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not uniformly applied across critical access points. Specifically, it is not required for email access (Q1) or for accessing sensitive data systems (Q3). This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access through compromised credentials.

  • Lack of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an employee acceptable use policy (Q4) leaves employees without clear guidelines on appropriate use of organizational assets, potentially leading to misuse, data breaches, or compliance violations.

  • Incomplete Security Awareness Training (SAT): While new employees receive training (Q5), annual security awareness training for all employees (Q6) is not conducted. This oversight can lead to a degradation of security awareness over time, making employees more susceptible to social engineering attacks and other common threats.

Recommendations

Based on the identified risks and gaps, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Granite Shield’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions

  • Implement MFA Across Critical Systems:

    • Extend mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to all employee email accounts to prevent unauthorized access.

    • Enforce MFA for access to all systems containing sensitive organizational or client data.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines permissible and impermissible uses of company IT resources.

    • Communicate the AUP to all employees and require acknowledgment of understanding and compliance.

  • Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Establish a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, covering topics like phishing, password hygiene, data handling, and social engineering.

    • Track completion rates and reinforce key concepts throughout the year.

Long-Term Strategic Recommendations

  • Address Pre-existing Risks Systematically:

    • Investigate the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk (if it exists on other hosts, has been intermittently active, or applies to public-facing assets). If port 80 is confirmed open on any public-facing or internal server, ensure traffic is redirected to HTTPS (port 443) with a valid SSL/TLS certificate, or close the port if not required.

    • Regularly review and update the organization’s risk register, correlating documented risks with active scanning and assessment results to ensure accuracy.

  • Continuous Security Monitoring:

    • Implement continuous monitoring solutions to detect configuration drift, unauthorized changes, and suspicious activities across the network.

    • Periodically conduct vulnerability scans and penetration tests on both internal and external assets to proactively identify weaknesses.

Conclusion

While the recent network scan on 192.168.0.5 did not reveal any open ports, the comprehensive assessment identified several critical policy and procedural gaps within Granite Shield’s cybersecurity posture. The lack of pervasive MFA across critical systems, the absence of a formal Acceptable Use Policy, and the inadequacy of annual security awareness training represent significant areas of concern. Addressing these fundamental issues, alongside systematically managing documented pre-existing risks, will significantly enhance the organization’s resilience against common cyber threats. Proactive implementation of the recommended actions will contribute to a more robust and mature security environment for Granite Shield.
