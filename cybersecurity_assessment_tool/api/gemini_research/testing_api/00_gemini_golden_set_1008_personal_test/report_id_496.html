Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive assessment of the cybersecurity posture for North Star Education, derived from an analysis of organizational data, an internal network scan, and an evaluation of existing risks. While the technical scan of an internal host revealed a minimal network attack surface with no open ports detected, significant vulnerabilities were identified in critical security controls. Key findings highlight the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computer logins, alongside a lack of essential security policies and employee training. These deficiencies represent high-risk areas that expose the organization to prevalent cyber threats such as phishing, credential theft, and insider threats. Immediate recommendations are provided to implement MFA, establish foundational security policies, and initiate robust security awareness training programs to bolster the organization’s resilience.

Analysis of Control Gaps

The questionnaire results expose significant gaps in critical security controls. Notably, the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for both email access and computer logins introduces a high risk of account compromise. While MFA is correctly implemented for sensitive data systems, its lack in these other fundamental areas makes the organization highly susceptible to attacks like phishing, credential stuffing, and password reuse. Furthermore, the non-existence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and the complete absence of security awareness training (both for new hires and annually for all staff) highlight a critical unaddressed human risk factor. These policy and training voids can lead to employee error, policy violations, and increased susceptibility to social engineering attacks, profoundly impacting the overall security posture.

Recommendations

To effectively mitigate the identified risks and strengthen the organization’s overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided, categorized by priority:

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Enable and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational email services. This is a fundamental control to protect against credential theft and phishing.

    • Consider phishing-resistant MFA options where feasible.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Deploy and enforce MFA for all user logins to internal computers and systems. This adds a critical layer of security to prevent unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised.

  3. Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Develop and implement a mandatory, engaging security awareness training program for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training for all existing employees to keep them informed about current threats, organizational policies, and best security practices.

    • Incorporate regular phishing simulations to test employee vigilance and reinforce training.

Mid-Term Actions (Medium Priority)

  1. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, concise, and comprehensive AUP that outlines the proper and secure use of all organizational IT resources, data, and systems.

    • Distribute the AUP to all employees and require formal acknowledgment of understanding and compliance.

  2. Conduct External Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing:

    • Commission an external vulnerability assessment and penetration test specifically targeting the organization’s external-facing assets (e.g., and associated web applications/services).

    • This will provide a crucial understanding of the organization’s perimeter security.

Long-Term Considerations

  1. Review and Harden Network Segmentation and Firewall Rules:

    • Periodically review and optimize network segmentation and firewall rules to ensure proper isolation of sensitive systems and services within the internal network.

    • Implement egress filtering to control outbound network traffic.

  2. Develop and Practice an Incident Response Plan (IRP):

    • Develop a comprehensive Incident Response Plan (IRP) that outlines procedures for effectively preparing for, detecting, analyzing, containing, eradicating, recovering from, and post-incident reviewing security incidents.

    • Conduct regular tabletop exercises to test the IRP and ensure staff readiness.
