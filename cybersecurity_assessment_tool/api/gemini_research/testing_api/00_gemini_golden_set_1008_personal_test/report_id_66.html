Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Midnight Oil Studios, derived from a combination of an organizational security questionnaire, a network scan of an internal host (192.168.0.5), and a review of previously identified risks. The analysis reveals several critical security gaps primarily related to organizational policies and employee training, notably the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins, the absence of a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and infrequent annual security awareness training.

While the network scan on the specified internal target revealed no open ports, which is a positive technical finding, it also contradicted a pre-existing risk concerning an "Unencrypted Web Server" on Port 80, suggesting potential remediation or a different scope. The most significant risks identified are procedural and human-centric, requiring immediate attention to strengthen the overall security posture. Addressing these policy and training deficiencies will significantly reduce the organization’s attack surface and improve resilience against common cyber threats.

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve Midnight Oil Studios’ cybersecurity posture.

Policy and Procedure Recommendations

  1. Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all internal computer logins. This should cover both local and domain-joined machines.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of credential compromise and unauthorized access, aligning with best practices for identity and access management.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) outlining appropriate use of company IT resources, data handling, internet usage, and security expectations.

    • Action: Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge (sign) the AUP, ideally on an annual basis or upon significant updates.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces misuse, enhances legal and compliance posture, and sets expectations for employee behavior regarding IT assets.

Training and Awareness Recommendations

  1. Mandate Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory, annual security awareness training program for all employees, supplementing the new employee training already in place.

    • Action: Training should cover current threats such as sophisticated phishing attacks, social engineering tactics, ransomware, password hygiene best practices, and data protection policies.

    • Benefit: Educates employees on evolving cyber threats, improves their ability to recognize and report suspicious activities, and strengthens the organization’s "human firewall" against common attacks.

Technical and Verification Recommendations

  1. Verify Remediation of "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk:

    • Action: Conduct a comprehensive review to confirm that the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk, previously associated with Port 80, is indeed fully remediated across all relevant systems. While the Nmap scan of 192.168.0.5 shows Port 80 closed, this should be verified for all external and internal web services within the organization’s scope.

    • Action: Ensure that any public-facing web services utilize HTTPS with valid SSL/TLS certificates for encrypted communication.

    • Benefit: Ensures consistency in security posture, resolves potentially lingering vulnerabilities, and protects sensitive data in transit.
