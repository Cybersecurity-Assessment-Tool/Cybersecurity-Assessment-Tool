Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The review highlights several critical areas requiring immediate attention:

  • MFA for Email Access: The absence of MFA for email access significantly increases the risk of account compromise through phishing or credential stuffing attacks. Email accounts often serve as a gateway to other critical systems.

  • MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Failure to enforce MFA for sensitive data systems leaves critical organizational data vulnerable to unauthorized access, potentially leading to data breaches and regulatory non-compliance.

  • Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees are often prime targets for social engineering attacks due to their unfamiliarity with organizational security policies and common threat vectors. The lack of immediate training represents a significant initial vulnerability.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Top Tier, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts across the organization. This should be a top priority due to the critical nature of email in business operations and as an attack vector.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Extend the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication to all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational or client data. Prioritize systems based on data classification and potential impact of compromise.

  • Establish New Employee Security Awareness Training: Develop and implement a mandatory cybersecurity awareness training program specifically for all new hires. This training should be conducted as part of the onboarding process, before employees gain full access to organizational systems.

  • Regular Security Audits: Conduct periodic comprehensive security audits that include penetration testing, vulnerability assessments, and policy reviews to proactively identify and remediate weaknesses.

  • Continuous Employee Training: While annual training is conducted, consider increasing the frequency or introducing smaller, more targeted training modules throughout the year to reinforce security best practices and address emerging threats.

Conclusion

While the technical scan on the target 192.168.1.100 showed positive results with no open ports, the organizational security review revealed significant policy and procedural deficiencies, particularly concerning the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication and security awareness training for new employees. Addressing these gaps is paramount to fortifying Top Tier’s defense against common and sophisticated cyber threats. Proactive implementation of the recommended controls will significantly reduce the organization’s overall risk exposure.
