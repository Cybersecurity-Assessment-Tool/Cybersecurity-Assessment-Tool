Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

Stellar Pathways has commendably implemented Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across critical access points (email, computers, sensitive data systems), which significantly enhances protection against unauthorized access. The commitment to annual security awareness training for all employees is also a positive step.

However, two significant security gaps were identified:

  • Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of a formal AUP means employees may lack clear guidelines regarding the appropriate use of company IT resources, data handling, and internet usage. This can lead to increased risks of misuse, unintentional data leakage, and compliance violations.

  • Absence of Security Awareness Training for New Employees: While existing employees receive annual training, the lack of initial security awareness training for new hires leaves them vulnerable during a critical onboarding period. New employees are often prime targets for social engineering attempts and may inadvertently introduce risks without proper early education on security best practices.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Stellar Pathways. These are categorized into immediate actions and future considerations that depend on the availability of technical data.

Immediate Actions

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines employee responsibilities regarding the use of all IT assets, including computers, networks, internet access, email, and data handling procedures.

    • Ensure the AUP covers topics such as prohibited activities, data classification, privacy expectations, and incident reporting.

    • Formally disseminate the AUP to all current employees, requiring a signed acknowledgment of understanding and compliance. Integrate AUP review and acknowledgment into the annual training cycle.

  • Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Integrate a dedicated security awareness training module into the onboarding process for all new hires.

    • This training should cover essential topics such as recognizing phishing and social engineering attempts, strong password practices, data protection principles, clean desk policy, and the procedure for reporting suspicious activities or incidents.

    • Ensure this initial training is distinct from, but complementary to, the annual security awareness training for all employees.

Future Considerations (Contingent on Technical Data Availability)

Once comprehensive network scan data and existing vulnerability assessments become available, the following general best practices should be investigated and implemented:

  • Conduct Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Systematically review scan results to identify and prioritize patching and remediation of all identified vulnerabilities, especially those related to outdated software, unpatched systems, and misconfigured services.

    • Establish a recurring schedule for internal and external vulnerability scans and periodic penetration tests to proactively identify and address weaknesses in the network and applications.

  • Review Network Configuration and Firewall Rules:

    • Scrutinize all open ports on the external IP address to ensure they are strictly necessary for business operations. Close any unnecessary ports.

    • Ensure that all exposed services are securely configured, utilizing strong encryption (e.g., TLS 1.2+), strong authentication mechanisms, and minimal privileges.

    • Implement robust firewall rules to restrict access to internal resources from external networks to only authorized and necessary traffic.

  • Implement Robust Asset Management and Patch Management Policies:

    • Maintain an accurate and up-to-date inventory of all IT assets, including hardware, software, operating systems, and their respective versions.

    • Establish and enforce a strict patch management policy to ensure that all systems, applications, and network devices are regularly updated with the latest security patches and firmware.
