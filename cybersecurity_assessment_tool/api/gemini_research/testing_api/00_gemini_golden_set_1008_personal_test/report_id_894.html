Risk Assessment

This section correlates findings from the questionnaire, technical scan, and pre-existing risk data to provide a comprehensive view of the organization’s risk landscape.

Identified Critical Gaps (from Questionnaire)

Based on the security control review, the following critical gaps have been identified, posing significant risks to the organization’s security and data integrity:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Without Multi-Factor Authentication, email accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks. A compromise could lead to unauthorized access, data breaches, and business email compromise (BEC), which often results in financial losses.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Failure to enforce MFA on systems containing sensitive organizational or client data significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access should credentials become compromised. This could lead to data exfiltration, regulatory non-compliance, and severe reputational damage.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): An AUP is a foundational policy for setting clear expectations for employee conduct regarding IT assets and data. Its absence can lead to misuse of resources, increased risk of malware infections, data leakage through improper handling, and potential legal liabilities due to unauthorized activities.

The following risk was provided as a current vulnerability for the organization:

  • Risk Name: Unencrypted Web Server

  • Overview: Port 80 is open.

  • Severity (CVSS Score): 5.0 (Medium)

  • Affected Elements: Port 80

Further investigation is critically required to confirm the applicability and current status of this risk.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and critical gaps, and to improve the overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all employee email accounts. This is a fundamental control to significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access, even if passwords are compromised through phishing or other means.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational or client data. Prioritize systems based on data classification and impact.

  • Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP outlining acceptable use of company IT resources, internet, email, and data handling practices. Ensure all employees are thoroughly trained on, and formally acknowledge, this policy.

  • Investigate "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk:

    • Verify if the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk pertains to an asset other than 192.168.0.5, specifically any external-facing web servers or other internal servers that may not have been in scope for the provided Nmap scan.

    • If the risk is still valid for an existing server, immediately ensure all web traffic is encrypted using HTTPS with up-to-date and properly configured SSL/TLS certificates. Redirect all HTTP traffic to HTTPS.

    • If the risk was indeed tied to the scanned asset 192.168.0.5 and port 80 is now confirmed closed and unnecessary, formally document the mitigation and close the risk in the risk register.

  • Continue and Enhance Security Awareness Training: Maintain and regularly update the existing security awareness training programs for all employees. Emphasize identification of emerging threats like phishing, social engineering tactics, and the importance of strong password hygiene and MFA.
