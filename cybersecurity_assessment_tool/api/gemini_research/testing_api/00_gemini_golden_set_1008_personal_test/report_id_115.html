Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of the organizational data and technical scan results, the following key risks have been identified:

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access Without Multi-Factor Authentication, email accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks. Compromised email can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data exfiltration, and further network intrusion. High
Lack of MFA for Computer Logins Without MFA for computer logins, an attacker gaining initial access to credentials can easily compromise internal systems, leading to lateral movement and privilege escalation. High
Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) A lack of an AUP means employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding IT asset usage, data handling, and secure computing practices, increasing the risk of misuse or negligence. Medium
No Security Awareness Training (New Employees) New employees, lacking initial security awareness training, are more likely to fall victim to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing), introduce malware, or violate security policies inadvertently. Medium
No Annual Security Awareness Training Without regular security awareness training, employees’ knowledge of current threats and best practices can degrade, making the organization more vulnerable to evolving attack techniques. Medium
Open SSH Service An open SSH port, while legitimate, presents a potential attack surface. Without proper configuration (e.g., strong passwords/key-based auth, disabled root login, rate limiting, up-to-date versions), it can be exploited for unauthorized access. Low

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Wildfire Communications, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Broadly:

    • Priority 1 (High): Implement MFA for all email accounts immediately.

    • Priority 2 (High): Implement MFA for all computer logins, especially for administrative accounts and systems processing sensitive data.

    • Review and ensure MFA is consistently applied to all sensitive data systems.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a clear and comprehensive AUP outlining appropriate use of company IT resources, data handling guidelines, and security expectations for all employees.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and acknowledge the AUP, and integrate it into onboarding processes.

  • Establish a Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Priority 1 (High): Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • Priority 2 (High): Conduct mandatory security awareness training for all employees at least once per year, covering current threats like phishing, social engineering, and data protection best practices.

    • Incorporate simulated phishing exercises to test and reinforce employee awareness.

  • Secure SSH Service on :

    • Review the configuration of the SSH service on .

    • Implement best practices: disable root login, enforce key-based authentication (disabling password authentication), use strong ciphers, and implement rate limiting or IP restrictions to prevent brute-force attacks.

    • Ensure the SSH server software is up-to-date to patch any known vulnerabilities.

  • Conduct Advanced Network Scanning:

    • Perform more detailed network scans that include service version detection to identify potentially outdated or vulnerable software.

    • Expand the scope of scans to cover all publicly accessible assets and internal network segments.

Conclusion

Wildfire Communications faces significant risks due to unaddressed foundational security controls. The immediate implementation of MFA, an Acceptable Use Policy, and a robust security awareness training program are crucial steps to elevate the organization’s cybersecurity posture. Addressing the configuration of the identified open SSH service and conducting further detailed technical assessments will also contribute significantly to reducing the attack surface. Consistent adherence to these recommendations will foster a more secure and resilient environment against cyber threats.
