Executive Summary

The cybersecurity posture assessment for Clear Path has identified several high-priority risks requiring immediate attention. Key findings include:

  • Critical MFA Gaps: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is not required for email access or computer logins, significantly increasing the risk of unauthorized access through compromised credentials.

  • Policy Deficiencies: The organization lacks an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), which is crucial for defining expected employee conduct regarding technology use and data handling.

  • Critical Technical Exposure: An Nmap scan revealed an open port 8080 on target 10.5.5.5 exposing a service explicitly titled "TOP SECRET DB".

  • Severe Risk Management Discrepancy: Clear Path’s internal risk register indicates "Port 8080" as "secured" with a zero severity, directly conflicting with the live scan results. This contradiction represents a critical failure in the organization’s risk identification and assessment processes, indicating a potential unknown and unmitigated exposure of highly sensitive information.

These findings collectively indicate a need for urgent security enhancements, particularly concerning access controls and the accuracy of internal risk assessments.

Discrepancy Highlight: Port 8080 Status

A critical discrepancy has been identified between the live technical scan results and the organization’s existing risk register:

  • Technical Scan (Current State): Confirms port 8080 is open on 10.5.5.5 and exposes a service explicitly titled "TOP SECRET DB".

  • Existing Risk Register (Assessed State): Lists a vulnerability "Port 8080 Secured" with an overview stating "Port 8080 is confirmed secure and false positive", assigned a CVSS score of 0.0.

This contradiction is a severe finding. It indicates that Clear Path has either:

  1. Misidentified, misclassified, or completely missed a critical exposure.

  2. Experienced a change in their environment (e.g., a new service deployment) that was not reflected in their risk assessment process.

  3. An internal system has been compromised or misconfigured to expose this data.

Regardless of the cause, the current state presents an unacknowledged and highly critical data exposure that warrants immediate and thorough investigation.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve Clear Path’s cybersecurity posture:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Action: Urgently deploy and enforce MFA for all employee email accounts and for all computer login processes.

    • Rationale: MFA is a fundamental security control that significantly reduces the risk of credential theft leading to unauthorized access.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, approve, implement, and formally communicate a comprehensive AUP to all employees. Ensure mandatory acknowledgment and regular review.

    • Rationale: An AUP establishes clear expectations for appropriate technology use, reducing internal risks and promoting a culture of security.

  3. Immediate Investigation and Remediation of Port 8080 Exposure:

    • Action: Immediately investigate the service on port 8080 on 10.5.5.5. Determine the nature of the "TOP SECRET DB", the sensitivity of data it contains, and its intended accessibility.

    • Action: Based on the investigation, either secure the service with strong authentication/authorization and network segmentation, or if it’s not required, immediately shut down the service and close the port.

    • Rationale: This is a critical exposure that appears to be unmitigated and misidentified internally. Prompt action is necessary to prevent potential data breaches.

  4. Review and Overhaul Risk Management and Asset Inventory Processes:

    • Action: Conduct a thorough review of the organization’s vulnerability management, risk assessment, and asset inventory processes. Identify why the discrepancy regarding port 8080 occurred and implement measures to prevent recurrence.

    • Action: Ensure that technical scan results are regularly correlated with the internal risk register and that risk assessments are updated promptly based on new findings.

    • Rationale: Accurate and up-to-date risk assessment is crucial for effective cybersecurity posture management.

  5. Reinforce Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Continue with the existing security awareness training programs, with particular emphasis on social engineering, phishing, and the importance of adhering to security policies (once the AUP is established).

    • Rationale: A well-informed workforce is the first line of defense against many cyber threats.

By addressing these recommendations, Clear Path can significantly enhance its security posture, reduce its attack surface, and improve its ability to identify and respond to cybersecurity threats effectively.
