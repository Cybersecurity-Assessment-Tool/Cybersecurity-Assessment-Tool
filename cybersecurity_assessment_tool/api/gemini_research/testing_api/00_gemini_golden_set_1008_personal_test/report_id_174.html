Executive Summary

This report details the cybersecurity posture of Brimstone Manufacturing, based on a correlation of a recent network scan, organizational security questionnaire responses, and documented pre-existing risks.

The organization demonstrates good adherence to security best practices in several areas, including employee acceptable use policies, initial and annual security awareness training, and the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins and sensitive data systems.

However, a significant vulnerability exists with the lack of MFA for email access. This oversight represents a critical attack vector that could lead to account compromise, phishing success, and broader network intrusion.

The technical scan of the internal target showed only Port 80 (HTTP) as closed, with no other open ports or active services identified. This finding contradicts a pre-existing risk identifying an "Unencrypted Web Server" on Port 80, suggesting either remediation or a discrepancy in scope.

Recommendations prioritize the immediate implementation of MFA for email, further investigation into the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk, and a continuous improvement approach to cybersecurity controls.

Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The most significant gap identified from the questionnaire is the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access. This is a critical deficiency, as email is a primary communication vector and a frequent target for phishing and account compromise attacks. Without MFA, compromised credentials can grant attackers full access to an employee’s inbox, potentially leading to further internal reconnaissance, data exfiltration, or business email compromise (BEC) scams.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Brimstone Manufacturing’s cybersecurity posture:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access (High Priority):

    • Immediately enable and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This is the most critical and impactful recommendation.

    • Evaluate suitable MFA solutions that integrate with the existing email infrastructure (e.g., Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, on-premise solutions).

    • Communicate the importance and provide training to all employees on the use of MFA.

  2. Review and Verify "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk:

    • Investigate the discrepancy between the pre-existing "Unencrypted Web Server" risk and the recent scan finding of Port 80 being closed on .

    • Determine if the risk was indeed remediated, or if it pertains to another server or public-facing asset not included in this scan.

    • Ensure all public-facing and internal web services transmitting sensitive data utilize HTTPS with valid SSL/TLS certificates. Implement redirection from HTTP to HTTPS where applicable.

  3. Expand Scope of Technical Scans:

    • Conduct regular, comprehensive vulnerability scans across all internal and external network assets.

    • Ensure scans include authenticated checks to identify vulnerabilities within operating systems and applications.

    • Implement robust asset inventory management to ensure all critical systems are regularly scanned and assessed.

  4. Continuous Security Improvement:

    • Maintain regular security awareness training, reinforcing topics like phishing recognition and the importance of MFA.

    • Periodically review and update the employee acceptable use policy to reflect current threats and organizational practices.

    • Consider engaging in simulated phishing exercises to test employee resilience against social engineering attacks.
