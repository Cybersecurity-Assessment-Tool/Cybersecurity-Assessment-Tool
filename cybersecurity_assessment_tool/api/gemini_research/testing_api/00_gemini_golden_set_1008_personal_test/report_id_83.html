Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity posture assessment for Ember Glow Hospitality, conducted by analyzing network scan data, reviewing organizational security practices, and correlating with existing risk information. The assessment identified several critical gaps, most notably the absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access and computer logins, which represent high-risk vulnerabilities. Additionally, an existing "Localhost Exposed" risk with a CVSS score of 10.0 was confirmed by technical scans, indicating a critical internal security concern. While basic security awareness training is in place, the lack of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) presents a governance gap. Immediate recommendations focus on implementing MFA, thoroughly investigating and remediating the localhost exposure, and establishing foundational security policies. Addressing these key areas will significantly enhance the organization’s overall cybersecurity posture.

Key Questionnaire Gaps

The following control deficiencies were identified based on the "No" responses:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: This is a critical security gap. Email accounts are frequent targets for phishing and business email compromise (BEC) attacks. Without MFA, a single compromised password can grant an attacker full access, leading to data breaches and further network infiltration.

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Login: Similar to email, the absence of MFA for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to endpoints. Stolen or weak credentials can allow attackers direct access to internal systems.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A lack of a formal AUP means employees may not have clear guidelines regarding the appropriate use of company IT resources. This can lead to increased risks of data leakage, malware infections from prohibited websites, and other security incidents due to unintentional misuse.

Detailed Risk Analysis

  • Localhost Exposed (127.0.0.1): The Nmap scan confirmed an open port 22 on 127.0.0.1, directly correlating with the "Localhost Exposed" risk previously identified with a CVSS score of 10.0. This indicates a critical vulnerability within the system itself. Although localhost services are not directly Internet-facing, they can be exploited by an attacker who has already gained an initial foothold, potentially leading to privilege escalation or lateral movement. Immediate investigation into the service running on port 22 and its security context is paramount.

  • MFA Gaps: The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication for both email access and computer logins are two of the most severe security findings. Weak or compromised credentials are a leading cause of data breaches. Without MFA, Ember Glow Hospitality is significantly more susceptible to attacks like phishing, brute-forcing, and credential stuffing, which can lead to widespread system compromise and data loss.

  • No Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of a formal AUP creates ambiguity regarding expected employee behavior concerning IT resources. This gap can lead to increased risks from insider threats (both malicious and unintentional), compliance violations, and a general lack of control over the security posture resulting from user actions.

Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive assessment, the following recommendations are provided to enhance Ember Glow Hospitality’s cybersecurity posture, categorized by urgency and impact.

Immediate Actions (Critical/High Risks)

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Urgently deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all organizational email accounts. This should be a top priority to protect against credential theft and phishing attacks.

  • Implement MFA for Computer Login: Roll out and enforce MFA for all user and administrative logins to company computers and servers. This is crucial for protecting endpoints from unauthorized access.

  • Investigate and Remediate "Localhost Exposed" Risk: Immediately investigate the service running on port 22 of 127.0.0.1. Determine if this service is necessary, properly configured, and securely hardened. If it is not required, disable or remove it. If essential, ensure it is configured with the principle of least privilege and robust authentication. Given the CVSS 10.0 rating, this requires urgent attention.

Mid-Term Actions (Medium Risks)

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities for using company IT resources. Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge this policy. Integrate it into employee onboarding and annual reviews.

  • Enhance Security Awareness Training: While current training exists, tailor future security awareness campaigns to specifically address the identified gaps, such as the importance of MFA, recognizing phishing attempts, and adherence to the new AUP.

Long-Term Strategy

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing: Establish a program for regular internal and external vulnerability scanning and periodic penetration testing to proactively identify and address security weaknesses.

  • Robust Patch Management Program: Implement an automated and consistent patch management process for all operating systems, applications, and network devices to minimize vulnerabilities from outdated software.

  • Incident Response Plan Development and Testing: Develop a comprehensive incident response plan and conduct regular drills and tabletop exercises to ensure the organization can effectively detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents.

Conclusion

Ember Glow Hospitality demonstrates a foundational commitment to security awareness training. However, the assessment reveals critical vulnerabilities, particularly concerning the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication for email and computer access, and the high-severity "Localhost Exposed" risk. The absence of an Acceptable Use Policy further indicates a need for enhanced governance. By prioritizing and implementing the recommended actions, Ember Glow Hospitality can significantly strengthen its cybersecurity posture, reduce its attack surface, and build a more resilient defense against evolving cyber threats.
