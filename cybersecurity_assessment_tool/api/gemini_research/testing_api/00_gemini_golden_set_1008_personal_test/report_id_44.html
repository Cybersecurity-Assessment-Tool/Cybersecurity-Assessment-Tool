Risk Assessment

This section correlates findings from the security control review, technical scan, and pre-existing risk data to provide a comprehensive view of the organization’s current risk posture.

Identified Gaps and Vulnerabilities

  • Critical Gap: Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Overview: Without MFA, compromised credentials (e.g., via phishing attacks) can lead to unauthorized access to email accounts. This can enable business email compromise, data exfiltration, or serve as a pivot point for further internal network attacks.

    • Severity: High

  • Critical Gap: Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Login:

    • Overview: The absence of MFA for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems, workstations, and servers should user credentials be compromised. This could lead to data breaches, ransomware attacks, or unauthorized system control.

    • Severity: High

  • Technical Vulnerability: Open Port 80 (HTTP) with Unknown Service:

    • Overview: An open HTTP port indicates a potential web server or service accessible on the network. Without details on the service, product, or version, its purpose, configuration, and potential vulnerabilities cannot be assessed. This could expose the organization to web-based attacks, information leakage, or unencrypted data transmission if not properly secured, regularly patched, and monitored.

    • Severity: Medium (Pending further investigation)

Recommendations

Based on the assessment, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of Pioneer Pulse. Prioritizing these actions will help mitigate critical risks and strengthen overall defenses.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all email accounts across the organization, including both on-premise and cloud-based email services.

    • Rationale: MFA provides an essential and highly effective layer of security, significantly reducing the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised or stolen credentials.

    • Priority: Critical

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Login:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all user logins to company computers, workstations, and servers, especially those that access sensitive data or critical network resources.

    • Rationale: Protects against credential stuffing, brute-force attacks, and phishing attempts targeting login credentials for internal systems.

    • Priority: Critical

  3. Investigate and Secure Open Port 80 on :

    • Action: Conduct a detailed investigation into the service running on port 80 of 172.16.0.1. Identify its purpose, product, and version.

    • Action: If it is a necessary web service, ensure it is configured securely, patched to the latest version, and enforces HTTPS for all traffic to protect data in transit. If the service is not required, the port should be closed or access restricted to trusted sources only.

    • Rationale: Unidentified open ports can be entry points for attackers. Encrypting web traffic prevents eavesdropping and protects sensitive data.

    • Priority: High

  4. Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Vulnerability Scans:

    • Action: Implement a routine schedule for detailed vulnerability scanning of all internal and external network assets, including web application scans if applicable.

    • Rationale: Regular and in-depth scans help identify new vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and outdated software versions that could expose the organization to evolving threats.

    • Priority: Medium

Conclusion

While Pioneer Pulse demonstrates good foundational practices in areas such as security awareness training and acceptable use policies, significant vulnerabilities exist regarding the lack of multi-factor authentication for core systems like email and computer logins. The presence of an unidentified open port 80 on an internal target also warrants immediate attention and further investigation. Addressing these critical gaps through the recommended actions will substantially strengthen the organization’s cybersecurity posture, reduce its overall risk profile, and better protect against common attack vectors.
