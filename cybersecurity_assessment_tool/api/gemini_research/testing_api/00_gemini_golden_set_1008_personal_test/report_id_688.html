1. Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Atlas Mapping, derived from the analysis of network scan data, organizational security questionnaire responses, and a review of existing risk information. The assessment highlights critical security gaps, predominantly in the areas of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access and the implementation of robust security awareness training programs. While the network scan on the internal target indicated no immediately exploitable open ports, the identified procedural and policy weaknesses represent significant risks to the organization’s overall security. Addressing the recommendations outlined in this report is paramount for strengthening Atlas Mapping’s defensive capabilities against current cyber threats.

5. Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of security controls, technical scan results, and the absence of pre-existing vulnerabilities from the provided data, the following key risks have been identified:

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access Email accounts are highly targeted for phishing and credential compromise. Without MFA, a single compromised password can lead to full account takeover, facilitating lateral movement within the network, and potential data breaches. Critical
Inadequate New Employee Security Awareness Training New employees are often prime targets for social engineering attacks due to unfamiliarity with organizational policies and common threats. The absence of initial security training significantly increases the risk of security incidents. High
Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training Cybersecurity threats constantly evolve, and employee knowledge can degrade over time. Without regular, mandatory security awareness training for all employees, the workforce’s understanding of current risks and best practices will diminish, increasing susceptibility to attacks. High

6. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance Atlas Mapping’s overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all email accounts across the organization. This is a fundamental control against credential theft, phishing, and account takeover.

  • Establish Mandatory New Employee Security Awareness Training: Develop and implement a comprehensive security awareness training program that is mandatory for all new hires. This training should be completed before new employees gain access to organizational systems and sensitive data.

  • Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Implement a recurring (at least annual) security awareness training program for all existing employees. This training should be updated regularly to cover current threat landscapes, organizational policies, and best practices.

  • Regular Vulnerability Management Program: While the initial scan found no open ports, consider implementing a continuous vulnerability scanning program for all internal and external assets. This should include authenticated vulnerability scans for internal hosts to identify software misconfigurations and patch deficiencies.

  • Review and Test Incident Response Plan: Ensure an up-to-date and regularly tested incident response plan is in place to effectively manage and mitigate potential security incidents stemming from identified risks, especially those related to credential compromise or social engineering.
