Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and strengthen the cybersecurity posture of Iron Oak Furniture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Accounts:

    • Enable and rigorously enforce MFA for all organizational email accounts. This is a primary defense against phishing and credential stuffing attacks, significantly reducing the risk of email compromise.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Mandate MFA for all access to systems storing or processing sensitive organizational or customer data. This critical step protects against unauthorized access to high-value information.

  3. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a detailed AUP outlining the proper use of company IT resources, internet, email, and data.

    • Ensure all employees, new and existing, review and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally during onboarding and annually thereafter, with consequences for non-compliance.

  4. Enhance New Employee Security Awareness Training:

    • Integrate mandatory, comprehensive security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new hires. This training should be completed before they gain access to company systems.

    • Training should cover essential topics such as phishing identification, strong password hygiene, secure data handling, incident reporting procedures, and the organization’s AUP.

  5. Conduct a Valid External Network Scan:

    • Perform a comprehensive external network vulnerability scan to identify open ports, outdated services, misconfigurations, and potential vulnerabilities on all internet-facing assets.

    • Regularly review and remediate findings from these scans to reduce the external attack surface.

  6. Establish a Robust Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Implement a systematic process for identifying, assessing, prioritizing, tracking, and remediating vulnerabilities across all IT systems and applications.

    • Maintain an accurate and up-to-date register of known vulnerabilities and their remediation status to facilitate continuous improvement.

Conclusion

While Iron Oak Furniture currently employs some foundational security practices (e.g., MFA for computer login, annual security training), significant vulnerabilities exist due to the lack of MFA in critical areas (email, sensitive data systems) and the absence of fundamental security policies (AUP) and initial employee training. Addressing these identified gaps immediately and implementing the provided recommendations will substantially reduce the organization’s risk profile, enhance its resilience against sophisticated cyber threats, and improve overall compliance. Continuous monitoring, regular assessments, and ongoing improvement of security controls are essential for maintaining a strong and adaptive cybersecurity posture.
