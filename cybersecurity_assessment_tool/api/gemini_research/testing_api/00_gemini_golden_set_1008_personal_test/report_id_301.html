Executive Summary

This cybersecurity assessment for Clear Path has identified a mix of strengths and critical weaknesses. While the organization employs MFA for email and computer login, a significant gap exists with the lack of MFA for sensitive data systems. Furthermore, the absence of an employee acceptable use policy and annual security awareness training poses substantial risks to internal security. Technical scans revealed an outdated Nginx web server version on an internal host, presenting a known vulnerability. The identified risks, ranging from High to Critical, require immediate attention to mitigate potential exploitation and improve compliance. Recommendations focus on implementing robust access controls, enhancing policy and training frameworks, and maintaining up-to-date software.

Analysis of Gaps

The "No" responses highlight significant security gaps that could lead to increased risk:

  • MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Lack of MFA for sensitive data systems represents a critical vulnerability. If credentials are compromised, an attacker could gain direct access to critical information without a second layer of defense.

  • Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an AUP leaves the organization vulnerable to misuse of company assets, intellectual property theft, and non-compliance with regulatory requirements. It also creates ambiguity regarding employee responsibilities.

  • Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the lack of annual refreshers means that security knowledge can degrade over time, and employees may not be aware of evolving threats (e.g., new phishing tactics, social engineering techniques).

Technical Analysis

The most significant finding from the technical scan is the presence of Nginx version 1.18.0. Nginx 1.18.0 was released in 2020 and is now considered end-of-life (EOL). It no longer receives security patches, making it vulnerable to known exploits that have been discovered since its release. Running EOL software is a high-risk security posture as it leaves the service exposed to unpatched vulnerabilities that could lead to compromise, data breaches, or denial of service. The certificate’s common name ‘www.acme-corp.com‘ suggests this server might be serving an internal application or acting as a proxy.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance Clear Path’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Upgrade Nginx Server:

    • Action: Immediately plan and execute an upgrade of the Nginx server on to the latest stable version (e.g., Nginx 1.24.x or 1.26.x).

    • Mitigation: This will ensure the server benefits from the latest security patches and bug fixes, reducing the attack surface.

    • Priority: High

  2. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Conduct an inventory of all systems storing or processing sensitive data and implement mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all access to these systems.

    • Mitigation: Adds an essential layer of security, significantly reducing the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are stolen.

    • Priority: Critical

  3. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft and formalize a comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy outlining guidelines for the proper use of company IT resources, data, and internet access.

    • Action: Distribute the AUP to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgments, and integrate it into new employee onboarding processes.

    • Mitigation: Establishes clear expectations, reduces insider threats, and helps demonstrate due diligence for compliance purposes.

    • Priority: Medium

  4. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. The training should cover current threats, organizational policies, and best practices.

    • Action: Track completion rates and reinforce key messages periodically.

    • Mitigation: Educates employees on evolving cyber threats, strengthens the human firewall, and fosters a security-conscious culture.

    • Priority: Medium

  5. Implement a Patch Management Strategy:

    • Action: Develop and enforce a formal patch management policy and procedure for all operating systems, applications, and network devices.

    • Mitigation: Ensures all software is kept up-to-date with security patches, preventing exploitation of known vulnerabilities.

    • Priority: High (General, linked to Nginx vulnerability)

Conclusion

This assessment highlights both the progress made by Clear Path in certain security areas (MFA for email and computer login) and critical deficiencies that require immediate attention. By addressing the outlined risks and implementing the recommended actions, Clear Path can significantly improve its overall cybersecurity posture, reduce its attack surface, and enhance its resilience against cyber threats. Continuous monitoring, regular assessments, and a proactive security culture are paramount for long-term security success.
