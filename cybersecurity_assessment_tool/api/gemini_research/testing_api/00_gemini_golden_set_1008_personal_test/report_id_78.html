Executive Summary

This report provides a cybersecurity posture assessment for Fable & Lore, based on organizational data, a recent network scan, and an overview of pre-existing risks. The assessment highlights key strengths, identified vulnerabilities, and areas for improvement.

Overall, Fable & Lore demonstrates a foundational commitment to security, particularly in implementing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins and sensitive data systems, and conducting regular security awareness training. However, critical gaps were identified concerning the absence of MFA for email access and a formal employee acceptable use policy, posing significant risks.

The network scan of revealed positive findings, specifically that TCP port 80 (typically used for unencrypted web traffic) is currently closed. This suggests a potential successful remediation of the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk identified in the organization’s pre-existing vulnerability list, or that the previous risk pertained to a different asset. Despite this positive technical finding, the lack of MFA for email and a formal AUP remain pressing concerns requiring immediate attention.

Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The review highlights two significant gaps in security controls:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Not requiring MFA for email access is a critical vulnerability. Email is a primary vector for phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and account takeover attacks. Without MFA, compromised credentials can grant attackers full access to an employee’s inbox, leading to data breaches, further compromise of systems, and financial fraud.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of an AUP means there are no formal guidelines for employees regarding the appropriate use of company IT resources. This can lead to misuse of systems, increased risk of malware infections, data exfiltration, and legal/compliance issues due to undefined expectations for employee conduct.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance Fable & Lore’s cybersecurity posture.

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all email accounts across the organization. This is a critical control to prevent unauthorized access even if passwords are stolen.

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the proper use of organizational IT assets, including internet, email, software, and hardware. Distribute it to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgements, and integrate it into onboarding processes.

Medium to Long-Term Actions

  • Verify Remediation of Unencrypted Web Server Risk: Although the recent scan found Port 80 closed on , thoroughly investigate the "Unencrypted Web Server" pre-existing risk. Confirm if this vulnerability has been fully remediated across all relevant assets or if it pertains to a different scope. If any unencrypted web services are still required, ensure they are secured with SSL/TLS (HTTPS) and appropriate access controls.

  • Regular Policy Review and Updates: Periodically review and update all security policies, including the AUP, to ensure they remain relevant with evolving threats and technologies.

  • Advanced Security Awareness Training: While current training is in place, consider incorporating more advanced topics, simulated phishing exercises, and role-specific training to further enhance employee resilience against cyber threats.

These recommendations, when implemented, will significantly strengthen Fable & Lore’s defenses against common cyber threats and foster a more robust security environment.
