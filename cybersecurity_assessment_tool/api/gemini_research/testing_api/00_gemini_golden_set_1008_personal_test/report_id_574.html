6. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and further enhance the cybersecurity posture of Modern Myth, the following recommendations are provided:

6.1. High Priority Recommendations

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Research and deploy an MFA solution for all employee computer logins, integrating it with existing identity management systems where possible.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems, even if passwords are compromised.

    • Timeline: Immediate to Short-Term (1-3 months)

6.2. Medium Priority Recommendations

  • Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities, permitted and prohibited uses of company IT assets, internet usage guidelines, and data handling procedures. Once finalized, communicate it to all employees and require acknowledgment of understanding.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear expectations for employee behavior, minimizes risks of misuse, and supports compliance efforts. It also provides a basis for disciplinary action if policies are violated.

    • Timeline: Short-Term (2-4 months)

6.3. Ongoing Best Practices (Already Implemented / Commended)

  • Continue MFA for Email and Sensitive Data: Commendable that MFA is already in place for these critical access points. Maintain and regularly review these controls.

  • Maintain Robust Security Awareness Training Program: The current practice of training new employees and conducting annual training for all staff is excellent. Continue to update training content to reflect current threat landscapes.

  • Maintain Strong External Perimeter Security: The absence of open ports on the external network scan is a strong indicator of good perimeter security. Regularly review firewall rules and network configurations to ensure this posture is maintained.

7. Conclusion

Modern Myth demonstrates a solid foundation in several key areas of cybersecurity, particularly with its adoption of MFA for critical systems and a consistent security awareness training program. The external network’s limited exposure is also a significant strength. Addressing the identified gaps regarding MFA for computer logins and the formalization of an Acceptable Use Policy will further strengthen the organization’s defenses and cultivate a more secure operational environment.
