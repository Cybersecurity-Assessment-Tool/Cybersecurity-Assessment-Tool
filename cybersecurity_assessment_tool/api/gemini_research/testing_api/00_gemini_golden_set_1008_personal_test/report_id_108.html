Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity assessment conducted for Nomad Gear Co.. The assessment incorporated organizational security posture data via a questionnaire, and attempted to incorporate network scan results and pre-existing vulnerability data. Due to significant data unavailability for the network scan and pre-existing vulnerabilities, this report’s findings are primarily based on the organizational security questionnaire.

Key findings indicate critical gaps in multi-factor authentication (MFA) implementation and foundational security awareness and policy enforcement. Specifically, MFA is not universally required for email or computer logins, and the organization lacks an acceptable use policy and consistent security awareness training programs. These areas represent significant vulnerabilities that require immediate attention to enhance the overall security posture of Nomad Gear Co..

Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The questionnaire highlights significant weaknesses in several fundamental security controls, which are crucial for a robust security posture:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Gaps: The lack of MFA enforcement for email access and computer logins is a critical vulnerability. MFA is a fundamental security layer that drastically reduces the risk of account compromise due to stolen or weak credentials. While MFA is used for sensitive data systems, its absence in more common access points (email, general computer login) leaves significant attack vectors open.

  • Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may not fully understand their responsibilities regarding the proper use of company IT resources, internet, and data. This can lead to inadvertent or intentional misuse, increasing the risk of data breaches, malware infections, and compliance issues.

  • Lack of Security Awareness Training: The complete absence of security awareness training for both new and existing employees is a severe gap. Employees are often the first line of defense against cyberattacks. Untrained staff are highly susceptible to social engineering, phishing, and other human-centric attacks, making them the weakest link in the organization’s security chain.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve Nomad Gear Co.’s overall cybersecurity posture. These are prioritized based on severity and their foundational impact on the organization’s security.

Immediate Recommendations (Critical Risks)

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing company email systems (e.g., Office 365, Google Workspace, or on-premise solutions).

    • Benefit: Drastically reduces the risk of email account compromise, protecting against phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and unauthorized data access.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Integrate MFA into the login process for all company computers and workstations, especially for administrative accounts and those with access to sensitive data.

    • Benefit: Enhances protection against unauthorized access to internal systems, preventing lateral movement and privilege escalation by attackers even if a primary password is compromised.

High Priority Recommendations

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft a comprehensive AUP outlining proper use of company IT resources, internet, email, and data handling. Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the policy. Integrate it into onboarding and annual reviews.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces misuse, improves compliance with internal standards and external regulations, and provides a basis for disciplinary action if policies are violated.

  2. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Develop a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires as part of their onboarding process. This should cover common threats (phishing, malware), company policies (AUP, data handling), and best practices for secure computing.

    • Benefit: Equips new employees with essential knowledge to identify and avoid common cyber threats from day one, significantly reducing initial attack vectors into the organization.

  3. Implement Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Conduct annual (at minimum) mandatory security awareness training for all employees. This training should refresh their knowledge, update them on new and evolving threats (e.g., advanced phishing techniques, ransomware trends), and reinforce company-specific security protocols.

    • Benefit: Maintains a high level of security consciousness across the organization, reinforces best practices, and creates a more resilient human firewall against evolving cyber threats.

Further Action Recommendations (Data Dependent)

  1. Conduct a Comprehensive Network Vulnerability Scan:

    • Action: Perform an in-depth external and internal network vulnerability scan of all public-facing assets and internal infrastructure. This should include authenticated scans where possible to identify deeper vulnerabilities.

    • Benefit: Identifies open ports, outdated software, misconfigurations, and known vulnerabilities (CVEs) that could be exploited by attackers. This data is critical for a complete and technical risk assessment.

  2. Review and Address Pre-existing Vulnerabilities:

    • Action: If available from internal tracking or future assessments, analyze and prioritize remediation efforts for any identified pre-existing vulnerabilities. This includes patching software, updating configurations, and applying security fixes.

    • Benefit: Reduces the attack surface by patching known weaknesses and mitigating identified risks, improving the overall security posture and compliance.

Conclusion

While the absence of network scan data and pre-existing vulnerability information limits the scope of this assessment’s technical depth, the questionnaire results clearly indicate several critical security gaps that pose a significant risk to Nomad Gear Co.. Immediate action on implementing MFA for email and computer logins, alongside establishing foundational policies (AUP) and consistent security awareness training, is paramount. Addressing these fundamental issues will substantially enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture, protect its assets, and build resilience against common and evolving cyber threats.
