An open port 80 on the target IP warrants further investigation to ensure it is properly secured and monitored. Addressing these gaps is crucial for enhancing the overall security posture and reducing the attack surface.

Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The organization demonstrates strong adherence to MFA practices across email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems, which is a commendable security baseline. However, two critical areas require immediate attention:

  • Missing Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an AUP (Q4) leaves the organization vulnerable to employee misuse of IT resources, potential data breaches, and non-compliance with regulatory requirements.

  • Lack of Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training (Q5), the lack of annual training for all employees (Q6) means that staff may not be up-to-date on evolving threats, increasing the risk of successful social engineering attacks and phishing campaigns.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement a Comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Develop and formally implement an AUP that clearly outlines permissible and prohibited use of company IT resources, data, and internet access.

    • Distribute the AUP to all employees and obtain signed acknowledgments.

    • Integrate AUP review and acknowledgment into new employee onboarding and annual compliance processes.

  2. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Develop and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires.

    • Training should cover current threats such as phishing, ransomware, social engineering, password hygiene, and data handling best practices.

    • Use engaging and interactive training methods, followed by periodic simulated phishing exercises to test effectiveness.
