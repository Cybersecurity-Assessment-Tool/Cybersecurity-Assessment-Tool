Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Ember Glow Hospitality, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Comprehensive Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Develop and deploy a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires during their onboarding process.

    • Topics should include phishing detection, password best practices, incident reporting procedures, acceptable use policy review, and social engineering awareness.

  2. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Conduct mandatory refresher training for all employees at least once per year.

    • Incorporate current threat intelligence and recent security incidents relevant to the industry or organization.

    • Utilize engaging formats such as interactive modules, simulated phishing campaigns, and short video presentations.

  3. Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Network Vulnerability Scans:

    • Schedule authenticated and unauthenticated vulnerability scans of all external and internal network segments on a regular basis (e.g., monthly or quarterly).

    • Ensure scans cover all active IP ranges and common ports to identify open services, outdated software, and misconfigurations.

    • Implement a robust vulnerability management program to prioritize, track, and remediate identified vulnerabilities.

  4. Maintain an Up-to-Date Risk Register:

    • Establish a formal process for identifying, assessing, and documenting all cybersecurity risks.

    • Regularly review and update the risk register to reflect changes in the threat landscape, organizational environment, and control effectiveness.

  5. Review and Update Security Policies Regularly:

    • Ensure that all security policies, including the acceptable use policy, are reviewed and updated at least annually or when significant organizational or technological changes occur.

    • Communicate policy updates clearly to all employees.
