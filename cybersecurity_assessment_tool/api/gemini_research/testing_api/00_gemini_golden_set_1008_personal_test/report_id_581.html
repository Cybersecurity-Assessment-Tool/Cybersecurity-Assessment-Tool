Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The most significant gap identified in the questionnaire responses is the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for accessing sensitive data systems. This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to critical organizational data if credentials are compromised through phishing, malware, or other means. Without MFA, a single compromised password can lead to a severe data breach.

The discovery of an open RDP service (Port 3389) on is a significant finding. RDP, while essential for remote administration, is a frequent target for attackers due to its exposure and potential vulnerabilities, especially if not adequately secured with strong authentication (like MFA), network restrictions (e.g., VPN requirement, IP whitelisting), and regular patching. Correlation and Impact

The technical scan corroborates a pre-existing concern regarding RDP exposure. While the pre-existing risk highlights , the scan identifies another similar exposure on . The high severity associated with RDP exposure (CVSS 9.0) underscores the critical nature of these findings. Without appropriate controls, exposed RDP services can be exploited for initial access, leading to further lateral movement, data exfiltration, or ransomware deployment.

The absence of MFA for sensitive data systems significantly amplifies the impact of any successful RDP exploitation or other credential theft. If an attacker gains access to a system with an RDP vulnerability, and that system can access sensitive data, the lack of MFA means they can potentially bypass the final layer of defense to access critical information, leading to severe data breaches, regulatory non-compliance, and reputational damage.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of , the following recommendations are provided.

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  1. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all systems and applications that host or provide access to sensitive organizational data.

    • Benefit: This is a critical control to prevent unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised, significantly reducing the risk of data breaches.

  2. Secure Exposed RDP Services:

    • Action: For both and , evaluate the necessity of RDP exposure.

    • Action: If RDP must be externally accessible, enforce strict controls:

      • Implement MFA for RDP logins.

      • Restrict access to a whitelist of trusted IP addresses only.

      • Implement strong, complex password policies and consider account lockout thresholds.

      • Deploy a VPN for all remote access, making RDP accessible only after establishing a secure VPN connection.

      • Action: If RDP is not externally required, ensure port 3389 is blocked at the perimeter firewall and, if possible, implement host-based firewalls to restrict internal access to authorized hosts only.

      • Benefit: Reduces the attack surface and prevents unauthorized remote access attempts.

Long-Term and Continuous Improvements

  1. Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Action: Establish a routine schedule for internal and external vulnerability scanning and periodic penetration testing to proactively identify and remediate security weaknesses.

    • Benefit: Ensures ongoing visibility into the security posture and helps adapt to new threats.

  2. Patch Management Program:

    • Action: Ensure all operating systems, applications, and network devices, especially those with exposed services like RDP, are kept up-to-date with the latest security patches.

    • Benefit: Prevents exploitation of known vulnerabilities.

  3. Security Awareness Training Reinforcement:

    • Action: Continue and expand security awareness training, emphasizing the importance of strong passwords, identifying phishing attempts, and the role of MFA.

    • Benefit: Cultivates a security-conscious culture, making employees the first line of defense.

  4. Review Network Segmentation:

    • Action: Consider implementing network segmentation to isolate sensitive data systems and limit lateral movement by attackers if a compromise occurs in other network segments.

    • Benefit: Contains breaches and limits damage.
