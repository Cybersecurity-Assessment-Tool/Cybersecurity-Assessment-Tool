Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive assessment of the cybersecurity posture for Nomad Gear Co., synthesizing data from organizational questionnaires, a network scan, and pre-existing risk registers. The analysis reveals a commendable implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across critical access points. However, significant vulnerabilities were identified, including the absence of an employee acceptable use policy, insufficient annual security awareness training for all staff, and the exposure of a critical service on localhost. Actionable recommendations are provided to mitigate these risks and enhance the overall security resilience of the organization.

2.1. Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The organization demonstrates a strong commitment to identity protection by requiring Multi-Factor Authentication for email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems. However, two critical gaps were identified:

  • Absence of an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of a formal AUP creates ambiguity regarding acceptable employee conduct when using organizational IT resources. This increases the risk of misuse, data breaches, and potential legal liabilities due to undefined boundaries for system and data access.

  • Inadequate Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive initial security training, the absence of annual refreshers for all staff leaves the workforce susceptible to evolving cyber threats. Regular training is crucial to maintain a high level of awareness regarding phishing, social engineering, malware, and updated security protocols.

5. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

5.1. Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  • Mitigate Localhost Exposure (Port 22):

    • Investigate Necessity: Determine the purpose of the service running on port 22 on 127.0.0.1. If not absolutely essential, ensure the service is properly shut down or its port is closed.

    • Implement Strict Access Controls: If the service is intentionally exposed for specific local applications, implement host-based firewalls (e.g., iptables, Windows Firewall) to restrict access to only necessary processes or local users.

    • Detailed Assessment: Conduct a thorough investigation to identify the specific SSH server (e.g., OpenSSH), its version, and configuration. Patch any known vulnerabilities and enforce secure configurations (e.g., disable root login, use key-based authentication, strong ciphers).

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Policy Creation: Draft a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines the acceptable and unacceptable use of all company-provided IT assets, networks, and data. This should cover internet usage, email, software installation, data handling, and privacy expectations.

    • Employee Acknowledgment: Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and periodically thereafter.

5.2. Ongoing Improvements (Medium Priority)

  • Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training (SAT):

    • Program Implementation: Institute a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This should supplement the existing new-hire training.

    • Content Update: Training content should be regularly updated to reflect the latest threat landscape, organizational policies, and best practices (e.g., phishing detection, secure password management, incident reporting).

    • Effectiveness Measurement: Consider incorporating regular phishing simulations or quizzes to measure and reinforce employee understanding and vigilance.

Conclusion

Nomad Gear Co. has a strong foundation in protecting user identities through MFA. However, critical vulnerabilities stemming from policy and training deficiencies, coupled with an exposed critical localhost service, necessitate immediate attention. By diligently addressing the recommendations outlined in this report, the organization can significantly enhance its security posture, reduce its attack surface, and foster a more cyber-resilient environment.
