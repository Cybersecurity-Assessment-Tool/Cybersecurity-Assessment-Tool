Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided, prioritized by severity.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Enterprise-Wide:

    • Priority: Critical

    • Action: Immediately implement MFA for all email accounts, computer logins, and especially for access to any systems handling sensitive data. This is the single most impactful step to protect against credential theft and unauthorized access.

  2. Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • Priority: High

    • Action 1: Develop and enforce mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process. This training should cover common threats, organizational policies, and best practices relevant to their roles.

    • Action 2: Implement a mandatory annual (or more frequent) security awareness training program for all existing employees. This training should be regularly updated to reflect current threat landscapes and provide practical guidance on recognizing and responding to cyber threats.

  3. Regularly Review Network Segmentation and Firewall Rules:

    • Priority: Medium (proactive, based on general best practice)

    • Action: While the scan showed no open ports on the target, it is prudent to regularly review firewall configurations and network segmentation to ensure only absolutely necessary ports and services are exposed, even internally. Consider conducting regular internal vulnerability scans to identify potential weaknesses within the network.
