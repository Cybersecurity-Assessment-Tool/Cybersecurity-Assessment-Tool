Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive cybersecurity posture assessment for Willow Creek Health. The analysis is based on organizational data, a security questionnaire review, and technical network scan results. While the organization demonstrates positive practices in email MFA and security awareness training, several critical gaps were identified, particularly concerning Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for internal systems and the absence of a formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP). The network scan of the specified target 192.168.1.100 indicated no open ports, which is a positive finding regarding direct network exposure. Recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance the overall security posture.

Risk Assessment

Based on the security control review and technical findings, the following risks have been identified:

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Computer Logins Without MFA for computer logins, an attacker who compromises user credentials (e.g., via phishing) can gain direct access to internal workstations and network resources. This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access, lateral movement, and data breaches. High
Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems Absence of MFA on systems containing sensitive data allows attackers to access critical information simply by compromising a single password. This poses an extreme risk to data confidentiality and integrity, potentially leading to regulatory non-compliance and reputational damage. High
Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding IT asset usage, data handling, and security best practices. This can lead to increased human error, misuse of company resources, and a higher likelihood of security incidents from internal vectors. Medium

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and strengthen the cybersecurity posture of Willow Creek Health, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all employee computer logins, particularly for machines that access the internal network or sensitive data.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Prioritize the implementation of MFA for all applications and systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational or patient data.

    • Benefit: Provides an essential layer of security for critical assets, protecting against data breaches and enhancing compliance.

  3. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT assets, networks, and data. Ensure all employees review and acknowledge the policy annually.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces insider threats, minimizes accidental data exposure, and reinforces a culture of security.

  4. Regularly Review Network Security Configuration:

    • Action: While the current scan showed no open ports, conduct periodic internal and external network scans and penetration tests to identify any newly exposed services or configuration drift.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies potential vulnerabilities and ensures ongoing adherence to security best practices.

Conclusion

Willow Creek Health has a foundational understanding of certain cybersecurity practices, as evidenced by its use of MFA for email and regular security awareness training. However, the absence of MFA for computer and sensitive data system logins, alongside the lack of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy, represents significant vulnerabilities that could be exploited by adversaries. Addressing these critical gaps through the recommended actions will substantially enhance the organization’s resilience against cyber threats and protect its valuable assets and patient information.
