Critical Gaps Identified

Based on the questionnaire, the following critical security gaps require immediate attention:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Email accounts are primary targets for cyberattacks. Without MFA, a compromised password can lead to unauthorized access, phishing campaigns, and broader network compromise.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A formal AUP is crucial for setting expectations regarding the proper use of company IT resources, data handling, and internet access, mitigating risks of misuse and data leakage.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New hires often lack awareness of organizational security policies and common cyber threats, making them susceptible to social engineering attacks.

  • No Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Regular training ensures employees are updated on evolving threats and best practices, reinforcing a strong security culture and reducing human-related risks.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Verve & Vigor, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all organizational email accounts.

    • Benefit: This is a critical control that significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if passwords are stolen, protecting against phishing and BEC.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a clear, comprehensive AUP that outlines expectations for employee behavior regarding company IT systems, data handling, and internet usage.

    • Benefit: Ensures employees understand their responsibilities, reduces the risk of misuse, data leakage, and supports legal compliance efforts. All employees should acknowledge and sign the policy.

  3. Establish a New Employee Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Develop and implement mandatory security awareness training for all new hires before they gain access to company systems.

    • Benefit: Equips new employees with foundational knowledge of common threats (e.g., phishing, malware), password hygiene, and internal reporting procedures, reducing initial susceptibility to attacks.

  4. Initiate Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Implement a recurring, mandatory annual security awareness training program for all existing employees.

    • Benefit: Keeps employees informed about evolving threat landscapes, reinforces best practices, and strengthens the overall human firewall within the organization. Training content should be engaging and relevant.

  5. Conduct Regular Internal Network Vulnerability Scans:

    • Action: While the current scan on yielded positive results, establish a routine schedule for internal network vulnerability scanning across all internal assets.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies potential weaknesses and misconfigurations as the IT environment evolves, ensuring continuous security posture monitoring.

Conclusion

Verve & Vigor demonstrates a commendable technical security posture for the scanned internal host. Addressing these fundamental areas will significantly strengthen the organization’s defenses against prevalent cyber threats and cultivate a more resilient security culture. Proactive implementation of the recommended actions is crucial for safeguarding organizational assets and data.
