The external network scan, targeting 192.168.1.100, revealed no open ports, which is a positive indicator of a hardened perimeter. However, a significant number of critical security gaps were identified through the organizational questionnaire, primarily related to the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across key systems, the absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), and insufficient security awareness training for new employees. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided for review in the input data. The primary risks identified are procedural and policy-based, posing substantial internal threats if not addressed. Recommendations are provided to mitigate these findings.

Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The questionnaire results indicate significant policy and procedural weaknesses:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not required for email access, computer logins, or sensitive data systems. This is a critical vulnerability, as it leaves accounts highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks, potentially leading to unauthorized access and data breaches.

  • Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The organization lacks an AUP, which means employees may not be clear on appropriate use of company assets and data, increasing the risk of misuse, data leakage, and compliance violations.

  • Insufficient Security Awareness Training: While annual training is conducted, new employees do not receive initial security awareness training. This leaves new hires vulnerable to social engineering attacks during their initial employment period, potentially compromising the organization from day one.

Recommendations

To address the identified security gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Signal Flare, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) System-Wide (Critical):

    • Email Access: Immediately implement MFA for all email accounts.

    • Computer Logins: Roll out MFA for all employee computer logins, both local and domain-joined.

    • Sensitive Data Systems: Mandate MFA for accessing all systems containing sensitive data.

    • Rationale: MFA is a fundamental security control that significantly reduces the risk of account compromise even if passwords are stolen.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (High):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines appropriate use of company IT resources, data handling, internet usage, and security responsibilities.

    • Distribute the AUP to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgements, and include it in new employee onboarding.

    • Rationale: An AUP establishes clear expectations, reduces misuse, and helps protect the organization legally and operationally.

  3. Mandate Security Awareness Training for New Employees (High):

    • Implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • This training should cover topics such as phishing, malware, password best practices, and the organization’s security policies (including the new AUP).

    • Rationale: New employees are often targets of social engineering; early training reduces this vulnerability.

  4. Regularly Review and Update Security Policies (Good Practice):

    • Establish a regular cadence (e.g., annually) to review and update all security policies, including the AUP and training materials, to ensure they remain current with evolving threats and technologies.

    • Rationale: Cybersecurity is a constantly changing landscape; policies must evolve to remain effective.

  5. Consider Deeper Technical Assessments (Future Consideration):

    • While no open ports were found, consider more in-depth vulnerability scanning or penetration testing from an internal perspective.

    • Request or conduct scans that include service version detection to identify potential software vulnerabilities.

    • Rationale: A single external scan offers limited insight; comprehensive testing provides a more complete picture of the attack surface.
