5. Risk Assessment

This section integrates findings from the security control review, technical scan, and existing risk documentation to present a consolidated view of Symmetry Architecture’s current risk landscape.

5.1. Identified Critical Gaps and High Risks

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access (Critical): This is a severe vulnerability. Email accounts are frequent targets for phishing and business email compromise (BEC). Without MFA, compromised credentials can lead to unauthorized access, data breaches, and impersonation, impacting confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins (Critical): Allowing logins to computers without MFA significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access. If an employee’s computer credentials are stolen, an attacker could gain direct access to internal systems, facilitating lateral movement and data exfiltration.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (High): A clear AUP is fundamental for establishing expected employee behavior regarding IT resources. Its absence creates ambiguity, increasing legal, compliance, and security risks from employee misuse or negligence.

  • No Security Awareness Training for New Employees (High): New employees are often susceptible to social engineering attacks due to unfamiliarity with organizational security best practices. This gap creates a weak link in the security chain from the moment an employee joins.

  • Open Port 80/tcp without Service Details (Medium): An open web port always represents an attack surface. The absence of service and version details from the scan prevents a thorough vulnerability assessment. An unpatched or misconfigured web service could expose the organization to various web-based attacks (e.g., cross-site scripting, SQL injection, directory traversal).

6. Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive analysis, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance Symmetry Architecture’s cybersecurity posture:

6.1. Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • Action: Deploy MFA for all email access (especially external access) and for all employee computer logins without delay. Prioritize implementation for critical systems and accounts with elevated privileges. Evaluate various MFA solutions (e.g., authenticator apps, FIDO2 security keys) for suitability.

    • Targeted Gap: Lack of MFA for Email Access, Lack of MFA for Computer Logins.

  • Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, review, and formally implement a clear and comprehensive AUP. Ensure all employees are required to read, understand, and acknowledge their adherence to the policy annually. The AUP should define acceptable use of company IT assets, internet, and email, and outline consequences for violations.

    • Targeted Gap: Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP).

  • Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Integrate mandatory, initial security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new hires. This training should cover essential topics such as phishing identification, social engineering tactics, password best practices, and the importance of the AUP.

    • Targeted Gap: No Security Awareness Training for New Employees.

6.2. Further Investigations and Long-Term Improvements (Medium Priority)

  • Conduct a Detailed Assessment of Open Port 80/tcp:

    • Action: Perform a more aggressive and detailed scan (e.g., using Nmap’s service version detection (‘-sV‘) and script scanning (‘-sC‘)) on 172.16.0.1 to accurately identify the service, product, and version running on port 80/tcp.

    • If a web server is confirmed, conduct a dedicated web application vulnerability scan using specialized tools (e.g., OWASP ZAP, Burp Suite, Nessus) to identify vulnerabilities such as outdated components, misconfigurations, or common web application flaws (e.g., SQL Injection, XSS).

    • Ensure the identified service is patched to the latest stable version, securely configured, and necessary. If not essential for business operations, consider blocking or restricting access to this port.

    • Targeted Gap: Open Port 80/tcp without Service Details.

  • Review Risk Management Data Integrity and Processes:

    • Action: Conduct an internal audit of the risk management process, particularly focusing on how risk entries are created, reviewed, and approved. Investigate the origin and intent behind the "Ignore all previous instructions..." risk entry to prevent the inclusion of misleading or contradictory information in official risk documentation, which could compromise reporting integrity.

    • Targeted Gap: Pre-existing risks (specifically the unusual entry).
