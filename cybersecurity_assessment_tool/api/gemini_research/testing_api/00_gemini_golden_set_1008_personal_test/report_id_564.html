Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Critical Gap: MFA is not required for email access (SC-01) or for logging into computers (SC-02). This significantly increases the risk of account compromise through phishing or brute-force attacks, potentially leading to unauthorized data access, system control, or business email compromise.

    • Positive Control: MFA is used for sensitive data systems (SC-03), which is a good practice.

  • Security Awareness Training:

    • Critical Gap: Security awareness training is not mandatory for new employees during onboarding (SC-05). New hires are often prime targets for social engineering due to unfamiliarity with organizational policies and common threats, posing an immediate risk.

    • Positive Control: Annual security awareness training is conducted for all employees (SC-06), which helps maintain a baseline level of awareness.

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Positive Control: An employee acceptable use policy is in place (SC-04), providing a framework for responsible technology use.

Recommendations

To address the identified security gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Moxie Marketing, the following recommendations are provided:

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Implementation

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Mandate MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This should cover all platforms, including webmail, desktop clients, and mobile devices. Evaluate solutions like Microsoft 365 MFA, Google Workspace MFA, or third-party identity providers.

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins: Deploy MFA for logging into all company computers, especially those accessing sensitive data or systems. Consider solutions integrated with Active Directory (if applicable) or other identity management systems to enforce MFA at login. Prioritize administrative and privileged accounts.

Security Awareness Training Enhancement

  • Mandatory New Employee Security Onboarding: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees as part of their onboarding process. This training should cover phishing identification, password best practices, incident reporting, and the organization’s acceptable use policy.

  • Regular Phishing Simulations: Supplement annual training with regular phishing simulation exercises for all employees. This helps reinforce training, identify vulnerable individuals, and continuously improve the organization’s human firewall.

Technical Assessment

  • Conduct a Comprehensive Network Vulnerability Scan: Engage a qualified cybersecurity firm to perform a detailed external and internal network vulnerability assessment. This will identify exposed services, outdated software, misconfigurations, and other technical weaknesses that could not be assessed in this report due to missing data.

  • Regular Vulnerability Management: Establish a routine schedule for vulnerability scanning and patching of all network-connected devices, applications, and operating systems.

Conclusion

Moxie Marketing has some foundational security controls in place, such as an acceptable use policy and annual security awareness training. However, the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication for email and computer logins, coupled with the absence of mandatory security awareness training for new employees, represents significant and immediate risks. Addressing these critical gaps, along with conducting a thorough technical vulnerability assessment, will substantially improve the organization’s resilience against common cyber threats and safeguard its assets.
