Executive Summary

This report outlines the cybersecurity posture assessment for Terraform Global, synthesizing data from a recent network scan, responses to an organizational security questionnaire, and an inventory of pre-existing risks. The analysis reveals critical vulnerabilities stemming from insufficient Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation and a lack of comprehensive security awareness training for employees. While a recent network scan confirmed the absence of immediately exposed services on a key internal target, thereby mitigating a previously identified risk, the organizational controls require significant enhancement to bolster overall resilience against prevalent cyber threats. Recommendations are provided to address these gaps, focusing on strengthening identity and access management through universal MFA adoption and establishing robust employee training programs.

Analysis of Control Gaps

The questionnaire responses highlight critical deficiencies in two primary areas:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not consistently applied across the organization. It is notably absent for general computer logins (SC-02) and access to systems containing sensitive data (SC-03). This significantly elevates the risk of unauthorized access should user credentials become compromised through phishing or other means.

  • Security Awareness Training: There is a clear lack of formal security awareness training programs. Specifically, new employees do not receive mandated training (SC-05), and there is no annual recurring training for the entire workforce (SC-06). This leaves employees vulnerable to social engineering, phishing attacks, and other human-centric exploits, which can often be the initial vector for sophisticated attacks.

Recommendations

To significantly enhance Terraform Global’s cybersecurity posture and mitigate the identified risks, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • Prioritize and implement MFA for all employee computer logins to prevent unauthorized access even if passwords are stolen.

    • Extend MFA requirements to all systems storing or processing sensitive organizational data, ensuring critical assets are protected by a strong authentication layer.

    • Conduct a comprehensive audit to identify all systems that would benefit from MFA and develop a phased rollout plan.

  2. Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • Develop and mandate security awareness training for all new employees as a critical component of their onboarding process, covering fundamental cybersecurity principles.

    • Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all existing employees, focusing on current threat landscapes, phishing recognition, social engineering tactics, and safe computing practices.

    • Include periodic phishing simulations and interactive modules to test and reinforce training effectiveness and maintain employee vigilance.

  3. Regularly Review and Update Risk Assessments:

    • Continue to conduct periodic technical vulnerability scans and penetration tests to validate the state of network services and infrastructure, identifying new exposures promptly.

    • Ensure that the pre-existing risk inventory is regularly reviewed and cross-referenced with current technical scan data and organizational changes to accurately reflect the active threat landscape.

    • Update internal security policies and controls promptly to reflect new findings and implement effective mitigation strategies for identified risks.

  4. Proactive Vulnerability Management:

    • While the current scan found no open ports, implement continuous monitoring to detect any unauthorized service exposures.

    • Establish a robust patch management program to ensure all operating systems and applications are regularly updated to protect against known vulnerabilities.

Conclusion

Terraform Global has established some foundational security controls, such as an acceptable use policy and MFA for email. However, the assessment reveals critical vulnerabilities in the broader adoption of MFA and the absence of a comprehensive security awareness training program. Addressing these fundamental gaps proactively through the outlined recommendations will significantly enhance the organization’s resilience against a wide range of cyber threats, foster a stronger security culture, and protect its valuable assets.
