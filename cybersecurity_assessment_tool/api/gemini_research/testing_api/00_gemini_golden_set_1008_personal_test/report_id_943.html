Executive Summary

This cybersecurity posture assessment reveals several significant areas requiring immediate attention for Wildfire Communications. The organization currently faces critical risks due to insufficient implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computer logins, exposing user accounts to elevated compromise risks. Furthermore, the absence of an employee acceptable use policy and regular security awareness training creates a substantial human-factor vulnerability. Technically, an open SSH port was identified, which, while common, represents an attack surface that must be adequately secured and monitored. Addressing these fundamental security controls is paramount to mitigate potential breaches and enhance overall resilience against cyber threats.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Wildfire Communications, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • Priority: Critical

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all employee email accounts and computer logins. Consider hardware tokens or robust authenticator apps for the strongest protection.

    • Benefit: Drastically reduces the risk of credential compromise and unauthorized access.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Priority: High

    • Action: Create a clear, comprehensive AUP outlining the appropriate use of company IT assets, data, and internet access. Ensure all employees acknowledge and understand the policy.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines, reduces misuse of resources, and improves overall compliance.

  3. Establish and Maintain Security Awareness Training Programs:

    • Priority: High

    • Action: Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process. Conduct annual, engaging security awareness training for all existing employees, covering topics like phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, and data protection.

    • Benefit: Empowers employees to recognize and report threats, turning them into a strong defensive layer.

  4. Harden and Monitor SSH Services:

    • Priority: Medium

    • Action: Review the configuration of the open SSH service on . Implement best practices such as:

      • Disabling password authentication in favor of strong key-based authentication.

      • Disabling root login.

      • Limiting SSH access to specific trusted IP addresses or networks.

      • Implementing rate limiting for connection attempts.

      • Ensuring SSH software is up-to-date and patched.

      • Implementing robust logging and monitoring for all SSH access attempts.

    • Benefit: Reduces the attack surface of the SSH service and minimizes the risk of unauthorized remote access.

Conclusion

Wildfire Communications has a foundational security posture that requires significant immediate improvements, particularly in user authentication and employee training. By prioritizing the implementation of MFA across critical systems and establishing comprehensive security policies and awareness programs, the organization can substantially uplift its defense mechanisms against common and sophisticated cyber threats. Regular reassessments will be crucial to ensure ongoing resilience and adaptability to the evolving threat landscape.
