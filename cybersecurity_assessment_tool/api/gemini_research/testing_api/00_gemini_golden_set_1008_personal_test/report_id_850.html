Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive assessment of the cybersecurity posture for Summit Peak Partners. The analysis is based on a review of organizational security controls, an external network scan, and an evaluation of existing risk data.

The organization demonstrates a foundational understanding of security by implementing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for sensitive data systems. However, critical gaps were identified in several key areas. Specifically, the absence of MFA for email and computer logins, a lack of an employee acceptable use policy, and insufficient annual security awareness training for all employees represent significant vulnerabilities that could be exploited by adversaries.

The external network scan of 58.147.112.98 did not reveal any open ports, suggesting a robust external perimeter or that services are not exposed publicly on standard ports. While this is a positive finding, it’s crucial to acknowledge that an empty target in the scan metadata means the specific scope for external scanning was not fully defined or the scan didn’t complete for that particular target.

Overall, Summit Peak Partners has established some security controls, but there are high-priority areas requiring immediate attention to significantly reduce the attack surface and enhance overall resilience against cyber threats. The recommendations provided in this report aim to guide the organization in strengthening its security framework.

Risk Assessment

Based on the security control review and technical findings, the following risks have been identified. These risks primarily stem from gaps in foundational security practices.

Identified Cybersecurity Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Without MFA, email accounts are highly susceptible to phishing and credential stuffing attacks, leading to potential account compromise, data exfiltration, and business email compromise (BEC). High

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of Summit Peak Partners, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing email services.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if passwords are stolen.

  2. Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Integrate and mandate MFA for all employee computer logins, especially for devices accessing organizational resources.

    • Benefit: Prevents unauthorized access to workstations and servers, bolstering endpoint security.

  3. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, communicate, and enforce a clear AUP that outlines acceptable use of company IT resources, data handling, and internet browsing.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear guidelines for employees, reduces insider risk, and aids in compliance.

  4. Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, covering topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, and data protection.

    • Benefit: Educates employees on current threats, cultivates a security-aware culture, and reduces human-factor vulnerabilities.

  5. Regular External Network Scanning:

    • Action: Schedule periodic external vulnerability scans of all publicly exposed IP addresses and domains associated with Summit Peak Partners. Ensure a clear target scope is defined for all scans.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies new exposures, misconfigurations, and vulnerabilities before they can be exploited by attackers.

  6. Establish a Comprehensive Cybersecurity Program:

    • Action: Develop a holistic cybersecurity program that includes regular risk assessments, incident response planning, patch management, and continuous monitoring.

    • Benefit: Provides a structured approach to managing cybersecurity risks and building organizational resilience.

By prioritizing and implementing these recommendations, Summit Peak Partners can significantly enhance its security posture, mitigate identified risks, and better protect its assets from cyber threats.
