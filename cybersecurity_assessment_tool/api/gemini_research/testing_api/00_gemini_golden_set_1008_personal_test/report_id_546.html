Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of True North Travel.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Systems:

    • Email Access: Mandate MFA for all email accounts to significantly reduce the risk of phishing and credential stuffing attacks.

    • Computer Logins: Implement MFA for logging into company computers and network resources.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Formalize guidelines for appropriate use of company IT assets, data, and internet access.

    • Ensure all employees read and acknowledge the AUP, and integrate it into onboarding.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Conduct mandatory annual security awareness training covering topics like phishing, social engineering, data handling, and password hygiene.

    • Tailor training content to current threat landscapes and organizational context.

  4. Regularly Review and Remediate Identified Vulnerabilities:

    • Confirm the status of previously identified risks, such as the "Unencrypted Web Server" vulnerability. While the recent scan showed Port 80 closed, verify the intent and ensure no unencrypted services are running inadvertently on other ports or have been moved.

    • Schedule periodic internal and external vulnerability scans to proactively identify and address security weaknesses.

  5. Adopt a Proactive Risk Management Framework:

    • Continuously assess and update the organization’s risk register.

    • Prioritize remediation efforts based on risk severity and potential business impact.

Conclusion

While the immediate network attack surface appears limited based on the current scan, True North Travel faces significant risks due to gaps in foundational security policies, MFA implementation, and ongoing employee security education. Addressing these areas will substantially improve the overall security posture and resilience against common cyber threats.
