Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive assessment of Vertex Solutions’s current cybersecurity posture, synthesizing information from a network scan, organizational security questionnaire responses, and pre-existing risk data. The analysis reveals several critical vulnerabilities and significant gaps in foundational security controls. Key findings include insufficient Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) coverage, a notable absence of an employee acceptable use policy, and a lack of security awareness training for new employees. Furthermore, an Nmap scan confirmed an open SSH service on the internal loopback address, corroborating a pre-existing critical risk regarding "Localhost Exposed". Addressing these areas is paramount to enhancing Vertex Solutions’s resilience against cyber threats and protecting sensitive assets. Immediate and long-term recommendations are provided to guide strategic improvements.

Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The questionnaire responses reveal several critical gaps:

  • Insufficient MFA Coverage: Multi-Factor Authentication is not required for accessing email or logging into computers. This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access through credential theft.

  • Missing Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of an AUP leaves a critical void in defining expected employee behavior regarding technology use, potentially leading to misuse or unintentional security incidents.

  • Lack of New Employee Security Training: New employees are not receiving security awareness training, making them immediate targets for social engineering attacks and potentially unaware of organizational security protocols.

Correlated Risks and Gaps

Combining all data points, the following critical risks and gaps have been identified:

  • High Risk: Inadequate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Coverage. The absence of MFA for email and computer logins creates significant attack vectors for credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks. This is a fundamental security control often targeted by adversaries.

  • Critical Gap: Non-existent Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP). Without a clear AUP, employees lack guidance on the responsible and secure use of organizational IT resources. This increases the likelihood of human error, data leakage, and compliance issues.

  • Critical Gap: Deficient Security Awareness Training for New Hires. The lack of initial security awareness training means new employees are not equipped to recognize and respond to common cyber threats from day one, posing an immediate insider risk.

  • Critical Risk: Unsecured/Unassessed SSH Service on Localhost. The Nmap scan confirmed an open SSH service on . This finding directly validates the "Localhost Exposed" pre-existing risk. While loopback, such a service might be leveraged through other attack vectors if not properly secured, configured, and monitored. The lack of version information prevents an immediate assessment of known vulnerabilities.

Recommendations

To significantly enhance Vertex Solutions’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided, categorized into immediate actions and long-term strategic improvements.

Immediate Actions

  • Implement MFA for All Critical Systems: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all email access and computer logins. Prioritize deployment across the entire organization.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the responsible use of company IT assets. All employees must review, understand, and formally acknowledge this policy.

  • Mandate New Employee Security Awareness Training: Institute compulsory security awareness training for all new hires before they gain access to company systems.

  • Investigate and Secure Localhost SSH Service:

    • Determine the purpose of the SSH service running on .

    • If essential, secure the service by:

      • Disabling root login.

      • Using strong, key-based authentication instead of passwords.

      • Limiting access to specific users/IPs.

      • Ensuring the SSH daemon is patched to the latest stable version.

    • If not essential, disable or remove the service to reduce the attack surface.

Long-Term Strategy

  • Regular Security Awareness Training: Conduct mandatory security awareness training for all employees at least annually, covering evolving threats like phishing, ransomware, and social engineering.

  • Implement a Vulnerability Management Program: Regularly scan all internal and external assets for vulnerabilities. Prioritize and remediate findings based on severity and potential impact.

  • Develop Incident Response Plan: Create and test a robust incident response plan to ensure the organization can effectively detect, contain, eradicate, and recover from cyber incidents.

  • Security Policy Framework Development: Expand upon the AUP by developing a comprehensive suite of security policies (e.g., Data Handling Policy, Password Policy, Remote Access Policy).

Conclusion

Vertex Solutions currently faces several high-priority cybersecurity risks stemming from foundational control gaps and identified technical exposures. Proactive implementation of the recommended actions, particularly in strengthening MFA, establishing clear security policies, and enhancing employee awareness, will significantly improve the organization’s defensive posture. Continuous monitoring, regular assessments, and ongoing security education are crucial for maintaining a resilient and secure environment against the ever-evolving threat landscape.
