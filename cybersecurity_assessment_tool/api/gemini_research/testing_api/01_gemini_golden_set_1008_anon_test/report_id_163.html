Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of the cybersecurity posture for , based on a combination of organizational policy review, technical network scanning, and an assessment of known risks. The evaluation was conducted on 2026-01-30.

The analysis identified several critical and high-risk security gaps that require immediate attention. The most significant findings include:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not enforced for accessing email or other sensitive data systems. This represents a critical vulnerability, as compromised credentials could lead directly to a significant data breach.

  • Exposed Management Service: The technical scan revealed that a Secure Shell (SSH) service on port 22 is publicly accessible. Without proper controls, this exposes a key administrative interface to brute-force attacks and exploitation.

  • Policy Gaps: The organization currently lacks a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) for employees, creating ambiguity regarding security responsibilities and acceptable behavior.

While the organization has implemented positive controls, such as MFA for computer logins and regular security awareness training, the identified gaps substantially increase the risk of unauthorized access and business disruption. This report details these findings and provides actionable recommendations to mitigate the identified risks.

Analysis of Control Gaps

The lack of MFA on email and sensitive data systems are the most critical findings from this review. Email is a primary target for phishing attacks, and a compromised account can serve as a launchpad for further infiltration. Similarly, sensitive data systems without MFA are highly vulnerable to credential theft. The absence of an Acceptable Use Policy can lead to inconsistent security practices and a lack of legal recourse in the event of insider misuse.

Analysis of Technical Findings

The open SSH port is a significant finding. While SSH is a secure protocol, its exposure increases the attack surface of the organization. If secured with weak passwords, or if it is unpatched, it could be exploited by an attacker to gain a foothold within the network. This risk is compounded by the lack of MFA on sensitive internal systems, as a successful SSH compromise could lead to lateral movement with minimal resistance.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of .

Immediate Priority (Critical Risks)

  1. Implement MFA on All Critical Systems: Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing email (e.g., Office 365, Google Workspace) and any systems identified as storing or processing sensitive data. This is the single most effective control to prevent account takeovers.

High Priority

  1. Secure the Exposed SSH Service:

    • If the SSH service on is not required for public access, it should be firewalled off from the internet immediately.

    • If public access is required, restrict access to a whitelist of trusted IP addresses.

    • Enforce strong, key-based authentication and disable password-based logins for SSH.

Medium Priority

  1. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Draft a formal AUP that clearly defines the rules for using company assets, handling data, and internet usage. Require all employees to read and acknowledge the policy as part of their employment terms.

Ongoing Improvements

  1. Continue Security Awareness Training: Maintain the existing program for security awareness training for new and existing employees. Consider incorporating modules that specifically address the new AUP and the importance of MFA.
