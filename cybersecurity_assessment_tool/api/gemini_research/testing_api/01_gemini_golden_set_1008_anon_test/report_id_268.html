Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity assessment conducted for [Organization Name]. The evaluation combined a technical network scan with a review of organizational security controls and pre-existing risks.

The primary finding is a significant disparity between the organization’s technical and procedural security postures. The external network scan of the target system revealed a strong configuration with no open ports detected, which effectively minimizes the external attack surface.

However, the review of security controls identified several critical and high-risk deficiencies in administrative and policy-based safeguards. The most severe gaps include:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access, which exposes the organization to a high risk of business email compromise (BEC) and account takeovers.

  • Absence of a formal employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP), leading to a lack of clear guidelines for system usage and data handling.

  • Complete lack of a security awareness training program, leaving employees vulnerable to phishing and social engineering attacks.

While the current network perimeter appears secure, these procedural gaps represent a significant threat to the organization’s overall security. Immediate action is recommended to address these findings, focusing first on implementing MFA for email services.

Recommendations

Based on the identified risks, the following prioritized recommendations are provided to strengthen the security posture of [Organization Name].

Priority 1: Remediate Immediately

Implement MFA for Email (Risk R-01):

This is the most critical action to take. Enforce MFA for all users accessing the email system, whether through a web client or a mobile application. This single control is highly effective at preventing account takeovers, even if passwords are stolen.

Priority 2: Remediate within 30-60 Days

Establish a Security Awareness Training Program (Risk R-02):

  • Develop or procure a training module for all new hires that covers fundamental topics like phishing, password security, and data handling.

  • Implement an annual refresher training program for all employees to ensure their knowledge remains current.

  • Consider periodic phishing simulations to test and reinforce employee awareness.

Priority 3: Remediate within 60-90 Days

Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (Risk R-03):

  • Draft a clear and concise AUP that outlines the rules for using company networks, computers, and data.

  • Have the policy reviewed by management and/or legal counsel.

  • Distribute the policy to all employees and require them to formally acknowledge that they have read and understood it.
