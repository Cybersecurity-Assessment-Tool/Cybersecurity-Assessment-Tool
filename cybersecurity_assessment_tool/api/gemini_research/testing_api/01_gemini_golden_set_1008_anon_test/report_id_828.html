Executive Overview

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity posture assessment conducted for [Organization Name]. The primary goal of this assessment was to identify security gaps, evaluate existing controls, and provide actionable recommendations to enhance the organization’s overall security posture.

The analysis revealed several critical and high-risk security gaps based on a review of organizational security controls. Key findings include:

  • Critical Risk - Lack of MFA on Email: The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on employee email accounts presents a significant and immediate risk of account compromise and business email compromise (BEC) attacks.

  • High Risk - Inadequate Security Awareness Training: The organization lacks a formal security awareness training program for new and existing employees. This deficiency increases susceptibility to phishing, social engineering, and other human-targeted cyber threats.

Data Limitations: It is important to note that the external network scan data and the list of pre-existing vulnerabilities were found to be corrupted or were otherwise unavailable for this assessment. Consequently, this report’s findings are based primarily on the analysis of the provided organizational questionnaire. A full technical vulnerability assessment could not be completed.

Recommendations have been prioritized to address the most severe risks first.

Analysis of Gaps

The review identified three significant control gaps:

  1. MFA for Email: Email is a primary target for attackers. The lack of MFA makes it highly vulnerable to credential stuffing and phishing attacks, which can lead to data breaches and financial loss.

  2. New Employee Training: New hires are often unfamiliar with corporate policies and are prime targets for social engineering. The absence of onboarding security training is a major oversight.

  3. Annual Employee Training: The threat landscape evolves continuously. Without regular, recurring training, employees’ ability to recognize and respond to new threats diminishes over time.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks and strengthen the security posture of [Organization Name].

Priority 1: Remediate Critical Risks

Recommendation 1.1:

Immediately Enforce MFA for All Email Accounts.

  • Action: Enable and enforce MFA across the entire email environment (e.g., Microsoft 365, Google Workspace). All user accounts, including administrative and service accounts where possible, must be covered.

  • Impact: This is the single most effective control to prevent unauthorized account access and will drastically reduce the likelihood of a successful business email compromise attack.

Priority 2: Remediate High Risks

Recommendation 2.1:

Establish a Comprehensive Security Awareness Program.

  • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program. This program must include:

    1. An initial training module for all new hires during their onboarding process.

    2. An annual refresher course for all employees covering topics like phishing, password security, and acceptable use.

  • Impact: A well-trained workforce serves as a human firewall, reducing the organization’s susceptibility to common cyberattacks and fostering a culture of security.

Priority 3: Improve Security Visibility

Recommendation 3.1:

Reschedule Technical Security Assessments.

  • Action: Coordinate with the security team to perform a new external network vulnerability scan. Additionally, provide a valid data export of any internally tracked risks or vulnerabilities.

  • Impact: Completing these assessments will provide necessary visibility into the technical attack surface, allowing for a more comprehensive understanding of the organization’s risk profile.
