This discrepancy requires immediate investigation to confirm whether the risk has been remediated or if the scan scope was incomplete.

This report outlines the detailed findings and provides a prioritized list of actionable recommendations to mitigate the identified risks and strengthen the overall security posture of [Organization Name].

Analysis of Control Gaps

  • Lack of MFA: The absence of MFA for email and computer logins is a critical vulnerability. Compromised credentials could directly lead to unauthorized access to sensitive communications and internal network resources.

  • Missing Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, there is no clear standard for employee behavior regarding company assets, increasing the risk of insider threats and unintentional data exposure.

  • No New Hire Training: Failing to train new employees on security best practices from day one leaves the organization vulnerable, as new staff may be unaware of policies, threat identification, or proper data handling procedures.

Analysis of Technical Findings

The scan of [Target IP] revealed no open TCP ports. Port 80, commonly used for unencrypted web traffic, was explicitly identified as closed.

Crucially, this technical finding contradicts the information provided in the "Current Risks" data, which lists an active vulnerability related to an open Port 80. This discrepancy suggests one of the following possibilities:

  1. The previously identified risk has been successfully remediated.

  2. The current scan targeted a different asset than the one associated with the pre-existing risk.

  3. The service on Port 80 is only intermittently available or protected by a firewall that blocked this specific scan.

Further investigation is required to resolve this conflict and validate the organization’s external attack surface.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks. Recommendations are prioritized by severity.

Priority 1: Critical Risks

  1. Implement MFA Across All Critical Systems:

    • Action: Immediately enforce MFA for all users on the primary email system (e.g., Microsoft 365, Google Workspace).

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all remote and on-site computer/endpoint logins.

Priority 2: High Risks

  1. Develop and Implement Foundational Security Policies:

    • Action: Draft, approve, and distribute an official Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP). Require all employees to read and acknowledge it.

    • Action: Integrate mandatory security awareness training into the new employee onboarding process. This should occur before a new hire is granted access to sensitive systems.

Priority 3: Verification and Investigation

  1. Investigate Port 80 Discrepancy:

    • Action: Conduct a comprehensive vulnerability scan across all known external IP addresses for the organization to confirm the status of Port 80 and other services.

    • Action: If Port 80 is found open on any asset, immediately implement the long-term recommendation of migrating the service to HTTPS (Port 443) and closing Port 80.

    • Action: Update the central risk register to accurately reflect the findings of the investigation.
