Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive cybersecurity assessment for [Organization Name], based on an analysis of organizational security controls, an external network scan, and a review of pre-existing risks.

The assessment identified several critical and high-risk security gaps related to organizational policies and identity management. The most severe findings are the complete absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems. This exposes the organization to significant risks of account compromise and unauthorized access. Additionally, the lack of an employee Acceptable Use Policy and mandatory annual security training for all staff weakens the human element of the security posture.

On a positive note, the external network scan of the target IP address revealed a strong perimeter defense. No open ports were detected, indicating a well-configured firewall that effectively limits the external attack surface.

Immediate action is recommended to address the identified policy and MFA deficiencies to mitigate the most critical risks. Detailed findings and actionable recommendations are provided in the subsequent sections of this report.

Identified Risks and Severity
Risk ID Risk Name Description Severity
RISK-001 Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) The absence of MFA for email, endpoints, and sensitive systems makes user accounts highly susceptible to takeover via credential theft or phishing. Critical
RISK-002 Inadequate Security Policies The lack of a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) creates ambiguity for employees and increases the risk of insider threats and unintentional data breaches. High

Recommendations

Based on the identified risks, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture.

Immediate Actions (1-30 Days)

For RISK-001 (Critical):

Implement MFA Across All Critical Systems.

  • Prioritize the deployment of MFA for email access (e.g., Office 365, Google Workspace) immediately.

  • Subsequently, enforce MFA for all remote access solutions (VPNs), administrative accounts, and systems containing sensitive data.

  • Develop a roadmap to enable MFA for all employee computer logins.

Short-Term Actions (30-90 Days)

For RISK-002 (High):

Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP).

  • Draft a clear and comprehensive AUP that governs the use of company assets, including computers, networks, and data.

  • Communicate the policy to all employees and require them to formally acknowledge their understanding and agreement.

For RISK-003 (High):

Establish a Mandatory Annual Security Training Program.

  • Select and deploy a security awareness training platform for all employees.

  • The training should cover key topics such as phishing, password security, social engineering, and the new AUP.

  • Track completion to ensure 100% participation.

Ongoing Recommendations

Maintain Strong Perimeter Security:

The external network scan showed excellent firewall hygiene. Continue to enforce a "default deny" policy and conduct regular vulnerability scans to ensure this posture is maintained.
