Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity posture assessment conducted for [Organization Name]. The assessment combined an external network scan, a review of existing risk documentation, and an analysis of organizational security controls based on a provided questionnaire.

The assessment identified several critical and high-severity risks that require immediate attention. The most significant finding is the discovery of a publicly accessible service on port 8080 of the external IP address [Target IP]. This service presents a title, “TOP SECRET DB”, which suggests a highly sensitive database is exposed. This finding directly contradicts the existing risk register, which incorrectly lists this port as a secure false positive.

Furthermore, critical gaps were identified in internal security controls. The lack of mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for computer logins significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access from a compromised employee account. This is compounded by the absence of a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, which heightens the organization’s susceptibility to phishing and social engineering attacks.

Immediate remediation is required to address the exposed service and enforce endpoint MFA. A comprehensive review of the risk management process is also strongly recommended to prevent such dangerous discrepancies in the future.

Risk Assessment

The following risks have been identified and prioritized based on the correlation of technical findings, control gaps, and existing documentation.

Synthesized Risk Summary
Risk ID Description Severity Affected Systems
RISK-001 A potentially sensitive database or application titled “TOP SECRET DB” is exposed to the public internet on port 8080. Critical External Server: [Target IP]
RISK-002 Lack of MFA for computer logins allows an attacker with a single compromised password to gain full access to an employee’s endpoint and internal network resources. Critical All employee workstations and laptops.
RISK-003 The risk register is outdated and inaccurate. It incorrectly states that port 8080 is secure, while active scanning proves it is open and exposing a sensitive service. This indicates a failure in the risk management process. High Security Governance & Risk Management Program.
RISK-004 Lack of mandatory annual security awareness training for all staff increases the likelihood of successful phishing and social engineering attacks, leading to credential compromise. High All employees.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks. Recommendations are prioritized based on severity.

Immediate Priority (Critical Risks)

  1. Remediate Exposed Service (RISK-001):

    • Immediately investigate the service running on port 8080 of [Target IP].

    • If the service is not intended for public access, place it behind a firewall and restrict access to authorized IP addresses only.

    • If the service must be public, ensure it is fully patched, hardened, and requires strong authentication.

    • Change the service title to remove sensitive information.

  2. Implement Endpoint MFA (RISK-002):

    • Procure and deploy an MFA solution for all employee computer and laptop logins (Windows, macOS, etc.).

    • Enforce this policy for all users, including privileged administrators and executives.

High Priority

  1. Update Risk Management Processes (RISK-003):

    • Conduct a full review of the current risk register to identify and correct other inaccuracies.

    • Implement a formal process for validating risk assessments with periodic technical scans.

  2. Establish Annual Security Training (RISK-004):

    • Implement a mandatory security awareness training program that all employees must complete at least once per year.

    • Include modules on phishing, password security, and acceptable use.
