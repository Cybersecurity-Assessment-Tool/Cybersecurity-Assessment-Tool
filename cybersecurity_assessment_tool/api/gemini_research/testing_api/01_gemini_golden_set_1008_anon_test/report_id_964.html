Analysis of Gaps

The review highlights critical deficiencies in two key areas:

  1. Access Control: While MFA is commendably enforced for email and computer logins, its absence on sensitive data systems constitutes a critical risk. This gap allows a compromised user account to potentially gain direct access to the organization’s most valuable information assets.

  2. Security Awareness: The complete lack of a security awareness training program means employees are not equipped to recognize or respond to common cyber threats like phishing, malware, or social engineering. This significantly increases the organization’s vulnerability to human-centric attacks.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and improve the overall security posture, the following actions are recommended with high priority.

  1. Implement MFA for All Sensitive Systems (RISK-001):

    • Action: Immediately prioritize the deployment and enforcement of multi-factor authentication across all applications, databases, and administrative interfaces that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data.

    • Impact: Drastically reduces the risk of unauthorized access and data breaches resulting from stolen or weak passwords.

  2. Establish a Comprehensive Security Awareness Program (RISK-002):

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires upon onboarding. This training should cover, at a minimum, acceptable use, phishing identification, password hygiene, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Action: Institute a mandatory annual security awareness refresher course for all employees to ensure their knowledge remains current.

    • Impact: Creates a "human firewall" by empowering employees to act as the first line of defense, reducing the likelihood of successful phishing and social engineering attacks.

Conclusion
