1. Executive Summary

This report provides a cybersecurity assessment for [Organization Name], based on an analysis of organizational data, a network vulnerability scan, and a review of current risks. The assessment was conducted on 2026-01-30.

The organization demonstrates a strong commitment to identity and access management, with Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) consistently enforced across email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access through compromised credentials.

However, the assessment identified two critical gaps in fundamental administrative controls. The absence of a formal Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) and the lack of mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees represent high-risk deficiencies. These gaps expose the organization to significant threats, including insider threats, social engineering, and non-compliance with industry standards.

The external network scan of the target IP address did not identify any open ports or services, indicating a secure external network perimeter at the time of the scan. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were reported for assessment.

Recommendations focus on immediately addressing the identified policy and training gaps to build a more resilient and security-conscious culture.

6. Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of [Organization Name].

Immediate Priorities (High Risk)

  1. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines the rules and responsibilities for all employees regarding the use of company networks, devices, email, and data.

    • Impact: Establishes a baseline for secure employee behavior, provides a legal framework for enforcement, and is a foundational component of most compliance standards (e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2).

    • Timeline: Within 30 days.

  2. Establish a Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Procure or develop a security awareness training module and make it mandatory for all employees to complete annually. The training should cover current threats such as phishing, ransomware, password security, and social engineering.

    • Impact: Reduces the likelihood of human error leading to a security breach. Fosters a culture of security and empowers employees to become the first line of defense.

    • Timeline: Within 60 days.

Ongoing Improvements

  1. Maintain Proactive Network Scanning:

    • Action: Continue to perform regular, authenticated and unauthenticated vulnerability scans of all external and internal network assets.

    • Impact: Ensures that the secure network posture observed in this assessment is maintained and that any new vulnerabilities introduced by system changes are quickly identified and remediated.

    • Timeline: Ongoing (Quarterly).
