Executive Overview

This report provides a comprehensive cybersecurity assessment for [Organization Name], synthesizing data from a network vulnerability scan, a security controls questionnaire, and a review of pre-existing risks.

The assessment reveals a mixed security posture. The organization demonstrates strong technical access controls, with Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) widely implemented across key systems. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access via compromised credentials.

However, critical gaps exist in foundational administrative controls. The absence of an employee Acceptable Use Policy and a formal security awareness training program presents a high risk. These gaps leave the organization vulnerable to insider threats, social engineering, and phishing attacks, which can bypass even strong technical defenses.

This proactive or coincidental remediation is a positive security development that should be verified and documented.

Immediate focus should be placed on developing and implementing the missing policies and training programs to address the most significant areas of exposure.

Analysis of Control Gaps

The questionnaire reveals critical deficiencies in administrative and policy-based controls:

  • No Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of an AUP means there are no formally documented rules for how employees should use company assets, data, and networks. This increases the risk of data misuse, unauthorized software installation, and legal liability.

  • No Security Awareness Training: The complete absence of a security awareness training program for both new and existing employees is a significant vulnerability. Employees are the first line of defense, and without training, they are far more likely to fall victim to phishing, malware, and other social engineering attacks.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of [Organization Name].

High Priority

  1. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft a formal AUP that clearly defines the rules for using company technology, networks, and data. The policy should be reviewed by legal counsel, approved by management, and communicated to all employees.

    • Impact: Reduces insider risk, establishes a baseline for acceptable behavior, and provides a legal framework for enforcement.

  2. Establish a Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Procure or develop a security awareness training module covering key topics like phishing, password security, and data handling. This training must be mandatory for all new hires upon onboarding and for all existing staff on an annual basis.

    • Impact: Creates a "human firewall" by empowering employees to identify and report security threats, drastically reducing the likelihood of a successful phishing or social engineering attack.

Verification and Documentation

  1. Verify Remediation of Port 80:

    • Action: Confirm with the IT or network team that the closure of Port 80 on [Target IP] was an intentional act to remediate the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk.

    • Impact: Ensures the risk is formally closed in the risk register and that the secure configuration is maintained.
