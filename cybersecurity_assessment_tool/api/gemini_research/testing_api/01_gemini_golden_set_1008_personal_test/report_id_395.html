Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity assessment conducted for Terraform Global. The assessment combined a review of organizational security controls, an external network scan, and an analysis of pre-existing risk data.

The analysis revealed several critical security gaps that significantly increase the organizationâ€™s risk of compromise. The most pressing issues are the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and sensitive data systems, and the complete absence of a security awareness training program. These deficiencies create a high susceptibility to phishing, business email compromise, and unauthorized data access.

Furthermore, a technical scan confirmed a pre-existing critical risk related to a service exposed on the localhost interface (127.0.0.1). While not directly accessible from the internet, this misconfiguration could be exploited by an attacker who has already gained an initial foothold on the system.

Immediate remediation is required to address these findings. Recommendations focus on implementing foundational security controls, such as MFA and employee training, to build a more resilient security posture.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of Terraform Global. Recommendations are prioritized based on severity.

Critical Priority

  1. Implement and Enforce MFA:

    • Action: Immediately enable and mandate the use of MFA for all user accounts on email systems (e.g., Office 365, G Suite) and all systems identified as containing sensitive data.

    • Justification: This is the single most effective control to prevent account takeover and unauthorized access resulting from compromised credentials.

  2. Establish a Security Awareness Program:

    • Action: Procure and deploy a security awareness training platform. Enroll all current employees immediately and integrate the training into the new-hire onboarding process. Conduct annual refresher training and periodic phishing simulations.

    • Justification: An educated workforce is a critical layer of defense against phishing and other social engineering attacks.

  3. Remediate Localhost Exposure:

    • Action: Investigate the service running on 127.0.0.1:22. Determine its business purpose. If the service is not required, disable it. If it is required, ensure it is securely configured according to best practices and accessible only by authorized processes.

    • Justification: Eliminates a potential pivot point for an attacker who has already gained a foothold on the system.

High Priority

  1. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft a formal AUP that clearly defines the rules for using company IT assets, data, and internet access. The policy should be reviewed by management, communicated to all employees, and acknowledged via signature.

    • Justification: An AUP establishes a baseline for secure behavior, reduces organizational liability, and provides a framework for enforcing security standards.
