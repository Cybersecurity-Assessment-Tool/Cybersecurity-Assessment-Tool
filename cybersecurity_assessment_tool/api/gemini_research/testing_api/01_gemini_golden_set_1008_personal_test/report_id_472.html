Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity assessment conducted for Tidal Wave Sports. The assessment incorporated a review of organizational security controls, a technical network scan of a key internal asset, and an analysis of pre-existing risks.

The primary finding of this assessment is the presence of critical gaps in identity and access management controls. The organization does not enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email, computer logins, or access to sensitive data systems. This represents a significant and immediate risk, as it greatly increases the likelihood of a successful account compromise via phishing or credential theft. A secondary high-risk finding is the lack of security awareness training for new employees, creating a window of vulnerability during their initial tenure.

On a positive note, the technical network scan of the target host 192.168.1.100 revealed no open ports. This indicates a strong network-level configuration for this specific asset, reducing its external attack surface.

Immediate remediation efforts should focus on implementing a comprehensive MFA policy across all critical systems and integrating security training into the employee onboarding process.

Analysis of Control Gaps

The review identified critical weaknesses in access control and employee training:

  • Lack of MFA: The absence of MFA for email, computer, and sensitive data access is a critical vulnerability. This single point of failure allows an attacker with valid credentials (e.g., from a phishing attack) to gain unauthorized access to key organizational resources.

  • Onboarding Training Gap: While annual training is in place, the lack of security training for new hires means that the newest, and often most vulnerable, employees are not equipped to identify and resist social engineering or phishing attacks from day one.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of Tidal Wave Sports.

RISK-001: Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

  • Immediate Action: Procure and deploy an MFA solution across the organization.

  • Tactical Steps:

    1. Enable and enforce MFA for all user accounts on the primary email platform (e.g., Microsoft 365, Google Workspace).

    2. Implement MFA for all remote access solutions (e.g., VPN).

    3. Deploy MFA for logging into company-managed computers (e.g., via Windows Hello for Business, Duo, etc.).

    4. Enforce MFA for all applications containing sensitive or critical business data.

  • Priority: Critical. This should be the top remediation priority.

RISK-002: Inadequate Employee Onboarding Security Training

  • Immediate Action: Develop a security awareness training module specifically for new hires.

  • Tactical Steps:

    1. Integrate a mandatory 30-60 minute security training session into the formal onboarding process for all new employees, to be completed within their first week.

    2. The training should cover, at a minimum: phishing identification, password security policies, acceptable use of company assets, and how to report a security incident.

    3. Ensure the employee acceptable use policy is reviewed and signed as part of this onboarding training.

  • Priority: High.

Conclusion

The security posture of Tidal Wave Sports is characterized by a mix of strengths and significant weaknesses. While network-level controls on the assessed asset appear robust, the organization’s procedural and identity controls are critically underdeveloped. The lack of Multi-Factor Authentication is the most severe risk and exposes the organization to a high probability of a security breach.

By implementing the recommendations outlined in this report, particularly the rapid deployment of MFA and the enhancement of the employee training program, Tidal Wave Sports can substantially reduce its risk exposure and build a more resilient security foundation.
