Executive Summary

This report provides a cybersecurity posture assessment for Apex Legends Group. The analysis is based on a review of organizational security controls provided via a questionnaire.

Important Note: The technical network scan data (Input 1) and the list of pre-existing vulnerabilities (Input 3) were found to be corrupted and could not be analyzed. Therefore, this assessment is limited to the procedural and policy-based controls self-reported by the organization.

The primary findings indicate several critical security gaps. The complete absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across all key systems, including email, endpoints, and sensitive data repositories, presents a severe and immediate risk of account compromise and unauthorized access.

Furthermore, significant weaknesses were identified in the organization’s security policies and training programs. The lack of a formal Acceptable Use Policy and the failure to provide annual security awareness training for all employees create an environment where risky behavior is more likely to occur and go unaddressed.

Immediate remediation of these issues is strongly recommended to reduce the organization’s attack surface and mitigate the high likelihood of a security incident. A follow-up technical assessment is required once the network scan issues are resolved.

Risk Assessment Summary

Based on the available data from the security questionnaire, the following risks have been identified. The pre-existing risk data was unavailable for correlation.

Identified Risks from Questionnaire Analysis
Risk Name Severity Overview
Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Critical The absence of MFA for email, endpoints, and sensitive data exposes the organization to a high risk of credential theft, phishing, and unauthorized access to critical systems.
Inadequate Security Training Program High Training only new hires and not providing annual refreshers for all staff leads to knowledge decay. Employees are less likely to recognize and report modern phishing and social engineering attacks.
Missing Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) High Without a formal AUP, there are no clear guidelines for employees on the safe and acceptable use of company assets. This increases the risk of insider threat, data leakage, and malware infection.

Recommendations

The following actionable steps are recommended to mitigate the identified risks. They are prioritized based on severity and potential impact.

  1. [Critical] Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Priority 1: Immediately enable MFA on the organization’s email platform (ApexLegendsGroup.net). This is the single most effective control to prevent business email compromise.

    • Priority 2: Enforce MFA for all remote access solutions (e.g., VPN) and access to systems containing sensitive data.

    • Priority 3: Roll out MFA for all employee computer logins.

  2. [High] Establish a Comprehensive Security Awareness Program:

    • Develop or procure a security awareness training module that covers key topics like phishing, password security, and data handling.

    • Mandate that all employees complete this training annually. Track completion to ensure compliance.

  3. [High] Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a formal AUP that clearly defines the rules for using company networks, computers, and data.

    • Require all employees to read and formally acknowledge the policy as a condition of their access.

  4. [Procedural] Remediate Security Visibility Gaps:

    • Conduct a new external network scan against 228.119.178.97 to identify technical vulnerabilities.

    • Investigate and resolve the cause of the corrupted scan and risk data to ensure future assessments are based on complete information.

Conclusion

While Apex Legends Group has initiated a security program by providing training to new hires, there are critical deficiencies that require immediate attention. The lack of fundamental controls like Multi-Factor Authentication and a comprehensive policy framework places the organization at significant risk.

By implementing the prioritized recommendations in this report, the organization can substantially improve its security posture, build resilience against common cyber threats, and create a stronger foundation for its security program. A full technical review is pending the successful completion of a network scan.
