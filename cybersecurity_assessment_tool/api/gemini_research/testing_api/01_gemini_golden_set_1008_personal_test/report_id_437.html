Executive Summary

This report presents a cybersecurity assessment for Blue Horizon Initiative, synthesizing data from network scans, organizational questionnaires, and pre-existing risk registers. The analysis provides a consolidated view of the organization’s current security posture, highlighting key vulnerabilities and offering actionable recommendations for remediation.

The assessment identified several areas of significant concern requiring immediate attention. The key findings are:

  • Critical - Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email: The absence of MFA on the primary email system (BlueHorizonInitiative.org) represents a critical vulnerability. This exposes the organization to a high risk of business email compromise (BEC), phishing attacks, and subsequent account takeovers.

  • Critical - Systemic Insecure RDP Exposure: A network scan identified a new host () with an open Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) port (3389). This finding, correlated with a pre-existing risk for a similar issue on another host, points to a systemic problem in managing remote access controls, significantly increasing the risk of unauthorized network access.

  • High - Gaps in Employee Security Training: The organization lacks a mandatory security awareness training program for new employees. This creates a window of vulnerability where new hires, who are often targeted, are not equipped to recognize and respond to social engineering or phishing threats.

The overall security posture has critical gaps that undermine existing controls. We strongly advise prioritizing the remediation of these findings to reduce the likelihood of a significant security incident.

Analysis of Control Gaps

The "No" responses indicate critical weaknesses:

  • No MFA for Email: Email is the primary entry point for many cyberattacks. Without MFA, a compromised password is all an attacker needs to gain access to an employee’s mailbox, potentially leading to data theft, financial fraud, and a launchpad for further attacks on the internal network.

  • No Security Training for New Employees: New hires are particularly vulnerable to social engineering attacks as they are unfamiliar with company policies and personnel. Failing to provide immediate security training upon hiring creates an unnecessary risk that could be easily mitigated.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks. Recommendations are prioritized based on severity and potential impact.

Priority 1 (Critical): Remediate Email and RDP Risks

  1. Enforce MFA for All Email Accounts:

    • Action: Immediately enable and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing the BlueHorizonInitiative.org email system.

    • Impact: Drastically reduces the risk of email account takeover, even if passwords are stolen. This is the single most effective control to implement.

  2. Secure Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) Access:

    • Immediate Action: Create firewall rules to block all external access to TCP port 3389. For internal access, restrict RDP on 10.10.10.51 and 10.10.10.50 to a limited set of administrative jump hosts.

    • Long-Term Action: Implement a secure remote access solution, such as a Virtual Private Network (VPN) with MFA or a Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) gateway. This ensures all remote administrative access is authenticated and encrypted.

    • Audit: Conduct a full internal network scan to identify and remediate any other systems with exposed RDP or other risky remote management services.

Priority 2 (High): Improve Security Training

  1. Implement Onboarding Security Training:

    • Action: Integrate a mandatory security awareness training module into the new employee onboarding process. This training should cover phishing, acceptable use, password hygiene, and how to report security incidents.

    • Impact: Reduces the likelihood of new employees falling victim to common cyberattacks and establishes a security-conscious mindset from their first day.
