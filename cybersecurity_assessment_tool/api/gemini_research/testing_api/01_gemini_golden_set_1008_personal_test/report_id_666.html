Executive Summary

This report summarizes the cybersecurity posture of Grizzly Peak. The assessment combines a review of organizational security controls, a technical network scan, and an analysis of pre-existing risks.

While the technical scan of the target host 192.168.0.5 revealed a strong posture with no open ports, significant gaps were identified in organizational security controls. Critical deficiencies include the lack of multi-factor authentication (MFA) for email and sensitive data systems, and the absence of an employee acceptable use policy. These gaps present a high risk of account compromise and unauthorized data access.

The previously identified risk of an "Unencrypted Web Server" on port 80 appears to have been remediated, as our scan found the port to be closed. Immediate action is recommended to implement MFA across all critical systems and to develop foundational security policies to strengthen the organization’s overall security posture.

Recommendations

Based on the assessment, we provide the following prioritized recommendations to mitigate the identified risks.

  1. Implement MFA for Email (Priority: Critical): Immediately enforce MFA for all user access to the GrizzlyPeak.net email system. This is the single most effective control to prevent business email compromise and unauthorized account access.

  2. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (Priority: Critical): Deploy MFA on all systems identified as containing sensitive or critical business data. This should be prioritized based on data sensitivity and regulatory requirements.

  3. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (Priority: High): Create a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that outlines the rules and responsibilities for all employees when using company IT assets. Ensure all employees read and acknowledge the policy as part of their onboarding and annual training.

  4. Validate and Close Old Risks (Priority: Low): Formally confirm that the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk associated with port 80 is fully resolved and update the internal risk register to reflect its closed status. This ensures accurate tracking of the current security posture.
