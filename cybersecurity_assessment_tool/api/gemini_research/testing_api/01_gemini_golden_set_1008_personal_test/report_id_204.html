Analysis of Gaps

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of a formal AUP is a critical administrative gap. This policy is the foundation for setting user expectations regarding the use of company technology and data. Without it, there is no formal basis for enforcing security standards or taking disciplinary action in case of misuse.

  • Annual Security Awareness Training: The threat landscape evolves continuously. Training provided only to new hires becomes outdated quickly. A lack of mandatory, annual training for all employees significantly increases the likelihood that an employee will fall victim to a phishing email or other social engineering attack, potentially leading to a major breach.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of Borealis Tech.

Immediate Actions (Next 30 Days)

  1. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (RISK-001):

    • Draft a comprehensive AUP that covers data handling, internet usage, email security, use of personal devices, and consequences for non-compliance.

    • Require all current employees to read and formally acknowledge the new policy.

    • Integrate the AUP acknowledgement into the standard onboarding process for all future hires.

  2. Establish an Annual Security Training Program (RISK-002):

    • Procure and schedule a mandatory security awareness training module for all employees to be completed within the next quarter.

    • The training must cover modern threats, including phishing, ransomware, business email compromise, and password hygiene.

    • Plan for this training to be a recurring, annual requirement for all staff.

  3. Execute a New Network Scan:

    • Conduct a new, validated external vulnerability scan against the public IP address 114.211.251.84.

    • Provide the scan results to the security team for a comprehensive technical analysis and an updated report.

Strategic Actions (Next 6-12 Months)

  1. Implement Phishing Simulations:

    • Following the initial training, begin a program of regular (e.g., quarterly) phishing simulations.

    • Use the results to identify individuals or departments that require additional, targeted training.

  2. Policy Review Cycle:

    • Establish a formal annual review cycle for the new AUP and any other security policies to ensure they remain relevant and effective against emerging threats.
