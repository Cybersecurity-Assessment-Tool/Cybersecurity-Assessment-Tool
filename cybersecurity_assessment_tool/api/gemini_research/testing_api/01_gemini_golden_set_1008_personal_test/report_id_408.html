Executive Summary

This report provides an assessment of the cybersecurity posture for Aeon Pharmaceuticals. The analysis is based on a review of self-reported organizational security controls. It is critical to note that the provided technical network scan data and the existing risk register data were corrupted and could not be processed. Consequently, this assessment does not include an analysis of external-facing technical vulnerabilities.

The review of security controls revealed several critical and high-risk gaps in the organization’s defenses. While positive controls such as mandatory Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and a consistent security awareness training program are in place, these are undermined by significant weaknesses.

Key findings include:

  • Critical Risk - Lack of MFA: MFA is not enforced for logging into computers or for accessing sensitive data systems. This exposes the organization to a high risk of unauthorized access and data breach via compromised credentials.

  • High Risk - No Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The absence of a formal AUP creates ambiguity regarding the secure and appropriate use of company assets, increasing legal and insider threat risks.

Immediate remediation of these findings is strongly recommended to reduce the risk of a significant security incident. A comprehensive external vulnerability scan must also be conducted as a top priority to gain visibility into the technical attack surface.

Recommendations

Based on the findings of this assessment, the following actions are recommended to improve the cybersecurity posture of Aeon Pharmaceuticals. Recommendations are prioritized by severity.

  1. Implement MFA for Endpoint Logins (Priority: Critical)
    Justification: To mitigate the risk of unauthorized access from compromised credentials (G-001), enforce MFA for all user logins to workstations and laptops.
    Suggested Solutions: Leverage modern authentication systems such as Windows Hello for Business, or third-party solutions like Duo Security or Okta.

  2. Enforce MFA on All Sensitive Data Systems (Priority: Critical)
    Justification: To protect the organization’s most valuable data assets (G-002), mandate MFA for access to all systems identified as containing sensitive, proprietary, or regulated information.
    Suggested Solutions: Integrate applications with a central identity provider that supports MFA. Use RADIUS, SAML, or OpenID Connect to extend MFA protection to a wide range of systems.

  3. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) (Priority: High)
    Justification: To mitigate legal and insider risks (G-003), an AUP must be drafted, approved by management and legal counsel, and communicated to all employees. All employees should be required to formally acknowledge the policy.

  4. Conduct a Full External Vulnerability Scan (Priority: High)
    Justification: To address the critical visibility gap from the failed data input, a comprehensive, authenticated and unauthenticated vulnerability scan of the external IP address (98.52.42.246) and any other internet-facing assets must be performed immediately.
