Executive Overview

This assessment reveals critical deficiencies in the foundational cybersecurity controls at Golden Gate Gaming. The most significant findings stem from the organizational security questionnaire, which highlights a lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computer access, and a complete absence of a security awareness training program for employees.

These gaps expose the organization to a high likelihood of successful phishing attacks, business email compromise, and unauthorized access to sensitive systems. While some positive controls are in place, such as an acceptable use policy and MFA for sensitive data systems, the weaknesses in frontline defenses undermine the overall security posture.

It is imperative to note that the external network scan data and the list of pre-existing vulnerabilities were unavailable for this analysis due to corrupted input files. Consequently, this report cannot comment on the external attack surface or previously tracked risks. A new technical scan and a review of existing risk documentation are strongly recommended as immediate follow-up actions.

The recommendations in this report prioritize the implementation of MFA and the establishment of a formal security awareness training program to mitigate the most severe risks.

Analysis of Gaps

The questionnaire reveals four major security gaps:

  1. No MFA for Email: This is a critical vulnerability. Email accounts are a primary target for attackers seeking to perform reconnaissance, launch internal phishing campaigns, or commit business email compromise (BEC) fraud.

  2. No MFA for Computers: Lack of MFA on endpoints allows an attacker with stolen credentials to gain direct access to a user’s machine and potentially the internal network.

  3. No New Employee Training: Failing to train new employees on security best practices from day one leaves a critical window of vulnerability.

  4. No Annual Training: The threat landscape evolves continuously. Without annual refresher training, employees’ ability to recognize and respond to new threats diminishes over time.

Recommendations

The following actionable recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of Golden Gate Gaming.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (High Priority):

    • Action: Immediately begin a project to enable and enforce MFA across the organization.

    • Scope: Prioritize all email accounts (e.g., via Office 365 or Google Workspace policies). Following email, enforce MFA for all computer logins (e.g., using Duo, Okta, or Windows Hello for Business).

    • Justification: This is the single most effective control to mitigate the risk of GGG-001 and prevent unauthorized access from compromised credentials.

  2. Establish a Security Awareness Training Program (High Priority):

    • Action: Procure and implement a security awareness training solution.

    • Scope: The program must include mandatory training for all new hires as part of their onboarding process. Additionally, all existing employees must complete a training module annually. Consider regular phishing simulations to reinforce learning.

    • Justification: This directly mitigates risk GGG-002 by equipping employees to serve as a human firewall against phishing and social engineering attempts.

  3. Conduct an External Network Vulnerability Scan (Medium Priority):

    • Action: Schedule and execute a new authenticated and unauthenticated vulnerability scan against the external IP address 94.162.97.132 and any other public-facing assets.

    • Justification: The initial scan data was unusable. A successful scan is essential to identify and patch technical vulnerabilities on the network perimeter before they can be exploited by attackers.

  4. Review and Consolidate Risk Documentation (Medium Priority):

    • Action: Perform an internal audit to locate and consolidate all existing risk registers and vulnerability tracking documents.

    • Justification: The provided list of current risks was inaccessible. Maintaining a central and up-to-date risk register is a fundamental component of a mature cybersecurity program.
