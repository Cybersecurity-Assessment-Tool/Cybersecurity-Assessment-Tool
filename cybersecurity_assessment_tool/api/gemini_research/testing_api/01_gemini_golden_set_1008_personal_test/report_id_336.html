Executive Overview

This report provides a cybersecurity posture assessment for Astraeus Aerospace. The analysis is based on a review of organizational security controls provided via a questionnaire. It is critical to note that the technical network scan data (Input_1_Network_Scan_JSON) and the list of pre-existing risks (Input_3_Current_Risks_JSON) were unavailable or corrupted at the time of this assessment. Consequently, this report focuses primarily on procedural and policy-based controls.

The assessment reveals several critical and high-risk security gaps that significantly increase the organization’s vulnerability to cyber threats. The most severe findings include the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computer access, which exposes the organization to account takeover and unauthorized access.

Furthermore, the absence of a formal Acceptable Use Policy and a security training program for new employees indicates foundational weaknesses in the organization’s security culture and governance. While some controls are in place, such as MFA for sensitive systems and annual training for existing employees, the identified gaps require immediate attention to establish a baseline security posture.

Risk Assessment

The risk assessment is based on the findings from the Security Control Review, as the pre-existing risk data (Input_3_Current_Risks_JSON) was also unavailable. The identified risks are documented and prioritized below.

Summary of Identified Risks
Risk ID Risk Name Description
RISK-001 Critical: Lack of MFA on Email Accounts Without MFA, email accounts are highly susceptible to compromise via phishing or password spraying. A compromised email account can lead to data breaches, financial fraud, and further internal network compromise.
RISK-002 Critical: Lack of MFA on Workstations The absence of MFA on computer logins allows an attacker with stolen credentials to gain direct access to an employee’s workstation and potentially the internal network.
RISK-003 High: No Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding company assets and data, increasing the risk of insider threat, misuse of resources, and non-compliance.
RISK-004 High: No Security Training for New Hires New employees are not equipped with the necessary security knowledge from day one, making them more likely to fall victim to social engineering attacks and mishandle sensitive data.

Recommendations

The following prioritized recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of Astraeus Aerospace.

  1. [Critical] Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Immediately enforce MFA for all users accessing email, whether on-premises or cloud-based (e.g., Office 365, Google Workspace).

    • Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all workstation and laptop logins, especially for users with administrative or sensitive data access.

  2. [High] Develop Foundational Security Policies:

    • Develop, approve, and communicate a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) to all employees. This policy should clearly define the rules for using company IT assets, data handling, and internet usage.

  3. [High] Enhance Security Awareness Program:

    • Integrate mandatory security awareness training into the new employee onboarding process. This ensures that security principles are established from the beginning of employment.

  4. [Informational] Re-initiate Technical Assessment:

    • Schedule and conduct a new, authenticated external network vulnerability scan against the public-facing IP address and other known assets.

    • Review and consolidate any existing risk management documentation to ensure a complete and accurate picture of the organization’s risk landscape.
