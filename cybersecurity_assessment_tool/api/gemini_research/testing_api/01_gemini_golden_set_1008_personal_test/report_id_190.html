Executive Summary

This report details the findings of a cybersecurity posture assessment conducted for Copperhead Cables. The assessment integrated an external network scan, a review of existing risks, and an analysis of organizational security controls based on a questionnaire.

The primary findings indicate significant gaps in foundational security controls. While the external network scan of the target IP address revealed no open ports—a positive indicator of a well-configured perimeter firewall—critical vulnerabilities exist within the organization’s access control and employee security awareness policies.

The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email and computer access represents a Critical risk, exposing the organization to credential theft, business email compromise, and unauthorized system access. Furthermore, the lack of a formal security awareness training program for new and existing employees constitutes a High risk, leaving the organization vulnerable to phishing and other social engineering attacks.

Immediate remediation efforts should focus on implementing MFA across all critical systems and establishing a comprehensive security awareness training program to mitigate these identified risks.

Analysis of Gaps

The questionnaire reveals critical deficiencies in two key areas:

  1. Access Control: The failure to enforce MFA on email and computer logins is a severe weakness. Email is a primary target for attackers, and compromised accounts can lead to data breaches and financial fraud. Unprotected computer logins remove a crucial layer of defense against unauthorized access.

  2. Security Awareness: The complete absence of a security awareness training program means employees are likely unprepared to identify and respond to common threats like phishing, malware, and social engineering. This significantly increases the organization’s "human" attack surface.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended to mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall security posture of Copperhead Cables.

  1. Implement MFA for All Email Accounts (Priority: Immediate)
    Rationale: To address the critical risk of business email compromise.
    Action: Enforce MFA for all user accounts on the CopperheadCables.org email platform. Provide clear instructions and support to all employees during the rollout.

  2. Enforce MFA for Workstation and Remote Access (Priority: Immediate)
    Rationale: To prevent unauthorized access to the internal network and company devices.
    Action: Deploy and mandate the use of MFA for all computer logins (Windows, macOS) and any remote access solutions (e.g., VPN).

  3. Establish a Security Awareness Training Program (Priority: High)
    Rationale: To reduce the risk of human error and strengthen the "human firewall."
    Action: Procure and implement a security awareness training solution. The program must include initial training for all new hires and mandatory annual refresher training for all staff. Conduct regular phishing simulations to measure effectiveness.
