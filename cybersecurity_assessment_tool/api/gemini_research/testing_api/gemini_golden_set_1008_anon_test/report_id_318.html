Executive Summary

This report provides a cybersecurity posture assessment for [Organization Name], based on a review of organizational security controls, technical scan results, and pre-existing risk information.

The assessment identifies several strengths, particularly in the consistent application of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) across email, computer login, and sensitive data systems. However, critical gaps in policy and ongoing security awareness training have been identified. Specifically, the absence of an employee acceptable use policy and the lack of annual security awareness training for all employees represent significant risks to the organization’s security posture.

Technical scan results indicate a relatively secure external footprint, with the primary finding being that port 80 (HTTP) is currently reported as closed. This finding positively contradicts an existing risk entry regarding an "Unencrypted Web Server" on port 80. Further investigation is recommended to reconcile this discrepancy, either confirming mitigation or identifying if the existing risk refers to a different scope.

Overall, while technical exposure appears minimal based on the provided scan data, the identified policy and training deficiencies present a substantial internal risk that requires immediate attention. Recommendations provided in this report aim to address these areas and enhance the overall security posture.

Analysis of Gaps

Based on the questionnaire results, the following critical policy and process gaps were identified:

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A fundamental policy defining the acceptable use of organizational IT resources is missing. This can lead to misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance with regulations, as employees lack clear guidelines on responsible technology usage.

  • Lack of Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: While new employees receive training, the absence of recurring annual training for all staff means that security knowledge can become stale, new threats may not be understood, and a consistent security culture may fail to develop. Human error remains a leading cause of security incidents, and ongoing training is crucial for mitigation.

Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name].

High-Priority Recommendations

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Draft, review, and formally implement a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines expectations for employee behavior regarding IT systems, data handling, internet usage, and device policies.

    • Justification: An AUP provides a foundational security control, clarifies responsibilities, and reduces risk from accidental or malicious misuse of company resources.

    • Timeline: Immediate (within 1-2 months for initial draft and implementation).

  2. Establish and Execute Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Develop a program for mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, not just new hires. This should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), data protection best practices, and the organization’s specific security policies (including the new AUP).

    • Justification: Ongoing training ensures employees remain vigilant against evolving threats, reinforcing a strong security culture and reducing the likelihood of human-error-induced incidents.

    • Timeline: Immediate planning, with first annual training conducted within 3-4 months.

Medium-Priority Recommendations

  1. Reconcile "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk vs. Scan Results:

    • Action: Investigate why the pre-existing risk of "Unencrypted Web Server (Port 80 is open)" contradicts the recent scan showing Port 80 as closed. Determine if the risk has been mitigated, if it pertains to a different target or internal system, or if the original assessment was inaccurate.

    • Justification: Clarifying this discrepancy ensures the risk register accurately reflects the current state of assets and prevents allocation of resources to already mitigated issues, or overlooking actual threats.

    • Timeline: Within 1 month.

General Recommendations

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning: Implement a schedule for regular external and internal vulnerability scans to proactively identify and address potential weaknesses.

  • Maintain a Comprehensive Risk Register: Ensure the organization’s risk register is regularly updated with new findings, mitigation statuses, and re-assessments.
