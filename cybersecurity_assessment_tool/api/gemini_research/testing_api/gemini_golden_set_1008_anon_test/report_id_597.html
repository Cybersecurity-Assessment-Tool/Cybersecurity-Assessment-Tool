Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified security gaps and mitigate the associated risks. They are prioritized based on their assessed severity and potential impact.

Immediate Actions (Critical Risks)

  1. Investigate and Secure Port 8080:

    • Immediate Closure/Blockade: If the service is not authorized or necessary for external access, immediately close Port 8080 or block all external inbound traffic to on this port.

    • Thorough Investigation: Conduct an urgent and thorough investigation into the service running on Port 8080, specifically the application identified by "TOP SECRET DB." Determine its purpose, what data it contains or exposes, and who has access.

    • Security Hardening: If the service is legitimate and required, implement stringent access controls (e.g., IP whitelisting), robust authentication (including MFA), encryption (HTTPS), and ensure the application itself is hardened against common vulnerabilities.

    • Risk Re-evaluation: Formally update and correct the existing risk assessment for Port 8080, acknowledging its critical status.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Email Access: Deploy and enforce MFA for all email accounts across the organization.

    • Computer Login: Implement MFA for all computer logins, especially for administrative accounts and sensitive workstations.

    • Sensitive Data Systems: Enforce MFA for access to all systems and applications that store, process, or transmit sensitive data.

Mid-Term Actions (High Risks)

  1. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Policy Creation: Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of organizational IT resources, including internet, email, software, and hardware.

    • Communication and Acknowledgment: Ensure the AUP is formally communicated to all employees, and obtain documented acknowledgment of their understanding and agreement upon hiring and annually thereafter.

    • Enforcement: Establish clear disciplinary actions for AUP violations and enforce them consistently.

  2. Establish Security Awareness Training Program:

    • New Employee Training: Develop and implement mandatory security awareness training for all new employees during their onboarding process. This should cover fundamental security concepts, phishing recognition, password best practices, and AUP review.

    • Annual Training: Implement mandatory annual security awareness training for all existing employees to refresh knowledge, introduce new threats, and reinforce secure behaviors.

    • Phishing Simulations: Consider periodic phishing simulations to test employee awareness and identify areas for further training.
