Risk Assessment

Based on the available information, the following critical risks and observations have been identified:

Critical Gaps from Security Questionnaire

The "No" responses from the security questionnaire highlight several significant security control gaps:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Email is a primary vector for phishing and account compromise. The absence of MFA significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to employee email accounts, leading to potential data breaches, business email compromise (BEC) attacks, and further network penetration.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Failure to enforce MFA for systems containing sensitive data allows attackers who compromise a single set of credentials to gain access to critical organizational assets without an additional authentication factor. This directly jeopardizes data confidentiality and integrity.

  • Absence of Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees are often the most susceptible to social engineering attacks due to unfamiliarity with organizational policies and common threats. Delaying or omitting initial security awareness training leaves the organization vulnerable to human-factor risks from the outset of an employee’s tenure.

Lack of Technical Visibility

The absence of any network scan data prevents a complete technical assessment of the organization’s external exposure. This lack of visibility is a risk in itself, as unknown vulnerabilities or misconfigurations on exposed services cannot be identified or remediated.

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access: Mandate MFA for all email accounts across the organization. This significantly reduces the risk of account compromise due to stolen or phished credentials.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems: Extend MFA requirements to all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational data. Prioritize systems based on the criticality and sensitivity of the data they handle.

  3. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Develop and implement a robust security awareness training program that all new employees must complete during their onboarding process. This training should cover phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, and data handling best practices.

  4. Conduct Comprehensive Network and Vulnerability Scans:

    • Perform regular external network scans against all public-facing IP addresses to identify open ports, services, and potential vulnerabilities.

    • Conduct internal vulnerability scans across the network and endpoints to detect misconfigurations, unpatched software, and other security weaknesses.

    • Include the specific target [Target IP] (or other relevant external IPs) in future scanning efforts.

  5. Establish and Maintain a Centralized Risk Register: Develop a comprehensive risk management program, including a centralized risk register to track, assess, and manage identified vulnerabilities and risks, whether technical, operational, or policy-related. Regularly review and update this register.

  6. Review and Update Security Policies: Ensure that security policies, particularly those related to MFA implementation and security awareness training, are up-to-date, clearly communicated, and enforced.
