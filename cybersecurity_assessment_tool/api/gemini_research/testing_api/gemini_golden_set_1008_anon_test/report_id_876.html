6. Recommendations

Based on the identified gaps and associated risks, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all email accounts across the organization. Prioritize administrator and executive accounts due to their elevated access and potential impact.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, protecting against phishing, credential theft, and unauthorized access to critical communications.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy MFA solutions for user logins to all organizational computers, especially those accessing sensitive networks or data. This could involve smart cards, hardware tokens, or mobile authenticators.

    • Benefit: Adds a crucial layer of security, preventing unauthorized access to endpoints and internal systems even if primary credentials are stolen or brute-forced.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires. This program should be updated regularly to cover current threats, phishing recognition, social engineering tactics, password hygiene, and data handling best practices.

    • Benefit: Enhances employee vigilance and reduces the likelihood of human-factor incidents, making the organization more resilient to a wide range of cyberattacks.
