Key Findings from Questionnaire

The questionnaire responses reveal critical gaps in fundamental security practices:

  • MFA for Email (Critical): The absence of MFA for email access is a severe vulnerability, making email accounts highly susceptible to phishing and brute-force attacks. Compromised email can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data breaches, and further network penetration.

  • Security Awareness Training (Critical): The lack of security awareness training for new employees and the absence of annual refresher training for all employees represent significant human-factor risks. Untrained employees are more likely to fall victim to social engineering attacks, malware, and other threats, serving as a primary entry point for attackers.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified security gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of .

Critical Recommendations

  • Implement MFA for Email Access:

    • Action: Immediately deploy and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all email accounts, including administrative accounts.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access to email accounts, preventing phishing-related compromises and business email compromise (BEC).

  • Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • Action: Establish and enforce annual (or more frequent) mandatory security awareness training for all existing employees.

    • Benefit: Equips employees with the knowledge to identify and report phishing attempts, malicious links, and other social engineering tactics, reducing the human element as a primary attack vector.

Other Recommendations

  • Investigate Port 80 Discrepancy:

    • Action: Verify the true status of Port 80 on . Confirm if the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk has been remediated or if there are other instances of this service.

    • Benefit: Ensures that all assets are correctly identified and risks are accurately assessed, preventing overlooked vulnerabilities. If a service is indeed running on Port 80, ensure it is either closed, restricted, or upgraded to use HTTPS with a valid certificate.

  • Regular Network Scanning and Vulnerability Management:

    • Action: Implement a routine schedule for internal and external network vulnerability scanning to proactively identify new exposures.

    • Benefit: Provides continuous visibility into the attack surface and helps identify new or re-emerging vulnerabilities before they can be exploited.
