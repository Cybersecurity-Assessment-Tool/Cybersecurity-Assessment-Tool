Analysis of Questionnaire Responses

The review of security controls reveals two critical gaps:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Email is a primary vector for phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and credential theft. The absence of MFA significantly increases the risk of account compromise.

  • Absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): An AUP defines acceptable conduct for employees when using company assets and networks. Its absence can lead to misuse of resources, data breaches, and non-compliance with regulations.

The organization does, however, demonstrate good practices in requiring MFA for computer logins and sensitive data access, and in conducting security awareness training.

Recommendations

To enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided, prioritizing the identified high-severity risks:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Immediately enable and enforce MFA for all organizational email accounts across all users.

    • Evaluate and implement strong MFA methods (e.g., authenticator apps, hardware tokens) over weaker ones (e.g., SMS-based MFA).

    • Conduct user training on the importance and usage of MFA.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT assets, networks, and data.

    • Ensure the AUP covers topics such as password hygiene, data handling, internet usage, software installation, and communication etiquette.

    • Distribute the AUP to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgments, and integrate it into new employee onboarding and annual training.

  3. Conduct a Comprehensive Technical Vulnerability Scan:

    • Perform a detailed network vulnerability scan of all client-facing and internal assets to identify open ports, outdated software, misconfigurations, and known vulnerabilities.

    • Prioritize patching and remediation efforts based on the severity of identified technical vulnerabilities.

  4. Provide Complete Organizational Data for Future Assessments:

    • Ensure that future assessments are supported by complete and accurate organizational details (name, domain, IP addresses, etc.) to enable tailored reporting.

  5. Maintain a Current Risk Register:

    • Establish and regularly update a comprehensive risk register that tracks all identified vulnerabilities, their severity, potential impact, and remediation status.
