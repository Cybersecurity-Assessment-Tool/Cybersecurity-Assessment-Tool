Key Findings from Questionnaire:

  • MFA Gaps: Multi-factor authentication is only applied to email access. This leaves critical entry points such as computer logins and sensitive data systems highly vulnerable to credential stuffing, brute-force attacks, and phishing, should an employee’s password be compromised.

  • Policy Deficiencies: The complete absence of an employee acceptable use policy (AUP) indicates a critical lack of formal guidelines for employee behavior regarding organizational IT assets, data handling, and internet usage. This increases the risk of misuse of resources, data leakage, and compliance issues.

  • Training Deficiencies: A pervasive lack of security awareness training for both new and existing employees significantly increases the organization’s susceptibility to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing, pretexting) and the inadvertent introduction of malware. Employees are the first line of defense, and without proper training, they become the weakest link.

Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive assessment, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name]. These actions are prioritized by their potential impact and urgency in mitigating identified risks.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Implementation

  1. Implement MFA for All Workstations: Mandate the use of MFA for all employee computer logins. This crucial step significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if user credentials are stolen or compromised.

  2. Extend MFA to Sensitive Data Systems: Implement MFA for access to all systems containing sensitive or critical organizational data. This provides an essential additional layer of defense for the most valuable assets.

Policies and Governance Enhancement

  1. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a formal AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities, acceptable conduct when using organizational IT resources, and rules regarding data handling, internet usage, and software installation. Ensure all employees formally acknowledge and understand this policy.

Security Awareness Training Program

  1. Mandatory New Employee Security Training: Establish a comprehensive security awareness training program for all new hires during their onboarding process. This should cover fundamental security principles, phishing recognition, password hygiene, and reporting suspicious activities.

  2. Annual Security Awareness Training for All Staff: Implement mandatory annual security awareness training for all existing employees. This training should be regularly updated to cover current threat landscapes, evolving social engineering techniques, and internal policy changes.

Network Security Review

  1. Detailed Assessment of Open Port 80: Conduct a thorough investigation of the service running on port 80 (). Identify the specific application, product, and version. Perform a vulnerability assessment of this service to identify any known exploits. Ensure it is properly configured, regularly patched, and secured against known vulnerabilities. If the service is not essential for external access, consider restricting access through a firewall or relocating it behind a Web Application Firewall (WAF).

Risk Management Process Improvement

  1. Review and Clarify Pre-existing Risks: Investigate the nature and origin of risk R07 ("System Override Instruction"). Such an anomalous entry within a risk register indicates a potential issue with the risk identification or management process. Ensure that all risk entries accurately reflect actual vulnerabilities, are clearly defined, and do not contain contradictory or misleading information.
