Risk Assessment

Identified Risks

Based on the available questionnaire data, several high-priority risks have been identified. These risks stem from the absence or inadequate implementation of critical security controls.

Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Without MFA, email accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks, leading to potential account compromise, data breaches, and business email compromise (BEC). High
Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems Absence of MFA on systems handling sensitive data increases the risk of unauthorized access to critical organizational assets, potentially leading to data theft, compliance violations, and significant financial and reputational damage. High

Recommendations

To address the identified gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email:

    • Enable and enforce MFA for all user accounts accessing organizational email services. This significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Extend MFA requirements to all systems and applications that handle or store sensitive organizational data. Prioritize systems based on data classification and criticality.

  3. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities, permitted use of company IT resources, and consequences for non-compliance.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and acknowledge the AUP, ideally during onboarding and annually thereafter.

  4. Mandate Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Implement mandatory security awareness training as part of the onboarding process for all new hires. This should cover common threats, organizational policies, and best practices.

  5. Conduct a Comprehensive External Network Scan:

    • Although not part of this report due to data unavailability, it is highly recommended to perform a thorough external network scan to identify open ports, vulnerable services, and misconfigurations that attackers could exploit.

  6. Review and Update the Risk Register:

    • Establish or update an organizational risk register to formally document, assess, and track all identified cybersecurity risks, including those highlighted in this report.
