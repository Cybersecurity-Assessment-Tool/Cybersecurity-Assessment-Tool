Recommendations

Despite the positive findings from the questionnaire and the absence of identified issues from the provided (empty) technical data, general best practices are always recommended to maintain and continuously improve cybersecurity posture.

  1. Implement Regular and Comprehensive Network Scans: Establish a schedule for regular internal and external network vulnerability scans. This proactive measure will help identify open ports, outdated services, and misconfigurations that could lead to exploitable vulnerabilities.

  2. Develop a Robust Vulnerability Management Program: Once vulnerabilities are identified (via scans or other means), ensure a formal process is in place to assess, prioritize, and remediate them promptly. This includes timely patching of systems, updating software, and addressing configuration weaknesses.

  3. Periodically Review and Update Security Policies: While an acceptable use policy is in place, ensure all organizational security policies are reviewed annually and updated to reflect current threat landscapes, technological changes, and regulatory requirements.

  4. Enhance Security Awareness Training: While annual training is commendable, consider supplementing it with more frequent, shorter training modules, targeted alerts, or simulated phishing campaigns to keep employees vigilant against evolving threats.

  5. Establish and Test an Incident Response Plan: Develop a comprehensive incident response plan and conduct regular tabletop exercises or simulations to ensure the organization can effectively detect, respond to, contain, and recover from security incidents.

  6. Consider Advanced Threat Protection Technologies: Investigate and implement Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) solutions across all endpoints for advanced threat detection, proactive hunting, investigation, and rapid response capabilities.
