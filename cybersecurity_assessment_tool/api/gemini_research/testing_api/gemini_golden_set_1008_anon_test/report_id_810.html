3.1. Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The following critical gaps were identified from the questionnaire responses:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Email is a primary vector for phishing and account compromise. Not requiring MFA significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to employee accounts and potential internal system breaches.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): An AUP defines appropriate conduct for employees when using organizational IT resources. Its absence can lead to misuse of resources, data breaches, and legal liabilities, as employees may not be aware of expected behavior.

  • Inadequate Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees are often prime targets for social engineering attacks as they may not be fully familiar with organizational policies and security best practices. Lack of initial training leaves them vulnerable and introduces unnecessary risk to the organization.

6. Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name]. These actions address identified gaps and strengthen overall security.

6.1. Immediate Actions

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts across the organization. This is a critical control to prevent unauthorized access, even if credentials are compromised.

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Draft and roll out a comprehensive AUP for all employees. Ensure it clearly defines appropriate conduct for using company devices, network resources, and handling sensitive data. This policy should be acknowledged by all employees.

  • Introduce Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Integrate a mandatory, comprehensive security awareness module into the onboarding process for all new hires. This training should cover common threats (e.g., phishing, malware), secure password practices, data handling, and the procedure for reporting suspicious activities.

6.2. Long-Term Strategy

  • Regular Review and Update of Security Policies: Periodically review and update all security policies, including the AUP and training programs, to adapt to evolving threat landscapes, new technologies, and organizational changes.

  • Advanced Threat Simulation and Continuous Training: Consider conducting simulated phishing campaigns and advanced security training for all employees at regular intervals to continuously test and improve their resilience against sophisticated social engineering attacks.

  • Continuous Vulnerability Management: Implement a robust program for regular network vulnerability scanning and penetration testing across all external and internal assets. This proactive measure will help identify and address technical weaknesses as the infrastructure evolves. While no open ports were found in this scan, continuous monitoring is crucial for any changes.

  • Secure Web Server Configuration (Future Deployments): For any future web server deployments or existing internal web services, ensure they are configured to use HTTPS (SSL/TLS) exclusively, redirecting all HTTP traffic, and are regularly patched and monitored for vulnerabilities.
