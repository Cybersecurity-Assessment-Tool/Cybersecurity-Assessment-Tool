Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, concise, and comprehensive AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company assets (e.g., computers, networks, internet, email, data).

    • Ensure the AUP covers topics such as data handling, password policies, software installation, personal use, and prohibited activities.

    • Distribute the AUP to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgments, and integrate it into new employee onboarding processes.

    • Periodically review and update the AUP to reflect changes in technology, policies, and regulatory requirements.

  2. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Implement a mandatory security awareness training program specifically designed for all new hires.

    • This training should occur within their first week of employment and cover essential topics such as phishing, social engineering, password best practices, data classification, incident reporting, and the organizationâ€™s AUP.

    • Utilize engaging and interactive training materials to ensure comprehension and retention.

    • Track completion rates and reinforce training through regular communications.

  3. Maintain Strong MFA Practices:

    • Continue the excellent practice of requiring MFA for email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems.

    • Regularly review MFA implementations for effectiveness and ensure all eligible systems and users are covered.

  4. Consider Regular External Vulnerability Scans:

    • While the initial scan showed no open ports, consider scheduling regular external vulnerability scans (at least quarterly) to continually monitor the external perimeter for any unintentional exposures as network configurations change over time.

Conclusion

[Organization Name] demonstrates a good starting point with strong MFA controls. However, the identified gaps in acceptable use policy and initial security awareness training for new employees present significant risks that require immediate attention. By proactively implementing the recommended policies and training programs, the organization can significantly strengthen its defense against common cyber threats and foster a more robust security culture. Continuous monitoring, regular policy review, and ongoing employee education are essential for maintaining a strong and adaptable cybersecurity posture.
