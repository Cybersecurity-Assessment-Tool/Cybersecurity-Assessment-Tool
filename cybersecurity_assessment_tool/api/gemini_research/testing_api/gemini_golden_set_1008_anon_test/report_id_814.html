Analysis of Questionnaire Findings

The questionnaire results indicate significant gaps in fundamental security hygiene:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): While MFA is utilized for sensitive data systems, its absence for email and general computer login poses a severe risk. This makes the organization highly vulnerable to credential stuffing, brute-force attacks, and phishing, as compromised credentials could grant attackers easy access.

  • Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of an AUP means there are no formal guidelines for employees regarding the proper use of organizational IT resources. This can lead to misuse, data breaches, and a lack of accountability for employee actions.

  • Security Awareness Training: The complete absence of security awareness training for both new and existing employees is a critical vulnerability. Employees are often the weakest link in a security chain; without training, they are more susceptible to social engineering, phishing, and other human-centric attacks.

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and significantly improve the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided. These are actionable steps prioritized to tackle the most critical findings first.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Universally:

    • High Priority: Deploy MFA for all email accounts.

    • High Priority: Implement MFA for all computer logins, especially for administrative accounts and systems processing sensitive data.

    • Consistently enforce MFA across all critical systems and services accessible by employees.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • High Priority: Create a clear, comprehensive AUP that outlines acceptable and unacceptable use of company IT assets, data, and networks.

    • Ensure the AUP covers topics such as password hygiene, data handling, internet usage, email etiquette, and incident reporting.

    • Mandate all employees to read, understand, and sign the AUP annually.

  3. Establish and Maintain a Robust Security Awareness Training Program:

    • High Priority: Implement mandatory security awareness training for all new hires during onboarding.

    • High Priority: Conduct annual security awareness training for all employees, covering current threats like phishing, social engineering, malware, and safe browsing practices.

    • Incorporate regular simulated phishing exercises to test employee vigilance and provide targeted remediation.

  4. Conduct Comprehensive External and Internal Network Scans:

    • Perform thorough, authenticated vulnerability scans and penetration tests from both external and internal perspectives to accurately map the attack surface and identify hidden vulnerabilities.

    • Regularly schedule these scans (e.g., quarterly or biannually) and promptly address any discovered findings.

  5. Develop an Incident Response Plan (IRP):

    • Create and regularly update an IRP that outlines procedures for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents.

    • Conduct periodic tabletop exercises to test the effectiveness of the IRP and ensure all relevant personnel are familiar with their roles.
