Executive Summary

This report details the findings from an initial cybersecurity posture assessment for [Organization Name]. The assessment was primarily based on a security questionnaire provided by the organization. No network scan data for the target [Target IP] or existing organizational risk data was available for analysis in this report.

Adherence to best practices in these areas is essential to enhance the organization’s resilience against modern cyber threats.

Risk Assessment

Based on the available information from the security questionnaire, the following risks have been identified. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided for inclusion in this assessment.

Identified Cybersecurity Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access The organization does not require Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for accessing email. This significantly increases the risk of account takeover via phishing, credential stuffing, or brute-force attacks, potentially leading to business email compromise and data breaches. High
Lack of MFA for Computer Login Multi-Factor Authentication is not required for users to log into computers. This leaves internal systems vulnerable to unauthorized access if a user’s password is stolen or guessed, potentially leading to lateral movement within the network and system compromise. High
Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training While new employees receive security awareness training, all employees do not receive training at least once per year. This leads to reduced effectiveness over time, making employees more susceptible to social engineering attacks, phishing, and other human-centric vulnerabilities as threats evolve. Medium

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all employee email accounts. Prioritize administrative and privileged accounts.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of email account compromise, even if passwords are stolen.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all workstation and server logins, especially for users with elevated privileges.

    • Benefit: Adds an essential layer of security, preventing unauthorized access to systems with compromised credentials.

  3. Establish Regular Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, reinforcing best practices and educating on evolving threats (e.g., advanced phishing techniques, ransomware).

    • Benefit: Improves employee vigilance, reduces the likelihood of successful social engineering attacks, and fosters a strong security culture.

  4. Conduct a Comprehensive Network Scan:

    • Action: Engage in a thorough network vulnerability scan of internal and external IP addresses, including the specified target [Target IP].

    • Benefit: Identifies open ports, services, outdated software versions, and known vulnerabilities that could be exploited by attackers, providing a clearer technical risk landscape.

  5. Regularly Review and Update Risk Register:

    • Action: Establish a process for regularly identifying, assessing, and documenting cybersecurity risks within the organization.

    • Benefit: Ensures that the organization maintains an up-to-date understanding of its risk posture and can prioritize mitigation efforts effectively.

Conclusion

The initial cybersecurity posture assessment for [Organization Name] highlights critical areas requiring immediate attention, primarily related to the implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication and consistent security awareness training.
