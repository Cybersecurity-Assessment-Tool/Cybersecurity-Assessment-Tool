Risk Assessment

Identified Risks and Vulnerabilities

Based on the security questionnaire responses, the following critical risks and vulnerabilities have been identified:

  • Risk Name: Missing Acceptable Use Policy

    • Overview: The organization lacks a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) for employees. An AUP defines appropriate use of company assets, IT resources, and data, which is crucial for mitigating insider threats, preventing misuse, and establishing clear behavioral expectations.

    • Severity: High

  • Risk Name: Incomplete New Employee Security Training

    • Overview: New employees are not currently receiving security awareness training. This leaves new hires potentially unaware of critical security policies, common threat vectors like phishing and social engineering, and best practices for data handling from their first day of employment. This significantly increases the risk of human error leading to security incidents.

    • Severity: High

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and further enhance the organizationâ€™s cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear, comprehensive, and legally reviewed Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) outlining appropriate use of company IT resources, data, and communication systems.

    • Ensure all existing employees review and formally acknowledge the AUP, preferably on an annual basis, and integrate it into the onboarding process for new hires.

    • Clearly communicate consequences for non-compliance.

  • Mandate Security Awareness Training for New Hires:

    • Establish a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees during their initial onboarding process.

    • The training should cover essential topics such as phishing and social engineering recognition, strong password hygiene, proper data handling and classification, incident reporting procedures, and the importance of privacy.

    • Supplement initial training with access to ongoing educational resources and periodic refreshers.

  • Conduct Regular and Comprehensive Security Assessments:

    • Implement a schedule for periodic security assessments, including external and internal network vulnerability scans, web application scans (if applicable), and penetration tests.

    • These assessments should proactively identify and help remediate technical vulnerabilities across the IT infrastructure before they can be exploited by malicious actors.

    • Consider engaging third-party security experts to ensure objectivity and expertise.

  • Continuous Review and Update of Security Policies and Procedures:

    • Regularly review and update all security policies and procedures (including the AUP, incident response plan, and training programs) to ensure they remain relevant, effective, and aligned with evolving threat landscapes and regulatory requirements.

    • Ensure all policies are easily accessible to employees and that changes are communicated effectively.
