Risk Correlation and Impact

The questionnaire results highlight significant gaps in fundamental access control mechanisms (MFA) for internal systems and sensitive data. These deficiencies pose a high risk of unauthorized access and data breaches should employee credentials be compromised. The lack of security awareness training for new employees further exacerbates this risk by increasing the likelihood of successful phishing or social engineering attacks that lead to credential compromise.

Regarding technical risks, the recent scan showing Port 80 as closed is a positive indicator that a previously identified risk (PR-1, Unencrypted Web Server) might have been addressed. However, it’s crucial to confirm if this risk referred to the exact target scanned and if its resolution is complete across all relevant assets. If the risk indeed remains active for other systems, it would indicate an ongoing exposure to unencrypted communication.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to improve ’s cybersecurity posture. Recommendations are prioritized based on their potential impact and severity.

High-Priority Recommendations

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins (Addressing QR-1):

    • Action: Mandate MFA for all user logins to company computers and endpoints.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if passwords are stolen or guessed.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems (Addressing QR-2):

    • Action: Enforce MFA for accessing all systems containing sensitive or critical organizational data.

    • Benefit: Provides a crucial layer of defense for the most valuable assets, protecting against insider threats and external breaches.

Medium-Priority Recommendations

  1. Establish Security Awareness Training for New Employees (Addressing QR-3):

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees during their onboarding process.

    • Benefit: Educates new hires on common threats (e.g., phishing, social engineering) and safe computing practices from the outset, reducing the human element of risk.

  2. Verify and Re-evaluate "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk (Addressing PR-1):

    • Action: Investigate the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk from Input 3. Confirm if it applies to the scanned target () or another asset. If Port 80 is confirmed closed on the relevant system as per the latest scan, formally retire or update this risk. If it pertains to another service or IP, ensure appropriate encryption (e.g., SSL/TLS) is implemented.

    • Benefit: Ensures the risk register is accurate and reflects the current state of security controls, preventing misallocation of resources.

Conclusion

The cybersecurity posture of shows areas of strength, such as existing MFA for email and an annual security awareness program for all employees. However, critical vulnerabilities exist in the lack of MFA for computer and sensitive data system access, and the absence of initial security training for new employees. Addressing these fundamental gaps should be the primary focus to build a more resilient defense against common cyber threats. Continuous monitoring and regular security assessments are vital to maintain and improve the organization’s security posture over time.
