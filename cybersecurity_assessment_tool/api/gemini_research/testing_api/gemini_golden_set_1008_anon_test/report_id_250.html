Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of the security control review and the absence of identified pre-existing vulnerabilities, the primary risks for [Organization Name] stem from gaps in fundamental security practices.

Risk Name Overview Severity
Inadequate MFA for Computer Logon Lack of multi-factor authentication (MFA) on computer logons significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access via stolen credentials or brute-force attacks, potentially leading to system compromise and data breaches. High
Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without an AUP, employees may be unaware of permissible and impermissible use of organizational IT resources, leading to potential misuse, security incidents, and legal liabilities. Medium
Lack of New Employee Security Awareness Training New employees are a common target for social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing). Without initial training, they may lack the knowledge to identify and report threats, making the organization vulnerable from day one. High
Lack of Annual Security Awareness Training Regular security awareness training reinforces best practices and educates employees on evolving threats. The absence of annual training leads to diminished awareness and increased human error vulnerabilities over time, making the organization susceptible to current attack vectors. High

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and strengthen the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Computer Logon:

    • Deploy and enforce multi-factor authentication for all user logons to computers and workstations. This significantly reduces the risk of credential theft and unauthorized access.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a clear and comprehensive AUP that outlines the rules for using organizational IT resources, including internet, email, software, and hardware.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and acknowledge the AUP, and provide regular refreshers.

  3. Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Establish a mandatory security awareness training program for all new hires, to be completed as part of their onboarding process.

    • Training should cover topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, data handling, and reporting suspicious activities.

  4. Conduct Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement an ongoing program for annual security awareness training for all employees.

    • Training content should be updated regularly to address new and emerging threats.

  5. Conduct Comprehensive External Network Scans:

    • Perform regular and thorough external network vulnerability scans on all public-facing assets to identify open ports, services, and potential vulnerabilities. Ensure scan results are successfully captured and analyzed.

    • Implement a process for prompt remediation of identified technical vulnerabilities.
