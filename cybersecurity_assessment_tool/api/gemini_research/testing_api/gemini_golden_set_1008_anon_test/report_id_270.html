Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The questionnaire responses indicate several critical security control deficiencies:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is not required for accessing email, logging into computers, or accessing sensitive data systems. This significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

  • Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of an AUP means there are no clear guidelines for employees regarding the proper use of organizational IT resources, which can lead to misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance.

  • Insufficient Security Awareness Training: While annual training is conducted, new employees do not receive initial security awareness training. This leaves new hires vulnerable to social engineering attacks and potentially unaware of critical security protocols during their initial onboarding period.

Risk Assessment

This section synthesizes the findings from the security control review, technical scan, and pre-existing risk register.

Identified High-Level Risks (from Questionnaire Analysis)

  1. High Risk: Credential Compromise and Unauthorized Access

    • Overview: The absence of MFA for email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems significantly elevates the risk of successful credential compromise leading to unauthorized access. Phishing, brute-force attacks, or stolen credentials can provide attackers with direct access to critical organizational resources.

    • Severity: Critical

    • Affected Elements: Email systems, employee workstations, sensitive data repositories.

  2. High Risk: Insider Threat and Policy Violations

    • Overview: Without an employee acceptable use policy, there are no clear guidelines for appropriate technology use, increasing the likelihood of misuse, accidental data exposure, or malicious insider activities without a formal framework for accountability. Severity: High

    • Affected Elements: All organizational IT systems and data.

  3. High Risk: Social Engineering and New Employee Vulnerability

    • Overview: The lack of security awareness training for new employees leaves them particularly susceptible to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing, pretexting) and unaware of crucial security best practices, potentially compromising the organization from day one.

    • Severity: High

    • Affected Elements: New employees, organizational network and data via human vector.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance [Organization Name]’s cybersecurity posture:

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Systems:

    • Action: Mandate MFA for email access, computer logins, and all sensitive data systems.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if credentials are compromised.

  2. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive AUP outlining proper use of company assets, data handling, and internet/email usage. Distribute it to all employees and obtain signed acknowledgments.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear expectations, reduces misuse, and provides a framework for addressing policy violations.

  3. Establish Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Action: Integrate mandatory security awareness training into the onboarding process for all new hires.

    • Benefit: Ensures new employees are immediately aware of security best practices, organizational policies, and common threats like phishing.

Ongoing and Strategic Recommendations

  1. Conduct Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Action: Perform comprehensive external and internal vulnerability scans regularly (e.g., quarterly) and engage in periodic penetration tests to identify and remediate weaknesses before they can be exploited.

    • Benefit: Proactively identifies new vulnerabilities and validates the effectiveness of existing security controls.

  2. Maintain a Robust Asset and Risk Management Program:

    • Action: Continuously update the risk register, tracking identified risks and their remediation status. Ensure all external-facing assets are identified and regularly scanned.

    • Benefit: Provides a clear picture of the attack surface and helps prioritize security efforts.

  3. Regularly Review and Update Security Policies and Procedures:

    • Action: Periodically review all security policies, including the AUP and training materials, to ensure they remain relevant and effective against evolving threats.

    • Benefit: Keeps the security posture aligned with current threats and best practices.
