Risk Assessment

Based on the security control review, several high and critical risks have been identified. No technical risks were found via the Nmap scan (due to no open ports), and no pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided.

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access Without Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email, accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks, potentially leading to unauthorized access, data breaches, and business email compromise. Critical
Lack of MFA for Computer Login Absence of MFA for computer login significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems should credentials be compromised, enabling lateral movement and privilege escalation. Critical

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across the Board:

    • Email Access: Immediately implement MFA for all email accounts. This should be a mandatory requirement for all users.

    • Computer Login: Deploy and enforce MFA for logging into all company computers, especially those accessing sensitive data or networks.

    • Sensitive Data Systems: Ensure all systems containing sensitive data are protected by mandatory MFA.

  • Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines the expected behavior for employees regarding company IT assets, data handling, internet usage, and security protocols.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, with regular re-affirmations.

  • Regular and Comprehensive Security Awareness Training:

    • Expand the security awareness training program to include all employees, not just new hires.

    • Conduct mandatory security awareness training for all employees at least once per year, with refresher courses or micro-training modules throughout the year to cover emerging threats and best practices.

    • Include topics such as phishing detection, social engineering, password hygiene, safe browsing, and incident reporting procedures.

  • Consider Deeper Network Scans/Vulnerability Assessments:

    • While the current scan found no open ports, this was a limited external assessment. Consider performing authenticated internal vulnerability scans and penetration testing to identify weaknesses within the internal network that are not visible externally.

  • Establish a Robust Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Implement processes for regularly identifying, assessing, and remediating vulnerabilities across all systems and applications.

    • Maintain an up-to-date inventory of all assets and known vulnerabilities.

Conclusion

The current cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name] exhibits significant vulnerabilities stemming from critical gaps in fundamental security controls, particularly the lack of pervasive MFA and essential policy/training frameworks. Addressing these foundational weaknesses through the recommended actions is paramount to establishing a more resilient and secure operating environment against prevailing cyber threats.
