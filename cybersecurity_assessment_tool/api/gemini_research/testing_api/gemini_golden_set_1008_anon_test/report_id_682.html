Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of the security control review and the absence of pre-existing vulnerabilities, the following risks have been identified.

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Email Access The absence of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for email access significantly increases the risk of account compromise through phishing, credential stuffing, or brute-force attacks. Compromised email accounts can lead to business email compromise (BEC), data breaches, and further network intrusion. High
Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems Not requiring MFA for access to systems containing sensitive data leaves these critical assets vulnerable to unauthorized access if primary credentials are stolen or guessed. This directly impacts data confidentiality and integrity. High
Inadequate New Employee Security Training A lack of security awareness training for new employees means they may not be immediately aware of organizational security policies, common threats (e.g., phishing), or best practices, making them susceptible to social engineering attacks and potentially introducing vulnerabilities from day one. Medium

Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Email Access (High Priority): Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts. This is a fundamental security control that drastically reduces the risk of email account compromise.

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems (High Priority): Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all systems and applications that store or process sensitive organizational data. This adds a critical layer of security to protect valuable information assets.

  • Introduce Mandatory New Employee Security Awareness Training (Medium Priority): Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees as part of their onboarding process. This ensures that new hires are immediately educated on security policies, threat recognition, and best practices.

  • Review Existing Security Awareness Training: While annual training is conducted, ensure its comprehensiveness and effectiveness, especially given the gap in new employee training. Regularly update content to reflect current threat landscapes.

  • Comprehensive Vulnerability Assessment: Given the limited scope of the provided scan data, it is recommended to conduct a full, authenticated internal and external vulnerability assessment and penetration test to identify potential technical weaknesses that were not covered by the current analysis.
