Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name].

Priority Recommendations (High Severity)

  1. Implement a Comprehensive Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Develop and formally implement a clear and concise Employee Acceptable Use Policy.

    • The AUP should define acceptable and unacceptable use of all company IT resources, including but not limited to networks, internet access, email, software, hardware, and mobile devices.

    • Ensure the policy covers data handling, privacy expectations, and consequences for non-compliance.

    • Conduct mandatory training for all employees on the new AUP and obtain written acknowledgment of their understanding and commitment to adhere to the policy.

    • Integrate AUP review into annual security awareness training and new employee onboarding processes.

Ongoing Best Practices

  1. Maintain Strong Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Controls:

    • Continue the excellent practice of requiring MFA for email access, computer logins, and access to sensitive data systems.

    • Regularly review MFA implementations for effectiveness and expand coverage to other critical systems as needed.

  2. Sustain Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Continue with regular security awareness training for all employees, including specialized training for new hires and annual refreshers for the entire workforce.

    • Update training content periodically to address emerging threats and organizational policy changes (e.g., the new AUP).

  3. Implement Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • While no open ports were identified in this specific scan, implement a schedule for routine internal and external network vulnerability scans.

    • Consider engaging in periodic penetration testing to identify more sophisticated vulnerabilities that automated scans might miss.
