Recommendations

To address the identified security gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all computer and workstation logins across the organization. This should apply to both local and network-based authentications.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access stemming from stolen or phished credentials, serving as a critical layer of defense.

    • Priority: Critical

  • Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a comprehensive AUP outlining acceptable use of company IT resources, data handling procedures, and security expectations. Distribute it to all employees, ensure mandatory reading, and require formal acknowledgment of understanding and compliance.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear boundaries for IT asset usage, reduces the likelihood of misuse or accidental data breaches, and fosters a culture of security awareness and accountability.

    • Priority: High

  • Implement Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Action: Extend security awareness training to all employees at least annually. This training should cover current cyber threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware, business email compromise), organizational policies, and best practices for data protection and incident reporting.

    • Benefit: Keeps employees informed and vigilant against evolving cyber threats, transforming them into a strong defensive line against social engineering and other attacks.

    • Priority: High

  • Secure Open Port 22 (SSH):

    • Action:

      1. Identify Service: Determine the exact service and version running on port 22.

      2. Harden Configuration: Ensure the service is securely configured by:

        • Disabling root login directly via SSH.

        • Enforcing strong, key-based authentication instead of passwords where possible.

        • Restricting access via firewall rules to only trusted IP addresses or networks.

        • Implementing an intrusion detection/prevention system (IDS/IPS) to monitor for malicious activity.

      3. Patch Regularly: Keep the service and underlying operating system fully patched to their latest stable versions to mitigate known vulnerabilities.

      4. Minimize Exposure: If the service is not strictly required to be externally accessible, consider closing the port or severely limiting its exposure.

    • Benefit: Minimizes the attack surface, protects against potential exploits targeting SSH or other services on this port, and reduces the risk of unauthorized remote access.

    • Priority: Medium

Conclusion

The current cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name] demonstrates significant policy and control deficiencies, particularly concerning MFA implementation for computers, the absence of an Acceptable Use Policy, and insufficient annual security awareness training.
