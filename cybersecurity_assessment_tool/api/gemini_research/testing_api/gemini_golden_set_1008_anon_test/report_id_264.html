Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The questionnaire highlights significant deficiencies in critical security areas:

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Deficiencies: The absence of MFA across email, computer login, and sensitive data systems is a severe vulnerability. This leaves the organization highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks, which can lead to unauthorized access and data breaches.

  • Lack of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without an AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding the use of organizational IT resources, increasing the risk of misuse, data leakage, and compliance failures.

  • Insufficient Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the lack of annual refresher training for all employees means that the workforce may not be up-to-date on the latest threats and best practices, negating the long-term effectiveness of initial training.

Recommendations

To address the identified gaps and enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Immediately deploy and enforce MFA for all email accounts, computer logins, and access to sensitive data systems. This is a foundational control that significantly reduces the risk of credential compromise.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines appropriate use of organizational IT resources, data handling, and security expectations for all employees.

    • Ensure all employees review and acknowledge the AUP, with regular refreshers.

  3. Establish Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires. This training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, social engineering), data protection, and policy adherence.

  4. Provide Complete and Valid Data for Future Assessments:

    • For any future assessments, ensure that all requested input data, specifically network scan results (e.g., Nmap XML output) and pre-existing risk registers, are provided in a correct and parseable format. This is crucial for a comprehensive technical and risk evaluation.

  5. Conduct a Comprehensive Technical Vulnerability Scan:

    • Once valid network scan data can be obtained, perform a detailed technical vulnerability assessment to identify and prioritize any outdated software, misconfigurations, or known vulnerabilities on network-accessible systems.

  6. Review and Address Pre-existing Vulnerabilities:

    • Upon receiving valid pre-existing risk data, conduct a thorough review to ensure all previously identified vulnerabilities are being tracked, prioritized, and remediated effectively.
