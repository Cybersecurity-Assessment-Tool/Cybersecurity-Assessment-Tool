Analysis of Security Controls

The organization has implemented strong MFA requirements across email, computer logins, and sensitive data systems, which is a commendable practice significantly reducing the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials. Regular annual security awareness training for all employees is also a positive control.

However, two critical gaps were identified:

  • Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): An AUP is fundamental for setting expectations regarding the use of organizational IT resources, protecting company assets, and mitigating legal and operational risks. Without one, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities, potentially leading to misuse, data breaches, or compliance issues.

  • Lack of Security Awareness Training for New Employees: While annual training is conducted, the absence of specific training for new hires means that individuals are onboarded without essential security knowledge from day one, exposing the organization to risks during a critical period.

Risk Details

  • Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy: This foundational control is absent, leaving the organization vulnerable to insider threats, data leakage, and compliance failures due to undefined boundaries for employee conduct with IT assets.

  • No New Employee Security Awareness Training: While annual training is positive, the lack of initial training for new employees means they may operate without critical security knowledge during their most vulnerable initial period, increasing the risk of phishing, malware, or social engineering incidents.

  • Pre-existing Risk: Unencrypted Web Server: The organization’s existing risk register identifies an "Unencrypted Web Server" with Port 80 being open. However, the current network scan performed for this assessment found Port 80 to be closed on the target [Target IP]. This discrepancy suggests either the risk is associated with a different asset, has been remediated since its initial identification, or the scope of the current scan did not cover the affected asset. This risk, if active, could lead to sensitive data exposure and compromise.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actionable recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name].

  1. Develop and Implement an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a comprehensive AUP outlining appropriate use of company devices, networks, software, and data.

    • Communicate the AUP clearly to all employees, requiring acknowledgement of understanding.

    • Integrate the AUP into employee onboarding and ongoing training programs.

    • Review and update the AUP annually or as organizational policies and technologies evolve.

  2. Introduce Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees:

    • Develop a dedicated security awareness module for all new hires, to be completed within their first week of employment.

    • Cover essential topics such as phishing identification, password hygiene, data handling, and reporting suspicious activity.

    • Ensure this initial training complements the existing annual security awareness program.

  3. Investigate "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk Discrepancy:

    • Validate the scope and current status of the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk identified in the organization’s existing vulnerabilities.

    • Determine if the affected element ("Port 80 is open") applies to an asset not covered by the current network scan, or if it represents a historical finding that has since been remediated.

    • If the risk is still active on any organizational asset, ensure appropriate remediation (e.g., closing the port, implementing HTTPS with valid SSL/TLS certificates) is prioritized to protect data in transit.

  4. Maintain Strong MFA Controls:

    • Continue to enforce MFA for email access, computer logins, and sensitive data systems.

    • Regularly review MFA implementations for effectiveness and expand coverage to other critical systems as needed.

  5. Continue Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Maintain the current program of annual security awareness training for all employees to reinforce best practices and educate on evolving threats.

Conclusion

[Organization Name] demonstrates a good foundation in critical areas like Multi-Factor Authentication and recurring security training. However, the absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy and specific training for new employees represents significant organizational risks that could be exploited. While the network scan provided a positive finding of no open ports on the target, the pre-existing "Unencrypted Web Server" risk requires further clarification and potential remediation. Addressing these identified gaps will substantially strengthen the organization’s overall cybersecurity posture and resilience against modern threats.
