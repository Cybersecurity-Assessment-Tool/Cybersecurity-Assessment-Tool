Questionnaire Analysis

The review of the security control questionnaire highlights critical areas where the organization’s security posture is deficient:

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: While MFA is used for email and computer login, its absence for sensitive data systems presents a significant vulnerability. Compromised credentials could directly lead to unauthorized access to critical data.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without an AUP, employees may lack clear guidelines on appropriate use of company resources, data handling, and security expectations. This can lead to increased risks of misuse, policy violations, and accidental data breaches.

  • Inadequate Annual Security Awareness Training: Although new employees receive training, the lack of recurring annual training for all employees means the workforce may not be updated on evolving threats, social engineering tactics, and current security best practices. This increases the organization’s susceptibility to human-factor exploits.

Recommendations

To address the identified security gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Immediately evaluate and implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all systems storing or processing sensitive organizational data.

    • Prioritize critical systems and data repositories for MFA deployment.

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly outlines employee responsibilities regarding the use of company IT resources, data handling, internet usage, and security protocols.

    • Distribute the AUP to all employees, obtain signed acknowledgments, and integrate it into new employee onboarding.

    • Conduct regular reviews and updates of the AUP.

  • Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Develop and implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires.

    • The training should cover current threats such as phishing, ransomware, social engineering, data protection, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Regularly update training content to reflect the latest threat landscape and organizational policies.

  • Conduct Regular Network Scans:

    • Implement a schedule for regular, authenticated network vulnerability scans to identify open ports, services, and configuration weaknesses.

    • Ensure that scan results are properly parsed, analyzed, and integrated into the risk management process.

  • Maintain a Vulnerability Management Program:

    • Establish a robust vulnerability management program to track, prioritize, and remediate identified vulnerabilities.

    • This includes subscribing to threat intelligence feeds and regularly updating security baselines.

These recommendations, when implemented, will significantly improve the organization’s defensive capabilities against common cyber threats and foster a stronger security-aware culture.
