6. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

6.1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

  • Recommendation: Immediately implement and enforce MFA for all email accounts across the organization.

  • Recommendation: Extend MFA requirements to all systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational or client data.

  • Rationale: MFA significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if passwords are stolen or compromised, providing a critical layer of defense against prevalent attack vectors.

6.2. Establish Comprehensive Security Awareness Training

  • Recommendation: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program for all new employees during their onboarding process. This should cover common threats, organizational policies, and reporting procedures.

  • Recommendation: Institute a mandatory annual (or more frequent) security awareness training program for all existing employees. This training should be updated regularly to reflect current threat landscapes and best practices (e.g., phishing simulation exercises).

  • Rationale: Employees are often considered the "human firewall." Educating them on cybersecurity best practices and common attack methods is crucial in preventing social engineering attacks and fostering a security-conscious culture.

6.3. Continuous Improvement

  • Recommendation: Regularly review and update security policies, including the acceptable use policy, to align with evolving threats and organizational changes.

  • Recommendation: Consider conducting regular vulnerability assessments and penetration tests, both internally and externally, to proactively identify and remediate technical weaknesses.

  • Rationale: Cybersecurity is an ongoing process. Continuous monitoring, assessment, and improvement are essential to maintain a strong defensive posture against dynamic threats.
