Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Develop, disseminate, and enforce an AUP that clearly outlines the acceptable use of all IT resources, including email, internet, hardware, and software.

    • Ensure all employees read and acknowledge the AUP, ideally during onboarding and annually thereafter.

  2. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Institute a program for mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, not just new hires.

    • Training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), organizational policies (e.g., AUP, data handling), and best practices for securing data and systems.

    • Utilize engaging and relevant content to ensure maximum employee retention and engagement.

  3. Conduct Comprehensive Network Scans (External & Internal):

    • Perform regular, targeted network vulnerability scans of all internet-facing assets to identify open ports, vulnerable services, and misconfigurations.

    • Include authenticated vulnerability scans of internal networks and systems to uncover issues that may not be visible externally.

    • Regularly review and remediate findings from these scans.

Conclusion

The current cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name] shows a commendable effort in implementing Multi-Factor Authentication across several critical access points. However, fundamental gaps exist in policy enforcement and continuous employee education, specifically regarding an Acceptable Use Policy and annual security awareness training. Addressing these areas is crucial for establishing a stronger human firewall and reducing the overall attack surface. Regular, comprehensive technical assessments, combined with robust policy implementation and ongoing training, will significantly enhance the organization’s resilience against evolving cyber threats.
