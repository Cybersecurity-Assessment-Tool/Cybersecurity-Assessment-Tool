Risk Assessment

This section correlates findings from the security control review, technical scans, and pre-existing risk data to provide a comprehensive view of the current risk landscape for .

Critical Gaps from Security Control Review

Based on the questionnaire responses, several critical security control gaps have been identified:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Without MFA, email accounts are highly susceptible to credential stuffing, phishing, and brute-force attacks, potentially leading to business email compromise (BEC).

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: Absence of MFA for computer logins increases the risk of unauthorized access to workstations and internal network resources if credentials are stolen.

  • Absence of Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding company IT assets, increasing legal and security risks.

  • Insufficient Security Awareness Training for New Employees: New employees are particularly vulnerable targets for social engineering and phishing attacks due to a lack of immediate security awareness, posing an immediate risk upon onboarding.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis of the security posture, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the security of :

Immediate Actions

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication for all email access across the organization. This is a critical control against credential compromise.

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins: Extend MFA requirements to all computer login processes, especially for privileged accounts and endpoints accessing sensitive data.

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP for all employees, clearly outlining the proper use of company IT resources, data, and internet access. Ensure all employees acknowledge and understand this policy.

  • Re-evaluate "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk: Due to the discrepancy between the network scan and the existing risk entry, conduct an immediate review to determine if Port 80 is indeed closed and whether the web server in question is no longer exposed or has been secured. Update the risk register to reflect the current status.

Security Awareness and Policy

  • Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees: Integrate a mandatory, comprehensive security awareness training module into the onboarding process for all new hires before they gain access to company systems.

  • Regular Policy Reviews: Establish a schedule for regular review and updates of all security policies, including the AUP and MFA policies, to ensure they remain relevant and effective.

Technical Improvements (Long-Term)

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning: Implement a routine schedule for internal and external vulnerability scanning to continuously identify and address potential weaknesses.

  • Patch Management Review: Ensure a robust patch management program is in place to keep all operating systems and applications up-to-date, minimizing exposure to known vulnerabilities.

  • Access Control Review: Regularly review access controls to ensure that only authorized personnel have access to sensitive systems and data, aligning with the existing MFA implementation for sensitive data systems.

Conclusion

While the technical scan showed a relatively clean external perimeter with no immediately exploitable open ports on the target , the identified gaps in foundational security controls represent significant vulnerabilities. Addressing the lack of MFA for email and computer logins, coupled with establishing a robust security awareness program and an AUP, should be top priorities for . Furthermore, the discrepancy concerning the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk highlights the importance of keeping risk registers current and aligned with actual technical posture. Proactive implementation of these recommendations will significantly strengthen the organization’s cybersecurity defenses against prevalent threats.
