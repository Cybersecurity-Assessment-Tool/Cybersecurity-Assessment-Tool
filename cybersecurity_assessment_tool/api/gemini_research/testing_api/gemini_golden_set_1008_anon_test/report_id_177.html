Risk Assessment

This section synthesizes findings from the security control review, technical scans, and an existing risk register to provide a consolidated view of identified risks.

Identified Gaps from Security Control Review

The following high-level risks were identified from the questionnaire responses:

  • Lack of MFA for Email Access: Significantly increases the risk of account compromise, leading to phishing, data breaches, and impersonation. Email is a primary vector for cyberattacks, and without MFA, a simple password compromise can have severe consequences.

  • Absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may not understand their responsibilities regarding company IT assets and data. This can lead to misuse of resources, unauthorized data handling, compliance issues, and an increased likelihood of security incidents due to unclarified user behavior.

  • Infrequent Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, annual or regular refresher training for all employees is crucial. Cyber threats evolve rapidly, and infrequent training leaves the workforce vulnerable to new social engineering tactics, phishing campaigns, and malware distribution methods, increasing human error as a attack vector.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are provided to address the identified risks and improve the overall cybersecurity posture. Recommendations are prioritized based on their potential impact and foundational importance.

High Priority Recommendations

  • Implement Mandatory MFA for Email Access: Immediately mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all organizational email accounts. This foundational security control significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials, phishing, or brute-force attacks.

  • Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) that clearly defines the proper use of all company IT resources, network access, data handling procedures, and employee responsibilities regarding cybersecurity. Ensure all employees formally acknowledge and understand this policy upon hiring and annually thereafter.

  • Establish Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees: Implement a mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees, not just new hires. This training should cover current threat landscapes, social engineering techniques (e.g., phishing, vishing), malware identification, secure password practices, and data protection best practices.

Validation and Review Recommendations

  • Re-validate Port 8080 Security Status: Conduct an immediate and thorough investigation into the open port 8080, specifically focusing on the application displaying the title. Verify the existing assessment that this port is "secured" and a "false positive" against the live scan findings.

    • If the port is indeed intentionally open and secured (e.g., by strong access controls, network segmentation, or it’s an internal-only interface), ensure comprehensive documentation of these mitigation controls exists and is regularly reviewed.

    • If the port is found to be unsecure, misconfigured, or inappropriately exposed, it must be secured or closed immediately to prevent unauthorized access to potentially sensitive data.

Conclusion

This assessment highlights several critical areas for improvement in [Organization Name]’s cybersecurity posture, particularly concerning foundational controls like MFA for email, the establishment of an acceptable use policy, and regular security awareness training. Addressing these gaps, alongside the urgent re-validation of existing risk assessments (specifically for port 8080), will significantly enhance the organization’s resilience against evolving cyber threats and improve its overall security maturity.
