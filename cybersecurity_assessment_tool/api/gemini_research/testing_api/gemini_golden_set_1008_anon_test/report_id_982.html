Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the organization’s cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement MFA for Email Access: Immediately implement and enforce Multi-Factor Authentication for all email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of account compromise through phishing, credential stuffing, or brute-force attacks.

  2. Implement MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Deploy and mandate MFA for access to all systems that store or process sensitive organizational data. This adds a crucial layer of security, protecting valuable assets even if primary credentials are compromised.

  3. Conduct Regular Network Vulnerability Scans: Establish a routine schedule for external and internal network vulnerability scans. Regularly analyze these scan results to identify open ports, outdated software, misconfigurations, and other technical vulnerabilities. Prioritize and remediate findings based on severity.

  4. Develop a Vulnerability Management Program: Formalize a process for identifying, assessing, triaging, and remediating vulnerabilities. This includes regular reviews of technical findings and updates to risk assessments.

  5. Continuous Security Awareness: While security awareness training is already in place, consider reinforcing the importance of MFA and vigilance against phishing attacks, especially in light of the current MFA gaps.

Conclusion

The cybersecurity posture assessment reveals critical vulnerabilities, primarily driven by the lack of Multi-Factor Authentication in key areas such as email access and sensitive data systems. These gaps represent high-priority risks that require immediate attention. While the organization demonstrates strengths in security awareness training and acceptable use policies, these are foundational controls that can be undermined by the absence of stronger authentication mechanisms.

Addressing the identified MFA deficiencies and establishing a robust vulnerability management program, including regular technical scans, will significantly enhance the organization’s defense against modern cyber threats. Proactive mitigation of these risks is paramount to protecting organizational assets, data, and reputation.
