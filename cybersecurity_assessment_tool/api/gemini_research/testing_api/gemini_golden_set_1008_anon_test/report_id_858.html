Risk Assessment

This section integrates findings from the security control review, technical scan results, and pre-existing risk documentation to provide a comprehensive view of the organization’s risk landscape.

Identified Security Gaps (from Questionnaire)

Based on the security control review, the following critical gaps have been identified, posing significant risks to the organization:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: The absence of multi-factor authentication for computer logins is a critical vulnerability. It dramatically increases the risk of unauthorized access to internal systems and data in the event of compromised employee credentials. This represents a High Severity risk.

  • Lack of Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the failure to provide annual security awareness training to all employees leads to knowledge decay and leaves the workforce susceptible to evolving cyber threats, such as sophisticated phishing campaigns and social engineering attacks. This constitutes a Medium-High Severity risk.

  1. The risk has been successfully mitigated on the scanned target, and the risk register entry is outdated.

  2. The risk pertains to a different asset within the organization’s infrastructure that was not covered by the current scan.

  3. The overview description in the risk register is inaccurate or has become inaccurate for the scanned asset.

Recommendations

To address the identified security gaps, reconcile discrepancies, and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

Immediate Actions

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins: Mandate and implement multi-factor authentication for all computer logins across the organization. This is a critical control that will significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials.

  • Establish Annual Security Awareness Training Program: Develop and implement a mandatory, comprehensive annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threats (e.g., phishing, ransomware), secure data handling, password hygiene, and organizational security policies.

Risk Management Actions

  • Reconcile "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk Status: Conduct an immediate investigation into the discrepancy between the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk (indicating Port 80 open) and the network scan results (Port 80 closed on ).

    • If the risk has been mitigated for the scanned asset, formally update its status in the risk register to "Mitigated" or "Closed."

    • If the risk pertains to other assets not covered by this scan, identify those assets and implement appropriate controls, such as implementing SSL/TLS encryption, closing the port if not required, or placing the service behind a protective firewall.

    • If the scan target itself was not representative of the original risk, clarify the scope of the risk and conduct targeted scans on relevant assets.

  • Implement Regular Risk Register Reviews: Establish a formal process for periodic (e.g., quarterly) review and update of the organization’s risk register. This ensures accuracy, reflects current technical realities, and helps prioritize risk mitigation efforts.

General Best Practices

  • Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing: Conduct regular internal and external vulnerability scans, complemented by periodic penetration tests, to proactively identify and address technical weaknesses across the entire IT infrastructure.

  • Policy Enforcement and Review: Regularly review and update all security policies, including the acceptable use policy, to ensure they remain relevant, are effectively communicated to employees, and are consistently enforced.

Conclusion

[Organization Name] has a foundational set of security controls in place, demonstrating a commitment to certain aspects of cybersecurity. However, the identified gaps in pervasive MFA for computer logins and comprehensive annual security awareness training, along with the need to actively reconcile existing risk documentation with current technical findings, present clear and actionable areas for immediate improvement. By diligently addressing these recommendations, the organization can significantly strengthen its defenses against prevalent cyber threats, enhance its overall resilience, and foster a more mature security posture.
