5. Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive assessment, the following actionable recommendations are provided to mitigate identified risks and significantly enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name].

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Broadly:

    • Priority Action: Immediately implement mandatory MFA for all email accounts across the organization.

    • Next Step: Extend MFA implementation to all computer logins, including desktop, laptop, and server access, as well as VPN connections.

    • Benefit: Drastically reduces the risk of unauthorized access stemming from stolen or weak credentials, a primary vector for cyberattacks.

  2. Develop and Enforce a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a clear and comprehensive AUP that outlines the proper and secure use of all organizational IT assets, networks, and data by employees.

    • Implementation: Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, preferably on an annual basis.

    • Benefit: Establishes clear behavioral guidelines, reduces the likelihood of misuse, and strengthens the organization’s legal and compliance posture.

  3. Establish Mandatory New Employee Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Develop and implement a mandatory security awareness training program specifically for all new hires during their onboarding process.

    • Content: Training should cover critical topics such as identifying phishing and social engineering attempts, strong password practices, data handling best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Benefit: Equips new employees with foundational security knowledge from day one, significantly reducing human-related vulnerabilities.

  4. Harden SSH Server Configurations:

    • Action: Conduct a thorough review and harden the configurations of all SSH servers where port 22 is open.

    • Best Practices: Disable root login, enforce key-based authentication exclusively, use strong passphrases for keys, restrict SSH access to a whitelist of trusted IP addresses, implement rate limiting to deter brute-force attacks, and ensure the SSH daemon is regularly updated to the latest stable version. Consider integrating MFA for SSH access if technically feasible.

    • Benefit: Minimizes the attack surface for remote access and significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized exploitation of SSH services.

  5. Initiate Regular Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing:

    • Action: Schedule and conduct periodic internal and external vulnerability scans to proactively identify technical weaknesses in systems and applications.

    • Advanced Step: Engage qualified third-party experts to perform annual penetration tests.

    • Benefit: Provides a proactive and adversary-centric view of the organization’s security posture, identifying exploitable vulnerabilities before malicious actors can discover and leverage them.
