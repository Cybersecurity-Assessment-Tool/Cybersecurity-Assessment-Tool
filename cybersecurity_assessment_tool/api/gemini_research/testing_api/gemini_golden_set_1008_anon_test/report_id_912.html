3.1. Key Findings from Questionnaire Analysis

  • Critical Gap: No MFA for Email. This is the most severe vulnerability identified. Email accounts are frequent targets for phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and account takeover attacks. New hires are often the most vulnerable to social engineering and targeted phishing campaigns due to their lack of familiarity with organizational security policies, common threat indicators, and reporting procedures. This gap creates an unnecessary entry point for attackers.

  • Positive Controls Identified: The organization correctly implements MFA for logging into computers and accessing sensitive data systems. The presence of an employee acceptable use policy and annual security awareness training for all employees are also positive indicators of foundational security efforts.

6. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and significantly enhance [Organization Name]’s overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are strongly advised:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access (Critical Priority).

    • Enable and rigorously enforce MFA for all email accounts across the organization immediately.

    • Conduct an awareness campaign to educate users on the importance of MFA and provide clear instructions for enrollment and usage.

    • Consider deploying phishing-resistant MFA methods (e.g., FIDO2 security keys) for highly privileged accounts.

  2. Establish Mandatory Security Awareness Training for New Employees (High Priority).

    • Develop and implement a comprehensive security awareness program that all new employees must complete as an integral part of their onboarding process.

    • The training should cover essential topics such as identifying phishing attempts, strong password practices, data handling protocols, and the procedure for reporting suspicious activities.

  3. Conduct a Comprehensive Technical Vulnerability Scan and Penetration Test.

    • Initiate regular, authenticated vulnerability scans and external/internal penetration tests across all network assets (servers, workstations, web applications) to identify and remediate technical weaknesses.

    • Prioritize patching and configuration hardening based on vulnerability severity, exploitability, and asset criticality.

  4. Regularly Review and Update Security Policies.

    • Ensure that all cybersecurity policies, including the Acceptable Use Policy, are reviewed and updated at least annually to align with evolving threat landscapes, regulatory requirements, and best practices.

  5. Maintain and Enhance Annual Security Awareness Training.

    • Continue the annual security awareness training for all employees, ensuring the content is regularly updated to include the latest threat intelligence, attack vectors, and organizational-specific risks.
