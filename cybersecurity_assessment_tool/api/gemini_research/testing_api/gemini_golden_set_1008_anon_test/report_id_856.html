5. Risk Assessment

Based on the analysis of the questionnaire and technical scan, the following key risks have been identified. No pre-existing vulnerabilities were provided in the input data.

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of MFA for Computer Access Users can log into computers without a second factor, significantly increasing the risk of credential theft, unauthorized access, and lateral movement by attackers if a single factor (e.g., password) is compromised. High
Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without a formal AUP, employees lack clear guidelines for appropriate use of company assets, data, and internet resources. This can lead to misuse, data leakage, legal non-compliance, and introduces ambiguity regarding employee responsibilities. High
Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training Employees are not regularly updated on current cybersecurity threats, best practices, and organizational policies. This increases their susceptibility to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing, vishing) and reduces overall organizational resilience against human-factor exploits. High

6. Recommendations

To address the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Computer Logins:

    • Mandate MFA for all user logins to company computers and endpoints, especially those accessing sensitive data or internal networks.

    • Explore hardware tokens, authenticator apps, or biometrics as MFA options.

    • Provide clear instructions and support for employee adoption.

  2. Develop and Implement a Comprehensive Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Draft a clear, concise, and legally compliant AUP that outlines employee responsibilities regarding the use of company IT resources, data handling, internet usage, and personal devices.

    • Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP, ideally during onboarding and annually thereafter.

    • Integrate AUP principles into ongoing security awareness training.

  3. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Implement a recurring security awareness training program for all employees, conducted at least once per year.

    • Training should cover current threat landscapes (e.g., phishing, ransomware), password best practices, data handling, incident reporting procedures, and the organization’s specific security policies (including the AUP).

    • Use engaging and interactive formats to maximize effectiveness.

  4. Expand Technical Security Assessments:

    • Conduct more comprehensive vulnerability scans and penetration tests against all internet-facing assets and internal networks.

    • Review firewall rules and network segmentation regularly.

    • Implement continuous monitoring solutions to detect and respond to threats in real-time.
