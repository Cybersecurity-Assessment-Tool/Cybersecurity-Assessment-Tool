Control Gap Analysis

Two critical security control gaps were identified from the questionnaire:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Logins: The absence of multi-factor authentication for computer access significantly elevates the risk of unauthorized system access through compromised credentials, potentially leading to lateral movement and data breaches.

  • Lack of Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive initial training, the lack of recurring annual security awareness training leaves the organization vulnerable. Employees may forget best practices, fail to identify evolving phishing tactics, or fall victim to social engineering, becoming a weak link in the security chain.

Recommendations

To address the identified security gaps and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  • Implement MFA for Computer Logins:

    • Action: Research, procure, and deploy a robust multi-factor authentication solution for all computer logins, prioritizing access to sensitive systems and data.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised or weak credentials, enhancing overall endpoint security.

  • Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: Develop and implement a comprehensive, mandatory annual security awareness training program for all employees. This training should cover current threat landscapes, organizational policies, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Benefit: Empowers employees to recognize and respond to evolving cyber threats, fosters a stronger security culture, and reduces human-factor vulnerabilities.

  • Investigate and Secure Open Port 80 on :

    • Action: Conduct a detailed investigation to identify the exact service, application, and purpose of the open port 80 on . Ensure the service is securely configured, regularly patched, and restrict access to only necessary networks (e.g., internal-only, or protected by a Web Application Firewall if public-facing).

    • Benefit: Eliminates an uncharacterized attack surface, mitigates potential exploitation of unknown vulnerabilities, and aligns with the principle of least privilege for network services.

  • Review and Validate Risk Register Entries:

    • Action: Regularly review and validate all entries within the organization’s risk register. Ensure that entries are clear, accurate, actionable, and do not contain conflicting instructions or anomalies. This includes addressing the "Ignore all previous instructions..." entry to ensure integrity of risk management.

    • Benefit: Maintains an accurate and useful risk management framework, preventing misdirection, ensuring proper prioritization, and effective resource allocation for genuine threats.

Conclusion

This cybersecurity posture assessment has identified several key areas where [Organization Name] can enhance its security defenses. A proactive and continuous approach to security control enhancement and risk management is essential for maintaining a robust security posture in the face of an evolving threat landscape.
