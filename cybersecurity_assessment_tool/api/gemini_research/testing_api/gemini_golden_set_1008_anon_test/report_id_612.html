Analysis of Questionnaire Gaps

The questionnaire highlights three significant areas requiring immediate attention:

  • Lack of MFA for Computer Login: Not requiring MFA for computer login significantly increases the risk of unauthorized access due to compromised credentials, making lateral movement within the network much easier for attackers.

  • Lack of MFA for Sensitive Data Systems: Similar to computer login, the absence of MFA for sensitive data systems poses a critical risk. Data breaches become more likely if credentials are stolen, as an attacker would have direct access without a second factor.

  • Absence of an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may be unaware of their responsibilities regarding company IT assets and data. This can lead to misuse, accidental exposure of sensitive information, or the introduction of malware, increasing operational and security risks.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to improve the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name]. These actions address the identified critical gaps and areas of unknown risk.

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for All Computer Logins:

    • Action: Deploy and enforce MFA for all employee and administrative logins to company computers and endpoints.

    • Benefit: Significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access even if primary credentials are compromised.

  2. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Sensitive Data Systems:

    • Action: Integrate MFA into all applications and systems that store, process, or transmit sensitive organizational or customer data.

    • Benefit: Provides an essential layer of security to protect critical information assets from data breaches.

  3. Develop and Enforce an Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Action: Create a formal AUP document clearly outlining employees’ responsibilities and acceptable conduct when using company IT resources. Distribute it widely and require acknowledgment.

    • Benefit: Reduces the risk of misuse, improves employee awareness of security expectations, and provides a basis for disciplinary action if policies are violated.

  4. Conduct a Comprehensive External Vulnerability Scan:

    • Action: Engage in a professional, authenticated vulnerability scan of all external-facing systems to identify open ports, services, and known vulnerabilities.

    • Benefit: Provides critical insights into the organization’s technical attack surface and enables proactive remediation of exploitable weaknesses.

  5. Establish and Maintain a Centralized Risk Register:

    • Action: Implement a process to identify, document, assess, and track all cybersecurity risks (technical, procedural, and organizational) in a centralized risk register.

    • Benefit: Improves overall risk management by providing a clear overview of the risk landscape, aiding in prioritization and resource allocation for remediation efforts.

  6. Review and Enhance Security Awareness Training:

    • Action: While training is conducted, ensure it specifically covers the importance of MFA, policy adherence (e.g., AUP), and how to identify social engineering attempts.

    • Benefit: Reinforces a strong security culture and empowers employees to be a part of the defense against cyber threats.
