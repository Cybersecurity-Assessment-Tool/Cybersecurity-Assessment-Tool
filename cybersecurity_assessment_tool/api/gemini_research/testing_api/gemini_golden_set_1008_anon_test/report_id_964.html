Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) The absence of MFA for email, computer login, and sensitive data systems significantly increases the risk of credential theft and unauthorized access through phishing, brute-force attacks, or stolen credentials. Absence of Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) Without an AUP, employees may not be aware of appropriate use of company resources, leading to potential misuse, data leakage, or introduction of malware. This lack of governance creates ambiguity and increases operational risk. Medium

Recommendations

To significantly improve the cybersecurity posture of , the following actionable recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

    • Deploy MFA for all email accounts to prevent unauthorized access via compromised credentials.

    • Implement MFA for all computer logins, especially for administrative accounts and critical workstations, to strengthen endpoint security.

    • Enforce MFA for access to all systems containing sensitive data to protect against data breaches.

  2. Develop and Enforce an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Create a comprehensive AUP that clearly defines appropriate use of company assets, data, and networks.

    • Ensure all employees review and formally acknowledge the AUP annually to maintain awareness and compliance.

  3. Enhance Security Awareness Training Program:

    • Mandate annual security awareness training for all employees, not just new hires, to keep knowledge current.

    • Training should cover current threat landscapes (e.g., advanced phishing, ransomware), phishing recognition techniques, data handling best practices, and clear incident reporting procedures.

  4. Regular Vulnerability Management:

    • Conduct regular internal and external vulnerability scans to continuously identify and address potential weaknesses.

    • Consider engaging in periodic penetration testing to identify exploitable weaknesses that automated scans might miss and to test the effectiveness of existing controls.

  5. Review and Update Policies Regularly:

    • Establish a schedule for regular review and updates of all security policies and procedures (e.g., AUP, incident response plan, data handling policies) to adapt to evolving threats and organizational changes.
