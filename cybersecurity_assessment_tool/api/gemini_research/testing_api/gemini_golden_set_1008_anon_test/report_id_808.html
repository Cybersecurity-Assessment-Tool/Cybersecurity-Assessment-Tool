6. Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following recommendations are provided:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email Access:

    • Enable and enforce MFA for all organizational email accounts. This significantly reduces the risk of account compromise due to stolen or weak credentials.

    • Educate employees on the importance of MFA and provide clear instructions for setup and usage.

  2. Establish Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training:

    • Develop and implement a comprehensive security awareness training program for all employees, conducted at least once per year.

    • Training should cover topics such as phishing, social engineering, password hygiene, data handling, and incident reporting.

    • Consider simulated phishing campaigns to test and reinforce training effectiveness.

  3. Regularly Review and Update Security Policies:

    • Ensure that the Acceptable Use Policy and other security policies are regularly reviewed and updated to reflect current threats and best practices.

    • Communicate policy updates clearly to all employees.

  4. Conduct Regular Technical Vulnerability Assessments:

    • Although the current scan showed no open ports, regular scans (internal and external) are crucial to detect newly exposed services, misconfigurations, or unpatched systems.

    • Consider authenticated vulnerability scans to identify weaknesses within systems themselves, beyond just network accessibility.
