Risk Assessment

Based on the security control review (questionnaire results), several high-priority risks stemming from policy and training gaps have been identified. These risks primarily relate to the human element, which is often the weakest link in cybersecurity.

Identified Risks
Risk Name Overview Severity
Lack of Employee Acceptable Use Policy The absence of a formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) means employees may not be fully aware of their responsibilities regarding company IT assets, data handling, and acceptable online behavior. This increases the risk of misuse, data breaches, and non-compliance with industry regulations. High
Absence of New Employee Security Awareness Training New employees are not receiving essential security awareness training upon onboarding. This makes them highly susceptible to social engineering attacks (e.g., phishing, pretexting) and increases the likelihood of unintentional security incidents from the start of their employment. High
Absence of Annual Security Awareness Training Without regular (at least annual) security awareness training, employees may forget critical security best practices, fail to adapt to evolving threat landscapes, and become complacent. This significantly increases the organization’s vulnerability to human-factor risks over time. High

Recommendations

To mitigate the identified risks and enhance the overall cybersecurity posture, the following actionable recommendations are provided:

Policy Development and Implementation

  • Implement a Formal Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Develop and formally implement a clear, comprehensive AUP that explicitly outlines employee responsibilities for using company resources, handling sensitive data, internet and email usage, and general security best practices.

    • Ensure all employees, new and existing, formally acknowledge, understand, and agree to adhere to the AUP upon hiring and annually thereafter.

    • Integrate AUP review into annual performance discussions or policy attestations.

Security Awareness Training Programs

  • Establish Mandatory New Employee Security Awareness Training:

    • Develop a mandatory, robust security awareness training program for all new hires during their onboarding process.

    • This training should cover essential topics such as recognizing phishing and social engineering attempts, strong password hygiene, proper data handling procedures, reporting suspicious activities, and the importance of the AUP.

    • The training should be interactive and accessible.

  • Institute Mandatory Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Implement a mandatory, annual security awareness training program for all employees, regardless of their role or tenure.

    • Regularly update training content to address new and emerging threats (e.g., AI-driven phishing), recent security incidents, changes in organizational policies, and relevant regulatory requirements.

    • Consider incorporating simulated phishing campaigns and other interactive exercises to reinforce learning and assess the effectiveness of the training program. Provide immediate feedback and additional resources for those who fall victim to simulations.
