Analysis of Questionnaire Responses

  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for Email: The absence of MFA for email access is a significant vulnerability. Email is a primary vector for phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and account takeover attacks. Without MFA, compromised credentials can lead to unauthorized access to sensitive communications and systems.

  • Employee Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): The lack of an AUP leaves employees without clear guidelines on the acceptable use of organizational IT resources. This can lead to security incidents due to misuse, unauthorized software installations, or risky online behavior.

  • Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive training, the lack of annual refreshers for all employees means that critical security knowledge may fade or become outdated, increasing the risk of human-factor incidents.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are provided to enhance the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name]. These recommendations are actionable and prioritized to address the most critical findings.

Immediate Actions (High Priority)

  • Implement MFA for Email Access: Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts accessing organizational email. This is a critical control to prevent unauthorized access from compromised credentials.

  • Verify "Unencrypted Web Server" Risk: Investigate the status of the "Unencrypted Web Server" risk. Determine if this applies to any active asset within the organization and, if so, ensure the server is either closed, protected, or configured to use HTTPS/SSL for all communications.

Short-Term Actions (Medium Priority)

  • Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Create a comprehensive AUP outlining the acceptable use of organizational IT assets, data, and networks. Communicate the AUP to all employees and obtain their acknowledgement.

  • Institute Annual Security Awareness Training: Implement mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees. Training should cover current threats, phishing detection, password hygiene, incident reporting, and the newly established AUP.

Long-Term Strategic Recommendations

  • Regular Risk Assessments: Establish a schedule for regular, comprehensive cybersecurity risk assessments that include both technical vulnerability scanning and policy/control reviews.

  • Incident Response Plan Development: Develop and maintain a formal Incident Response Plan (IRP) to guide the organization’s actions in the event of a security incident. Conduct tabletop exercises to test the plan’s effectiveness.

  • Continuous Monitoring: Implement continuous monitoring solutions to detect and respond to security events in real-time, enhancing the ability to identify and mitigate threats proactively.

Conclusion
