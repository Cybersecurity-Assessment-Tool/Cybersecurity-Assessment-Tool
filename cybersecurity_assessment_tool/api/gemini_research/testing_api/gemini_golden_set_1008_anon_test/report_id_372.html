Analysis of Security Control Gaps

The questionnaire highlights significant vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malicious actors:

  • Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): The complete absence of MFA across critical access points (email, computer login, and sensitive data systems) is a severe vulnerability. This significantly increases the risk of successful credential theft, phishing, and brute-force attacks leading to unauthorized access.

  • No Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Without a formal AUP, employees may lack clear guidelines regarding the appropriate use of company IT assets and the handling of sensitive data. This can lead to increased risks of data leakage, misuse of resources, and potential non-compliance with regulatory requirements.

  • Insufficient Annual Security Awareness Training: While new employees receive initial training, the lack of mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees means that critical security best practices may not be consistently reinforced or updated. This leaves the organization vulnerable to evolving threats such as sophisticated social engineering and advanced phishing campaigns.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are crucial for immediately enhancing the cybersecurity posture of [Organization Name], focusing on the identified control gaps and the need for comprehensive data:

  1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Across All Critical Systems:

    • Priority 1 (High): Mandate MFA for all email access (e.g., via Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, on-premise solutions).

    • Priority 1 (High): Implement MFA for all computer logins, especially for administrative accounts and systems processing sensitive data.

    • Priority 1 (High): Deploy MFA for access to all sensitive data systems, cloud applications, and VPN connections.

  2. Develop and Implement an Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

    • Priority 2 (Medium): Draft a comprehensive AUP outlining employee responsibilities, permitted use of company IT assets, data handling procedures, and prohibited activities.

    • Priority 2 (Medium): Ensure all employees read, understand, and formally acknowledge the AUP upon hiring and periodically thereafter (e.g., annually).

  3. Establish and Maintain Annual Security Awareness Training for All Employees:

    • Priority 1 (High): Implement mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees, covering essential topics such as phishing identification, social engineering tactics, password hygiene, data protection best practices, and incident reporting procedures.

    • Priority 2 (Medium): Conduct regular simulated phishing exercises to gauge employee susceptibility and reinforce training effectiveness.

  4. Conduct a Comprehensive Technical Vulnerability Scan and Assessment:

    • Priority 1 (High): Immediately re-run a full external and internal network vulnerability scan using reputable tools, ensuring valid and parsable output is generated.

    • Priority 1 (High): Incorporate the findings into a dedicated vulnerability management program, prioritizing patching and mitigation efforts.

    • Priority 1 (High): Provide complete and valid data on existing vulnerabilities for a holistic risk assessment to identify and address any known, unmitigated threats.
