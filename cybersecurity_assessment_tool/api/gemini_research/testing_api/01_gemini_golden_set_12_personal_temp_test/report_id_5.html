Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of the cybersecurity posture for Modern Myth, based on a combination of network scanning, a security controls questionnaire, and a review of pre-existing risks. The assessment identified several critical and high-risk vulnerabilities that require immediate attention to mitigate the threat of unauthorized access, data breach, and potential ransomware attacks.

Key findings indicate a pattern of insecure Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) exposure, significant gaps in Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for critical systems like email and sensitive data repositories, and a procedural deficiency in security training for new employees.

The overall security posture is assessed as having a high level of risk. Prioritized, actionable recommendations are provided in Section 6 to address these findings and strengthen the organization’s defenses.

Correlated Risk Assessment

This section synthesizes findings from the security control review, the technical scan, and pre-existing risk data. By correlating these data points, we can identify systemic issues and prioritize remediation efforts.

Severity Finding Description
Critical Systemic RDP Exposure The technical scan identified RDP open on 10.10.10.51. This finding, combined with a pre-existing risk for RDP on 10.10.10.50, indicates a pattern of insecure remote access configurations. This significantly increases the risk of a network compromise.
Critical Lack of MFA on Critical Systems The questionnaire revealed that MFA is not enforced for email or access to sensitive data systems. This gap allows an attacker with compromised credentials (e.g., from a phishing attack or RDP brute-force) to gain direct access to the organization’s most valuable assets.
High Inadequate Employee Onboarding Security The lack of mandatory security awareness training for new employees creates a window of vulnerability. New hires may be unaware of policies and more susceptible to social engineering or phishing attacks, creating an immediate weak link in the organization’s security.

Recommendations

The following recommendations are prioritized to address the identified risks.

Remediate Systemic RDP Exposure (Critical)

  • Immediate Action: If RDP access is not essential for business operations on 10.10.10.51 and 10.10.10.50, disable the service or block port 3389 at the firewall immediately. If access is required, restrict it to specific, trusted source IP addresses.

  • Long-Term Solution: Implement a secure remote access solution, such as a Virtual Private Network (VPN) or a Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) platform. All remote administrative access should occur through this managed and monitored channel, not via direct exposure on the internet.

Implement Comprehensive MFA (Critical)

  • Immediate Action: Prioritize the deployment of MFA for all email accounts (ModernMyth.org). This is the single most effective control to mitigate business email compromise (BEC) and phishing-related account takeovers.

  • Strategic Action: Develop and enforce a policy requiring MFA for all systems, with the highest priority on those containing sensitive or critical data. Conduct an inventory of systems to identify and enable MFA capabilities.

Strengthen Employee Security Training (High)

  • Immediate Action: Integrate the organization’s existing annual security awareness training into the mandatory onboarding process for all new employees and contractors. This ensures a baseline level of security knowledge from day one.

  • Continuous Improvement: Supplement annual training with regular phishing simulations and security bulletins to maintain a high level of awareness among all staff.
